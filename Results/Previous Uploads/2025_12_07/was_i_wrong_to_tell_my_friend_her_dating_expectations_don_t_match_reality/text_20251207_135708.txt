Was I Wrong to Tell My Friend Her Dating Expectations Don’t Match Reality?

A close friend of mine recently joined a few dating apps for the first time, and she’s been venting nonstop about how disappointing the experience has been. She’s in her late teens and often talks about wanting a relationship, feeling lonely being single, and hoping to find someone soon. In terms of appearance, she’s pretty average—nothing extreme in either direction, just a normal-looking person.

Within a few days, she started complaining that nearly every guy who liked her was “unattractive,” “too short,” or simply “not her type,” and that she wasn’t matching with any of the people she considered good-looking. Out of curiosity, I asked her to show me who she meant. When she scrolled through her likes, the guys there seemed totally reasonable—regular, decent-looking people who looked about on the same overall level as her. I gently suggested she consider giving some of them a chance, especially since she frequently talks about wanting a serious relationship.

She immediately shut that down and insisted she was far more attractive than the men liking her. I pointed out that the people she considers very attractive aren’t matching her back, which probably means the match is uneven. That set her off, and she snapped that I was being rude and insulting.

Now she says I crossed a line by telling her she’s not above the guys she’s dismissing. I’m wondering if being straightforward about the mismatch between her expectations and her actual matches was out of line.
