Was I wrong for pulling the bus stop cord early so I don’t miss my stop?

I take a connecting bus to work every morning in a mid-sized West Coast city. Public transit here isn’t exactly smooth, but this particular route gets me close to my office, so I rely on it.

The issue is that this bus line doesn’t announce every stop, only the major hubs. On top of that, the bus won’t automatically stop at smaller stops unless someone pulls the cord. If no one signals, the driver just keeps going. I’ve asked a couple of drivers before if they could let me know when my stop is coming up, but most either shrug it off or seem too busy to help.

I’ve tried using navigation apps and the city’s transit tracker, but the updates lag. By the time the app shows I’m at my stop, we’ve already passed it. I also try looking out the window, but my bus leaves at 5:50 in the morning, and during the winter months it’s still dark. A lot of the stops look similar, so it’s hard to judge visually.

After missing my stop several times and having to walk nearly 25 minutes instead of the usual 4, I started doing something different. When I think I’m about one stop away, based on timing and counting intersections, I pull the stop cord. The bus will pause briefly at the next stop, no one gets off, and then it continues. That pause helps me confirm where we are so I can pull it again at the right moment for my actual stop.

I don’t love doing this because I know it causes a short, unnecessary stop. But if I don’t, I risk being late for work. Yesterday the driver clearly noticed and called me out in front of other passengers, saying I was misusing the signal. I apologized, but I felt embarrassed.

I genuinely don’t know what the better solution is. I’m not trying to inconvenience anyone, I’m just trying to get to work on time without adding a long walk in the dark.

Was I wrong for handling it this way?
