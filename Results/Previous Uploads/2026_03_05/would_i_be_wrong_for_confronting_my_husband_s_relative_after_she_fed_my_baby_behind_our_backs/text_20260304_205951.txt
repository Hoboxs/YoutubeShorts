Would I be wrong for confronting my husband’s relative after she fed my baby behind our backs?

My son is ten months old and has a severe allergy to corn and anything derived from it. This isn’t a mild sensitivity. If he consumes even a small amount, he ends up with a full-body eczema flare, intense stomach pain, and bloody, mucus-filled stools for up to three days. It’s awful to watch.

Everyone in the family knows about this. I’ve spent countless hours researching ingredient labels, cross-contamination risks, and safe brands. When my husband took our two kids, our four-year-old daughter and the baby, to his family’s rural property overnight while I stayed in the city for an obligation, I packed every single thing our son would need to eat. Every purée, snack, and bottle was prepared in advance so there wouldn’t be any guesswork.

For context, the property has several houses close together. His great-aunt and great-uncle live in one home, and his grandparents live in another a short walk away.

After breakfast at his relative’s house, our son started getting fussy for his bottle. My husband had washed the bottles and left them drying at the grandparents’ place, which is only a few hundred meters away. He ran over to grab one and was gone for less than three minutes.

When he came back, his aunt proudly announced that the baby “just loved” her homemade cinnamon rolls. My husband immediately panicked and reminded her about the allergy, saying we can’t give him random food without checking ingredients. She brushed it off and said it was “just a couple of bites” and that he’d be fine.

He was not fine.

It’s now a day later and I’ve been holding a miserable, screaming baby who is covered in inflamed eczema and clearly in gastrointestinal pain. He’s had bloody stools and hasn’t been able to settle. I am exhausted and furious.

What makes it worse is how quickly and casually it happened. She waited until my husband stepped away for literally a few minutes. It feels reckless at best and sneaky at worst. This isn’t about differing parenting styles, this is about a documented medical issue that causes my child real suffering.

I want to message her and tell her exactly how harmful and irresponsible her decision was. Part of me wants to be blunt about how unacceptable it is to feed someone else’s child anything without explicit permission, especially when there’s a known allergy involved. But I also know that once I send something heated, I can’t take it back.

Would I be wrong to send a strongly worded message telling her how serious this is and how angry I am about what she did?
