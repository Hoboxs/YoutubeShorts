Is It Wrong That She Stopped Coming Over After One Complaint?

I live in a shared house where I pay rent, along with another long-term tenant who rents the lower level. My partner works demanding overnight shifts at a medical facility, usually from early evening until early morning. After finishing work, she would sometimes come to my place around sunrise to shower quickly and sleep. It was never loud or drawn out—just a short rinse before resting after a long night.

One morning, the downstairs tenant told the homeowner that the sound of the shower woke him up. The message was passed to me with a request that my partner wait to shower until “everyone was awake.” When I relayed this to her, she felt uncomfortable continuing to come over in the mornings. After working all night, she didn’t want to feel like she was inconveniencing people just by doing something basic, so she quietly decided to stop coming by altogether.

Later, when the homeowner found out, she became upset—not about the shower itself, but about the fact that my partner chose not to come over anymore. She insisted that wasn’t what she meant, but from our perspective, being asked to avoid showering after an overnight shift made it clear that her presence wasn’t welcome.

Now I’m stuck in the middle. I didn’t argue or escalate things, I just passed along the concern, and my partner made her own calm decision. Was it unreasonable for her to step back after feeling like a disruption, or for me not to push harder against the complaint in the first place?
