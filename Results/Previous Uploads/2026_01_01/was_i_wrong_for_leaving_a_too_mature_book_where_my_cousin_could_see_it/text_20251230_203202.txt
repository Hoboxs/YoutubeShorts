Was I Wrong for Leaving a “Too Mature” Book Where My Cousin Could See It?

I’m a college student in my early twenties, and for the past few weeks my uncle and his teenage daughter have been staying with my family while their place is being renovated. Because of space, my cousin—who’s in her early teens—has been sleeping in my bedroom. Before they arrived, my mom asked me to put away any books she felt were inappropriate for someone that age. I complied and locked several novels in a small drawer, but there wasn’t enough room for everything, so I had to make judgment calls about what stayed out.

I left a few dystopian novels on my shelf, including one I’d studied years ago for a literature exam. That particular book was filled with my old notes and messy annotations from when I was a teenager myself, and I honestly didn’t think it would interest her at all. Between the dense themes and my borderline-illegible handwriting, it didn’t seem like something she’d pick up casually.

I was wrong. One afternoon I came home to find out my cousin had been reading it, and my uncle had discovered this and immediately shut it down, telling her she was far too young for that kind of material. Then he turned on me. He accused me of being irresponsible and careless, saying that by leaving the book on my shelf I had exposed her to something inappropriate and that it was my fault for not locking it away like the others.

I didn’t intend for her to read it, and I genuinely thought I’d taken reasonable precautions given the limited space I had. Still, his reaction made me second-guess myself. Was I actually wrong for leaving that book out, or was this an overreaction to something I couldn’t realistically control?
