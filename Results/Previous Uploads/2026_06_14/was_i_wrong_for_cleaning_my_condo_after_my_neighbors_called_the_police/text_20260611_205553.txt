Was I Wrong for Cleaning My Condo After My Neighbors Called the Police?

I’m a 36-year-old man living with my husband in a three-story condominium building in Melbourne, Australia. We live on the middle floor, with one unit above us and another below.

For the past several months, our downstairs neighbors have repeatedly complained about what we consider normal household noise. Their first complaint was that the vibration from our phone alarms, which go off shortly after 5:00 a.m. because my husband works at a hospital, was waking them up. We disabled the vibrations and adjusted our routine, but the complaints continued.

They also began objecting to everyday sounds like walking around the condo, opening and closing doors, and what they described as “stomping.” The building has hardwood floors, and we hear similar noise from the unit above us, which we’ve always considered a normal part of apartment living.

Things escalated when I installed a portable cooling unit in our bedroom during a heatwave. I started early in the evening and finished well before quiet hours, yet the neighbors confronted us the next day and threatened to contact authorities.

At that point, we reported the ongoing harassment to the property management company. After management spoke with them, the direct confrontations mostly stopped.

Last Friday, I took a day off work and spent part of the late morning and early afternoon vacuuming, cleaning, and reorganizing a few rooms. Nothing unusual—just routine household chores.

About 30 minutes later, a police officer knocked on my door.

The officer explained that my downstairs neighbors had reported that I was deliberately stomping around the unit to retaliate against them after management had become involved. After a brief conversation, it was obvious there was no issue, and the officer left without taking any action.

The incident left me frustrated and uneasy. We’ve already modified our routines, avoided conflict, and tried to be accommodating, yet it feels like normal activities—walking around, vacuuming during the day, or setting an alarm for work—are being treated as unacceptable.

My husband thinks we should continue documenting everything and only involve management if necessary. Some friends think the neighbors are being unreasonable, while others wonder if there could be a noise issue we’re unaware of.

Now I’m wondering whether I’m being too defensive or whether it’s reasonable to expect that people in a shared building can perform ordinary daily activities without having the police called on them.

Was I wrong for continuing to live normally in my own home rather than changing my routine further?
