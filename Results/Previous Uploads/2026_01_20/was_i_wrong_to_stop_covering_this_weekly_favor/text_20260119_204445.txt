Was I Wrong to Stop Covering This Weekly Favor?

Last autumn, my partner Alex and I fell into a routine around their Sunday evening shifts. Alex works those nights, and I usually have Sundays off. The problem is that Alex tends to sleep until about twenty minutes before they need to leave. Lately, that has meant panicked mornings and last minute requests. More than once, Alex asked if I could take their car and fill it with gas before they left for work, since there was not enough time to get dressed, fuel up, and still arrive on time.

At first, I did not mind. I would take the car, stop for fuel, sometimes grab a coffee or a snack, and occasionally run errands like picking up groceries while Alex slept. It was never framed as an expectation, more like a favor, and it did not happen every single week. I also gently suggested more than once that it might help if Alex filled the tank earlier in the week or woke up a little sooner on Sundays. They agreed in theory and said they would try, so I kept helping when it fit into my plans.

This weekend was different. We had heavy snow overnight and through most of the day. I made a point of shopping ahead of time so I could stay in, and I was honestly looking forward to a quiet day at home in comfortable clothes, not braving icy roads. I also really dislike driving in winter conditions, especially in a car that is not mine. When Alex asked again if I could go fill the tank, I told them no. I explained that I did not feel safe driving in the snow and that I had no reason to go out anyway.

Alex pushed back, saying the gas station was close and that they were running out of time. I repeated that I was uncomfortable and did not want to risk it. They left for work clearly upset, skipped saying goodbye, and later sent me a message saying they ended up stopping for gas themselves and arrived a few minutes late. The tone made it clear they blamed me for the stress and delay.

Now I am sitting here wondering if I crossed a line by saying no this time, especially since I had helped so often before. I see it as a favor that became an expectation, while Alex seems to see it as something I should have continued doing regardless of circumstances. I cannot tell if I was setting a reasonable boundary or being unsupportive when it mattered.
