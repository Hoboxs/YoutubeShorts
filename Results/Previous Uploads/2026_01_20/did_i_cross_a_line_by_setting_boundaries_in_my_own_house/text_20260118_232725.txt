Did I Cross a Line by Setting Boundaries in My Own House?

I am a forty eight year old parent, and I own a small house where my two sons, aged twenty two and twenty, currently live. I moved out last year but kept the house in their names to give them stability while they get on their feet. I only ask that they split the utility bills evenly, keep one room available for me when I visit, and most importantly respect each other and the shared spaces. Money is tight for everyone, so I have tried to be generous and flexible. Growing up, the spare bedroom that is technically mine was always the main hangout space. It had the best television, the most seating, and became the default common area for years. When I moved out, my younger son asked if he could move his bed into that room, and I agreed on the clear condition that it would remain a shared space because his older brother still uses it regularly to relax and unwind.

The issue began with my younger son’s girlfriend, who I will call Mia. Over time she started letting herself into the house whenever she wanted, even on days when my younger son was not home. At first I did not see a problem, especially since my older son had no personal issues with her and tried to be polite. A few days ago, however, my older son told me something that made my stomach drop. Mia had come over while my younger son was out. My older son let her in, assuming it was fine. He went into the shared room to watch a movie on the futon, which is something he has always done. Mia followed him into the room, lay down on the bed that my younger son had moved in, and partway through the movie she told my older son she was uncomfortable with him being there and asked him to leave because she wanted to nap. Feeling awkward and unsure of his place, he left the room in silence, even though it was a common space in a house he lives in.

The next day, emotions boiled over. My older son confronted his brother and explained what had happened and how humiliated he felt being pushed out of a shared room in his own home by someone who does not live there. That was when I was finally told about the situation. I was upset not just on his behalf, but because it violated the clear understanding we had when I moved out. That room was never meant to become private territory, especially not for a guest. I stepped in and set a firm boundary. I told my younger son that his girlfriend is welcome only when he is physically home and that she is not to be at the house alone anymore. I explained that if she feels uncomfortable being around his brother in a shared home when he is not present, then she should not be there at all.

My younger son thinks this rule is unfair and controlling. From my perspective, I am protecting my older son’s right to feel comfortable in the home he lives in and enforcing the agreement we already had about shared spaces. I am now questioning whether I handled this the right way or if setting this boundary makes me the unreasonable one.
