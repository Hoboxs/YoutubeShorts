Was I wrong to take back an expensive wedding gift?

My partner and I had been planning a big trip to Europe for a long time, and by late 2025 we had everything booked and locked in. Flights, hotels, activities, all of it was carefully arranged months in advance so we could get good prices and avoid any last minute stress. It was something we were really looking forward to.

Around the same time, my partner’s sister announced that her wedding would take place the following fall. We were excited for her, and in the moment, we made what felt like a generous and thoughtful offer. We told her and her fiancé that as an early wedding gift, we would cover the cost for them to join us on our trip. That meant flights, accommodations, and activities, essentially the whole experience. We thought it would be a meaningful way to celebrate with them.

She turned the offer down at the time. She said it would feel a bit uncomfortable joining our trip and that she would rather save her time off for a different vacation she had in mind. We respected that decision and moved on, assuming that was the end of it.

Then, just recently, with less than two weeks before our departure, she reached out and said she had changed her mind and wanted to take us up on the offer after all. That completely caught us off guard. By that point, everything had already been booked for months, and prices had changed dramatically. When I looked into it, the cost to add two more people at the last minute was thousands more than it would have been earlier.

That is where things became difficult. While we are financially comfortable, the idea of spending that much extra money for something that could have been handled earlier did not sit right with me. It felt unreasonable to absorb such a large increase simply because the decision had been delayed. After thinking it through, I told my partner that I did not feel comfortable honoring the original offer under these new circumstances.

I decided to be the one to communicate that to her. I explained that we were no longer able to cover the trip due to how much the costs had risen so close to the departure date. As expected, the reaction was not great. It created tension within the family, and now things feel awkward whenever the topic comes up. Some people understand where I am coming from, while others feel that an offer is an offer, especially when it was tied to something as important as a wedding.

Now I am left questioning whether I handled this fairly. On one hand, the situation has changed in a significant way since the original offer was made. On the other hand, I did make that offer freely, and I can see why taking it back might feel disappointing or even hurtful. I cannot tell if I set a reasonable boundary or if I should have followed through no matter the cost.
