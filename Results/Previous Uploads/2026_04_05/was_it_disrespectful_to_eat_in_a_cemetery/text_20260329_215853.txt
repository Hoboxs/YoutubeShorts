Was it disrespectful to eat in a cemetery?

Earlier today, I had a medical appointment where I was specifically instructed not to eat or drink anything beforehand. By the time it was over, I felt drained, shaky, and extremely hungry. It had been hours since I last had anything, and I could feel it catching up with me. I knew I needed to sit down and eat fairly quickly, so I stopped at a nearby grocery store and picked up something simple, a croissant and a bottle of cola.

The problem was finding somewhere to actually sit and eat. The area around the store was crowded and loud, with traffic rushing by and not many places to rest. I did not feel great and just wanted a quiet spot where I could sit for a few minutes without all the noise and movement around me. The closest place that seemed calm and peaceful was a cemetery just a short walk away.

I went inside, walked a bit until I found a bench, and sat down. I was not near anyone, and there did not seem to be any kind of service or gathering happening nearby. The atmosphere was quiet, and honestly, it felt like the only place where I could properly relax for a moment. I quietly ate my food and drank my cola, trying to recover from how I was feeling. I was not making any noise, leaving any mess, or interacting with anything around me. I was just sitting there, minding my own business.

As I was finishing up, an older woman, probably in her seventies, walked past me. She paused and asked who I was there visiting. I answered honestly and told her I was not visiting anyone, just sitting somewhere quiet to eat. She looked surprised, almost taken aback, and then told me that I should be ashamed of myself for being disrespectful. After that, she walked away without saying anything else.

That moment stayed with me. I sat there for a bit longer, suddenly feeling out of place in a way I had not before. I finished what I had and left shortly after, but I could not stop thinking about what she said. On one hand, I understand that a cemetery is a meaningful and emotional place for many people. It is somewhere people go to grieve, remember loved ones, and feel a sense of connection. From that perspective, seeing someone there for a completely unrelated reason might come across as inappropriate or even insensitive, especially if someone is in the middle of mourning.

On the other hand, I was not treating the space carelessly or disrespectfully. I was not being loud, disruptive, or leaving anything behind. I simply needed a quiet place to sit after feeling unwell, and that was the only option nearby that provided that kind of environment. In my mind, I was using the space in a calm and respectful way, not interfering with anyone else’s experience.

Now I keep going back and forth in my head. I can see how my presence might have felt wrong to someone who views that space very differently, especially if they were dealing with loss. At the same time, I do not feel like my actions were intentionally disrespectful or harmful. It feels more like a clash of expectations about what is appropriate in a place like that.

I am left wondering if I unknowingly crossed a social boundary that I should have been more aware of, or if this was simply a situation where two people had very different views on how a quiet public space can be used.
