Did I ruin my wedding by refusing to invite their grandmother?

I feel like my wedding is turning into another battle from a divorce that happened years ago, and now I am wondering if I am being unreasonable or finally drawing a boundary I should have set long ago. About seven years ago, my marriage ended after a major conflict over my former wife’s mother. At the time, I was the parent staying home with the children while my wife worked, and she decided she wanted her elderly mother to move in with us permanently.

I was strongly against it. I knew exactly what would happen if she moved in. The responsibility of caring for her would fall on me, and I did not want to become a full-time caregiver. I suggested assisted living instead, but my former wife made it clear she was moving her mother in no matter what. Since she was the primary earner, she basically told me the decision was already made.

That conflict became one of the major reasons our marriage ended. After the divorce, I went back to work and only managed to get weekend custody. I tried for more time with the children, but financially I was not in a strong position back then.

Unfortunately, after the divorce, the caregiving responsibilities slowly shifted onto my children whenever they were at their mother’s house. What started as helping out became a major part of their lives. Even now, years later, my three children, all now adults, still live there and spend a huge amount of time caring for their grandmother while their mother works long hours to support everyone.

I have argued with my former wife about this for years because I believe my children were pushed into adult responsibilities far too young. They love their grandmother, but I can see how exhausted and resentful they have become. I have repeatedly offered for them to move in with me full time, but they always say they cannot leave because no one else would take care of her.

Now I am getting remarried, and I thought at least this milestone would be simple. My children already knew the wedding date long in advance, so when invitations went out, I assumed everything was fine. Then my oldest called and said their grandmother needed to be invited too because otherwise none of them could attend.

I said no immediately.

The truth is, I have never had a good relationship with her. She treated me badly for years, even before the marriage fell apart, and I do not want her at my wedding. I told my daughter their mother could arrange care for one evening, especially with so much notice. My daughter responded that her mother would likely still be working and that there would be no one else to watch her.

I still refused. I said their mother would need to figure something out because their grandmother was not invited.

That is when the conversation exploded. My daughter accused me of abandoning the family years ago and said this was just another example of me refusing to step up. I argued that I was not abandoning anyone and that my children were fully welcome to attend. I simply did not want someone at my wedding who had played such a negative role in my life.

Now all three of my children have told me they will not come unless their grandmother can come too.

At this point, I honestly do not know if I am standing my ground reasonably or letting old resentment destroy my relationship with my children even further.
