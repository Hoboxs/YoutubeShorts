Was I wrong for asking kids not to turn the park into a slip slide?

I am a parent to a three year old, and one of our daily routines is walking to the nearby park because she absolutely loves the slide. It is her favorite part of the playground, and it is something simple that makes her really happy. Recently, though, what should have been a normal outing turned into a frustrating experience.

When we arrived at the park, there were a couple of groups of adults sitting on the grass while their children played nearby. At first, everything seemed typical, but as we got closer, we realized something was off. The kids were dressed for water play and had poured water and soap all over every slide. They had essentially turned the entire play area into a slippery surface, and they were taking turns racing down and sliding off into the wood chips at the bottom.

It immediately raised concerns for us. Not only did we not want our daughter soaked in soapy water, but we were also worried about her safety. Watching older children slide at high speed and get thrown off at the bottom made it clear that it could easily lead to someone getting hurt. We tried to make the best of it by drying off one slide and standing nearby so she could use it safely. One of the kids even helped briefly, but overall, the situation did not change. The parents of those children stayed where they were, watching but not stepping in, even as their kids kept going back for more soap and water.

We made a few polite comments, hoping they would realize that what they were doing was affecting everyone else trying to use the park, but we were ignored. We chose not to push it further in the moment because we did not want to create a scene in front of our child.

Later, I shared the experience in a local online group, asking neighbors to be more mindful when using shared spaces. I suggested that activities like that might be better suited for places designed for water play, so that everyone could enjoy the park safely. Instead of understanding, the response was surprisingly negative. Many people defended the behavior, saying children should just be allowed to have fun and suggesting we should have let our daughter join in.

That reaction left me questioning everything. I understand wanting kids to enjoy themselves, but it felt like the entire space had been taken over in a way that excluded others. Now I am wondering if I was being too strict, or if it is reasonable to expect some level of consideration in a shared public place.
