Was I rude for snapping about my lunch at work?

I am someone who has always been a bit private about what I eat, and for reasons I cannot fully explain, it really bothers me when people comment on my food. Even simple remarks or questions can make me feel uncomfortable, almost like I am being judged or put on display. Because of that, I usually avoid eating around others and prefer to have my lunch alone.

Until recently, I managed that by eating in my car during breaks. It was quiet, and no one asked me anything. But after some landscaping work removed the shade from my usual parking spot, sitting in the car became unbearable, so I started using the breakroom again. That is where the issue began.

There is a coworker who loves talking about food. She is always asking people what they brought, how they made it, and sharing her own ideas. Every day since I started eating inside again, she has spent several minutes asking me questions about my lunch. At first, I tried to tolerate it, but it kept happening over and over, and I found myself getting more irritated each time.

Yesterday, it reached a breaking point. As soon as I sat down, she began asking the usual questions about what I had made, whether I cooked it myself, and how often I prepare that kind of meal. Without really thinking, I responded by asking why she was so focused on my food and said that I just wanted to eat quietly without having to explain everything on my plate. I tried to keep my tone light, but I meant what I said.

She immediately looked hurt, apologized in a short way, and left the room. Since then, she has been distant and barely speaking to me. Now I am left wondering if I handled it poorly. I do not regret wanting space, but I also did not expect things to turn so tense. Was I wrong for speaking up the way I did, or was it fair to set a boundary even if it came out a bit sharp?
