Was I wrong for calling my roommate’s mom after her boyfriend kept eating my food and coming over?

I’m a nurse and work long, unpredictable shifts. Because of that, every Sunday I meal prep for the entire week, nothing fancy, just chicken, rice, and vegetables portioned into containers so I can grab them and go. It’s the only way I stay fed during busy weeks.

My roommate has been dating a guy, I’ll call him Mike, for about three months. At first, him coming over was no big deal. Then it turned into him staying over three to five nights a week. I didn’t love it, but I tolerated it.

Two weeks ago, I came home after a brutal shift, exhausted and starving. I opened the fridge and found all my meal prep containers empty. Mike had eaten the entire week’s worth of food while my roommate was at work. He didn’t ask. He didn’t text. He just helped himself.

I was furious. I texted my roommate, and she apologized, saying Mike “felt bad.” I told her it wasn’t about whether he felt bad, it was about basic boundaries. You don’t eat someone else’s food without asking.

Last weekend, it happened again. This time, instead of fully empty containers, there were scoops taken out of each one. Like he’d sampled them all. That honestly felt worse somehow.

I snapped. I told my roommate that Mike couldn’t stay over anymore. I said I didn’t care if he promised not to do it again, I was too angry and uncomfortable at that point. It genuinely stressed me out knowing he was in the apartment while I was at work.

She didn’t take me seriously. Mike kept coming over. That made me feel completely powerless in my own home, like I had zero say.

Out of frustration, I called my roommate’s mother and told her what was going on. I asked her to talk to her daughter about having Mike stop coming over. After that, my roommate finally called me to “talk.” She said she thought we could just work it out between us and reminded me of the one time she and Mike brought me dinner when they went out. She accused me of making her choose between me and him.

Now she’s giving me the silent treatment and telling people I “lost it over food.”

I know calling her mom was extreme. But I felt ignored and disrespected in my own space. I don’t think this is just about food, it’s about boundaries and feeling safe in your home.

Did I overreact?
