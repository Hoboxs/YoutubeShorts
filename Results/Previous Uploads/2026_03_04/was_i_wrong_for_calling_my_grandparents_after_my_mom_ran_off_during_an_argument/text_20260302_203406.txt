Was I wrong for calling my grandparents after my mom ran off during an argument?

I’m 17 and I was recently at a sleep over with my best friend but asked my mom to pick me up early because I didn’t sleep well and just wanted to go home.

When she picked me up, she was already on a heated phone call with my stepdad. That wasn’t unusual, so I didn’t think much of it at first. But the argument escalated, and he told her he was leaving and hung up. My brother later confirmed he had walked out of the house, leaving my younger sister there too.

My mom immediately sped home to try to catch him. On the way, we saw him walking near a park by a school. Without hesitation, she stopped the car in the middle of the road, left it running, and jumped out to chase him, leaving me in the passenger seat with her phone and everything else.

Cars were coming from both directions. I don’t have a driver’s license, so I couldn’t legally drive. I panicked and tried calling my stepdad, but his phone was off.

A man who saw what happened came over and asked if I needed help. I know about stranger danger, but I was scared. He helped me move the car safely to the side of the road.

After that, I called my grandpa, the closest family member nearby. He told me to get somewhere safe and check on my siblings. Since we live about a five-minute walk from where the car was, I turned off the car, took the keys and phones, and walked home to make sure my brother and sister were okay. By that point, I had been sitting alone in the car for almost an hour.

Once home, I kept trying to call my stepdad so they wouldn’t panic about where the car and I were. When he finally answered, my mom started yelling at me for calling my grandpa. She said there was no reason to involve him and that I should have just stayed in the car and waited for her.

I felt scared and responsible in the moment. I wasn’t trying to cause drama, I just wanted to make sure everyone was safe.

Was I wrong for calling my grandpa?
