Should I Be Waking My Brother When He Keeps Hitting Snooze?

I’m 21 and my younger brother is 19. He’s always been a very heavy sleeper—even as a kid, our parents had to practically drag him out of bed. Lately, he’s been working at a retail job he really likes, but he’s started falling back asleep after his alarm, which has caused him to be late a few times. Even though we sleep in separate rooms, I can usually hear his alarm, and in the past I’ve had to go into his room to get him moving.

A while back, he asked me if I could start checking on him to make sure he actually gets up for work. I told him no. I feel like once his alarm goes off, it’s his responsibility. I also don’t want to take on that responsibility—if I agreed and he was still late, he might blame me. I want him to learn to manage his own schedule.

I mentioned it to my mom, and she said it would be a kind gesture to check on him if I’m already awake. I usually try to go back to sleep when his alarm rings, but I can see her point. Still, I feel like I’m setting a reasonable boundary because he’s an adult now and needs to take ownership of getting up on time.
