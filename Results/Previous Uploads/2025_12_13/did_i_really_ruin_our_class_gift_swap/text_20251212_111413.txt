Did I Really Ruin Our Class Gift Swap, or Are They Overreacting?

Today, our homeroom did a holiday gift exchange. A classmate named Lila was in charge of handing out the slips with everyone’s assigned person. She let me pick mine from the little basket, and I stepped out into the hallway where everyone else was waiting.

Almost immediately, another girl, Aria, asked, “Did you get yours yet?” I nodded, and then she followed up with, “So, who’d you pull?”

I honestly thought this was just a normal question. Nothing about her tone sounded like a trick, and no one reminded me that we weren’t supposed to say anything. So I told her the name on my slip.

The second the words left my mouth, the entire hallway froze. Then the complaints started. Lila grabbed all the papers back, announced we had to restart the whole thing, and told me to step outside while they “fixed the problem.”

While I waited in the corridor, I could clearly hear them talking about me—calling me clueless, saying I “ruined the fun,” and arguing about who would get stuck with my name now that everything had to be shuffled again. It felt like they were happier to blame me than admit the question shouldn’t have been asked in the first place.

I didn’t mean to spoil anything. I genuinely misunderstood. But now I feel embarrassed, unwanted, and like everyone thinks I sabotaged the whole event on purpose. I can’t tell if I should feel guilty… or if the girl who asked me put me in an unfair situation to begin with.
