Did I Just Offend My Friend by Rejecting a Gift?

So I’m turning 34, and my friend Rowan was ridiculously excited about my birthday present this year. He kept telling me I was “absolutely going to freak out” in a good way. When the day rolled around, he handed me this neatly wrapped box… and inside was a pair of noise-shielding earmuffs meant for my dog.

For context, I have a tiny Maru Island terrier who treats anything made of fabric like it owes her money. She’s adorable, but she destroys everything. And she absolutely hates having anything on her head. I’ve tried similar things before, and they never lasted more than a few minutes. Rowan knows all of this.

Just to be clear—I didn’t turn down the gift because it was technically for my dog. If it were something she’d enjoy or actually use, I would’ve accepted it without hesitation. I turned it down because I knew it would end up ripped apart instantly. Later, I found out he spent around fifty dollars on them, and I would’ve felt awful watching her destroy something that pricey.

I thanked him, told him I appreciated the thought, and explained why it wouldn’t work. I genuinely tried to be gentle about it. I’ll admit I did find it a little strange that he got my dog something for my birthday.

Now Rowan isn’t answering my messages or calls at all, which makes me wonder if I unintentionally hurt his feelings or if he’s just embarrassed.

So… was I wrong for declining the gift?
