Was I unfair for not changing plans when my friend never said she was struggling?

For my birthday, I planned a full-day trip from our small town to a nearby major city with my sister and a close friend, laying out the schedule clearly ahead of time: leaving mid-morning, several hours at a large museum, exploring the city afterward, and returning that evening. I invited her to the museum for free and knew she had diabetes and an old leg injury, so before we even left I told her I brought extra snacks—both fast-acting and longer-lasting carbs—just in case she needed them, and reminded her she could speak up at any time. We spent hours walking through exhibits, skipped one floor because neither of us was interested, and took breaks where she sat down without complaint. When we finished early, my sister and I openly suggested walking around nearby streets and using the subway to explore, and she didn’t object or say she needed food or rest, even when we repeatedly checked in and stopped for a smoothie and a bagel along the way. She kept one headphone in most of the day and never told us she was struggling. Days later, she blew up at me, saying I “didn’t warn her” about how much walking there would be, that she couldn’t prepare properly, and that I left her vulnerable—despite the original plan involving hours on our feet, despite us checking in constantly, and despite the fact we were surrounded by food options the entire time. Now she’s framing it as if I was careless with her health, and I’m stuck wondering if I genuinely failed her—or if she’s angry because she didn’t speak up and wants someone else to blame.
