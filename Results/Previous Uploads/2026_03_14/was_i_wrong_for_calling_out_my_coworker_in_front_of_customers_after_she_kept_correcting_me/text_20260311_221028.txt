Was I wrong for calling out my coworker in front of customers after she kept correcting me?

I’m 19 and recently started a new job. One of my coworkers, who’s in her mid-20s, has been there longer than I have. Since my first week, she’s had a habit of correcting me in front of customers.

At first I assumed she was just trying to help because I’m new. But it started to feel less helpful and more like she was trying to show me up. If I explain something to a customer, she’ll jump in and interrupt. If I say something slightly off, she’ll immediately correct me, and often it’s done loudly and in front of everyone.

What’s frustrating is that sometimes I’m not even wrong. She’ll still step in and repeat what I said, just using slightly different words.

I’ve mostly kept quiet because I didn’t want to cause tension at work, especially since I’m the new person.

Yesterday, though, something similar happened while we were helping a customer. The customer asked about a small detail and I explained it correctly. Before I even finished, my coworker cut in and said, “No, that’s not how it works,” and then gave essentially the exact same explanation I had just given, only phrased differently.

The customer looked confused by the contradiction.

So I calmly said, “That’s actually what I just said.”

She immediately went quiet and the customer gave an awkward laugh.

Later, she pulled me aside and said I embarrassed her and that it’s not okay to undermine coworkers in front of customers.

But that’s basically what she’s been doing to me repeatedly since I started working there.

Now I’m wondering if I should have just let it go again instead of responding in front of the customer.

Was I wrong for saying something?
