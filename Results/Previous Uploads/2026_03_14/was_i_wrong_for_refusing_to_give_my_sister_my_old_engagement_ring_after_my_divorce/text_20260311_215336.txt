Was I wrong for refusing to give my sister my old engagement ring after my divorce?

I’m 30 and went through a divorce last year. It was mutual and fairly calm — there wasn’t any major drama. When everything was settled, I kept the engagement ring. My ex and I had actually split the cost when we bought it because we were both early in our careers at the time, so it felt reasonable that I kept it.

Even though the marriage didn’t work out, the ring still means something to me. It represents a period of my life, and I’m just not ready to part with it yet. I don’t wear it anymore, but I’ve kept it stored away.

My younger sister, who is 25, recently got engaged. Her fiancé doesn’t earn much, so he proposed with a very simple ring. She seems happy with it, but she told me they eventually want to upgrade to something nicer when they can afford it.

A few days ago she asked if she could buy my old engagement ring from me at a discounted price since I’m not using it. She said it would help them save money and give her something nicer in the meantime.

I was honestly caught off guard and told her no. I explained that while I’m not wearing the ring, I’m also not interested in selling it right now.

She didn’t take that well. She said it was strange and “emotionally unhealthy” that I’m holding onto a ring from a marriage that ended. Since then, my parents have started hinting that I should reconsider — saying it would be a kind gesture and that it’s better for the ring to be used than sitting in a drawer.

I still feel like it’s my ring and my choice, especially since I helped pay for it. But the way my family is reacting has made me question whether I’m being unreasonable.

Was I wrong for refusing to give or sell the ring to my sister?
