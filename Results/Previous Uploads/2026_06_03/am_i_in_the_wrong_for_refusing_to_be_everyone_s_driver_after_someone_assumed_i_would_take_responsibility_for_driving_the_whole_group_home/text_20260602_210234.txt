Am I in the wrong for refusing to be everyone’s driver after someone assumed I would take responsibility for driving the whole group home?

Last Sunday I went out with a group of friends I have known for a long time. We had dinner and later went to a board game café where some people drank alcohol while others, including me, did not.

The day before, someone in our group chat asked how everyone was getting to the venue. I said I would be driving myself because I live quite far away and had an early morning work appointment the next day. I did not offer to drive anyone else, and nobody asked me to do so.

At the café, I stayed completely sober so I could drive home safely. Everything was going fine until near closing time when one of my friends said it was good that I would be driving everyone home.

I assumed there was a misunderstanding and clarified that I was only driving myself.

That is when I learned that another friend had been telling people during the evening that I would not be drinking and would therefore be responsible for driving everyone home.

The problem is that I never agreed to this arrangement. Several friends lived in completely different directions from my home, and driving everyone would have added more than an hour and a half to my trip. I also had an early morning work appointment and needed to be rested.

Some friends said ride-sharing services were expensive and that their phone batteries were low, so it would be easier if I helped.

I offered a compromise. I could drive the two friends who lived closest to me, and I would stay with the others while they arranged transportation. I also waited with them until their ride-share cars arrived so nobody was left stranded.

However, the friend who had told others I would drive everyone became upset. She said I embarrassed her because she had already promised people I would take them home.

I told her she should not have made promises involving my car, time, or responsibility without asking me first.

The rest of the night ended awkwardly, and now she is telling other people that I ruined the evening by refusing to drive everyone when I was not drinking anyway.

I do not think it is unreasonable to set boundaries around my time and responsibility, especially when I never agreed to take on that role. Still, I am wondering if I handled the situation too strictly.

Am I in the wrong?
