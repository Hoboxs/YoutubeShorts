Was I Wrong to Expect My Housemate to Replace What She Took?

I’m a 31-year-old man living with my housemate, Dana, who is 34. We keep almost everything in the apartment completely separate. She likes having strict systems, so from the day I moved in, we divided storage space down the middle. My groceries go on one side, hers on the other. My dishes stay in one section of the cabinets, hers in another. It works well because there’s never any confusion about what belongs to whom.

The one exception is our bedding storage. The closet isn’t set up well for shelves, so we each use large labeled bins to store spare linens. A couple of weeks ago, I bought a new set of thermal sheets because the weather has been getting colder. They were more expensive than I’d usually spend—around $180—and several friends recommended them because they stay warm during the winter. I washed them and packed them away, planning to put them on my bed the following weekend.

When I finally went to grab them, they were gone. After searching the apartment, I asked Dana if she had seen them. She casually told me she had taken them because her room had been chilly and she wanted to try them out. I was annoyed because she never asked for permission. I told her I needed them back so I could wash them and use them myself.

That’s when she informed me that I probably wouldn’t want them anymore because she and her partner had been sleeping on them. The situation immediately upset me because I have contamination-related OCD, something she has known about for years. Even though I’ve made a lot of progress through treatment, certain situations can still trigger intense anxiety. Hearing that someone else had used my brand-new sheets in that way made me feel like I could never comfortably use them again.

I told her that because she took them without asking and used them in a way she knew would create a problem for me, she should replace them. Dana refused. She said I was being unreasonable, that my anxiety wasn’t her responsibility, and that if I wanted another set, I could buy one myself. She also said that people share things all the time and that I needed to stop making such a big deal out of it.

What bothers me most is that it feels intentional. She knows how particular I am about my belongings, and she knows I struggle with contamination fears. Part of me wonders whether she mentioned using the sheets with her partner because she knew it would make me reluctant to take them back. She insists that idea is ridiculous and says I’m reading too much into it.

I understand that many people would simply wash the sheets and move on. The issue for me is that she took something that clearly belonged to me, never asked permission, and then returned it in a condition she knew would be difficult for me to accept. Because of that, I feel she should be responsible for replacing them rather than expecting me to absorb the cost myself. Am I wrong?
