Am I wrong for asking my friend to pay for the wine he drank while house sitting?

I (26F) asked a friend (27M) to stay at my place for a weekend to watch my cat. Before I left, I told him he could “help himself to whatever” in the kitchen. In my mind, that meant normal things—snacks, cereal, maybe a beer. Just basic hospitality.

When I got home, I noticed that a $120 bottle of vintage Barolo I had been saving to celebrate an upcoming promotion was gone. Not half gone. Completely finished. He drank the whole thing by himself while hanging out and watching Netflix.

When I brought it up, he said I had technically given him permission because it was in the kitchen and I told him to help himself. That felt like a stretch to me. To me, there’s a difference between grabbing chips or a soda and opening a special-occasion bottle of wine that’s clearly more expensive than the rest. It just seems like common sense not to open the nicest bottle in someone else’s home without asking.

I ended up sending him a Venmo request for $80 instead of the full $120 because I didn’t want to be over the top about it. Now he’s calling me tacky and telling our friend group I’m a bad host for charging him after I said he could help himself.

It’s not even entirely about the money. It’s more about the principle. I feel like there’s an unspoken rule that if you’re a guest—or house sitting—you don’t consume the most expensive or special item available without checking first.

He’s acting like I’m being dramatic over “just wine,” but I can’t help feeling like he took advantage of a casual offer.

So, am I actually being unreasonable here?
