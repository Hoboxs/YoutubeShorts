Did I go too far when I confronted a stranger who cut the line?

I was at Costco, when I realized just after checkout that I’d accidentally grabbed the wrong product. Annoyed but calm, I wheeled my cart over to the customer service counter and took my place behind another shopper who was clearly already waiting. It wasn’t busy, but it also wasn’t empty, and I expected to be there for a few minutes like everyone else. While I was standing there, a woman suddenly pushed past my cart, physically edging in front of me, and tossed me a casual little wave like we were old friends. Without even apologizing, she said, “I’ll be super quick, I just have a question,” as if that automatically granted her priority over everyone else.

Something in me snapped. I told her bluntly that I didn’t care how quick she thought she’d be and that she needed to wait her turn like the rest of us. She immediately looked offended, like I’d committed some huge social crime, and shot back that I was being incredibly rude and aggressive. That reaction only made me angrier. I pointed out that I also had a question and asked her what exactly made hers so important that it justified cutting in front of strangers. The exchange drew a few looks from nearby customers, and she scoffed, muttered under her breath, and stepped back, clearly acting like I was the problem for not letting her get away with it.

By the time I got home, the adrenaline had worn off, but I was still irritated. When I told my partner what happened, she agreed the woman was wrong for cutting the line but said I escalated things unnecessarily and shouldn’t have sworn or come in so hot. I get that I could’ve handled it more politely, but at the same time, I’m tired of people acting entitled and expecting everyone else to just accept it quietly. I don’t regret standing up for myself, but now I’m stuck wondering whether the way I did it crossed a line and turned me into the asshole instead.
