Did I Go Too Far Protecting a Friend’s Allergy on My Birthday?

I’m about to turn eighteen and planned a small birthday dinner at my house with just my closest friends. Nothing wild—some simple grilling, ordering pizza, and letting people bring food if they wanted, but with no pressure. One of my friends asked if he could bring his new girlfriend, and I agreed since he wasn’t even planning to stay long. She offered to make something for the dinner, and I thanked her but told her it wasn’t necessary. She insisted anyway. I later created a group chat for everyone attending and sent out the basic details, including one very clear and serious rule: one of my friends has a severe nut allergy, so anything brought needed to be completely nut-free, including avoiding cross-contamination. Everyone acknowledged the message, including the girlfriend, who reacted to it. The night before my party, she casually mentioned what she’d made and sent me a photo—it was a homemade cake that I immediately recognized as one that usually contains nuts. When I asked, she confirmed it did, but said it “wasn’t much” and that the allergic friend could just not eat it. I told her I wasn’t comfortable having it in my house at all and asked her not to bring it. She got angry and said she’d already put in a lot of effort, so either the cake comes or she doesn’t. I told her then she shouldn’t come, because I’d been clear from the start and I wasn’t risking my friend’s health for a dessert. She accused me of being disrespectful, said she had a right to be there because she was dating my friend, and things escalated fast. When her boyfriend called me, he yelled that I was cruel and ungrateful, and I snapped back, saying I didn’t care how long the cake took—nuts were the one thing I explicitly said not to bring. I also said some harsh things in frustration about her ignoring clear instructions. Now they’re both skipping the party and telling others I disinvited her after she worked hard on something “for me.” Most people not attending think I was wrong, while the few who are coming—especially the friend with the allergy—say I did the right thing. With my party happening tomorrow, I’m left wondering if standing firm about safety made me the villain, or if I was right to draw the line even when it blew up friendships.
