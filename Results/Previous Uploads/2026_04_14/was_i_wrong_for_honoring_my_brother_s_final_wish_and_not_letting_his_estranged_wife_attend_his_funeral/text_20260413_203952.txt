Was I wrong for honoring my brother’s final wish and not letting his estranged wife attend his funeral?

My older brother passed away recently after years of dealing with serious kidney problems. It wasn’t completely unexpected, but it was still devastating.

About six months before he died, his wife of five years told him she wanted a divorce. She said she wasn’t happy and wanted a more exciting life. Not long after, it became clear she was already seeing someone else. This all happened while my brother’s health was getting worse again, and it hit him really hard.

During his last hospital stay, he made sure everything was in order. He updated his will and was very clear about two things: he didn’t want his estranged wife to receive anything, and he did not want her at his funeral.

When he passed about a month later, we followed those wishes. On the day of the service, she showed up, but we didn’t allow her inside. It wasn’t about creating drama, it was about respecting what he had explicitly asked for.

Since then, she’s been calling me heartless. Some of her family members have also been messaging me, saying I went too far and had no right to keep her from saying goodbye. A lot of the anger also seems tied to the fact that she wasn’t left anything in his will. He had arranged for his savings to go toward my daughter’s education instead.

We haven’t tried to stop her from visiting his grave or doing anything else privately. The only boundary was the funeral itself, because that was one of his final, clearly stated wishes.

I understand that grief is complicated, even for someone he was separating from. But at the same time, this was something he felt strongly about and made clear before he died.

Was I wrong for honoring that?
