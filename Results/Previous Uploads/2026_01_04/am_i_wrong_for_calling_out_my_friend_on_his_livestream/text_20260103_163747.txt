Am I Wrong for Calling Out My Friend on His Livestream?

I (20M) have a friend (24M) who streams on Twitch. He’s been trying to turn streaming into a career, and we’ve been friends for almost three years. We met through gaming, and even though we live in different countries, we’ve only seen each other twice in person. Gaming has always been our bond.

Today, he was live-streaming while playing Apex Legends, one of his favorite competitive shooters, and I could tell he was stressed from all the chaos in-game. After a while, he said he wanted a break from multiplayer madness and wanted to play something single-player. I suggested Stardew Valley, a chill, relaxing game I thought he’d enjoy. He reluctantly agreed, clearly annoyed, saying, “Fine… I’ll try it.”

But from the moment he launched the game, it became a nightmare. He started trashing it to hundreds of viewers: “Look how fun this is… not,” “Bro, how do people even enjoy this?” and, “I swear, I’d rather grind Fortnite all day than do this.” His chat piled on, making jokes about him being bored and agreeing with every sarcastic comment.

Then he went after me personally. He said things like, “I’m stuck playing this garbage because of my friend,” and, “I don’t know what kind of weirdos like this game,” clearly referencing me without naming me. I felt humiliated. I had invited him to play a game I genuinely thought we could enjoy together, and instead, he was publicly blaming me to hundreds of people.

After the tutorial, I stayed silent for a few minutes, but I couldn’t take it anymore. I typed into his chat:

"Thanks for humiliating me and making me feel bad for inviting you to play. Really appreciate it."

The chat went completely silent. He muttered, “I don’t want to deal with this right now. I’m shutting the stream off,” and closed the stream mid-play. Later, he messaged me angrily, saying I “ruined the stream” and that I “needed to learn to take a joke because it’s just a game.”

I don’t see it that way. I didn’t insult him or the game—I only called out how I was being treated. I feel like I defended myself in front of hundreds of viewers who had been watching me be blamed unfairly.

But now I’m left wondering: did I overreact and ruin his stream, or was I justified in standing up for myself in front of everyone?
