Was I wrong for not telling my friend’s girlfriend that she didn’t get the job at my company?

I recently took over my family’s business with my sister. A few months ago, we opened a new position. One of the applicants turned out to be my close friend’s girlfriend. She apparently didn’t realize it was my company when she applied.

To keep things professional and avoid favoritism, I removed myself completely from the hiring process. My sister handled the interviews and communications.

On the day of her interview, I happened to be walking into the building as she was leaving. She faltered a bit when she saw me and said hello. I said hello back but kept walking, acting neutral. I didn’t acknowledge any personal connection because I didn’t want to blur professional lines. In hindsight, it may have come off as cold, but my intention was to avoid making things awkward or appearing biased.

Ultimately, we hired someone else.

A few weeks later, she sent a follow-up email asking for an update. Apparently, no one ever responded. My sister handles company emails, and I wasn’t directly involved, so I didn’t realize she hadn’t received a rejection.

Yesterday, I found out my friend has been distancing himself because of this. He told me he’s extremely disappointed in how I handled it. He said he understood her not getting the job — that was never the issue. What upset him was that she didn’t even get the courtesy of a rejection email, especially given our friendship. He also brought up how I acted like I barely knew her when she greeted me.

From my perspective, I thought I was doing the right thing by staying completely hands-off and unbiased. I didn’t want anyone to think I influenced the decision either way.

But he sees it as me hiding behind “professionalism” while failing to show basic respect and courtesy. He’s the type of person who, once he feels wronged, just quietly pulls away rather than arguing — and it feels like that’s what’s happening now.

I don’t think I intentionally did anything malicious. But I also understand how not responding at all might look careless.

So, was I wrong for staying out of it and not making sure she got a rejection email, or is this being blown out of proportion?
