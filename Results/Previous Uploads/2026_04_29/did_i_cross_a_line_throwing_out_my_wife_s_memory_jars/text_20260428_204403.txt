Did I cross a line throwing out my wife’s “memory jars”?

I have been married to my wife for nearly a decade, and for most of that time, she has had this habit that I never fully understood but tried to tolerate. She keeps empty glass jars. Not just a few here and there, but dozens of them. Old sauce jars, pickle jars, jam jars. She says each one holds a memory, even though they are completely empty. Over time, they have taken over our kitchen counters, shelves, and even the window ledges, just sitting there collecting dust.

I tried to ignore it for years, telling myself it was harmless, even if it felt cluttered. But last week something happened that made it feel less harmless and more like a problem. Our five year old son reached for one of the jars because he wanted to keep some insects he had found. The jar slipped, shattered on the floor, and glass went everywhere. No one was hurt, thankfully, but it was enough to scare me.

After that, I told my wife we needed to get rid of most of the jars. I was not asking to clear everything out, just to reduce what had become an overwhelming number. She refused. She said they might be useful one day and that each jar represented a memory. When I asked her to choose which ones mattered most, she could not. She would mention things like a jar from our first meal in the house or one from when she was pregnant and craving certain foods, but there was no clear line between what to keep and what to let go.

We went in circles and could not come to any agreement. I felt like nothing was ever going to change, and I was still worried about safety, especially with our child around. So the next day, while she was at work, I made a decision on my own. I threw almost all of them away. I kept two that she had specifically described with clear meaning, but the rest went into the bin.

When she came home, she noticed immediately. She did not yell. She just sat down on the floor and started crying, which honestly made it feel worse. Then she left and went to her mother’s house. Later, her mother called me to ask what had happened. I explained everything, and she did not take sides, but she did say that we probably should have found a compromise instead of letting it get to this point.

Now I am sitting here wondering if I crossed a line. Part of me still feels like something needed to change, especially after what happened with our son. But another part of me cannot ignore how deeply this affected her. It was never just about glass jars to her, even if it looked that way to me.

I am starting to question whether I owe her an apology, whether I should have handled it differently, and whether this is something bigger that we need to work through together instead of trying to fix it on my own.
