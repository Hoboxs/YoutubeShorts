Was I wrong to back out of housing at the last second?

I feel like the timing of what I did is what makes this situation look terrible, even though part of me believes I made the only decision I realistically could. I had been planning to move in with three other girls into a shared house, and everything seemed fine at first. They had already chosen the three bedrooms upstairs, which left me with the one downstairs. It was initially presented to me as having its own bathroom, which sounded like a fair trade.

But as things became clearer through conversations with the landlord, I realized that was not exactly true. The bathroom was not connected to the room at all. It was just located downstairs in a shared area, meaning it was not private in the way I had been led to believe. That alone made me hesitate, but what really pushed things into uncomfortable territory was finding out that the washer and dryer were actually inside my bedroom.

That meant anyone in the house would need to come fully into my personal space every time they wanted to do laundry. Even if I tried to create some kind of divider, it would take up a large portion of the room and still would not solve the issue of people constantly coming in and out. On top of that, I would be paying the exact same rent as everyone else, despite having what felt like a significantly less private and more intrusive setup.

I also have a small pet rabbit that I let roam freely in my space, and that made the situation even more complicated. I was worried about people opening the door frequently and about the strong smells from laundry products, since rabbits can be sensitive to that kind of thing. I brought up my concerns and asked if anyone would be willing to switch rooms, but all three of them said no.

At that point, I started to feel like I was being asked to accept a situation that just did not work for me. So I made the decision to back out. I did it before anyone signed the lease because I did not want them to be locked into the house and then have me leave afterward. The problem is that I made this decision the very morning we were supposed to give the final answer to the landlord.

I only fully understood what the room was actually like the night before, when I finally saw a clear photo that showed the full setup. That is when it really sank in that I would not be comfortable living there. Still, I cannot ignore the fact that the others had already spent time searching for places, touring homes, and planning this together.

Now I am going to be living alone in a one bedroom place, which I know will suit me much better. But I keep thinking about how this must have affected them, especially with how late I pulled out. I feel guilty for disrupting their plans, even though I also feel like staying would have been a mistake for me.

So I am stuck wondering if I did the right thing by protecting my own comfort and boundaries, or if the way I handled it, especially the timing, makes me the one at fault here.
