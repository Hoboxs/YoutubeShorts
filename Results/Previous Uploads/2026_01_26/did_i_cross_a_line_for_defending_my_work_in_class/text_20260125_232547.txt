Did I Cross a Line for Defending My Work in Class?

I am a sixteen year old student, and recently I was assigned a group presentation with two classmates, whom I will call Mira and Theo, with about a week to prepare. From the very beginning, it became clear that most of the responsibility was falling on me. I researched the topic in depth, wrote nearly the entire script, and designed the slides from scratch. Mira showed up once, handed me a few printed images that loosely related to the topic, and then disappeared for the rest of the week. Theo helped a little, mostly by offering small suggestions, but the bulk of the work was still mine. When presentation day came, all three of us presented together, and our teacher praised the group overall, saying we did well.

When grades were released, I was shocked. Mira received a perfect score, Theo received slightly less, and I received the lowest grade of the group. Confused and upset, I asked the teacher why my score was lower even though I had done most of the work. She told me that Mira appeared more confident while speaking and that Theo contributed to the visual aspect, and she explained that grades were based on presentation performance rather than effort behind the scenes. I tried to explain that without my research, writing, and organization, there would not have been a presentation at all, but she became visibly annoyed and said her grading was fair and final.

After that conversation, the situation escalated. My teacher contacted my parents and told them I had been rude and disrespectful, accusing me of acting entitled. Mira later told me I was being dramatic and should have just accepted the grade or asked more politely. From my perspective, I was not trying to be disrespectful. I felt frustrated and dismissed after putting in the majority of the work and then being penalized for it. I am still struggling to understand how doing the most work resulted in the lowest grade, and whether standing up for myself in that moment truly made me the one in the wrong.
