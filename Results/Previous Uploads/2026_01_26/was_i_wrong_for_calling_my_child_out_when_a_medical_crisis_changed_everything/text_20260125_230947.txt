Was I wrong for calling my child out when a medical crisis changed everything?

My adult child was set to get married this weekend, and everything had been planned down to the smallest detail, but a sudden medical emergency changed everything overnight when my partner’s remaining parent was rushed into a hospital in another province and doctors warned the situation was critical and likely fatal. This left my partner completely distraught and facing an impossible choice between attending the wedding or flying out immediately to say goodbye. After agonizing over it she decided she had to leave that evening because she knew she would never forgive herself otherwise; I supported her fully, offered to include her virtually in the ceremony, and believed forcing herself to attend while grieving would only make the day more painful for everyone. When we called our child to explain the situation the reaction was explosive, with accusations, cruel comments directed at my partner, and repeated angry messages that left her sobbing and blaming herself, and in a moment of anger and protectiveness I told my child that their reaction was selfish and lacking compassion because someone was dying and this was not about stealing attention from a celebration. This only escalated things further until I was told I should stay away from the wedding entirely if my partner could not attend, and now as I prepare to drive my partner to the airport while she grieves. I am left wondering if I crossed a line by calling out my child so bluntly or if standing up for my partner in the middle of a family crisis was the only reasonable thing to do.
