Was I Wrong for Finally Drawing the Line With My Brother?

I am in my early thirties, married, and I own a home with my spouse. My younger brother, who is just a couple of years younger than me, has never truly supported himself. He lived with our father well into adulthood, then moved in with a girlfriend’s family for several years. When that relationship ended, our father gave him a small apartment connected to his business. Instead of treating it like a lifeline, my brother trashed the place, ignored basic responsibilities, and burned through yet another car that was paid for by a relative. Eventually our father retired, sold the property, and my brother was forced out.

A few months ago, he showed up at my door desperate and asking for help. I already knew how this would go, but I agreed to let him stay temporarily. I told him clearly that he had a limited amount of time to get himself together. Like an idiot, I kept extending that deadline. From the beginning, I made one thing very clear. My wife was not responsible for him in any way. She would not cook for him, clean for him, or manage his life. This is her home too.

My wife works in a creative field that is extremely competitive. She spends most days preparing, auditioning, and taking classes, even though paid work comes infrequently. When she is not doing that, she handles most of the household responsibilities. That is the balance we agreed on as a couple, and it works for us. My brother immediately took issue with this. He left messes everywhere, dumped laundry on the floor, and argued that since he worked full time and she did not, she should pick up after him too. When I told him that was unacceptable, he said that because she handled things for me, she should do the same for him. He even questioned why I deserved home cooked meals and clean clothes while he did not, as if my marriage entitled him to free labor.

Things escalated when he started deliberately disrupting her work. He works nights, which meant he was home all day while I was gone. During important online auditions, he would blast noise, shout at the television, or otherwise cause distractions even after being asked to stop. When I confronted him, he admitted he did not take her career seriously and did not believe she would succeed, so he did not think it mattered. While he listened to me when I was present, he completely ignored her boundaries when I was not, to the point that she was breaking down in tears in her own home.

That was the final straw. I told him he had to leave. I did not scream or threaten. I simply told him that his pattern of disrespect, entitlement, and cruelty was done. Other family members immediately accused me of being heartless, saying he was mentally unwell and struggling. The truth is, he has been struggling for most of his life and has refused every opportunity for therapy, treatment, or accountability. Instead, he relies on guilt and excuses while expecting everyone else to sacrifice their peace for him.

I chose my wife, my home, and the boundaries we worked hard to build. I am being told that makes me cruel. I honestly think letting this go on any longer would have made me complicit.
