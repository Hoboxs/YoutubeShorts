Was I wrong to stop driving my coworker after she kept assuming I would?

I’m a 27 year old woman working at a small design firm about fifteen minutes from my place. I usually keep to myself, finish my work, and head home. A few months ago, a new coworker named Lila, who’s around 33, joined our team. We were friendly in a surface-level way—just normal office chatter. One afternoon, there was a heavy downpour, and as I was getting ready to leave, she asked if I could drop her at a metro station a couple minutes off my route. Since it was practically on the way, I said yes. I figured it was a one-time thing.

The next day, right before closing, she showed up at my desk with her bag and said, “Ready to go?” I told her I needed to stay late, and she looked puzzled before heading out. After that, she started acting like I was her built-in ride. She’d stand by the door at closing time, or message me asking if I was “heading out now,” even though I never said I’d drive her regularly. If I didn’t offer, she’d be chilly with me the following morning.

Last week, she texted me before the end of the day asking if I could take her to a shopping center after work—about twenty minutes in the opposite direction of where I live. I told her it was too far out of my way. She replied with a curt “Alright then.” The next day she barely said a word to me, and another coworker mentioned she’d been telling people I promised to help her and then backed out, which never happened.

All I did was give her one ride during bad weather. I don’t mind helping occasionally, but I don’t want someone assuming I’m responsible for their transportation, especially when it’s inconvenient and she never offers to contribute for fuel or anything like that.

A friend told me I was too accommodating at the start, and another said I should’ve just driven her to avoid tension. I genuinely don’t think I did anything wrong, but now it feels awkward at work.
