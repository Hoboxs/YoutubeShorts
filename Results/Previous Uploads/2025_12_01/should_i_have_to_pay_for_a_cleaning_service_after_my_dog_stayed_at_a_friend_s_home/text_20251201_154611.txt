Should I have to pay for a cleaning service after my dogs stayed at a friend’s home?

I had to return to the town where I grew up on short notice after a relative passed away. Everything happened quickly, and I had to drop my plans and head out. I let a longtime friend - let’s call her Mia—know what was going on, and she immediately offered to let me stay at her place for a few days. I told her I’d be bringing my two large dogs with me and completely understood if that wouldn’t work for her. She insisted it wasn’t a problem and said she was happy to help.

For context, my dogs are well-trained but big—one’s a golden mix and the other is a shepherd mix—so they can feel overwhelming in small spaces, especially for people who aren’t used to big dogs. I’m very aware of this and usually avoid bringing them to other people’s homes unless absolutely necessary.

The drive was around four hours, and when I arrived, Mia came out to greet me. As I unloaded the car, I let the dogs out to stretch and relieve themselves. My golden mix went straight to some bushes, but my shepherd decided to pee right on the paved front step—she has a weird preference for solid surfaces. Mia immediately panicked. I apologized, of course, but since it was outside, I didn’t understand why it upset her so much.

During the stay, the dogs were perfectly behaved. No accidents indoors, no damage, nothing. They let me know whenever they needed to go out, and I picked up after them right away. I kept a close eye on them the entire time.

After I got home, Mia texted me saying her place was “messy from the dogs” and that she planned to hire a cleaner. She asked if I would cover the cost. I was confused, so I asked what needed cleaning. She said there was dog hair everywhere.

Yes, my dogs shed. I know that. If she had asked me to vacuum before I left, I absolutely would have. But she never mentioned it. I also don’t think a professional deep clean is necessary—just a quick vacuum of the rugs, sofa, and bedding would have handled it.

She offered to host me. She knew about the dogs. They didn’t damage anything or cause indoor messes. Now I feel stuck, because I don’t want conflict, but I also don’t feel responsible for a full cleaning service when the situation was exactly what I told her to expect.
