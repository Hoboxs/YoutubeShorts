Was I Wrong for Exposing a Co-Worker’s Cosmetic Surgery After Months of Insults?

I’m a 30-year-old woman from Canada who has been living and working in Japan for several years. When I joined my current company, I was the only foreign employee in an office made up almost entirely of local staff. Most people were welcoming, including one colleague around my age who went out of her way to help me settle in.

Things changed after she happened to see a photo of my fiancé, who is Japanese.

Up until then, we had gotten along surprisingly well, but afterward her behavior gradually shifted. At first, the comments were subtle enough that I brushed them off. She would make remarks about how much food I was eating at lunch or joke that I must have an unusually fast metabolism. I genuinely thought she was trying to make conversation.

As time went on, though, the comments became harder to dismiss. She started asking odd questions about my family background, including whether my ancestors had been involved in various historical atrocities. The way she phrased things made it clear she wasn’t asking out of genuine curiosity. She also began making remarks about international relationships, complaining that too many foreign women were dating Japanese men and talking about how people should stay within their own culture.

By then I realized the issue probably wasn’t my eating habits or family history. It felt like she had developed some kind of resentment toward me after learning about my relationship.

For months I tried avoiding conflict. I stopped engaging in personal conversations with her and kept interactions strictly professional. If she made comments, I ignored them and focused on my work.

Then everything exploded.

A few weeks ago, several coworkers were gathered near our department’s shared workspace when she started criticizing my appearance. What began as joking comments quickly turned into direct insults. She mocked my face, my style, and even suggested that my fiancé could do better.

I was already frustrated from months of passive-aggressive behavior, and in the moment I completely lost my temper.

Without thinking, I fired back that at least I hadn’t spent a fortune on cosmetic surgery only to end up with a nose that looked worse than before.

The problem is that I said it loudly.

The entire section of the office heard me.

Almost immediately the room went silent.

What made it worse was that I knew she was deeply insecure about that surgery. Long before our relationship deteriorated, she had privately confided in me that she regretted the procedure and hated the results. Very few people at work knew about it, and she had shared that information with me in confidence.

The moment everyone turned to look at us, she started crying.

Management eventually separated us and told us both to cool down. Since then, opinions around the office have been split. A few coworkers think she had been provoking me for months and finally got a response she wasn’t expecting. Others believe that regardless of what she said, bringing up a deeply personal insecurity and revealing information shared in confidence crossed a line.

When I told my fiancé about the situation, he felt I had simply defended myself after enduring repeated insults. But several colleagues have said that even if I wanted to confront her, I should have done so without exposing something she trusted me with.

I don’t regret standing up for myself, but I do feel conflicted about the way I did it. I know I said it because I was angry, and I know I deliberately targeted something I knew would hurt.

Now I’m wondering whether my reaction was justified after months of disrespect, or whether revealing that personal information made me just as wrong as she was.
