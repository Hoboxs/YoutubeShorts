Was I wrong for saying my partner’s mom’s mashed potatoes were weird?

I’ve been with my partner for about five years, and his family does big Sunday dinners pretty regularly. I go most weeks, and everything’s always been fine.

Last weekend, his mom made mashed potatoes. I grabbed some like usual, took a bite, and immediately noticed they tasted sweet. Not slightly sweet, like noticeably sweet, almost like a dessert.

I kind of laughed and said, “Wait, are these sweet?” because I genuinely thought maybe I was imagining it.

She said yes, that’s just how she makes them.

So I replied, “Oh, I’ve never had sweet mashed potatoes before.” I wasn’t trying to be rude, I was just surprised. But the whole table went quiet in a really awkward way.

I tried to keep eating them, but I honestly couldn’t get past the taste. It just didn’t work for me, especially with gravy. So I stopped and focused on the rest of the food.

Then she noticed and asked if I didn’t like them. I said, “They’re just a bit sweet for me.”

At that point, my partner jumped in and said they weren’t sweet, which confused me because they definitely were. Then his mom made a comment along the lines of “some people just aren’t used to real cooking,” which rubbed me the wrong way.

So I said, “I just didn’t expect mashed potatoes to taste like cake,” and that’s when everything really went downhill.

Dinner got super tense after that. We left not long after, and in the car my partner told me I embarrassed him and that I should apologize. But from my perspective, I wasn’t rude at first, I only got a bit sharp after she made that comment.

Now she’s upset, and he’s acting like I caused the whole situation.

Did I handle this badly, or was my reaction understandable?
