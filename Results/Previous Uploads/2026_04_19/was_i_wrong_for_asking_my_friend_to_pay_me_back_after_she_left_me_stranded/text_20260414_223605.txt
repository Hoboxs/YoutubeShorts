Was I wrong for asking my friend to pay me back after she left me stranded?

I’m in my early 30s and had a longtime friend I’ll call Nina. We’d been close since we were teenagers, along with two other friends, Marcus and Joel. The four of us used to do everything together, but over the years I started noticing a pattern with Nina, especially when it came to Marcus. She always seemed overly focused on him. If he dated someone, she would pick them apart or make passive aggressive comments until things got awkward. Eventually, that same energy started getting directed at me. She’d make little remarks that made it feel like she was competing with me over who mattered more to him, even though I never saw it that way. I ignored it for a long time because I didn’t want to ruin the friendship.

Recently, Marcus got married, and Nina and I traveled together for the wedding and shared a place to stay. Almost immediately, the same behavior started up again. She made comments about how I wouldn’t even be there if it weren’t for her, even saying it in front of other people, which felt unnecessary and embarrassing. Later that night, back at where we were staying, she started complaining that Marcus hadn’t spent enough time with her during the wedding. At one point she actually said that even though we were all there, he should have made more time specifically for her. That’s when I finally said something and told her that was a strange thing to say out loud.

She didn’t take it well. She got defensive and started insisting that she and Marcus had always been closer than anyone else, bringing up random examples to prove it. I told her I didn’t care who was closer, but it was weird that she seemed so fixated on it. I also made a comment that it felt like she had feelings for him, which definitely escalated things.

She stormed out, and instead of coming back later, she just left entirely. She drove off without me, even though we had traveled together and I had no way to get home on my own. The next morning I was stuck several hours away with no realistic options. I couldn’t rent a car due to payment issues, so I ended up having to book a last-minute flight, which cost me around $900.

I messaged her afterward explaining what happened and asked her to cover the cost since I wouldn’t have needed the flight if she hadn’t left me there. She never responded, and we haven’t spoken since. We’re supposed to talk soon, and while I know what I said wasn’t the nicest, I still feel like being stranded like that crossed a much bigger line. Now I’m wondering if I was wrong for expecting her to pay me back.
