Was I wrong for repairing my daughter’s car after her boyfriend said he would handle it?

My daughter, “Lena,” is 21 and recently moved in with her boyfriend, “Marcus.” We’ve always been close, and while it was an adjustment when she moved out, I’m happy she’s starting her own life.

She drives an older sedan with a lot of miles on it. A few weeks ago she mentioned that the steering wheel had started shaking when she reached highway speeds and that the front of the car sometimes shuddered when she braked. She told me Marcus said he’d take care of it.

Another week passed and nothing had been done about it. Then last weekend Lena came by to visit and said the problem had gotten worse and that she was starting to feel nervous driving it.

I took the car for a quick drive and immediately noticed the vibration around 60 mph and the shaking when braking. I pulled the front wheels off in my driveway and saw the brake rotors were clearly warped and the pads were wearing unevenly.

I went to the auto parts store, bought new rotors and brake pads, and replaced them that afternoon. It took a couple of hours, and afterward the car drove smoothly again.

Lena was very grateful and thanked me several times. To me it didn’t feel like a big deal. I’ve worked on cars most of my life, and she’s my daughter. If I can fix something that’s making her car unsafe, I will.

A few days later Lena and Marcus came over for dinner. At one point Marcus pulled me aside and told me I shouldn’t have repaired the car. He said it was his responsibility as her boyfriend to handle things like that and that by doing it myself I stepped on his toes.

I told him I wasn’t trying to compete with him or prove anything. The car was getting worse, and I simply fixed it while Lena was visiting.

Since then he’s been a little distant, and the atmosphere has been awkward. Lena says he feels like I undermined him.

From my perspective, Lena is still my child. If something on her car is unsafe and I’m able to fix it quickly, I’m going to do it.
