Would I be wrong if I skipped my friends’ baby shower after they said I didn’t deserve my pregnancy?

Two years ago, when I was pregnant with my first child, I kept most of my pregnancy journey very quiet. Two close friends of mine (a married couple) had been struggling with infertility, and I knew it was deeply painful for them.

When my fiancé and I found out we were expecting, it was clear they were hurting. After spending time with us, they would apparently go home and cry. Because of that, I avoided talking about my pregnancy around them. I didn’t share updates, excitement, or milestones. I don’t have many friends, so it was incredibly hard to downplay such a big, joyful moment in my life, but I didn’t want to cause them more pain just by existing while pregnant.

Despite that, they told other friends things like:

That my baby was “an accident.” (It wasn’t. We intentionally prepared and tried, and it happened quickly.)

That we “didn’t deserve a child as much as they did.”

Hearing those comments broke me. I cried many nights over it. I had always imagined our kids growing up together someday, whether they conceived naturally, adopted, or used medical help. I tried to calm my fiancé by saying they were just hurting and didn’t truly mean it, but it still deeply hurt, especially since when we confronted them, they never genuinely apologized.

When we first introduced our baby to our friend group (not at our home), they didn’t acknowledge our child at all. No greeting, no interaction, just acted like the baby wasn’t there. I cried again that night. Later, they brought a gift, but they never actually said sorry for the hurtful things they’d said.

Now, they’re expecting, possibly twins, after insemination. They told us first. I hugged them, congratulated them, and even gave them a small gift I had prepared for the day they’d eventually get pregnant, because despite everything, I still considered them my friends.

But I have this sinking feeling. All the joy I minimized to protect their feelings, they’re going to celebrate openly and loudly. They love big public celebrations. I know I chose to downplay my own experience, but it still hurts knowing I swallowed so much of my happiness for them while they were saying cruel things behind my back.

Now I’m considering not attending their baby shower. Not to be petty, but because I know I’ll feel overwhelmed and hurt. I’ve been working on standing up for myself more, and if I don’t go, I would explain why.

At the same time, I feel guilty. As their friend, shouldn’t I show up and support them?

Would I be wrong if I chose not to attend because of the hurtful things they said about my pregnancy?
