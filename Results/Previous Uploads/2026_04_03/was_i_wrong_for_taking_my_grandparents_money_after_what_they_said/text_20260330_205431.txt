Was I wrong for taking my grandparents’ money after what they said?

I am an eighteen year old woman, and for most of my life, my grandparents were not part of it. I never really understood why when I was younger. All I knew was that there had been a serious falling out in my family when I was a child, and contact between my mother and my grandparents completely stopped.

Recently, my grandparents reached out and asked to meet me. I agreed, mostly out of curiosity. During that meeting, I finally learned what had happened years ago. When I was around eight, my parents were separating, and I was told they were getting a divorce. Not long after, my father passed away in a car accident. According to my grandparents, they saw me at the funeral, and that is where everything escalated. Because my parents were still legally married at the time, my mother inherited everything. During a heated argument, my grandparents said some very harsh things to her, including calling her degrading names. After that, my mother cut them off completely and did not allow them to see me again.

When I spoke to my mother about it, she confirmed the general situation, though it is clear that her experience of it is very emotional and painful. From her point of view, they were cruel and disrespectful during one of the worst moments of her life.

During my visit, my grandparents told me they had set aside a large amount of money for me, something they intended as a fund for my future. It is a significant sum, enough to cover education or help me get started in life. I accepted it and have stayed in contact with them since.

When my mother found out, she was extremely upset. She feels that by reconnecting with them and accepting the money, I have taken their side and dismissed what they said and did to her. During our argument, she brought up again how they had insulted her back then. In the heat of the moment, I said something harsh back to her, implying that their words were justified. That comment made everything worse.

Now things between us are tense. My sister thinks I should apologize, especially for what I said, because it deeply hurt our mother. At the same time, I feel like I am allowed to make my own choices about my relationship with my grandparents and my future, especially when it comes to something as important as financial support.

I cannot tell if I crossed a line by accepting their help and speaking the way I did, or if I am justified in making my own decisions even if it upsets my mother.
