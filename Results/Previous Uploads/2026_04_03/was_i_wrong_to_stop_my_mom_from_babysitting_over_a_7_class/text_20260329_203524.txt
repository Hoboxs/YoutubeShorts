Was I wrong to stop my mom from babysitting over a $7 class?

I am a mother to a sixteen month old girl, and I recently returned to work part time. To make things easier, my mother had been watching my daughter a couple of days each week. It felt like a good arrangement at first, especially since I trusted her and wanted them to spend time together.

Around the same time, I enrolled my daughter in a toddler program at our local community center. It is a simple, low cost class that includes playtime, songs, and small activities. The children get to explore different stations, interact with others, and try things like art and sensory play. It can be messy, but I see it as a great way for her to learn and grow. She has already started recognizing colors, playing more independently, and showing interest in other children.

My mother, however, never liked the class. She described it as overwhelming and unnecessary, and felt that quieter activities like walks or library visits were better. Even though she disagreed, she told me she would still take my daughter as I asked. I trusted her to follow through.

After a while, I started to notice something was off. My daughter had not brought home any of the little projects they usually make, and it had been weeks since I saw any sign that she had been attending. When I looked into it, I realized my mother had stopped taking her altogether without telling me.

That was what upset me the most. It was not just about the class itself, but the fact that she chose to ignore something I felt strongly about and then kept it from me. It made me question whether I could rely on her to follow my decisions as a parent. Because of that, I decided to find someone else to watch my daughter while I work. I hired a student who is willing to take her to the class and follow the routine I set.

My mother was deeply hurt by this decision. She says I am pushing her out and choosing a stranger over family, especially when her intention was to help and keep my daughter close to someone she knows. Now our relationship feels strained, and I am left wondering if I went too far.

Part of me feels justified because I need to trust the person caring for my child. But another part of me wonders if I overreacted and caused a bigger issue than necessary over something that, on the surface, seems small.
