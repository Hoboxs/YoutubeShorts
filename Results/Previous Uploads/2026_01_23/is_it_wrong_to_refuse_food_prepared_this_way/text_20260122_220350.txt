Is it wrong to refuse food prepared this way?

Last night I sat down to cook dinner with my partner. We both work long days and usually enjoy cooking together as a way to unwind. While I was outside tending to marinated fish on the grill, my partner was inside putting together a large bowl of salad meant for both of us. At first everything felt normal and relaxed, until I noticed a familiar habit starting up again.

As the salad came together, my partner kept tasting it directly from the main bowl, scooping bites with the same spoon and then dropping that spoon back in to stir everything around. This is something I have mentioned before because it genuinely turns my stomach. In my own family, serving dishes were always treated as shared spaces, and once a utensil went into someone’s mouth, it did not go back into food meant for everyone. Her family, on the other hand, treats this as completely normal, and I know that because I have seen it happen at their dinner table more than once.

I calmly asked her if she could either stop tasting from the main bowl or make herself a small portion to snack on while she worked. I explained that it made me uncomfortable and distracted me from enjoying the meal. She brushed it off, told me I was being uptight, and kept doing exactly what she had been doing. I decided not to push it further in that moment because I did not want to derail the entire evening, so I finished cooking and tried to let it go.

When we finally sat down to eat, she reached for the salad and offered to serve me, using the same spoon she had been tasting from all along. I politely said no and told her I would skip the salad. She immediately wanted to know why, and when I tried to deflect by saying I was not feeling like having any, she kept pressing the issue. After several rounds of questions, I finally repeated my original concern and admitted that seeing the same used spoon mixed back into the salad had put me off completely.

That is when the mood shifted. She accused me of being dramatic and said she had spent a long time making the meal for me. She framed it as though I was rejecting her effort rather than reacting to something that genuinely bothered me. Before leaving the room, she added that if I thought the salad was bad, I would have been horrified watching her prepare the rice, which I had already eaten without thinking twice. That comment lingered with me and made me feel unsettled for the rest of the night.

What frustrates me most is that I was not trying to control how she eats or shame her habits. I was simply asking for a basic boundary around shared food. I did not mind tasting with a clean spoon or grabbing a quick bite set aside separately. What bothered me was the repeated use of a wet utensil being mixed into food we were both expected to eat, and then feeling pressured to ignore my discomfort so her feelings would not be hurt. I am left wondering if I was truly unreasonable for refusing the salad, or if it is fair to expect a little consideration when it comes to shared meals.
