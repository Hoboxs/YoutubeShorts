When constant criticism turns a loving habit into resentment.

I am in my early thirties and live with my partner in a mid sized city far from where I grew up. I work a fairly demanding office job, and over the last few years I also invested time and money into learning how to cook through evening classes and earlier school programs. Cooking started as something I truly enjoyed and a way to show care in our relationship. At the beginning, I would plan meals, grocery shop after work, and spend a good chunk of my evenings cooking for both of us because it made me happy. Gradually, though, it became less about enjoyment and more about expectation. My partner knows how to cook and has even made meals before, but most nights it was simply assumed that I would handle dinner. What made this harder was that almost every meal came with commentary. If I cooked something lighter, it was called boring or lacking flavor. If I made something rich or comforting, I was told it was unhealthy or a bad choice. When I tried new dishes to keep things interesting, they were brushed off as not something my partner would ever ask for. Often, seasoning would be added before a single bite was taken, followed by jokes about how the meal needed fixing, which slowly drained any joy I had left in cooking.

The breaking point came recently after a long workday when I spent well over an hour preparing dinner from scratch. I was tired but still hopeful that maybe this time it would be appreciated. Instead, my partner took one bite, shrugged, and said it was fine but that I should have done it differently. Something in me finally snapped. I calmly but firmly said that I was done cooking for them. I explained that I would still cook for myself, but from now on they would need to make their own meals. I told them that constantly being criticized made me feel unappreciated and taken for granted, and that it was no longer worth the effort. Since then, the atmosphere at home has been tense. My partner is clearly upset and keeps acting as though I overreacted and created a problem out of nothing. Meanwhile, I feel a mix of relief and guilt, and I cannot stop wondering whether setting this boundary was justified or if I somehow handled the situation poorly.
