Was I wrong for quietly leaving a family wedding early?

I am a twenty seven year old man who attended my older sister’s wedding last weekend. We have a decent relationship but we are not especially close, and I was there simply as a guest, not part of the wedding party. I went in with normal expectations and fully understood that weddings are stressful and imperfect.

From early on, though, the day felt uncomfortable. There were several last minute changes and a lot of tension, which I tried to brush off. What bothered me was that my sister kept snapping at me over small things. At one point she asked me to change seats because I was apparently in the way of photos, and later she was annoyed that I had moved because she wanted family sitting together. I did my best to follow directions, but it felt like I was doing something wrong no matter what I did.

At the reception I was seated at a table with people I did not know, which was fine, except my place setting had been forgotten entirely. There was no name card, no meal selection, and no chair set for me. I eventually flagged down a server and was given a plate, but it was clear I was an afterthought. On its own that would not have mattered much, but combined with everything else it added to the feeling that I did not really belong there.

During the speeches my sister thanked a long list of people who had supported them, including friends, coworkers, and even neighbors. I was not mentioned at all. I know weddings are not about recognizing every individual guest, but hearing that still hurt more than I expected.

By the time dinner ended, I felt drained, awkward, and honestly unwanted. I hugged my parents, stayed calm, and left quietly without saying goodbye to my sister. I did not announce it or make a scene. I just removed myself.

The next day she texted asking where I had gone and said it was hurtful that I left early without telling her, especially since some people noticed. I told her I was not feeling well and did not want to draw attention. She said I was being passive aggressive and that I should have pushed through for one night. My mother agreed and said weddings are not about individual feelings. A friend of mine thought it was strange how I was treated and said leaving quietly was reasonable.

I genuinely was not trying to make a statement. I just reached a point where I no longer felt welcome and did not see a reason to stay.
