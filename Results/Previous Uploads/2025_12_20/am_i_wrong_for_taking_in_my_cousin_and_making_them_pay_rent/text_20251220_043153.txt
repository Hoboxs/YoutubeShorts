Am I Wrong for Taking in My Cousin and Making Them Pay Rent?

A few months ago, my 20-year-old cousin called me in a panic. They’d been kicked out of their parents’ house after an argument and were being forced to pay ever-increasing fees just to stay there, while their siblings were charged nothing. Their parents blamed them for minor things, including a tiny car scratch, ignoring far bigger issues caused by their siblings.

I offered them a place to stay. We packed their things, got their important documents, returned their car, and started fresh. I helped them open a personal bank account, loaned them money for a reliable car, and helped them enroll in community college. They now work part-time, babysit, and teach a small class.

Before they moved in, we agreed they’d contribute to household costs, but in a way that actually benefits them. They pay a modest rent that goes straight into savings under their name, contribute to groceries, share insurance costs, and slowly repay the car loan. We calculated their budget together so they could learn independence without being overwhelmed.

Their parents found out and went ballistic. They accused me of “convincing” my cousin to leave, of taking advantage of them, and of being a villain for making them contribute at all. Meanwhile, my cousin is thriving, learning to manage money, and finally has some control over their own life.

I feel like I did the right thing for their future—but now my cousin’s parents are angry and framing me as the bad guy. Did I overstep by helping them and having them pay their own way?
