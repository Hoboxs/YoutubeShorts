Was I wrong for admitting I do not like my daughter in law’s painting?

My daughter in law is very artistic and has a small side business selling her paintings. For years she has made artwork for relatives and friends as gifts, and most people seem to genuinely love what she creates.

The problem is that her style is simply not my taste.

I prefer peaceful landscapes, oceans, forests, and nature scenes without people in them. Her paintings are much more stylized and centered around people with exaggerated features and dramatic proportions. I can appreciate that she has talent, but personally I would never choose that kind of artwork to decorate my home.

Recently my husband and I downsized and moved into a smaller house. As a housewarming gift, my daughter in law painted a beach scene for us because she knows we enjoy coastal vacations. The painting showed a woman sitting near the shoreline, but the proportions were very exaggerated in the way she usually paints. The legs were unusually long, the body shape was unrealistic, and overall it just was not something I wanted hanging in my living room.

When she gave it to me, I thanked her warmly because I did appreciate the effort. I assumed that would be the end of it. I stored the painting in the garage while we finished unpacking and decorating.

Later, I bought a large ocean landscape print for the main living area instead. It fit the space perfectly and matched the calmer style I personally enjoy.

A few days ago my daughter in law came over to pick up some holiday decorations we no longer had room for. While we were in the garage, she spotted the painting leaning against the wall. She immediately looked surprised and asked why it was not hanging inside the house.

I tried to avoid hurting her feelings and simply said I had not found the right place for it yet.

But she kept pushing.

She pointed out the new artwork in the living room and started suggesting places where her painting could go instead. At one point she literally picked it up and started walking around talking about different walls where it might fit.

That was when I finally stopped dodging the question.

I told her gently but honestly that her style of artwork is just not really my taste and that I did not want to hang it in the house.

The mood changed instantly. She became very quiet, took the decorations, and left shortly afterward.

A few hours later my son called me furious. He said his wife had been crying because she spent many hours making the painting specifically for us and felt humiliated knowing I had hidden it in the garage. He told me I should apologize and put the painting up somewhere in the house anyway because it was made with love.

I told him I was sorry her feelings were hurt, but I was not going to hang artwork in my home that I genuinely do not like looking at every day.

Now the entire situation has become a family issue, and I am being treated like I cruelly rejected some heartfelt gift when from my perspective I simply want the freedom to decorate my own house the way I want.
