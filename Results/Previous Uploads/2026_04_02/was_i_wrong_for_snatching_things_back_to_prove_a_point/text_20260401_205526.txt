Was I wrong for snatching things back to prove a point?

I work in an office where most people get along pretty well, but there is one ongoing issue that drives me absolutely crazy. One of my coworkers, a man in his late fifties, has this habit of just taking things straight out of people’s hands without asking. It is not just once in a while either. It is constant. He will grab papers, snacks, pens, anything that happens to be within reach, and he does it so casually that it is like he does not even realize how intrusive it is.

To be fair, I do not think he is trying to be rude or mean. He is known for being a bit absent minded, and people joke about it. Most of the office just shrugs it off and deals with it, but for me, it really gets under my skin. I cannot stand having something taken out of my hands without warning. It feels invasive, and it puts me on edge every time it happens.

What makes it more frustrating is that I have talked to him about it more than once. I have tried to explain it in a calm and reasonable way, even giving examples so he could understand the difference between being offered something and just taking it. Every time, he apologizes and says he gets it, and for a short while it seems like he is making an effort. Then, without fail, he slips right back into the same behavior.

There have been small moments where I could let it go, like when he grabs a handful of shared snacks in the breakroom. But then there are moments that feel much more disruptive. Recently, I was filling out an important form, and he literally pulled the pen out of my fingers because he suddenly needed it. It ruined what I was working on and forced me to start over. That was the moment I decided I had had enough.

I figured that maybe the only way he would understand how frustrating it is would be to experience it himself. So I made a plan that the next time he grabbed something from me without asking, I would take it right back and remind him to ask first.

The opportunity came sooner than expected. I was in the breakroom with a bag of chips when he walked in and immediately took it from me like he always does. Without thinking twice, I took the bag back from him and told him, again, that he needed to ask first. Then I offered it to him properly.

Instead of taking the hint, he reacted badly. He grabbed the bag in a huff, and in the process, it tore open and spilled everywhere. We both had to clean it up, and he was clearly annoyed. He told me that I made him feel like a child.

Now I am wondering if I handled it the wrong way. I was trying to set a boundary and get through to him after multiple conversations that went nowhere, but I also see how my reaction might have come across as petty or immature. I am stuck between feeling justified in standing up for myself and questioning whether I escalated things unnecessarily.
