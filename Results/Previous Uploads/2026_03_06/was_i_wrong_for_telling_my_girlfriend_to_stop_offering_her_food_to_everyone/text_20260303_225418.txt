Was I wrong for telling my girlfriend to stop offering her food to everyone?

My girlfriend, “Elena,” has a habit of offering her food to everyone at the table whenever we go out to eat. She says it’s how she was raised, to make sure everyone else is taken care of before she starts eating. I’ve told her many times that it’s kind, but unnecessary. Everyone already has their own meal, and no one is judging her if she doesn’t offer bites of her plate.

The issue is that she doesn’t just offer shareable appetizers. She’ll offer pieces of her sandwich, forkfuls of pasta, even spoonfuls of soup. It often turns into people taking direct bites or using their utensils in her food, which, aside from being a little gross, usually leaves her with less than half of what she ordered.

It becomes a bigger problem when we eat with my friend “Derek.” He’s genuinely a good and loyal friend, but he has zero restraint around food and not much awareness in group dining situations. If something is offered, he takes it, generously. In the past, he’s eaten nearly an entire shared appetizer because the rest of us were chatting and being polite. He also tends to underpay when the bill comes. If something costs $14.75, he’ll toss in $15 and assume it covers everything, not accounting for tax or tip.

I’ve specifically asked Elena not to offer her meal when Derek is around because I know how it ends. She says she doesn’t want to single anyone out or change her behavior for one person.

Last night we went out with friends again, Derek included. As usual, Elena offered her meal to the table. I nudged her lightly under the table as a reminder, and she just shrugged. Sure enough, Derek ended up eating most of her food, on top of finishing his own plate. Elena didn’t say a word.

On the drive home, she asked if we could stop somewhere else because she was still hungry. I agreed, but I also told her this was exactly what I had warned her about. I said she shouldn’t be surprised when Derek eats more than she intended to give. I also admitted that it frustrates me feeling like I’m indirectly paying for Derek’s meal when he ends up eating most of hers and doesn’t properly cover his share.

I may have sounded irritated. This has been an ongoing conversation, and I feel like she dismisses my concerns. She was clearly upset after I said it, and things are tense now. Neither of us has outright called the other wrong, but it feels like we’re both waiting for an apology.

Was I out of line for pushing the issue?
