Did I Cross a Line by Changing My Sister’s Kids’ Routine While They Live With Me?

My sister and her three children recently moved into my home temporarily while she gets back on her feet. I’m not a parent myself, but I’ve been spending most of my time at home with the kids while she works and goes out more often.

From what I’ve observed, the kids are almost always on their phones or tablets. They stay up until around 11 at night, even on school nights, and in the mornings they seem exhausted. The oldest, who is around 12 years old, often looks tired with dark circles under her eyes, and the younger two are frequently irritable or emotional.

I brought it up to my sister and suggested that they might benefit from a more structured routine, earlier bedtimes, and less screen time, especially since school starts early in the morning. She told me they are fine and dismissed my concerns.

Since I’ve been the one at home with them most of the time, I started making some changes on my own. I limited their screen time, encouraged them to do other activities, bought art supplies for the younger child who really enjoyed them, and started taking the oldest child on short walks around the building with music. Over time, she actually started choosing to go on longer walks herself.

I also started enforcing earlier bedtimes while they are in my care. The younger kids now go to bed around 8, and the oldest between 9 and 10.

My sister found out and told me I was overstepping because her children normally go to bed at 11 and that my rules don’t apply to them. I told her that while they are in my home and I am caring for them, I believe the routine I’ve put in place has been working better.

She became upset and brought it up to other family members. Now several of them are saying I should apologize for interfering and that I’m not respecting her role as their mother.

I feel like I was acting in their best interest based on what I was seeing every day, but I also understand she is their parent and I may have crossed a boundary by changing things without her agreement.

So now I’m wondering if I went too far by stepping in instead of leaving it entirely up to her.
