Am I in the wrong for telling my sister she is the common factor in her failed relationships?

I am a 29-year-old man, and my sister is 34. We both grew up in Manchester, but she now lives in a modern apartment in Leeds after buying her place a few years ago. She has a well-paying job in marketing, a strong social life, and has never had trouble meeting people or starting relationships.

The issue is that every relationship she enters seems to fall apart within a few months.

Her most recent boyfriend was a man she met on a dating app in early spring while she was attending a work conference in Edinburgh. They connected quickly and began seeing each other almost immediately. Within a month, she was travelling down to London most weekends to stay with him, introducing him to our family over video calls, and talking about how she had finally met someone “serious.” She even mentioned that they had casually looked at engagement rings while shopping one afternoon in Covent Garden.

About ten days ago, she called our family in tears saying the relationship had ended. She said he had broken up with her suddenly and that she believed he had turned out to be manipulative and emotionally immature like her previous partners.

A few days later, she showed me parts of their message history. From what I saw, the same pattern I had noticed in past relationships was repeating itself. At the beginning, she tends to present a highly curated version of her personality. She will talk about loving hiking in the Lake District, cooking elaborate meals on weekends, and being very spontaneous with travel plans.

However, after the initial honeymoon period, her behaviour tends to shift quite sharply. She stops wanting to do outdoor activities, becomes frustrated by cooking plans, and prefers staying in or booking short luxury resort holidays instead. When her partner reacts with confusion or disappointment, she often interprets it as controlling behaviour or emotional pressure, which escalates into arguments.

In this most recent relationship, the final conflict started after a trip planned to a music festival in Manchester. They had agreed months earlier to sell part of their ticket package, but she changed her mind last minute and refused. When he disagreed, she became extremely angry, insulted him, and accused him of being controlling and selfish. He ended the relationship shortly after and blocked her.

Afterwards, she escalated the situation further by saying she was considering contacting his employer in London to report him for supposed emotional instability, claiming he should not be working in a role that involves public interaction. That is when I felt the situation had gone too far.

During a family dinner at my parents’ house in Cheshire, I told her that she is the common factor in her relationship breakdowns, and that if she was honest and consistent about who she is from the start, she would likely find people who actually match her lifestyle instead of repeating the same cycle.

She immediately became emotional, left the table, and drove back to Leeds that night. Later, my mother called me saying I had humiliated her and demanded I apologize.

I am not sure if I was too blunt, but from my perspective this is a repeating pattern that keeps causing the same outcome, and no one in the family has ever said it directly.

Am I in the wrong?
