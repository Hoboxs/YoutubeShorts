Am I in the wrong for refusing to hand over an expensive hoodie to a student’s mother without proof, even after she publicly accused me of stealing it?

I work as a middle school teacher, and this happened during the final week of the school year. We had a large field day event with multiple stations, games, and activities spread across the gym and outdoor fields. By the end of the day, the entire school looked like a storm had passed through it: backpacks unzipped, water bottles everywhere, and hoodies tossed into random piles.

After the event, I was asked to help at the lost and found table, which was already overflowing.

We have a strict rule for unlabelled items: if something does not have a name tag, the person claiming it must describe it in detail that cannot be easily guessed. We require specifics like brand details, pocket contents, unique marks, or anything that proves ownership.

Near the end of the day, a student dropped off a navy hoodie from a well-known expensive brand. It had no name tag. However, inside the front pocket was a folded robotics club schedule with handwritten initials and a small club sticker.

About ten minutes later, a parent arrived at the table visibly upset. Before I could even speak, she pointed at the hoodie and said it belonged to her son and demanded I hand it over immediately.

I explained the procedure and asked her to describe it. She said it was dark blue, expensive brand, medium size. When I told her that description matched at least half the school, she became angry and said I was deliberately making things difficult and “playing power games over a child’s property.”

I asked if there was anything more specific, like what might be in the pockets or any identifying marks. She snapped that she was not going to “perform a detailed interrogation” just to get her child’s hoodie back and accused me of treating her like a suspect.

At one point, her voice rose so much that other parents started looking over. She said I was humiliating her in front of everyone and implied I was either careless or intentionally withholding lost property.

I calmly repeated that I could not release an expensive item without confirmation, especially since there was no name and multiple identical hoodies had already been turned in that day. I offered for her to return the next morning with more details or for her son to identify it in person.

She stormed off and told a nearby staff member that I was refusing to return stolen property.

The next morning, the situation escalated further. The student who actually owned the hoodie came in, immediately identified it, and correctly described the robotics schedule with his initials and sticker inside the pocket, as well as a small stain on the sleeve. It was clearly his.

Later that day, I was informed that the parent had emailed the school administration claiming I had embarrassed her, falsely accused her of lying, and “humiliated her as a mother in front of the school community.” She also said I should have handed it over immediately and “dealt with consequences later.”

The administration reviewed it and said I followed proper procedure, but the parent is now demanding a formal apology and escalation of the issue.

I do not feel I did anything wrong. If anything, I feel that handing over an expensive item without verification would have been irresponsible.

Am I in the wrong?
