Am I wrong for not letting my friend use the photographer she refused to split with me?

I recently finished my doctoral degree in the United States. I am an international student, and unfortunately my family could not afford to travel for the graduation ceremony, so I attended alone.

A few months before graduation, I asked my close friend and roommate, who was also graduating, if she wanted to split the cost of hiring a professional photographer together. She told me it was unnecessary and a waste of money.

Since I knew I would not have family there, I later asked if her family could at least take a few pictures of me on my phone during the ceremony so I would have something to send home to my parents. She said they would be focused on her and would not really have time.

During graduation, a representative from my country’s consulate attended to congratulate students from our country. He asked if I needed anything, and I mentioned that I just hoped to get a few decent photos for my parents.

He then contacted the consulate’s media photographer, who came over and started taking some photos and short videos of me.

As soon as my friend noticed, she ran over and started asking if he could also record her walking across the stage and do a full photo session with her parents, advisor, and friends.

I told her she was welcome to ask him herself, but I did not feel comfortable requesting that on her behalf because I had not hired him personally. He worked for the consulate and was there for official reasons, not as a private photographer.

She became angry, accused me of being selfish, and walked away. Later that evening she sent me a message uninviting me from the graduation celebration we had been planning together for weeks. She said I ruined her graduation day and that she no longer felt comfortable having me there.

Now I am wondering if I handled the situation badly.

Was I wrong for not asking the photographer to include her after she previously refused to split the cost of hiring one with me?
