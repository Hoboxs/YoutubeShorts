Am I wrong for stopping financial support for my grandfather after I found out he secretly gave the apartment to my aunt?

My grandfather had a stroke almost two years ago, and after leaving the hospital he could no longer safely live completely independently. He was still mobile, but his memory had declined badly. He would forget things on the stove, leave the gas running, and sometimes call me in the middle of the night because he thought strangers were inside the apartment.

I live about twenty minutes away, so most responsibilities gradually became mine. I handled doctor appointments, medications, paperwork, grocery shopping, transportation, and emergencies. Eventually I hired a caregiver to help several days each week because things became too difficult for one person to manage alone.

His pension covered part of the costs, but post-stroke medications and proper caregiving were expensive, so I regularly paid a large amount from my own income. Some months I spent the equivalent of over a thousand dollars helping him.

The rest of the family barely helped. My aunt would occasionally visit with groceries, take photos with him, post online about “family,” and disappear again. My cousins almost never came around.

I honestly believed the apartment would eventually be left to me. My grandfather had repeatedly told me that I was the only one consistently helping him and that things would “be fair” in the future. I even gave up plans to move to another city because I knew he depended heavily on me.

To be clear, I never believed he was required to leave me the apartment. What hurt was finding out that decisions had already been made behind my back while everyone continued letting me believe we were being honest with each other.

A few weeks ago, I came home earlier than usual and overheard my aunt talking with my grandfather in the kitchen. She was telling him not to leave the apartment to me because I was “only helping for personal gain.” She argued that she had children, debts, and a “real family” to think about. My grandfather agreed with her.

That was when I learned he had already transferred ownership of the apartment to her six months earlier. Nobody told me. During all that time, I continued paying for caregivers and medications because I thought at least we were all being transparent with each other.

I did not argue or yell. I simply told my aunt that if the apartment legally belonged to her now, then responsibility for his care should also belong to her. I informed her that beginning next month I would stop paying for the caregiver and extra expenses.

Now the family atmosphere is tense. Nobody directly says I am wrong, but people keep making comments like, “He is an old man, why are you counting money?” and “You should not treat family this way over inheritance.”

Now I genuinely do not know how to feel. Part of me feels manipulated and taken advantage of. Another part of me worries that maybe I really do look like someone who only helped because I expected the apartment one day.

Am I wrong for stepping back financially after finding out the apartment had secretly been given to my aunt?
