Am I wrong for replacing my friend with my partner after she bailed?

I have been close with my friend for about four years now, and for a long time, we were inseparable. But everything shifted when she started dating her partner last year. Since then, it feels like I have been pushed to the side whenever he is around, which is almost always. Plans we make together rarely stay just between us. She either asks to bring him along, turns it into a group hangout by inviting my partner too, or simply shows up with him without even mentioning it beforehand.

What makes it harder is that our partners actually knew each other before any of us met, so we do end up spending time together as a group fairly often. That part is not the issue. The problem is that sometimes I just want time with my friend, the way things used to be. On the rare occasions we do spend time alone, she is glued to her phone, constantly messaging him, or she brings him up in nearly every conversation. I tried to be understanding at first, because I know how consuming a new relationship can be. But after more than a year, nothing has changed, and it feels like I no longer exist outside of her relationship.

They live together, work together, and even when they are apart, they are still on the phone. It is like they are never not connected. Meanwhile, I try to carve out time with her every few weeks, hoping for just a small piece of the friendship we used to have.

A few months ago, I invited her over for a craft night at my place. I planned everything, picked up a nice bottle of wine, and bought supplies so we could actually do something fun together. She agreed, we set a time, and I was genuinely looking forward to it. But on the day of, just as I was heading home with everything, she messaged me saying she was not feeling well and could not come. It was disappointing, but I brushed it off. Instead of wasting the evening, I ended up doing the activity with my partner. Later that night, he checked in with her partner, only to find out she was perfectly fine and just staying in watching a movie.

About a month later, she reached out and asked if we could try again since she missed the first one. I agreed, hoping things would be different. Once again, I went out, bought everything, and got ready. And once again, just minutes before we were supposed to start, she canceled. This time, she claimed she was in too much pain to come over. But once again, her story did not hold up. Her partner told mine that they were actually heading out for the evening.

So there I was, sitting with everything I had prepared for the second time, and once again I just spent the evening doing the crafts with my partner instead.

Then, about a week ago, she asked if I wanted to plan yet another craft night. At that point, I could not pretend everything was fine. I told her honestly that I did not want to go out and spend money again just to be canceled on at the last minute. That is when she turned it around on me. She said it should not matter because I end up doing the activities with my partner anyway, so it is not a waste.

That is when I tried to explain that it was never really about the money. It was about respect. If I knew I was not going to show up, I would never let someone go out of their way to prepare something for me. I told her it feels impossible to make plans with her because she always ends up choosing time with her partner instead. Somehow, she decided that I was the one in the wrong, saying I should have just rescheduled with her instead of doing the activities without her.

Now she is upset with me, and I am left wondering if I crossed a line by finally calling it out. Was I wrong for moving on with my evening and including my partner instead, or is this friendship starting to feel one-sided for a reason?
