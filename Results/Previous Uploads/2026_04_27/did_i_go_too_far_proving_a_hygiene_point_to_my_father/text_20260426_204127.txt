Did I go too far proving a hygiene point to my father?

This situation has been building for so long that I honestly do not know anymore if I handled it badly or if I finally did what needed to be done. I am twenty two and still living at home with my parents and my younger sister, who is fourteen. We share a small apartment with only one bathroom, which means all four of us are constantly using the same space.

The issue is something that sounds small at first but has turned into a daily source of frustration. My father refuses to lift the toilet seat when he uses the bathroom, and he regularly leaves it dirty. It is not just a visual problem. It smells, it is unpleasant, and it means that every time the rest of us need to use the bathroom, we have to stop and check before sitting down. My mother, my sister, and I have all asked him repeatedly over the years to be more careful or at least clean up after himself, but he either brushes it off or ignores us entirely.

At one point, when my sister was much younger, she sat down without thinking and ended up with a skin irritation, which made the whole situation feel even more serious. Still, nothing changed. It felt like no matter how many times we brought it up, we were just talking into the air.

Recently, I reached a breaking point. I told my father that if he kept leaving the seat in that condition, I would start cleaning it using his underwear. He laughed it off and clearly did not believe I would actually follow through. But I did. Every time I found the seat left in that state, I took a pair of his clean underwear and used it to wipe it down. I even used the extra pairs he kept aside.

It did not take long before he noticed. Eventually, he ran out of clean underwear, and the remaining ones had an obvious smell. That is when he realized what I had been doing. The fallout was immediate and intense. We argued loudly, and since then, we have barely spoken for two months. The atmosphere at home has been tense and uncomfortable ever since.

Some relatives have heard about what happened, and instead of addressing the actual problem, they have told me I am overreacting. They say it is just a habit he does not have and that I should not damage our relationship over something so minor. My sister also wants the conflict to end, but I cannot shake the feeling that this is about more than just a small inconvenience. It feels like basic respect and hygiene in a shared space.

I know what I did was extreme. I am not pretending otherwise. But after years of asking and being ignored, I felt like nothing else was getting through. At the same time, this standoff is starting to wear on me, and I can feel how much it is affecting the household.

Now I am stuck wondering if I crossed a line that I should not have, or if standing my ground in such a drastic way was the only way to be heard.
