Was I Wrong for Snapping at My Father After He Blamed Me for My Brother’s Accident?

I’m a 19-year-old woman, and my younger brother is 18. He has developmental challenges that affect his judgment and awareness of situations around him. While he is capable of handling most day-to-day activities, our parents have always been extremely protective of him, often excusing behavior that would normally result in consequences for someone his age.

A few weeks ago, my brother and I were riding home in a rideshare vehicle after spending the afternoon in the city. The driver pulled over on a relatively quiet residential street so we could get out. While I was still in the back seat handling payment through the app and confirming the trip details, I told my brother to wait until it was safe and then get out of the vehicle.

Instead of checking his surroundings, he immediately swung the door open toward the road.

Unfortunately, a passing car struck the door before either of us could react.

The impact caused significant damage to both vehicles. Thankfully, nobody was injured, but the situation was chaotic. Rather than stay and help deal with the aftermath, my brother panicked and ran down the street.

I stayed behind with the driver, exchanged information, and helped explain what had happened. Afterward, I called our mother and informed her about the accident. I suggested that at least part of the repair costs should come from my brother’s savings because he had ignored basic safety precautions and was directly responsible for opening the door into traffic.

That idea went over terribly.

My brother immediately refused and insisted he shouldn’t have to pay anything. He became angry and argued that it wasn’t his fault. Later that day, despite everything that had happened, our family continued with plans to go shopping and out for dinner. I privately asked my mother whether we should cancel the outing or impose some kind of consequence, but she said no. In fact, my brother ended up getting several things he wanted throughout the day while the accident was barely mentioned again.

The real conflict started when I got home.

My father called me and asked for my version of events. I explained exactly what happened, including that I had told my brother to get out only when it was safe.

The moment he heard that, he exploded.

He insisted that I should never have told my brother to exit from that side of the vehicle and argued that the entire situation was my fault. According to him, I had been irresponsible and reckless by giving that instruction in the first place. He repeatedly called my actions stupid and claimed I should have known better.

I was already frustrated that nobody seemed willing to hold my brother accountable, and eventually I lost my temper.

I pointed out that people exit vehicles from that side all the time when they first check for traffic. I said the issue wasn’t the instruction itself but the fact that my brother failed to look before opening the door. Then I asked my father whether he was being ridiculous for blaming me instead of the person who actually opened the door.

The argument escalated quickly after that. Voices were raised, insults were exchanged, and eventually my father demanded that I apologize for disrespecting him.

Since then, most of my relatives have focused on the fact that I argued with my father rather than the accident itself. Meanwhile, my brother received very little criticism and was largely defended because of his developmental condition. The overall message seemed to be that I should have anticipated his mistake and prevented it entirely.

I understand that I shouldn’t have insulted my father during the argument. At the same time, I feel resentful that everyone seems determined to blame me while refusing to acknowledge my brother’s role in what happened.

Now I’m wondering whether I was wrong for snapping back after being blamed, or whether my frustration was understandable given the circumstances.

Was I wrong?
