Was I Wrong for Refusing to Drive My Boyfriend to the Emergency Room a Second Time?

I’m a 30-year-old woman, and my boyfriend is 35. Over the past week, he has been dealing with what doctors have described as a viral infection. Five days ago, he had a severe headache that became so intense we went to the emergency room late in the evening.

We ended up staying there for nearly eight hours. While we were waiting, I tried to make him as comfortable as possible. I brought a blanket, noise-cancelling headphones, an eye mask, and ice packs, and I handled most of the logistics since he was feeling unwell. At the hospital, he was given intravenous fluids and a strong pain medication for his headache. After a full evaluation, including blood tests and a scan, everything came back normal. The diagnosis was a virus, and we were told to manage symptoms at home.

Since then, I have been juggling a new job that I only just started, while also taking care of him as he recovers. I’ve made sure he has food, drinks, and whatever he needs, and I’ve tried to be patient even when his symptoms flare up.

The issue started when he asked to go back to the emergency room a few days later. He said he wanted the same medication he was given for his headache because it had helped temporarily. From my perspective, his overall condition had improved. He was eating again, keeping fluids down, and getting some sleep. The headache was still there at times, but it didn’t seem like a sudden or worsening emergency.

Because of that, I told him I didn’t think another emergency room visit made sense. I suggested that if he still wanted to go, his sister had already offered to take him, especially since she understands I’m in my second week at a new job and need to stay focused. She agreed to help if needed.

He didn’t like that answer and insisted I take him instead. I said no, calmly, and repeated that if he felt strongly about going, his sister could drive him.

He eventually decided he would go on his own.

What has been bothering me most about this situation isn’t just the repeated request to go back to the hospital, but the contrast in how we’ve handled each other’s medical situations. A few weeks ago, I had a serious health scare involving internal bleeding. I went to the emergency room alone, managed everything myself, and returned home without him ever leaving work or checking in. I didn’t ask him to drop everything, and I even told him not to worry about coming.

That experience made it hard for me to understand why he expected me to take time off work again for what medical staff had already diagnosed as a virus, especially when alternative help was available.

I’ve tried to be supportive throughout his illness, but I also feel like I’m being asked to prioritize repeated hospital visits for a condition that has already been assessed, while I’m trying to adjust to a new job and manage my own responsibilities.

Now I’m left wondering whether I was being unsupportive by refusing the drive, or whether it was reasonable to set a boundary given the circumstances.
