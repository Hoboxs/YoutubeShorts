Did I Cross a Line by Turning Away Family at My Daycare?

I run a small in home childcare program and take care of several young children during the week, including a baby related to me through my partner’s side of the family. Having her in my care has been genuinely meaningful, and I love being involved in her milestones, but I also run my daycare with clear written policies that apply to every parent equally. One of those rules is about supplies, especially diapers. I always let parents know well in advance when their child’s stash is running low, first in person at pickup and then with reminders by message. If a child arrives with only a few diapers left and no replacements brought in despite multiple warnings, I cannot take them for the day because I am not allowed to use other children’s supplies or provide my own. Over the past few weeks, the baby’s parent repeatedly promised to bring more diapers and repeatedly forgot, brushing it off each time as a simple mistake. The night before the issue finally came to a head, I sent a very clear message explaining that diapers needed to be brought the next morning or I would have to refuse care for the day. When she arrived the next morning with nothing, she was furious that I followed through, insisting that family should be an exception, that I should bend the rules out of love, and that I clearly did not understand how hectic life is for parents. I stayed calm and explained that making exceptions for relatives would be unfair to everyone else and would undermine my professionalism, but she accused me of being cold and heartless for prioritizing rules over family. Now there is tension at family gatherings and whispers that I chose policy over compassion, even though I feel like I simply did my job the way I do for everyone.
