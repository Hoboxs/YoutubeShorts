Did I Betray My Family by Choosing My Own Future?

I am a nineteen year old woman who grew up being treated as half of a matched set with my twin sister Lina, where nearly every choice in my life revolved around what she wanted and what kept her comfortable. From childhood we shared a loud pastel bedroom because she liked bright colors, and I once got punished in primary school for hanging a sketch of a dragon because it was considered too gloomy for her taste. Our mother dressed us alike for years because she thought it was charming, and when Lina wanted to take dance classes in grade school, I was enrolled too even though I begged to try kickboxing, which my father shut down by saying I was selfish and my mother argued it was too aggressive for my sister. As we got older it only intensified, with me being forced to include her in every social plan, being accused of exclusion if I wanted time alone, and even watching her pursue boys I admitted liking almost immediately after I shared my feelings. By our final year of secondary school she had already decided that we would both attend a beauty academy together and our parents fully supported that plan without ever asking what I wanted. Feeling like my life was already written for me, I quietly applied to join the coast guard in another country shortly after my birthday and was accepted. When I finally sat my parents and Lina down to tell them I would not be attending the academy and would be leaving for training instead, they reacted with shock and anger, accusing me of being disloyal, cruel, and willing to abandon my family simply to do something on my own for once, leaving me questioning whether choosing independence really made me the villain they claim I am.
