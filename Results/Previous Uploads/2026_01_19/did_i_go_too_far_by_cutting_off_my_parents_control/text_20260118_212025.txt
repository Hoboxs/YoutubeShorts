Did I Go Too Far by Cutting Off My Parents’ Control?

I am a nineteen year old university student in my second year, and until recently my parents were covering most of my tuition and rent while I handled smaller expenses. In exchange they required that I keep a location tracking app active on my phone at all times so they could see where I was, which made me uncomfortable but I accepted because they were financially supporting me. Over winter break everything changed after a massive argument with my father when I switched my program from chemistry to practical healthcare, a decision he said was reckless and a waste of my potential even though it felt deeply right to me. After that fight my parents cut off all financial help completely, forcing me to take on two part time jobs and significant student loans to stay enrolled, and since then I have paid for everything on my own. A few weeks ago I went on a short road trip out of province with friends, and my mother began sending frantic messages demanding to know why I was so far from campus, which is when I realized they were still tracking me. Feeling shocked and violated, I deleted the app immediately, only to be flooded with angry calls from both parents accusing me of being irresponsible and unsafe without constant monitoring. I calmly told them that since I am now financially independent I need privacy and autonomy, and that being tracked no longer feels appropriate. My father exploded in anger and my mother broke down, insisting I was trying to abandon them entirely, and they have now threatened to exclude me from future family holidays unless I reinstall the app. I feel torn between guilt and relief, because while I love my parents, I also feel like I am finally breathing for the first time and choosing my own life.
