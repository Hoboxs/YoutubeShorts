Was I wrong for refusing to step in after my husband ignored what I know helps our daughter?

I’m 35 and my spouse is 38. We have a five-year-old daughter who is neurodivergent. I’ve spent a lot of time learning about how to support her, especially when it comes to managing overstimulation and preventing meltdowns. My husband, on the other hand, hasn’t shown much interest in learning about that side of things.

Another issue in our relationship is that he often feels I micromanage everything. I can understand why it might feel that way to him, but a lot of what I do is simply trying to anticipate our daughter’s needs and regulate her environment so she doesn’t become overwhelmed.

Because this tension has been affecting our relationship, I’ve been trying to step back more and keep my opinions to myself unless absolutely necessary.

Earlier today our daughter came home already overstimulated. I helped her settle down by letting her draw and playing calm music for a while. Later, my husband started playing a video game. Our daughter wanted his attention, so he decided to show her the game instead of playing alone.

The game has a lot of flashing visuals and loud sounds, and he turned the volume up. They played together for about an hour while I was cleaning our daughter’s room.

When he finally stopped playing, our daughter was very dysregulated and struggling emotionally. He tried to get her to go take a bath, but she was having a really hard time with it. At that point he came to me and asked me to take over.

Instead of stepping in like I normally would, I said no.

My reasoning was that he had chosen the activity and the environment that led to her becoming overstimulated, and I felt it was important that he handle the aftermath instead of expecting me to fix it.

Now I’m wondering if that was the wrong approach. Part of me worries it might have been unfair to our daughter, but another part of me feels like he needs to experience the consequences of ignoring the things I usually try to manage.

Was I wrong for refusing to step in?
