Was I wrong for locking away my hobby supplies from my spouse?

I’m really into tabletop modeling and spend a lot of my free time printing and painting figurines, so I’ve set up a dedicated workspace in the basement of our house with specialized tools, paints, and equipment that I’ve invested a lot of money and time into maintaining. My spouse, Lena, is also into arts and crafts, and while I don’t mind sharing some general items, she regularly takes my supplies without asking and never returns them to where they belong. She struggles with attention issues and organization, so I’ve found my high-end paints left open in random rooms, brushes ruined in sinks, and once even discovered my precision spray tool completely clogged because she used it and walked away without cleaning it, leaving me to spend hours fixing the damage. I’ve tried repeatedly to talk to her calmly about putting things back or at least letting me know when she borrows something, but nothing ever changes, and she admits she grabs my equipment because she can’t keep track of her own materials. After months of frustration and feeling like my boundaries weren’t being respected, I finally installed a lock on the cabinet that holds my personal supplies so I could at least rely on my things being usable when I needed them. When she noticed, she became furious and accused me of excluding her and treating our shared home like it wasn’t hers too, while I argued that I was just protecting items I paid for and maintained after every other approach failed—so am I the asshole for locking my stuff up?
