Am I wrong for feeling frustrated that my spouse won’t allow regular food in our home?

I am in my mid thirties and have been married for several years. Until recently, food was never a major source of conflict between my spouse and me. Over the past year, however, things have shifted in a way that has left me feeling increasingly boxed in and strangely uncomfortable in my own home.

My spouse has become extremely committed to preparing all food from scratch. At first it felt harmless and even impressive. Homemade bread, snacks, and desserts slowly replaced store bought versions, and I appreciated the effort and skill involved. Over time though, this approach expanded to nearly everything we eat. Sauces, staples, and even items that most people buy frozen or prepackaged are now made entirely at home. I will be clear that the food tastes good and I recognize how much time and energy goes into it. That part is not in question.

What has become difficult is that there is no room left for choice. Sometimes I simply want the familiar, convenient version of certain foods. Not as a rejection of her cooking, but because I enjoy them or find comfort in them. My spouse’s position is that because she does most of the cooking and grocery shopping, all food decisions should align with her values. She believes that processed or “junk” food does not belong in the house at all, and that allowing it would undermine what she is trying to do.

Her solution is that if I want those foods, I should eat them elsewhere. That means grabbing fast food on the way home, eating snacks at work, or keeping those preferences entirely out of sight. Over time, this has made me feel oddly secretive and juvenile, like I am hiding something instead of making a reasonable adult choice. I never expected that wanting a bag of chips or a simple store bought meal would turn into something I have to justify or relocate.

Whenever I try to explain how this makes me feel, the conversation turns into how ungrateful I am being and how I am minimizing her effort. That hurts, because I do appreciate what she does. I just do not want every single food decision in our shared home to be dictated by one person’s philosophy, especially when it leaves me feeling excluded from my own kitchen.

At this point, I am less upset about the food itself and more unsettled by the sense that my preferences do not matter. I am wondering whether I am truly being unreasonable, or if it is fair to expect some balance and autonomy in a home we both share.
