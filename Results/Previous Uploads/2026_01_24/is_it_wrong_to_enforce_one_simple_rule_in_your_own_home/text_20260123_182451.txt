Is it wrong to enforce one simple rule in your own home?

I am in my late twenties and grew up in a household where taking off shoes indoors was the norm. It was simply how my family lived, and as an adult I have kept that same rule in my own place. I do not wear shoes inside, and when people come over I politely ask them to remove theirs as well. I understand that not everyone grew up this way, but it is something that matters to me and helps my home feel clean and comfortable.

Recently, I hosted a small dinner at my apartment for several friends. Most people had no issue at all and took their shoes off without comment. One guest, however, immediately pushed back. She said her shoes were an important part of her outfit and that she did not want to remove them. I calmly explained that I really do not allow shoes indoors and that I was asking everyone to follow the same rule.

She became irritated and insisted that I was being unreasonable. I apologized but stood my ground, explaining again that this was simply how my home operates. At that point, she got angry, accused me of being controlling, and left the apartment altogether.

Since then, I have been wondering if I handled the situation poorly or if I should have just let it go to avoid conflict. I never intended to offend anyone, but I also feel like it is fair to set boundaries in my own living space.
