Was I wrong for refusing to let my younger brother move into my flat for free?

I’m 27 and live alone in my own flat. I’ve been there since 2019 and have built a comfortable life for myself. My younger brother “Dave” is 19 and still lives with our mum. He doesn’t pay rent, doesn’t contribute to food, and generally has no real financial responsibilities. The only thing he reliably pays for is when he goes out to eat or drink.

Lately, he and our mum have been constantly arguing. I only know how bad it’s gotten because they both call me separately to complain about each other. Some of it is genuinely petty — like him calling her awful names because she asked him to wash dishes.

My mum recently suggested that Dave move into my flat temporarily for about eight weeks. The idea would be to convert my living room into a second bedroom for him.

I immediately dismissed it.

First, based on how he behaves at home, I have serious doubts about what he’d be like in my space. Second, there are financial consequences. If he moves in, I lose my single-person council tax discount, which would significantly increase my monthly expenses. On top of that, he’d bring his gaming PC and use more electricity. Realistically, the extra costs would add up to around £400 a month — and that’s before factoring in food.

I actually discussed this with Dave before the current arguments escalated. I told him if he ever moved in, he’d need to contribute around that amount to cover increased bills. He flat-out refused, assuming he’d live with me for free.

Now that tensions at home are worse, my mum keeps pushing the idea of him staying with me “temporarily.” But I don’t believe it would actually be temporary. Given his lack of urgency to work or pay his way now, I can easily see it stretching far beyond eight weeks.

I’ve suggested that if things are so bad, our mum could set firmer boundaries or even ask him to move out since he’s an adult. She refuses and instead keeps pressuring me to step in.

Recently, I ran into Dave and the topic came up again. During the conversation, he casually said that if our mum died tomorrow, he wouldn’t care. That comment honestly shocked me and made me even more hesitant about inviting that energy into my home.

Now I’m stuck wondering if I’m being selfish by protecting my space and finances, or if I’m simply setting reasonable boundaries.

So, was I wrong for refusing to house him for free and potentially letting their conflict continue?
