Was I wrong for actually trying when my date told me not to go easy on her?

For context, I (24M) play pool regularly and at a fairly competitive level. Two of the guys I practice with compete in national tournaments, so I’m used to playing serious games. I don’t consider myself a pro, but I’m definitely above average.

Last Saturday I went on a date with a woman who had mentioned she liked playing pool. I suggested a pool bar I know well. We met up, grabbed some drinks, and started playing. At first, I was taking it easy — just enjoying the vibe, not overthinking shots, keeping it fun and relaxed.

After a few casual games and some food, I mentioned that a couple of my friends compete at nationals. That’s when she smiled and said, “Okay, next game, don’t go easy on me.”

So I adjusted. I didn’t go full tournament mode, but I made my play more precise — better positioning, fewer risky shots. She still had chances, and the games were competitive.

Right before we left, we decided to rack up one last game. I broke, sank two stripes off the break, and had a clean layout. Since she’d said not to go easy on her, I just played it straight through the way I normally would. I ended up clearing the table in one run.

We finished our drinks, I paid, and drove her home. On the ride back she was noticeably quieter, but I figured she was just tired.

When I got home, I saw a message from her saying:

“I asked you not to go easy on me, but I didn’t mean turn it into a statement. It honestly felt a bit like an ego thing at the end, and it made me uncomfortable.”

That genuinely surprised me. From my perspective, I just respected her request. I wasn’t trash talking, celebrating, or trying to embarrass her. I had a good break and played it out cleanly. I even paid for the night and kept things polite and low-key.

I responded explaining that it wasn’t about ego — I just took her at her word and played normally. She hasn’t replied.

Now I’m wondering if I misread the situation. Was I supposed to balance competitiveness with keeping it socially “safe”? Or is it reasonable to take someone at their word when they say not to go easy on them?

So, was I in the wrong for playing my best that last game?
