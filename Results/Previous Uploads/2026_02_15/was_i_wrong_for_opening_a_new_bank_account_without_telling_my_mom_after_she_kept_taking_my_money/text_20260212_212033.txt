Was I wrong for opening a new bank account without telling my mom after she kept taking my money?

I’m 19 and trying to get my finances under control for the first time in my life. Growing up, my parents didn’t let me work during high school because they wanted me focused on academics. I did well — I graduated valedictorian — but it also meant I never really had control over my own money.

A few years ago, my grandmother gave me about $7,000 to help me get started with college. Any time I had checks or cash to deposit growing up, my mom would put it into a joint savings account that she said was “mine,” but I didn’t have direct access to it.

When I finally went to open my own checking account at 18, my mom came with me and suggested we just keep the existing savings account joint. I didn’t think much of it at the time. But when I checked the balance, there was only about $1,000 left. She had previously told me she occasionally borrowed from it and would pay it back, and I believed her. I had no idea the balance had dropped that much.

Not long after that, my old 1999 Mercury Grand Marquis finally died. My mom’s side of the family pushed hard for me to get a newer car. I was hesitant, especially when I found out the monthly payment would be around $900, but I was told I wouldn’t have to shoulder the full amount right away. My dad and his mom gave me another $7,500 to help, and my mom’s parents put about $3,000 down on the car.

Recently, I was told that the money from my dad and grandmother had run out and that I’d need to start contributing again. I’ve been actively job hunting — applications, interviews, the works — but since I didn’t have job experience in high school, I’ve had trouble landing something stable. I’ve been doing DoorDash in the meantime, but it’s inconsistent.

I also received about $2,000 back from my college financial aid refund. I wanted to set some aside for daily expenses and use a portion to plan something special for my partner for Valentine’s Day. When the refund hit my account, my mom immediately insisted the money needed to go toward the car and said I couldn’t use it freely. Because the savings account is still joint, I technically needed her approval to move funds.

I transferred $200 for some early shopping and date plans. A few days later, I checked my account and nearly all of the refund was gone — about $50 remained. When I confronted her, she said I should have listened to her and that I couldn’t be trusted with my own money. She also brought up the fact that I moved in with my dad recently and implied that if I wanted independence, I should be prepared to cover the entire $900 monthly car payment myself.

At this point, I’m seriously considering opening a completely separate account at a different bank without telling her, just so I can have control over my own finances. I feel conflicted because she insists she’s trying to teach me responsibility, but it feels more like I’m being shut out of decisions about my own money.

So, am I wrong for wanting to open a new account and handle my finances independently?
