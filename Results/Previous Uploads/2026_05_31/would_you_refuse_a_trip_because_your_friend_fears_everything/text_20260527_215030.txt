Would You Refuse a Trip Because Your Friend Fears Everything?

A few months ago, one of my close friends, Maya, started talking nonstop about taking a road trip to see the red rock parks in northern Arizona. I have wanted to go there for years, so she assumed I would immediately say yes when she invited me. Instead, I told her I was going to pass.

She looked genuinely shocked and kept pushing for an explanation. At first, I blamed finances because it felt easier than being honest, but she immediately called me out and said she knew that was not the real reason.

The truth is that traveling with her has become exhausting because she is terrified of almost everything. She is twenty-five years old, but she refuses to fly unless someone she knows is sitting beside her the entire time. She panics about taking rideshares alone, constantly asks friends to share live locations, and acts like every stranger is seconds away from attacking her. I understand being careful, especially as a woman, but with her it goes far beyond normal caution.

Every outing turns into managing her anxiety. One time we were eating at a café with a group of friends, and she insisted we leave halfway through because she thought a man across the room was staring at her in a threatening way. In reality, he just looked irritated because our table was being loud. Situations like that happen constantly.

I have gently suggested therapy before because it honestly seems like she lives in a constant state of fear, but she completely rejects the idea and says everyone else is too trusting.

When she kept demanding the real reason I would not go on the trip, I finally told her that traveling with her stresses me out because she seems scared of the world all the time. I tried to say it kindly, but she still got really hurt. Then she asked if I would take the same trip with someone else, and I admitted that yes, I probably would.

That started a huge argument. Since then she has ignored all of my messages, and now I feel guilty wondering if I was too harsh. At the same time, I do not think I should have to spend an entire vacation calming someone down every few hours because they think danger is everywhere.
