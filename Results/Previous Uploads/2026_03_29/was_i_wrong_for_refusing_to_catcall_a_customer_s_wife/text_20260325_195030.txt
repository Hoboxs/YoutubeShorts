Was I wrong for refusing to catcall a customer’s wife?

I am a thirty three year old man working as a cook in a restaurant where the kitchen is fully open, so guests can see us and even talk to us while we prepare food. A couple of days ago, during a slow moment, a man walked up to our station and asked for what he called a favor. He said his wife would be walking past soon and wanted us to whistle at her and call out compliments as she passed by.

There were three of us working that station at the time. Before I could even react, one of my coworkers quickly agreed on behalf of all of us. As soon as the man left, the other cook and I made it clear that we were not comfortable with what had just been promised. We told him directly that we would not be taking part, but he seemed annoyed and insisted that it had already been agreed to. We reminded him that he had spoken for himself, not for us.

A few minutes later, a server came by and said the woman was about to walk past. It seemed like several people on staff already knew about what was supposed to happen. When she finally walked by, she carried herself with confidence, almost like she was expecting the attention. The coworker who had agreed started whistling and cheering, while the two of us stayed focused on our work and said nothing.

After she sat down, the reaction from some of the staff caught me off guard. The coworker who participated, along with a few servers, criticized us for not joining in. They said we had made the moment awkward and taken the fun out of it. From my perspective, it did not feel like harmless fun. I was uncomfortable participating in something like that, especially given how it could be perceived coming from me in that setting. It felt like a situation that could easily be misunderstood or cross a line, regardless of what the husband intended.

Since then, the atmosphere at work has been tense, and it feels like some people think I overreacted. I cannot help but wonder if I should have just gone along with it to avoid the conflict, or if standing my ground was the right choice even if it made things uncomfortable.
