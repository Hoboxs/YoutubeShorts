Was I Wrong to Cut Her Off and Force Insurance After She Turned Hostile?

During a brutal winter storm, my car skidded on unseen ice and smashed straight into my neighbor’s mailbox. I was horrified and immediately owned up to it, gave her my insurance details, and apologized repeatedly. A few days later, she told me the insurance company wanted her to pay out of pocket first and wait for reimbursement, which she claimed she couldn’t do. Wanting to make things right as quickly as possible, I offered to personally buy a new mailbox and pay someone I know to install it, explaining that there would be a short delay because the mailbox wouldn’t arrive until the end of the month and the installer was out of town with family.

When the mailbox finally arrived, she called me while I was dealing with a medical emergency involving my child that landed us in the hospital. I couldn’t answer, but I immediately reached out to the installer and had him contact her directly. He confirmed he had the mailbox and scheduled the installation for early the following week.

Instead of being relieved, she started sending pointed, sarcastic messages accusing me of not caring at all. I explained—again—that my child had been hospitalized and that I hadn’t ignored her, I’d just been overwhelmed and still made sure the repair was scheduled. That’s when her tone escalated. She told me I should be ashamed, that I was disrespectful, and that I hadn’t apologized enough, despite the fact that we’d already spent hours on the phone where I’d taken full responsibility and expressed how sorry I was.

When I finally said that if the timeline wasn’t acceptable she could just proceed through my insurance, she completely exploded. She sent a long message calling me immoral, insulting my character, and mocking me for even thinking I could handle the repair myself. At that point, something snapped. I told her I was returning the mailbox, that she should go through insurance using the information I already provided, and that I was done being verbally abused over an accident I was actively trying to fix. Then I blocked her.

Now I’m left wondering if I crossed a line by pulling my help and forcing the insurance route after she turned the situation into a personal attack.
