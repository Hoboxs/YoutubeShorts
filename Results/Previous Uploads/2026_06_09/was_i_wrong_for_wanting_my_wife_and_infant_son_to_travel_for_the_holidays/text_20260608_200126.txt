Was I Wrong for Wanting My Wife and Infant Son to Travel for the Holidays?

I’m a 35-year-old man, and my wife is 33. We are expecting our first child this September, and while we’re both excited about becoming parents, a disagreement about the holidays has turned into one of the biggest conflicts of our marriage.

We currently live in New Zealand near my wife’s relatives. My family, however, lives in Singapore, which requires a long international flight followed by several hours of additional travel. Before we got married, we had many conversations about maintaining strong connections with both sides of the family despite the distance. The understanding was that major holidays would be split fairly, with one side of the family seeing us for some celebrations and the other side seeing us for others.

The complication is that my parents have never fully embraced my wife. They were disappointed when I decided to build my life overseas and have often made comments suggesting I should eventually move back home. While they have remained civil, there has always been some tension beneath the surface, and my wife is very aware of it.

Recently, another factor entered the picture. My younger brother and his wife are expecting a baby in October, and by the end of the year there will be a rare opportunity for nearly my entire extended family to be together in one place. My grandparents are getting older, several cousins are planning to attend holiday gatherings, and there is a real possibility that this could be the only time in years that everyone is available at the same time.

Because of that, I suggested that we travel there during the holiday season. By then, our son would be around four months old. I was excited about the idea of my grandparents meeting their first great-grandchild, my relatives meeting the baby, and our son meeting his new cousin.

My wife immediately rejected the idea.

She told me she is not comfortable taking such a young infant on a long international journey. She also said that she has no interest in spending the holidays surrounded by relatives who have never fully welcomed her, especially while she is still adjusting to life as a new mother. She believes that traveling with a baby that young would be stressful, exhausting, and potentially miserable for everyone involved.

I understand where she is coming from. I know the first months after childbirth can be physically and emotionally demanding, and I recognize that my family has not always made things easy for her.

At the same time, I feel disappointed because the expectations we discussed before marriage seem to have changed significantly. I always imagined our children growing up with strong relationships on both sides of the family, and opportunities for my relatives to gather together are incredibly rare. It hurts knowing that my grandparents, parents, siblings, cousins, and new nephew may all be together while my wife and son stay home.

The more I try to explain why the trip matters to me, the more frustrated my wife becomes. She says I am prioritizing my family’s wishes over her comfort and recovery. I feel like I am simply trying to preserve the family traditions and commitments we discussed long before a baby entered the picture.

Now I’m wondering whether I am being unreasonable for continuing to push for the trip, or whether it is fair to hope that we stick to the plans and expectations we originally shared as a couple.
