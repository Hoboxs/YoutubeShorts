Was I wrong for stepping in when a man didn’t want to be filmed?

I’m a 29-year-old man, and yesterday I was waiting outside a subway station in a busy district of Manchester, England, after finishing a late shift when I noticed a man in his late 50s sitting against a brick wall nearby who looked exhausted and like he’d been struggling for a while. A few minutes later, a couple in their early 20s arrived carrying a tote bag of supplies and a smartphone, and they immediately started recording as they walked toward him. At first I assumed it was some kind of street outreach video, but it quickly became uncomfortable because the man visibly turned his face away from the camera, raised his hand to shield himself, and pressed closer to the wall while clearly avoiding eye contact. The woman kept speaking directly to the phone about “helping someone in need today,” while the man on camera held out the bag and encouraged him to take it even though the older man hesitated and kept looking down, clearly uneasy with being filmed. The woman insisted he was just shy and continued recording despite his discomfort, so I walked over and told them directly that he didn’t seem okay with being on camera and that they should either stop filming or help him without recording him. She told me they blur faces later anyway and that it was fine, but I said that if the priority was truly helping him, the camera shouldn’t matter at all. The guy filming got annoyed, stopped recording, and said I had ruined usable content for their social media page, and I replied that if the footage mattered more than the person in front of them then it wasn’t really charity. They eventually left the supplies with him and walked off, and once they were gone the man quietly thanked me, took the bag, and left around the corner without saying much else. Now I’m second-guessing myself because I may have interfered in something that wasn’t my business, but it really didn’t sit right with me watching someone who clearly didn’t consent being filmed for content, so I’m wondering if I overstepped or did the right thing.
