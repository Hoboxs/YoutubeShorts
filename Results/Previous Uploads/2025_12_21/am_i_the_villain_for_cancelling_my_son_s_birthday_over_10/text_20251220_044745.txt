Am I the Villain for Canceling My Son’s Birthday Over $10?

I (34F) spent weeks planning what I thought would be my son’s most memorable birthday yet. He’s turning 13—a big milestone—and money is tight, so I stretched myself to make it special. I rented batting cages for the kids, ordered a cake, planned snacks and games, and even organized a scavenger hunt outside with a small cash prize to make it exciting. I wanted this birthday to feel magical, even though we’re not wealthy.

A few days before the party, I pulled out the $20 I had set aside for the scavenger hunt prize and separated it into four $5 bills. Later that day, only my son and one of his friends were in the apartment while I was briefly working in my room. When I returned, I noticed my wallet and purse were open in a way that’s not normal for me. That’s when I saw it—$10 missing. I immediately confronted my son. He swore he didn’t take it. I asked about his friend. Same answer.

Here’s the thing: my son has never stolen anything, but he has lied about small things before. I’ve caught him sneaking snacks and telling little fibs, and it’s been a recurring issue. I wanted to give him the benefit of the doubt, but everything pointed to him knowing what had happened. I couldn’t ignore it. This wasn’t just $10—it was a lesson in trust and honesty.

After arguing, discussing, and trying to get to the truth, I realized I couldn’t in good conscience let him have the party. If he did take the money and I let the celebration go on, I’d be rewarding dishonesty and sending the message that lying has no consequences. So I made the gut-wrenching decision to cancel the party entirely.

Now my son is devastated. He’s heartbroken that his milestone birthday is ruined, and I can’t stop feeling guilty. Part of me wonders if I overreacted over $10, but another part of me knows I had to stand firm. I wanted to teach him a lesson he’d remember—but at what cost?
