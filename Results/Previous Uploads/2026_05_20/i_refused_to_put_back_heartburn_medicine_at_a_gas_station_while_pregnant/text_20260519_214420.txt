I Refused to Put Back Heartburn Medicine at a Gas Station While Pregnant.

I’m 26 and currently about five months pregnant. Lately the acid reflux has been brutal. Some days it feels like I’m breathing fire, especially at night or after eating anything greasy.

The other evening my boyfriend and I went out for dinner at a little diner known for heavy comfort food. Before we even arrived, I could already feel the heartburn starting. Since most pharmacies were already closed, I asked him to stop at a gas station so I could grab something for it.

The station only had individual travel packs of the medicine instead of full bottles. Since I’ve been dealing with reflux almost daily, I figured it made sense to stock up a bit so I could keep some in my purse, some in my boyfriend’s car, and some at home.

I took around ten of the single packets from the display box, leaving several behind.

When we got to the register, the cashier immediately asked if I had left any in the box. I told him yes, and he actually walked over to check. Apparently he didn’t think I left enough because he started complaining that he uses that medication regularly himself.

My boyfriend apologized and quietly suggested I could maybe put a few back, but I said no. I explained that I genuinely needed them and that this has become an everyday problem for me lately.

The cashier stopped arguing, but he clearly looked annoyed. My boyfriend then mentioned that I’m pregnant, which sort of ended the conversation. We paid and left.

Once we got back to the car though, my boyfriend asked if I really needed to take that many. In my mind, ten small packets didn’t seem excessive because I usually need two at a time, and I wanted extras available so I wouldn’t get stuck without any again.

Now I’m wondering if I was being inconsiderate for taking most of what was there, or if it was reasonable given the situation.
