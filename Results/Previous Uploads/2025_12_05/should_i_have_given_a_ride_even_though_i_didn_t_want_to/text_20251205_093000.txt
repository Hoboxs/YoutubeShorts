Should I Have Given a Ride Even Though I Didn’t Want To?

I recently started a job where the schedule runs ten days on and ten days off. It was my first full stretch, and since I live about an hour and a half from the site, I always drive in. A lot of the others come from different parts of another country, so most of them fly.

About halfway through the shift, a guy I’d never met before joined our team. He was a constant smoker, always stepping out for cigarettes. When our rotation wrapped up, someone asked if I could take him to the airport because he knew I had my car with me. I told them I couldn’t, and then I left.

What I didn’t say was that I didn’t want my car to end up smelling like smoke, and I also didn’t want to become the person everyone automatically relies on for airport rides every time we finish a shift. Now I’ve heard people are talking about it, and apparently he’s annoyed with me for not taking him.

I still believe I had the right to say no — but does that make me the bad guy?
