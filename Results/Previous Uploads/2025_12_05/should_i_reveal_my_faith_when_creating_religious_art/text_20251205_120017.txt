Should I Reveal My Faith When Creating Religious Art?

I create commissioned artwork and accept projects from a wide variety of clients, as long as the subject isn’t hateful, discriminatory, or sexually explicit. Recently, I completed a commissioned piece featuring Christian imagery—an ornate cross, a saint, and calligraphed scripture. The client was pleased with the work and paid me, but then asked what my favorite scripture was. I honestly said that I don’t really have one, because I’m not Christian and don’t personally follow the faith.

The client became upset, saying he felt misled because he had seen other examples of my Christian-themed artwork and assumed I was a believer. He argued that the spiritual value of the piece was diminished since it wasn’t created with “faithful intention.” I explained that I create art for anyone regardless of my personal beliefs, and that I’m happy to produce Christian-themed artwork even if I don’t practice the religion myself.

This left me wondering whether I should be disclosing my personal beliefs when commissioned for religious art. Is it necessary to share my faith in these situations, or is it reasonable for me to focus on the client’s vision without revealing my personal convictions?
