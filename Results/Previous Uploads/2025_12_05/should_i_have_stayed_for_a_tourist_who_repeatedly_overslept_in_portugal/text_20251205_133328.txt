Should I Have Stayed for a Tourist Who Repeatedly Overslept in Portugal?

I’m a 23-year-old tour guide from Romania who leads groups around Portugal. On a recent trip, a solo traveler in her late 30s repeatedly overslept despite clear instructions. Each day, I provide the group with printed schedules, announce departure times the night before, and share my phone number in case anyone needs a reminder. On the first morning, she was fifteen minutes late for a day trip to Sintra, which I excused, but I reminded everyone that I cannot wait beyond twenty minutes without prior notice.

The following morning, she was thirty minutes late despite my calls, and she barely managed to catch the bus due to a last-minute hotel issue. I reminded the group again that waking up on time is each person’s responsibility. That evening, she asked me to knock on her door the next day, but I explained that I couldn’t wake every tourist and that alarms should be set individually.

On the third day, during a trip to Coimbra, she didn’t show up. I waited twenty minutes past the scheduled departure time, called her twice with no answer, and eventually left with the rest of the group. An hour later, she called in a panic, screaming that I had ruined her life, demanded a full refund, and threatened to sue the tour agency for emotional distress. She even posted scathing reviews across multiple travel sites, tagging me personally and claiming I “abandoned a vulnerable woman,” which caused the agency to get dozens of angry emails and nearly lost me future contracts. I had warned her multiple times that I couldn’t be responsible for waking her, so I want to know if leaving her behind—even knowing this could escalate—was unreasonable.
