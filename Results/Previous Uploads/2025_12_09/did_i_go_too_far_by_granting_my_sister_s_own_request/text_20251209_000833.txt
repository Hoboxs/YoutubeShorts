Did I Go Too Far by Granting My Sister’s Own Request?

In my early thirties and preparing for a 2029 wedding in a small coastal town in New Zealand, my fiancée and I have kept a steady peace with both of our families—except for one corner of it. My older sister, Mara, five years my senior, has a history of undermining the very people who help her, and there are more examples than I can count.

At our engagement gathering this past spring, she and her partner, Leo, made a spectacle of tossing around comments about how marriage is “a financial trap,” insisting we should just live together indefinitely instead of signing anything. Their only gift—which we didn’t expect gifts anyway—was a compilation of breakup ballads. It was meant as a joke, apparently, though it felt more like a performance for our parents.

Months later, while planning the holiday visit, Mara—who hasn’t joined a family holiday in nearly a decade—announced she wanted financial help and remarked that “anyone who can throw money at a wedding should be grateful to support us.” She also insisted that everyone start addressing her by Leo’s last name, even though their country doesn’t recognize informal partnerships and she hasn’t changed anything legally. She uses his name on social platforms, at her office, and even signs emails with it, so one would assume she had everything lined up behind the scenes.

Trying to be generous, especially because some of the money would support my niece’s swim lessons, I sent her a check—addressed exactly as she requested, using Leo’s surname rather than her legal one. Predictably, the bank rejected it. Apparently, financial institutions still require actual legal names to process checks.

Now she’s furious, claiming I did it intentionally to humiliate her. I didn’t. I truly thought I was respecting her preference. Of course, I’ll resend the money using her real name—I care about my niece, and I’m not interested in needless conflict.

But I can’t help wondering: was I wrong for following her instructions to the letter, even when her own story didn’t match reality?
