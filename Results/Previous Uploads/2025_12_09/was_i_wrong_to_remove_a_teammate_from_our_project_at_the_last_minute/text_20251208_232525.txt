Was I Wrong to Remove a Teammate From Our Project at the Last Minute?

I’m taking an online biology course at a small community college, and our final lab assignment was a group project. We were supposed to form teams of three, pick an experiment to analyze, and put together a digital presentation. The instructor opened the sign-ups almost five weeks before the deadline, and I contacted my group right away. One of the members, whom I’ll call Lena, barely responded to anything. She cancelled our first planning call ten minutes after it was supposed to start and kept disappearing for days at a time. I understand people have busy lives, but sending a quick update isn’t hard.

Two weeks before the presentation was due, the other teammate and I had already finished our sections. All that was left was Lena’s small portion—just two slides out of the whole project. I messaged her repeatedly, reminding her to finish her part so we’d have time to review everything. Nothing. Eventually, I sent a final note saying I could complete her section myself, but if I did, I’d be leaving her name off. Magically, she finally replied, promising she’d get it done the next evening. She didn’t.

At that point, with three days left, I finished her slides myself, removed her name from the presentation, and submitted it early. Before doing so, I explained the situation to our instructor, who said she appreciated knowing about group members who didn’t contribute. She formally removed Lena from the team roster so the grade would only reflect the work actually done.

Now, after weeks of silence, Lena suddenly messaged our group asking whether I accidentally submitted her slides blank. It became obvious she’d never so much as opened the document—she didn’t even have edit access. She still hasn’t asked why her name isn’t on the submission and seems completely unaware she’ll be getting a zero.

I can’t help feeling guilty. The assignment wasn’t hard for me, and I finished it quickly. Losing these points might seriously hurt her final grade. But at the same time, she had almost a month, ignored every reminder, and left us hanging right up until the deadline. I’m stuck wondering if I was too harsh—or if I simply followed through on a boundary she chose to ignore.
