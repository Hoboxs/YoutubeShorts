Am I wrong for not wanting to take care of my sister’s four children while she gives birth?

My wife and I live in a small two-bedroom apartment with our two children, who are both elementary school age and already share one bedroom. Space is tight as it is.

My sister is pregnant with her fifth child and recently asked if I could take care of her four children while she is in the hospital giving birth, and then keep them for an additional day afterward as well.

Her children are ages twelve, ten, eight, and two.

I told her honestly that I did not think it would work well in our apartment. Between the limited space, our own children, and my work schedule during the week, it would place a lot of pressure on my wife.

My sister responded by telling me I should just take time off work or let all the children “squeeze together” for sleeping arrangements because it would only be temporary.

I still told her no because I genuinely do not think we can realistically accommodate six children and two adults comfortably in our apartment for multiple days.

She became upset and said she was stressed because she needed dependable childcare. I asked why she could not use her regular babysitter, since she already has one. She admitted she did not want to spend the money and also wanted an extra day alone at home with her husband and the new baby before the older children returned.

I apologized, but I still said no.

She kept trying to convince me and eventually became angry, saying I was her only nearby family member and that family is supposed to help each other.

It has now been two days without hearing from her, which is unusual because we normally talk every day.

Also, before anyone suggests it, staying at her house is apparently not an option because she specifically wants the second day alone at home with her husband and newborn without the other children there.

Am I wrong for refusing to take in her four children under these circumstances?
