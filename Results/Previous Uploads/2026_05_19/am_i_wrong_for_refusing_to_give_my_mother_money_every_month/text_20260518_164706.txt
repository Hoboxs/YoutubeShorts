Am I wrong for refusing to give my mother money every month?
My mother recently told me and my two siblings that she expects each of us to give her the equivalent of about three hundred dollars every month for her retirement. According to her, supporting her financially is our responsibility as her children.
Under different circumstances, I probably would not have an issue helping. But the part that bothers me is that she is not struggling financially. She owns several rental properties that bring in more income every month than most of us earn from our jobs.
All three of us are in our thirties and have our own families to support. My sisters are stay-at-home mothers, so realistically their husbands would be the ones paying this money each month.
I asked my mother if she gives her own parents monthly payments like this. She said no, although she has occasionally given them money when needed.
I also pointed out that we are all trying to raise children during a difficult economy with high living costs. Her response was that three hundred dollars is “not that much.” For context, the average monthly salary where we live is around one thousand dollars.
I told her I could never imagine demanding fixed monthly payments from my own children while they were trying to build their own lives and raise families. She responded by saying that children have a duty to take care of their parents.
To be fair, my parents did spend a significant amount of money on our education growing up. We all attended private schools, and because of that I now have a relatively stable job that pays above average.
Still, this feels less like support and more like entitlement to me.
Another important detail is that we are Asian, so there is definitely a cultural expectation of helping parents financially as they age. However, neither my father nor any of my in-laws ask for this kind of monthly payment. My father has his own retirement savings. My mother does not.
What worries me is the long-term expectation. If every parent expected several hundred dollars every month from their adult children, many people would end up financially supporting multiple sets of parents while also trying to raise children of their own.
Am I wrong for refusing to give my mother monthly payments even though culturally it is somewhat expected?
