Was I wrong for contacting my friend’s insurance after they tried to bill me for an accident?

A few weeks ago my daughter (10) went to a birthday party for a friend who had just turned eleven. It was held at the friend’s house, and most of the kids ended up outside playing games in the backyard. At one point they started tossing a football around. The birthday girl’s dad was throwing passes while the kids ran routes to catch them.

Some background matters here. The previous summer, while this family was out of town, their above-ground pool had collapsed and flooded their yard. It caused a lot of damage, and their homeowners insurance had been involved because of structural issues and repairs. During the reconstruction, heavy equipment had been used in the backyard to rebuild a retaining wall, which left deep ruts and uneven ground in the area where the kids were playing.

While running to catch a pass, my daughter tripped in one of those ruts. When she fell, she slammed into a large metal housing box that contained the pool’s pump and water connections. The impact broke part of the equipment, and water started pouring out into the yard. They managed to shut off the water, but because of the way the pump had apparently been patched together after the pool incident, they ended up needing to shut off the entire water main. The family was without running water for about two days until a plumber could come out and fix everything properly.

Unfortunately, my daughter was hurt in the fall. She broke her collarbone and badly sprained both her ankle and her knee.

For a while we thought the situation was just an unfortunate accident. Then about a week ago my husband and I received a certified letter from our friends. In it, they said they expected us to pay for the repairs to the damaged pump, the water damage to their yard, and the inconvenience of being without water for 48 hours.

That completely caught us off guard.

As soon as we received the letter, I contacted their homeowners insurance company. I already had the information because my friend had talked about the insurance situation during the previous pool incident. I reached out to submit my daughter’s medical bills related to the injury.

To be clear, we do have health insurance for our child, and before getting that letter we had planned to cover her medical costs ourselves. But after being asked to pay for damage that happened when she tripped on their property, it felt different.

Now my friend is furious with me. She said that reporting the incident to their insurance has made things worse for them. Apparently their premiums had already increased after the previous claim, and now they’ve been notified that their policy is being canceled due to multiple claims.

She says I should never have contacted their insurance and that I did it out of spite.

From my perspective, they tried to charge us for something that happened on their property while my child got seriously hurt.

Still, I’m wondering if I escalated things unnecessarily.

Was I wrong for contacting their insurance company after receiving that letter?
