Was I Wrong for Demanding My Parking Pass Back?

A few months ago, I moved into a condominium in Vancouver with a roommate. The building has a strict parking policy: every unit receives two parking passes, and vehicles parked without one can be ticketed or towed. Management takes the rule seriously, and several residents have already had their cars removed from the lot.

Because I occasionally switch between my personal vehicle and a company vehicle for work, I usually keep my parking pass in a small basket near the front entrance. It is my assigned pass, and I’m the only one who uses it.

One Friday evening, after finishing a late shift, I came home and noticed the pass was missing. I searched my car and my bags before messaging my roommate to see if she had seen it. She casually replied that she had loaned it to one of her friends for the evening.

I was stunned. I asked why she would give away my parking pass without asking me first. She explained that she assumed I would be spending the night at my partner’s place, so she figured I wouldn’t need it. She had already used her own pass for her vehicle and decided mine was available.

I pointed out that I had never told her I wouldn’t be coming home and that it wasn’t her property to lend out. I told her I needed the pass returned immediately.

She pushed back, saying her friend had already gone out for dinner across town and that it would be awkward to ask her to come back. Instead, she suggested I leave my car in a nearby shopping plaza overnight and retrieve it the next morning.

I refused. That lot is poorly lit, people often gather there late at night, and I wasn’t comfortable leaving my vehicle there because my roommate had given away something that belonged to me. I told her I wasn’t willing to risk a ticket, towing fees, or potential damage to my car because of a decision she made without consulting me.

Eventually, her friend returned about forty-five minutes later and handed over the parking pass. I thanked her, apologized for the inconvenience, and explained that I had only insisted because the pass belonged to me and I genuinely needed it. The friend seemed understanding, but my roommate was clearly annoyed.

Since then, my roommate has accused me of overreacting and embarrassing her in front of her friend. She says it was a small favor and that I made a much bigger issue out of it than necessary. From my perspective, the real problem was that she treated my parking pass as if it were hers to give away and expected me to deal with the consequences.

Now I’m wondering whether I was justified in demanding it back right away or whether I should have simply found another solution for the night. Was I wrong?
