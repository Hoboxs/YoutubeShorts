Was I wrong for taking my baby and leaving my parents’ house?

I’m a twenty-something new mom with a four-month-old son who arrived early and struggled with feeding at first. Because of that, I exclusively pump and bottle-feed breast milk. It works well for him, but it means my schedule revolves around pumping six times a day, including overnight. Once he started sleeping in longer stretches and feeding reliably, my parents invited us to stay with them one night a week so I could get some uninterrupted rest. We agreed, and it became a regular thing. My father has always been incredible with babies and had everything set up to safely care for one overnight, so I trusted him completely.

My mother, however, has a long history of being inattentive with other grandchildren in the family, and everyone has more or less agreed she shouldn’t be left alone with kids. Even so, I decided to give her a chance with very clear limits. I was comfortable with her doing simple things like feeding or changing the baby, nothing more. A few weeks ago, after I came downstairs from a shower, I noticed she was feeding my son a bottle that felt unusually hot. When I asked about it, she brushed it off. I snapped a photo and sent it to my sister, who immediately said it looked like breast milk that had been reheated in the microwave. I calmly explained to my mother why microwaving breast milk isn’t safe, including the risk of burns and nutrient loss. She said she understood, and I tried to move on.

The following week, while cleaning up, I found another bottle near where she usually sits that had clearly been microwaved. This time I was more firm and told her that if it happened again, I would take the baby and leave. A few days later, early in the morning, I went to grab my pumping supplies and realized my son wasn’t with my father or in his crib. I found my mother feeding him yet another bottle that had obviously been reheated the same way. I brought it upstairs to my husband, who agreed with me, and we immediately started packing. When we told my parents we were leaving, my mother denied everything and my father claimed he had prepared the bottle himself, even though I was certain that wasn’t true. When I took my baby from her, she launched into a dramatic rant about how she must be a terrible person.

We left, and I made it clear to my father that this wasn’t about him and that I loved him. He was devastated because this is his first biological grandchild and they’ve formed a strong bond. Later, my mother admitted to him that she had microwaved the milk, but she has refused to apologize or take responsibility. Instead, she’s been telling other family members that I’m cruel and that I’m keeping her grandson from her.

I’m heartbroken about how this has affected my dad, but I feel like I had to protect my child and follow through on the boundary I clearly set. Even so, with all the fallout and guilt being thrown my way, I can’t help but wonder if I handled this wrong.
