Was I wrong for saying no to a last-minute, middle-of-the-night airport ride?

I’ve known my friend Marcus for years, and overall we’ve always had a solid relationship. A little over a week ago, I visited him out of state for my birthday, and he surprised me with an incredibly generous gift worth several hundred dollars. I was genuinely touched by it and thanked him repeatedly, because it wasn’t something I ever expected and I know it came from a place of generosity rather than obligation.

A few nights later, my phone rang in the middle of the night, jolting me awake around 3 a.m. I answered immediately because I assumed something was seriously wrong. Instead, Marcus asked if I could get dressed, hop in my car, and drive him to an airport that was nearly two hours away so he could catch an early-morning flight. That would have meant a long drive there, turning around immediately, and getting back home close to sunrise after being awake all night. I said no, told him I couldn’t do that on such short notice, and went back to sleep.

Later that morning, I woke up to a message from a mutual friend telling me Marcus was furious with me for refusing. Apparently, he felt that after the expensive birthday gift he had given me, I should have been willing to help him out, especially since I didn’t have plans the next day. From his perspective, this was a favor between close friends that I should have said yes to without hesitation.

From my side, the situation felt very different. Marcus is extremely well-off and could easily afford a ride service or other transportation to the airport. This wasn’t a sudden emergency or a situation where he had no alternatives; it was simply a preference to get home earlier. What bothered me most wasn’t just the request itself, but the expectation that I would wake up from a dead sleep, with zero warning, and commit to a long and exhausting drive in the middle of the night. Had he asked me the day before, or even earlier that evening, I likely would have planned around it and agreed. Being put on the spot at 3 a.m. felt unreasonable.

I only picked up the phone because I truly thought something serious had happened, and if it had been an actual emergency, I wouldn’t have hesitated to help. But once I realized it was about transportation that he could easily arrange himself, I didn’t feel right about doing it. Now there’s tension, and I’m being told I was ungrateful and selfish for drawing that boundary, even though I still believe the request itself crossed a line.
