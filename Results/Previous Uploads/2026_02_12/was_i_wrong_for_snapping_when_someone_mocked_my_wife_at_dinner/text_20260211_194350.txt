Was I wrong for snapping when someone mocked my wife at dinner?

My wife is originally from Northern Europe, but her mother was born in Eastern Europe, and she inherited a lot of those features. Because of that, she stands out a bit where we live in the U.S., and people often assume she’s from somewhere completely different. She’s also fairly new to the country, and while we’re married, she isn’t eligible for citizenship yet. All of that is already a sensitive topic for her, especially since she’s had moments where people made fun of her accent or pointed out things she doesn’t understand culturally, which really hurts her.

A few nights ago, I went out to dinner and drinks with some close friends. One of them brought along a longtime friend of his who was visiting town. I’ve known this guy casually for years and have never been fond of him. He’s the type who prides himself on “edgy humor,” constantly pushing jokes past the point of being funny, especially once he’s had a few drinks. I usually tolerate him because he’s part of the extended friend group, but I already had a bad feeling going into the night.

Almost immediately, he started asking about my wife, referring to her as “my Russian wife, or whatever she is.” I corrected him and briefly explained her background. He didn’t seem interested in understanding and instead compared her to a famous political figure from Eastern Europe, which already felt dismissive. Then he joked about how much it “cost” to bring her to the U.S. A couple of people laughed, and I tried to brush it off with a sarcastic response, hoping that would be the end of it.

It wasn’t. Throughout the night, he kept circling back to her, calling her by that nickname, exaggerating an accent, and making jokes that were clearly leaning into stereotypes. Some of his questions were harmless, but they were buried under constant digs. I could tell others at the table were getting uncomfortable, but no one said anything. The final straw was when he joked that she’d probably leave me once she got what she wanted from being here, followed by jokes about immigration authorities showing up at our house and mocking the way she speaks.

At that point, I lost it. I told him very bluntly to stop, and when he pushed back, I fired off some pretty harsh comments of my own. It escalated quickly, my voice got loud, and I’m sure people nearby heard the whole thing. The mood was completely ruined, and the dinner basically ended after that. I felt bad for blowing things up, especially since my wife wasn’t even there, but the idea of someone talking about her like that made my blood boil. I know how deeply those kinds of jokes affect her, and I couldn’t let it keep going.

Now I’m stuck feeling conflicted. On one hand, I regret causing a scene and derailing the night. On the other, I feel like letting those comments slide would have been a betrayal. I keep wondering if I should’ve handled it more calmly or pulled him aside instead of snapping publicly. So I can’t help but ask myself: was I actually wrong for standing up for my wife the way I did?
