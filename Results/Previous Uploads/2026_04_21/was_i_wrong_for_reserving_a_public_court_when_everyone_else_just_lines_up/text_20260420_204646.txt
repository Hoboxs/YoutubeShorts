Was I wrong for reserving a public court when everyone else just lines up?

There’s a popular park in my area with several pickleball courts, and it’s usually really busy. I’d never played there before, but some friends and I decided to go, so I booked one of the courts for a couple of hours about a week in advance. The booking system was open and showed availability, so I didn’t think much of it, even though I was a bit surprised more slots weren’t taken.

When we arrived, everything seemed normal. The group on our court finished right when our time started, and we began playing. No issues at first.

About an hour in, some other friends stopped by, so we paused briefly to chat. While we were off to the side, another group walked onto the court and acted like it was their turn. I told them we had the court reserved for another hour. Most of them left without saying anything, but one person seemed annoyed and questioned why I would reserve a court on a busy weekend morning. When I said I’d booked it ahead of time, he called it an inconsiderate move and walked off.

Later, another person approached and asked if we had a reservation in a pretty frustrated tone. When I confirmed, he seemed surprised that reservations were even allowed during peak times and went to double-check on his phone.

Afterward, we found out that there’s an informal system most regulars follow. Instead of reserving, people typically wait in line and rotate onto courts when games finish. It’s kind of an honor system, and apparently most people stick to it.

From my perspective, I used the official booking system that’s available to everyone. I didn’t skip a line intentionally, I just planned ahead using the tools provided. At the same time, it seems like there’s an unspoken local etiquette that I wasn’t aware of, and breaking it upset some people.

Now I’m wondering if I should have known better or if it’s reasonable to rely on the reservation system, even if most people don’t use it.

Was I in the wrong for booking the court?
