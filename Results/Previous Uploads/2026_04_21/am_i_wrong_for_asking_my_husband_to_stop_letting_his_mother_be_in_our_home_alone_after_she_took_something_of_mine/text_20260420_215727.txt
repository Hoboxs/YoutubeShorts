Am I wrong for asking my husband to stop letting his mother be in our home alone after she took something of mine?

I’m a 34-year-old woman married to my husband (36), and we have two young kids together. For years now, his mother has had a habit of crossing boundaries in our home, especially when I’m not around. She’ll go through things that aren’t hers, opening my mail or packages, reorganizing areas like the garage, and even moving around my personal items like skincare, makeup, and kitchen tools. It’s always done casually, like it’s no big deal, but it’s never something I’ve been comfortable with. My own family has never behaved that way in our home.

Last winter, my husband gave her some money and asked her to take our kids shopping so they could pick out gifts for us. My youngest chose a dish drying rack for me with her help. While she guided him, it was paid for by my husband and given to me as a gift from my child, which made it meaningful to me.

A few weeks ago, she came over to watch the kids while I was working and my husband was out. When I got home, I noticed things in the pantry had been rearranged, which wasn’t unusual for her, but then I realized the drying rack was gone. I had seen it earlier that same day, so I knew it hadn’t just been misplaced.

I asked my kids and my husband if they had moved it, and no one had. Based on her past behavior, I had a strong feeling she took it. When my husband texted her to ask, she admitted it right away. Her reasoning was that we “weren’t using it enough,” so she decided to take it back with her.

That really upset me. To me, it felt like she made a decision about something in my home that wasn’t hers to decide, and just removed it like it belonged to her. It wasn’t just about the item, it was the pattern of behavior and the lack of respect for boundaries.

My husband doesn’t see it as a big issue and thinks I should just move on. But I told him I’m no longer comfortable with her being in our house alone. I don’t trust that she won’t continue going through or taking things, and I feel like that’s a reasonable boundary to set.

Now he thinks I’m overreacting and making things more serious than they need to be. I don’t see it that way, but I’m wondering if I’m being too rigid.

Am I in the wrong here?
