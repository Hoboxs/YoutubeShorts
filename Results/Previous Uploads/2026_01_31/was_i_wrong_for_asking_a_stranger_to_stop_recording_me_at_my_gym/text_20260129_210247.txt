Was I wrong for asking a stranger to stop recording me at my gym?

I’m a man in my early thirties who goes to the same fitness center most weekday mornings before heading to my office job, and while it’s not some influencer-heavy gym, people do occasionally film their workouts. There are posted rules saying members shouldn’t record others without permission, but enforcement is pretty inconsistent. A month or so ago, a woman I’ll call Lauren started setting up a tripod near the functional training area, and at first I didn’t think much of it since I wasn’t trying to micromanage how anyone else trains.

Over time, though, I started noticing that I was repeatedly ending up in the background of her videos, especially because she was angling her phone toward the mirrors, which reflected a wide section of the gym floor. One morning, I realized I could literally see myself moving through the frame on her phone screen while walking past, and even though I don’t think she was intentionally filming me, I really didn’t like the idea of being captured half-awake, sweaty, and unprepared in someone else’s content.

The first time I addressed it, I kept things light and waited until she finished a set before mentioning that I seemed to be in the background and asking if she could adjust the angle. She sighed, shifted the tripod slightly, and said she was just trying to check her form. About a week later, the same thing happened again, and this time I was more direct, telling her I wasn’t comfortable being in her recordings and asking her to keep the camera tighter or pointed away from shared areas. She got irritated and told me that if I didn’t like it, I should work out somewhere else because it was a “public” space.

That response frustrated me, and I told her it wasn’t a public place but a private gym, and that I wasn’t the one recording people. After she brushed me off and walked away, I went to the front desk to clarify the policy. Staff explained that filming is allowed, but members are expected to adjust if someone doesn’t want to be included, and that they can intervene if it keeps happening. Since I had already tried addressing it myself twice, they went over and spoke to her.

Later, she confronted me near the changing area and accused me of being creepy, said I was watching her, claimed I was trying to control women at the gym, and told me that involving staff made her feel singled out and anxious about coming back. Now I’m second-guessing whether escalating it was too much, even though all I wanted was not to appear in someone else’s videos. I understand how going to staff can feel like a big step, but I also feel like I ran out of options, and I’m genuinely unsure if I handled this badly.
