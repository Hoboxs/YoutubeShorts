Was I wrong for not wanting to share my food after she said she didn’t want any?

I am a man in my late twenties, and recently my girlfriend and I decided to go out for a movie night together. There was a special theatrical re release of a very long fantasy film playing in our city, and we do not live together, so it felt like a fun excuse to spend an evening out. Right next to the theater is a restaurant that I absolutely love, mostly because they make one specific dish that I rarely treat myself to. It is a special style of fries cooked in beef fat, topped with herbs, bacon, green onions, and served with an incredible house sauce. You can order a small portion meant for one person or a much larger portion meant for two people for only a little extra money.

Before the movie, I asked if she would be up for going to this place afterward because I was really craving those fries. At the time she agreed, but she did not realize the movie would be close to four hours long. By the time it ended, it was around ten at night, and she said she was exhausted and wanted to head home instead. I told her that was completely fine and asked if she minded if I quickly ordered my fries to go. She said she did not mind at all.

I then asked her twice if she wanted anything for herself, including fries to share, and both times she very clearly said no and told me she was not hungry. Because of that, I ordered the smaller portion meant for one person. When we went to pick up the food, she smelled it and immediately said it smelled amazing and asked if she could have a few. I had no issue with that at all and happily let her try some.

After that, she suggested that we sit outside the restaurant and eat the entire portion together. I told her no, explaining that I had asked her multiple times if she wanted any before ordering, that I would have gladly ordered the larger portion if she had said yes, and that the smaller portion was something I had really been looking forward to eating myself. I said that I was okay sharing a few bites, but I did not want to split the whole thing.

She got upset and told me I was being selfish and refusing to share my food. I admitted that I was being selfish in that moment, but said I was being honest and that I had communicated clearly beforehand. She eventually cooled down, but the next morning she brought it up again and said that next time I should just share anyway. I told her I am usually more than happy to share food, but in situations like this, I felt she needed to be clearer about what she wanted before I ordered. That led to her getting upset again, and now I am wondering if I handled the situation poorly or if my expectations were reasonable.
