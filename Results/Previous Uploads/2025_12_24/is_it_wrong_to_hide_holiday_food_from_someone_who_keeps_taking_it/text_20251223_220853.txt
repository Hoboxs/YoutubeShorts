Is It Wrong to Hide Holiday Food From Someone Who Keeps Taking It?

For the past several years, I (41F) have taken on the role of hosting a small but meaningful Christmas dinner at my home with my husband. It’s never been a huge gathering—usually just us, my childhood friend and her boyfriend, and occasionally my mother-in-law—but it’s something I put a lot of time, money, and emotional energy into. I plan the menu weeks in advance, budget carefully, and try to make it a warm, stress-free tradition everyone can enjoy.

About three years ago, my brother-in-law moved in with us. At first, things were mostly fine, but a recurring issue quickly surfaced: food. The first Christmas he lived with us, just two days before the dinner, I went to check on the ham I had bought specifically for Christmas—and discovered that half of it was gone. I was shocked. When I brought it up, he shrugged and said he thought he was “part of everyone,” so he didn’t see the problem. The thing is, while he lives with us, he doesn’t actually participate in the holiday at all. He stays in his room the entire time and only comes out once the guests have left.

After that incident, I tried to be proactive. The following two years, I very clearly told him, “Please don’t open or take the ham before Christmas dinner. It’s meant for everyone, and it isn’t very big.” I buy a small, expensive holiday ham, not a massive one, because we’re a small group. Each year, he acknowledged me—and each year, he still helped himself anyway. He never told me, never asked, and never replaced it. I’d only find out at the worst possible moment, right before hosting, when I realized half the main dish was missing again.

This problem doesn’t stop with holidays. On a regular basis, when he needs groceries, he’ll give us a list and his credit card, and we’ll pick up exactly what he asks for. Despite that, he continues to eat food my husband and I buy for ourselves—especially items I specifically purchase for my own meals. It’s reached the point where I feel like I can’t rely on anything staying where I put it, even after repeated conversations.

Now, with another Christmas coming up, I’m exhausted just thinking about repeating the same cycle. I have a mini fridge in my home office, and I’m seriously considering storing the holiday food there so it won’t disappear before Christmas Day. But part of me feels resentful that I even have to do this in my own house. It feels petty, secretive, and unfair—but I’m also tired of being ignored and having my efforts undermined year after year.

So… am I wrong for wanting to hide the Christmas food just so it actually makes it to the table this time?
