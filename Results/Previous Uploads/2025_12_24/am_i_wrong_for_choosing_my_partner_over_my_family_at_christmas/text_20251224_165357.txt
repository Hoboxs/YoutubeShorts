Am I wrong for choosing my partner over my family at Christmas?

I’m a 26-year-old woman who recently moved in with my partner, a 30-year-old man I’ve been dating for just over five months, and this Christmas feels like it’s turning into an emotional minefield. Until now, my family’s holidays have always been split because my parents divorced years ago. The routine was predictable: a quiet meal with my father at one house, then later drinks, gifts, and forced cheer with my mother at another. My siblings, both in their twenties, stuck to this system too, while their partners always spent Christmas elsewhere with their own families. It wasn’t perfect, but it was familiar.

This year changed everything. My partner’s parents invited us to their home outside the city and even extended an open invitation to my entire family for Christmas dinner. It felt like a big step for us as a couple, especially since we’ve just started building a life together. I tried to include my side of the family so no one would feel left out, but when I invited my mother and siblings, she shut it down immediately. She said Christmas had “never been done this way” and insisted it should be spent at her house, just like always, with her children under one roof. The tone made it clear that my growing independence wasn’t welcome.

Trying to keep the peace, I came up with a compromise. I invited my father to join us at my partner’s family dinner, thinking my siblings could stay with my mother, and I’d stop by for drinks and presents before heading out. My mother agreed on the surface, but her disappointment was obvious. When I mentioned that next year we could do Christmas her way, she brushed it off, saying she doesn’t plan that far ahead, which somehow made it feel heavier, like a quiet warning that time is running out.

Then, just a day before Christmas Eve, everything collapsed. My father canceled at the last minute, saying he wanted to be alone this year because he had to work early the next morning. Suddenly, I was left staring at an impossible choice: cancel on my partner’s family, who had already planned and welcomed me, or go through with it and risk this being one of the last Christmases I ever spend away from my parents. The guilt is crushing, and the decorations and lights just make it worse, like they’re mocking me for growing up.

So now I’m torn between tradition and the future I’m trying to build, between my parents’ expectations and my own happiness. Am I really the villain here for changing how Christmas looks and choosing to spend it with the person I love?
