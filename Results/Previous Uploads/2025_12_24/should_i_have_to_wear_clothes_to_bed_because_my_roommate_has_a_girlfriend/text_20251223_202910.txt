Should I Have to Wear Clothes to Bed Because My Roommate Has a Girlfriend?
I’m in my mid-twenties and share an apartment with a housemate I’ve lived with for a while. Ever since I was a teenager, I’ve slept without clothes because I overheat easily and it’s the only way I can actually rest. I’m careful about it: any time I leave my room, even just to grab water or head to the shower, I’m dressed or at least covered, and we both normally knock before entering each other’s rooms. This routine has never been an issue, and he’s always known how I sleep.

The problem started when he invited his partner to stay over for a few nights. We’ve all hung out before and generally get along, but this was the first time they stayed overnight. One night we came back late after drinking, and everyone went straight to bed. At some point in the middle of the night, his partner got up to find the bathroom and accidentally opened my bedroom door instead, since it’s right next to it. I was half asleep and barely registered it before the door closed again.

The next morning, both of them were upset with me. She was uncomfortable because she saw me naked, and they said I was wrong for sleeping that way at all when there was a guest in the apartment. They told me I should start wearing clothes to bed since she’ll likely be staying over more often. I told them I understood the situation was awkward, but I was in my own private room, and the door was only opened by mistake. I didn’t think I should have to change how I sleep in my own space.

Since then, things have escalated. My housemate keeps bringing it up, and once he even opened my door on purpose while I was sleeping to check whether I was naked. That crossed a line for me. I don’t feel embarrassed about him seeing me, but it feels invasive and unnecessary, especially when the original issue came from someone entering my room without knocking. I still don’t think it’s reasonable to expect me to change what I do in private just because it makes his partner uncomfortable.
