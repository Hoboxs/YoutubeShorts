Was I Wrong for Not Wanting My Husband to Leave Me Alone With a Newborn for a Weekend Trip?

I am expecting my first child, a daughter, and my husband and I have recently found ourselves in a disagreement that has become much bigger than either of us expected.

My husband moved here from overseas several years ago for work, which is how we met. Because of the distance, he has not seen his family in quite a long time, and I know how difficult that has been for him. I genuinely want him to have time with them when they visit.

The issue began when he told me he wanted to bring his mother here shortly after the baby is born. I explained that I was not comfortable having visitors during the first month after delivery. This is our first child, and everything I have learned suggests that those early weeks can be physically and emotionally overwhelming. I will be recovering from childbirth, dealing with sleep deprivation, adjusting to caring for a newborn, and hopefully establishing a feeding routine.

To keep things fair, I made the same rule for my own family. My parents and siblings were told that visits would need to wait unless there was a specific reason or arrangement. I suggested that his mother come about a month after the birth instead, and after some discussion, he agreed.

I also made it clear that I had no problem with him spending time showing his mother around the country while she was here. My only request was that I be given advance notice so I could arrange support from my own family if I needed help while he was away.

The disagreement escalated when he revealed that he wanted to take his mother on a weekend trip to a city roughly eight hours away by car. The plan would involve leaving for several days while I stayed home alone with our newborn.

I immediately told him that I was not comfortable with that.

I suggested several alternatives that were much closer to home. There are plenty of places to visit nearby, and I even pointed out that I have lived here my entire life and have never managed to visit the destination he has chosen. My thought was that there would be plenty of opportunities in the future to make that trip together as a family once our daughter is older.

He dismissed those suggestions and said he really wanted his mother to experience that particular city while she was visiting.

At that point, I told him that I felt he was losing sight of the purpose of the trip. As far as I was concerned, the primary reason for his mother’s visit was to spend time with her new granddaughter. I have absolutely no issue with them doing tourist activities, but leaving me alone with a very young baby for an entire weekend while they travel hundreds of miles away feels unreasonable.

I also pointed out that he plans to take several months of parental leave after the birth. In my mind, that leave exists so both parents can care for the baby and so the recovering parent has support. If he feels comfortable leaving for a multi-day trip during that period, I struggle to understand why he considers that leave necessary in the first place.

His response was that I am overreacting and letting pregnancy emotions make this into a bigger issue than it really is.

Now I am wondering whether I am being unreasonable for expecting him to stay relatively close to home during those early months, or whether my concerns about being left alone with a newborn are justified.
