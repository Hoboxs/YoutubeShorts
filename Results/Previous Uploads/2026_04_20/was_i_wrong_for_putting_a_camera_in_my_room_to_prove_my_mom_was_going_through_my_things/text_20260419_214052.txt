Was I wrong for putting a camera in my room to prove my mom was going through my things?

I’m 19 and still living at home with my mom. Lately we’ve been arguing a lot, mostly about the same issue: I think she’s been going into my room when I’m not there, and she keeps denying it.

It started with small things. I’d leave for work and close my bedroom door completely, but when I came back it would be open. At first I thought maybe I just forgot, but then it kept happening. On top of that, I started noticing things being moved around or missing entirely. After a while, I was sure it wasn’t just me being forgetful.

Every time I asked her, she said she hadn’t gone in my room. But it was happening multiple times a week, so I stopped believing that explanation.

Eventually, I decided to get proof. I bought a small camera and set it up in my room. Before leaving for work, I made sure it was working and shut my door like usual.

While I was at work, I got a motion alert and checked the footage. It showed my mom going through my room, opening drawers, looking through my closet and desk, and even taking some items and tossing them out into the hallway. I saved the video and planned to confront her when I got home.

When I got back, I asked her again if she had been in my room. She said no, just like always. That’s when I showed her the video.

Instead of apologizing, she got extremely angry. She yelled at me for putting a camera in her house without permission. I asked why she was going through my things, and she said she has the right to because she’s my mother. Then she started questioning things she had found in my room, and I told her that my personal belongings weren’t her business.

After that, I went to my room to cool off. Not long after, I started getting messages from other family members who had apparently heard her side. Almost all of them were telling me I was wrong for installing the camera and acting like I crossed a line. The only person who agreed with me was my dad, but he doesn’t live with us anymore and can’t really step in.

I feel like I just wanted privacy and proof that I wasn’t imagining things, but now everyone’s acting like I’m the one who did something extreme.

Was I wrong for setting up the camera to catch what was happening?
