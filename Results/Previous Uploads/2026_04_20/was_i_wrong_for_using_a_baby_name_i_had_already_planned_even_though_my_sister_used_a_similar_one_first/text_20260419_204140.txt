Was I wrong for using a baby name I had already planned, even though my sister used a similar one first?

My sister and I were both pregnant at the same time last year. I was expecting my first child, a girl, and she told everyone she was having a boy. What she didn’t share at the time was that she was actually expecting twins and wanted to keep that part a surprise.

While I was pregnant, I talked openly about the name I planned to use for my daughter. I wanted to honor both our mom and my partner’s mom by combining their names, and I loved the idea of using a nickname that came from that. I mentioned this to my sister more than once, and she knew it was the name I intended to go with.

She, on the other hand, said she and her partner were keeping their baby name a secret until the birth.

A month before I gave birth, she had her babies and surprised everyone by announcing twins. She chose names that were clearly inspired by our mom’s name, just split into two parts for each child. I thought it was a sweet idea and didn’t think much of it beyond that.

When my daughter was born, I went ahead and used the name I had been planning the entire time. It meant a lot to me, and I had already shared it, prepared for it, and even had personalized items made.

Since then, my sister has been upset. She keeps bringing up how similar the names are between all three kids and says it’s confusing. She also makes a point of telling people that her children were born first, as if that settles the issue.

From my perspective, I didn’t take anything from her. I had been clear about my choice well before the babies were born, and she chose not to share her plans. If I had known in advance, I probably would have reconsidered to avoid overlap, but I wasn’t given that chance.

Now I’m considering whether I should rearrange my daughter’s name just to reduce tension, but it feels like giving in when I don’t think I did anything wrong.

I’m honestly torn between wanting to keep the peace and feeling like I shouldn’t have to change something that was already decided long before.

Was I in the wrong for sticking with the name I chose?
