Was I wrong for giving my friend a harsh reality check about her crush?

I’ve been close friends with this woman since high school, about five years now. About a year ago, she met a guy while working her job at a café. He works in community theatre and picks up performance-related side gigs, and they talked a handful of times before they hooked up once. After that, he stopped making time for her and kept giving vague excuses. Even so, she became fixated on him. My other friends and I kept telling her he clearly wasn’t interested and that her energy was better spent on people who actually show up for her.

A couple weeks later, she started seeing another guy who treats her really well—steady job, patient personality, genuinely respectful. She told me he made her feel secure in a way she’d always wanted. Everything seemed to be going well… until the actor reached out again. The moment he messaged her, she fell right back into old feelings and said she felt guilty for liking the new guy because he “just isn’t like the first one.”

I tried explaining, again, that the actor has never shown consistent interest and keeps messing with her head. Then she told me she went to a card reader who said she and the actor were “spiritually linked,” and she’s been clinging to that. I didn’t judge her for using tarot for guidance—I do it occasionally myself—but she was treating it like divine instructions rather than perspective.

When she insisted this man was “destined” for her, I finally lost patience. I told her that the idea of this actor ending up with someone he barely knows from a café is the kind of storyline you see in dramas, not real life. She went quiet, said nothing, and hung up.

I didn’t mean it to be cruel—I was frustrated watching her get hurt over and over—but now I’m wondering if I crossed a line. Was I wrong for saying something that blunt?
