Is it unreasonable to ask my friend not to bring her dogs to my fall dinner?

I’m 29F, and this year I hosted our friend group’s annual autumn get-together for the first time. I planned the whole thing — decorations, menu, kids’ corner, the works. One of my friends, Tessa (27F), has two small dogs she brings everywhere. They’re not trained support animals; she just insists they go wherever she goes.

Most of the time I tolerate it during short visits, but two years ago at another friend’s gathering, one of her dogs jumped onto the buffet table and tore apart the roasted vegetables before anyone had even sat down. People laughed it off. I didn’t.

So when it was my home this year, I decided on a simple no pets rule. A few days before the dinner, I texted the group confirming the time and added, “Just a reminder — no animals this time.” I didn’t mention anyone by name.

Tessa immediately private-messaged me asking if I meant her dogs. I told her yes, because I didn’t want to monitor pets while cooking, didn’t want fur anywhere near the dishes, and we’d also have a handful of toddlers attending.

She got irritated right away. She told me she “can’t leave them alone at home at all,” because they “get emotionally distressed,” and she insisted they’re “far better behaved than most children.” She said I was excluding part of her “family,” and that if her dogs weren’t welcome, neither was she.

She was supposed to bring a chicken pot pie and a apple crumble, so I asked how she’d like me to pick them up. She said she wouldn’t be giving me anything since I “didn’t want all of her.” I told her I respected her choice, and ended up making a last-minute grocery run across town.

Now one of our mutual friends says I was being too rigid and should have been “more flexible since it’s a seasonal celebration.” But the way I see it, it’s my house, my kitchen, and my responsibility to keep things from turning into chaos again.

Was I wrong for standing my ground?
