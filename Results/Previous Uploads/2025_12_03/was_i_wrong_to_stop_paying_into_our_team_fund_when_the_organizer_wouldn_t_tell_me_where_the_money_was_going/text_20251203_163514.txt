Was I wrong to stop paying into our team fund when the organizer wouldn’t tell me where the money was going?

I’m 24 and recently started my first full-time job. Our team has a voluntary “sunshine pot” where everyone chips in a little each month for things like farewell cupcakes, small celebrations, or the occasional treat. It sounded harmless enough, so I agreed to join when my coworker Priya asked. She’s the one who collects the money and buys things for the group.

About a month in, I asked if she could send me a simple summary of what had been covered with the fund so far. Not because I suspected anything—just because I’m trying to track my spending better, and I grew up being told to pay attention to where my money goes. She said she’d send it later. She didn’t.

A few days later, instead of the summary, she messaged me reminding me my next contribution was due. I replied asking again for the breakdown. No answer. I sent a follow-up the next day—still nothing. She was active in our work chat talking about weekend plans and project updates, just ignoring anything I asked about the fund.

Last week she came over to my desk, smiling, and said, “Hey! Just need your monthly contribution.” I calmly told her I’d be happy to send it as soon as she let me know what the fund had paid for so far. Her expression changed immediately. She said I was “making this way too formal” and that everyone else was fine without a detailed list. I tried to explain I wasn’t accusing her of anything—I just needed it for my own budgeting—but she interrupted me and walked off.

Now two coworkers have hinted that I’m being dramatic and creating “extra hassle.” One said I should just trust Priya and stop nitpicking. I’m starting to feel awkward about the whole thing, but I still feel like asking where my own money is going is reasonable.

So was I wrong for refusing to keep contributing until she shows me what the fund is actually being used for?
