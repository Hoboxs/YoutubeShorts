Was I wrong for asking my neighbor not to bring gifts this Christmas?

I’m 39, my wife is 38, and we’ve lived in our current neighborhood for almost a decade. We’re close with the people on our street, especially the couple next door, who treat our two kids (5 and 2) like extended family. For years, they’ve joined us for Christmas dinner because they don’t have relatives nearby, and it’s always been a comfortable tradition—until recently.

Our neighbor, who I’ll call Carol, has a habit of going overboard with holiday gifts. She asks everyone what they want and then ignores it completely, convinced she “knows people better than they know themselves.” She also tends to fixate on one idea and repeat it endlessly. One year my wife casually mentioned liking scented candles, and Carol has given her a mountain of them every Christmas since—despite my wife donating most of them because the smell gives her headaches.

That kind of thing was harmless enough, but Carol gets strangely competitive with gift-giving. If someone receives something they’re excited about, she immediately swoops in with a “bigger, better” item to outshine it. It became a running joke between some of the neighbors, but it stopped being funny when it started affecting our kids.

We always keep Christmas morning just for our little family, and then we have people over later. Two years ago, our son was unbelievably happy about the train set he got from “Santa.” When Carol came over that evening, she seemed bothered that he wasn’t instantly playing with the toys she brought. She kept interrupting him and nudging her gifts in front of him, and at one point I found the train controller tucked behind her coat on a chair. I honestly don’t know if she hid it on purpose or set it down absentmindedly, but it was weird.

Last year, when we told our son that Carol and her husband were on their way, he quietly put away all his new toys in his closet. When I asked him why, he whispered, “I don’t want her to take them.” That was the moment my wife and I realized something needed to change.

This year, we decided it would be best if Carol and her husband came for dinner and time with the kids—but without bringing gifts. We want our kids to enjoy their things without feeling rushed, judged, or overwhelmed.

We told them this during a casual visit a few weeks ago. Carol immediately got upset, saying we were “robbing her of the joy of giving” and “excluding her from Christmas.” She even said maybe they just shouldn’t come at all. We tried to explain it wasn’t about excluding her—it was about keeping the day peaceful for our kids—but she shut down and barely spoke to us on her way out.

Now my wife is feeling guilty, and the whole situation has gotten tense. I don’t think we were unreasonable, but Carol clearly thinks we were out of line.

Were we wrong to set that boundary?
