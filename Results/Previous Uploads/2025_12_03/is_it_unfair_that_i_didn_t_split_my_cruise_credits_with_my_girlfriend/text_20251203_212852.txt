Is it unfair that I didn’t split my cruise credits with my girlfriend?

I’m 32M, my girlfriend is 30F. Earlier this year I messed up pretty badly in our relationship, and as part of making things right I told her I’d cover the entire cost of a 7-day cruise for the two of us. The base fare, taxes, fees—everything.

What we didn’t discuss was the automatic gratuities the cruise line charges (around $110 per person). I didn’t say I’d cover those, but once onboard I told her I’d use my onboard credit to take care of her portion and sent her the money for it.

Here’s where things went sideways:

I had $150 in ship credit from a booking promotion, plus another $200 credit from my credit card (I pay a hefty yearly fee for that benefit). So I had $350 total in onboard credit. I told her I had credits and would use them for the gratuities.

While I was at guest services, she saw the full amount and got genuinely angry. She accused me of hiding the total from her and said since we were on this trip as a couple, I should have split the credits 50/50. She felt that by not doing so, I was being selfish.

I explained that these weren’t “free” credits—one came from my credit card perks that I pay a lot for. And honestly, I already paid the entire cruise fare and her gratuity charges. I didn’t feel like the remaining credit was automatically something she was entitled to.

She still thinks I was wrong for not offering to divide it evenly. I feel like I already covered the major expenses, and the credits were tied to my card and my booking.

So was I being unfair, or was keeping the full credit reasonable under the circumstances?
