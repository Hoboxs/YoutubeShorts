Am I supposed to justify every word because of my diagnosis?

I’m 28 and was diagnosed with narcissistic personality disorder at 16, something I’ve worked on in therapy for years by choice. I was not a good person in high school, I’ve owned that, apologized where I could, and I actively work on correcting harmful behavior. I recently joined a new, small friend group, and after I felt safe enough to share my diagnosis, one friend, Rhea, started questioning nearly everything I said. It wasn’t subtle. If I empathized with an upset friend, she’d ask if I genuinely cared or was “performing empathy.” If I complimented her outfit, she’d ask if it was my real opinion or if I was “scoring points.” When I congratulated her on a promotion, she asked if I was implying she hadn’t been confident before. Almost every interaction ended with some version of, “Is that you talking, or the disorder?” I asked her to stop. Other friends asked her to stop. She dismissed it as “just checking,” while I was left overanalyzing every sentence I spoke.

The final straw came during a pregame at my place when someone canceled plans last minute and I said I felt frustrated because it happens often and feels disrespectful. Rhea immediately said, “But are you reasonably upset, or is the disorder feeling entitled to other people’s time?” I snapped. I told her that I already question myself constantly to avoid hurting people, that I wasn’t a case study, and that she was taking years of work and throwing it in my face. I asked her why she even wanted to be around me if she believed every word I said was manipulation. She responded that my reaction “proved her point” and that I wasn’t as healed as I thought. The night ended awkwardly, the group is now split, and some people think I should simply accept extra scrutiny because of my diagnosis, even though I’ve already spent years holding myself accountable.
