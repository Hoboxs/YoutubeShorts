Should I Keep My Child From a Wedding After Finding Out the Worst Way?

I’m a woman in my mid-twenties who split from my ex years ago after a long, exhausting relationship that left me doing most of the parenting alone. We share a young daughter, and while we’ve managed a surface-level co-parenting arrangement, our communication has always been strictly limited to necessities. I don’t pry into his life, and he rarely volunteers information—something I tolerated until it started affecting our child.

He’s been with his now-fiancée for years, and I didn’t even learn she existed through him. Our daughter, barely out of toddlerhood at the time, casually mentioned “Daddy’s new friend,” and that was my introduction. I swallowed my discomfort and stayed civil. Then one day, while scrolling through social media, I was blindsided by glossy photos of an engagement ring and captions celebrating forever. That’s how I learned my child’s father was getting married—through someone else’s post. Days earlier, he had told me he couldn’t financially support our daughter “for a while” because he was switching careers. I agreed, trying to be understanding, only to realize soon after that money wasn’t too tight for luxury trips and diamond rings.

The pattern continued. Less than a day before he was supposed to have our daughter, his mother messaged me saying she would cover some of his parenting time. He later texted that he was “busy.” I rearranged my entire schedule to stay home, assuming something urgent had come up. A few days later, vacation photos flooded social media—beaches, cocktails, smiling couple shots. No warning. No conversation. Just silence and a mess left for me to clean up.

When it was finally his turn to take our daughter again, I confronted him. I told him it wasn’t about jealousy or control—it was about respect and communication. Big life changes affect our child, and I deserved to hear about them directly. His response was cold and dismissive. He said his fiancée wouldn’t have any legal rights over our daughter, so it wasn’t my concern. When I asked whether it mattered that our daughter was gaining a step-parent, or that these milestones shaped her emotional world, he shut down. Shortly after, his fiancée blocked me entirely, which felt like a very loud statement: I wasn’t supposed to know, and I definitely wasn’t supposed to question it.

That was when I made a decision I never thought I’d have to make. I told him our daughter would not attend the wedding. As far as she knows, we already have plans that day. There isn’t even a confirmed wedding date anymore—they’re now expecting another child—but the damage was already done. I’ve been the one at every school event, every appointment, every hard night. The thought of watching my daughter walk down an aisle for the first time without me there—having her hair done by someone else, being dressed by someone else, while I find out afterward—felt unbearable.

Now I’m being told I’m selfish, that I’m punishing them, that I’ve ruined their plans. His family says I forced them to delay everything. My friends say I finally set a boundary that should’ve existed years ago. I feel guilty, but I also feel invisible in a situation where I’ve always carried the most weight. I’m standing firm, even though it hurts—because this isn’t about a wedding, it’s about being shut out of my own child’s life.
