Am I wrong for demanding peace at night instead of sleeping in a gas chamber?

I’m a 36-year-old woman married to a 41-year-old man, and while our relationship is solid, nights in our apartment have turned into something I genuinely dread because of his complete lack of consideration once the lights go out. I usually fall asleep first, and like clockwork he comes to bed later and immediately releases a series of thunderous, eye-watering fumes under the covers with zero attempt to restrain them, ventilate, or remove himself, to the point where I’m gagging, wide awake, and forced to flee the bedroom just to breathe. What makes it worse is that his doctor explicitly told him to cut out certain foods and take medication for the issue, advice he’s chosen to ignore entirely, while I go out of my way to leave the room if I so much as feel discomfort because I don’t want to subject him to the same thing. He insists it’s “natural,” that marriage means accepting everything, yet somehow he never behaved this way before we lived together and still manages to control himself everywhere else except the one place I’m trapped and half-asleep, and now I’m being made to feel unreasonable for asking him to at least step into another room or make any effort at all—so am I really the villain here for wanting basic respect and a night of uninterrupted sleep?
