Did I Go Too Far Telling My Sister to Stop Giving My Daughter Gifts With Conditions?

I recently got into a disagreement with my sister over something that started out very small but escalated quickly.

Years ago, she bought my daughter a pool toy when she was younger. My daughter outgrew it, so I eventually donated it during a routine clear-out of old toys and baby items.

Out of nowhere, my sister messaged me asking if I still had it. I told her I had already donated it because my daughter hadn’t used it in a long time. That’s when she said she thought she had previously told me to save anything she buys for my daughter and not give it away, and that going forward she wants me to check with her before getting rid of anything she purchases.

I was honestly confused because I only clearly remember her mentioning that once, and even then it was about a specific item, not a general rule for everything she has ever gifted.

From there, the conversation escalated. She started listing all the things she has bought for my daughter over the years and expressed frustration that some of those items were no longer in use or had been donated. To me, it started to feel like those gifts were being retroactively turned into something I owe her accountability for.

That immediately hit a nerve for me. I grew up in a household where gifts and help were often used later as a way to guilt or control, with constant reminders of “everything we’ve done for you.” Because of that, I’m very sensitive about anything that even remotely feels like emotional debt being attached to generosity.

I told her that if gifts come with conditions, expectations, or future control over how they’re used, then I would rather she not buy things for my daughter at all. I also said that my daughter shouldn’t grow up feeling like she owes people for normal childhood gifts.

My sister thinks I completely overreacted and that she was only asking to be told before sentimental items get donated. From her point of view, she just wants the option to keep meaningful things.

From my perspective, it’s not realistic to expect me to store or track every single item someone buys for my child indefinitely, especially when kids outgrow things constantly and we regularly declutter.

Now the conversation is tense, and I feel bad that it escalated, but I still feel strongly about the principle of not tying gifts to long-term obligations.

So I’m wondering if I took this too far or if I was right to set that boundary.
