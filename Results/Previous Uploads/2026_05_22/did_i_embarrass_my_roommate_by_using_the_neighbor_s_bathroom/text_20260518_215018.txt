Did I Embarrass My Roommate by Using the Neighbor’s Bathroom?

I live in a university dorm room that I share with another student who I'll call Emma. We only have one bathroom between us.

The issue is that Emma spends an extremely long time in the bathroom every day. It usually happens multiple times a day and can last anywhere from 30 minutes to over an hour each time. From what I’ve seen, she often smokes, scrolls through shows on her phone, and does her full skincare routine in there. Every time she comes out, she apologizes and says she doesn’t know why she does it, but then the same pattern repeats.

After a while, I stopped bringing it up because it never really changed anything.

A few days ago, Emma went into the bathroom again while I had just had several cups of tea. About half an hour passed and I needed to use the bathroom. I assumed she would be out soon, but she stayed in. I knocked and asked how long she needed, and she said about 15 more minutes.

I told her I really needed to go urgently, and she apologized again and repeated that she would be quick. Another 30 minutes went by and she was still inside. At that point I was genuinely uncomfortable and desperate.

I knocked again, and she once more said she needed about 15 more minutes.

I realized I could not wait any longer. I left our room and went to a nearby dorm room to ask if I could use their bathroom because mine was still occupied. The person who lives there is someone I know from classes, and he let me in without issue.

The next day, Emma seemed upset when she got home, but initially said nothing. When I asked her later, she told me she was angry because I had “embarrassed her” by telling other people she was in the bathroom for so long, and now people were joking about it.

I didn’t think I had done anything wrong at the time because I was just explaining why I needed to urgently use another bathroom. I was honestly more focused on how uncomfortable I felt in the moment than how it might come across.

Now I’m wondering if I should have handled it more privately instead of mentioning the situation when I asked my neighbor for help.
