Did I Overreact When My Spouse Gave Away Something I Cherished?

After returning from a trip to Portugal a couple of months ago, I brought home a carefully chosen bottle of aged port that I’d selected during a guided tasting—it was pricey, meaningful, and meant to be something my wife and I would eventually enjoy together. I’m not someone who drinks often, which made the choice feel even more personal. Around the same time, I’d also been given a sealed bottle of Spanish red by a close coworker. From the moment I unpacked, my wife repeatedly asked if she could gift the port to her brother, her uncle, or friends, and every single time I gave a firm, unmistakable no, explaining it was sentimental and tied to the trip. I even reminded her after she declined to try it once because she wasn’t feeling well, leaving nearly the entire bottle untouched. While I was out of town last week, she had friends over after a night out, and without telling me, she served them both the port and the unopened wine until nothing was left. When I noticed they were gone and asked about it, she shrugged and said she’d shared them, claiming she “forgot” my boundary and adding that she doesn’t keep track of things she considers unimportant. What hurt most was that I had already brought back a separate bottle of rum specifically for her to share with friends, yet she chose the two things I explicitly protected. When I tried to explain how deeply upsetting this was, she brushed it off, told me not to “damage the marriage over alcohol,” and suggested I was the one who needed professional help. Now I’m left reeling—not over the drinks themselves, but over how easily my clearly stated boundary was ignored and then dismissed as meaningless.
