Was I Wrong to Ask My Girlfriend to Shower?

My girlfriend barely showers—maybe once a week, twice if I’m lucky. We go to the gym together, and I can always tell she sweats, but she insists she doesn’t. I’ve tried to ignore it at first, but the smell is affecting our intimacy. I used to love being close to her, but now I avoid getting physically affectionate because it’s just… overwhelming.

I’ve tried dropping hints, like suggesting we shower together, but she refuses, saying there’s only room for one in the shower. One night, she invited me to lie down next to her on the floor and talk about travel plans. I lasted less than a minute before making an excuse to leave because I couldn’t handle being that close without gagging slightly.

After months of awkward hints and frustration, I finally told her directly: “I know you say you don’t sweat, but I can smell it. I’d really appreciate it if you showered more often.” She completely lost it, accusing me of thinking she’s disgusting and saying I was attacking her. Now our relationship feels strained, and I’m unsure how to fix it.

Am I wrong for expecting a partner to practice basic hygiene and take care of themselves?
