Did I Completely Betray My Family by Leaving for a Trip After It Was “Canceled”?

I’m a woman in my early twenties, and for my milestone birthday this year, my mother promised me a trip. We started talking about it months in advance, tossing around ideas and dates, and eventually settled on a winter getaway to a southern state right before the holidays. She pushed for it to fall during Christmas itself, calling it a “special holiday experience,” so I agreed, even though it meant rearranging work and plans.

By that point, I had already moved out and was living with my partner. We both work full time, and for weeks we pushed ourselves hard—extra side jobs, late nights, no days off—specifically to afford this trip. I requested the time off well in advance, counted down the days, and talked about it constantly. My mother knew how important it was to me and how much effort I was putting in.

The plans shifted repeatedly over the months, always at her direction, but I stayed flexible. Then, out of nowhere, I got a call from a sibling asking if I knew the trip was off. I didn’t. That’s how I found out. My mother hadn’t called me—she’d told one sibling, who told another, and it somehow made its way to me last.

When I confronted her, she said everything was “up in the air” because of family stress, relationship problems, money issues, and the fact that not everyone could attend. Some relatives live out of state now, and suddenly that meant the entire trip no longer made sense to her. No apology. No direct conversation. Just a quiet cancellation that somehow skipped the one person the trip was supposedly for.

By then, I’d already taken the time off, saved the money, and emotionally committed to going. So I made a decision: I went anyway, just not with her. I went with my partner. Now my mother is furious, accusing me of abandoning the family and “choosing a trip over Christmas,” even though she was the one who pulled the rug out without telling me.

What makes this harder is that this isn’t the first time something meant to celebrate me quietly unraveled and turned into something else entirely. I’m left feeling guilty, excited, angry, and exhausted all at once—wondering if I was selfish for still going, or if I finally just stopped letting my plans disappear for everyone else’s comfort.
