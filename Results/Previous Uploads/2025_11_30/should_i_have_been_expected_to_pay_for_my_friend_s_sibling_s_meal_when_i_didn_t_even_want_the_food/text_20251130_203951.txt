Should I Have Been Expected to Pay for My Friend’s Sibling’s Meal When I Didn’t Even Want the Food?

I went out for lunch yesterday with my older sister, a close friend of mine, and his younger brother, who’s 12. My friend picked the restaurant and insisted we all share a giant barbecue platter, even though my sister and I both wanted something totally different. He brushed off what we said and ordered the big shared meal anyway.

When the bill came, I divided it by four and said he should cover both himself and his brother, while my sister and I covered our own meals. He immediately got irritated and said that all adults should split the bill into thirds because “obviously” his little brother couldn’t contribute. This isn’t the first time he’s tried to get others to cover the kid’s portion, either. My sister reminded him that she never asked anyone to help pay for my meals when I was younger, but he kept insisting. I ended up paying extra just to avoid making a scene, but I was annoyed—especially since I spent more than I wanted on something I didn't even choose.

After we left, I told my sister I didn’t want to eat out with him anymore. I’ve said this before, and I only agreed this time because he originally suggested a budget-friendly place, then changed the plan at the last minute to somewhere much pricier. He overheard me, said he’d give us the money back, then called us selfish for expecting him to pay for his own brother.

For context, I work in retail, so my income isn’t exactly high. Both my sister and my friend are in better financial positions than I am—not wealthy, but definitely better off. He absolutely could have covered his brother’s meal without any trouble.

Was I wrong to think he should pay for the kid he brought, especially since he chose the restaurant and insisted on the shared meal?
