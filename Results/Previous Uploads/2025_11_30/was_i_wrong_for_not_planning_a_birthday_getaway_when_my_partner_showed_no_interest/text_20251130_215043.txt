Was I Wrong for Not Planning a Birthday Getaway When My Partner Showed No Interest?

My partner’s birthday is coming up in a couple of weeks, and she mentioned wanting to spend a night or two somewhere away from home—maybe a hotel or a short little escape. She also said she’d like to involve her sister in the plans. I considered organizing something a bit special, maybe even a small domestic trip (we live in Canada), but she didn’t want to travel far. We have kids we can’t really be away from overnight, and her sister has kids as well.

The issue is that whenever I bring up ideas—places to stay, things to do, possible locations—she shows almost no enthusiasm. Even a basic hotel stay around here would run at least $350 for a couple of nights, especially this close to the holidays, so I wanted to make sure she was genuinely interested before spending that much. I arranged a call with her sister so we could all talk about possibilities, but she didn’t engage at all and walked away halfway through. When I try to discuss options, I get the usual short responses like “whatever you think” or “it doesn’t matter,” which makes it really hard to plan anything meaningful.

I know she’d probably prefer if I handled everything from start to finish, but with money being tight around this season, it felt reasonable to expect at least a little bit of input. On top of that, the last two times I organized family trips, she spent most of the time inside the hotel or said she didn’t feel comfortable in the area, so I didn’t want a repeat of that.

Her sister eventually backed out because of the cost, and with no real guidance from my partner, I decided not to book anything. I told her we could just go out for a nice dinner instead. Now she’s upset, despite barely reacting whenever I tried involving her in the planning.

So now I’m wondering—was it wrong not to arrange a getaway when she didn’t show any interest in helping choose anything?
