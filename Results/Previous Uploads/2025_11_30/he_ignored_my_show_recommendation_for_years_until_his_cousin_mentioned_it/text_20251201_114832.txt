He Ignored My Show Recommendation for Years Until His Cousin Mentioned It.

I’m looking for a quick judgment on whether my reaction was unreasonable. This might sound small, but it really bothered me.

For years—basically since we started dating—I’ve been recommending a show I love, which I’ll call Ridgewood Sisters, to my husband. I even showed him clips, and every time he’d politely decline. That was fine with me; I know not everyone enjoys the same things. I’ve watched the series twice during our relationship, always on my own because he was never interested.

Last night we were at a family gathering with a lot of his relatives, including a cousin he’s really close to. Today, while we were watching TV, he opened our streaming app, searched for Ridgewood Sisters, and said his cousin told him that his partner got him hooked on the show and now it’s his guilty pleasure.

I honestly thought my husband was joking. I pointed out that I had recommended the show so many times and he never cared, but suddenly his cousin mentioning it made it worth trying. He looked confused and asked, “Are you sure this is the same show?” which made me even more frustrated. I reminded him I had watched it twice since we’ve been together and that he never wanted to join me. I told him he could watch it on his own now and went to our room.

I heard him play the show for about a minute before switching to something else.

I know it’s a small situation, but I still feel stung. Was my reaction unreasonable?
