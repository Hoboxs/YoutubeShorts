Was I wrong for not reacting happily when we found out we’re becoming an aunt and uncle?

My wife and I are both in our late 30s. For years, we hoped to have children, but that changed after she went through a serious illness a few years ago. She’s doing much better now, thankfully, but the treatments have made it very unlikely that we’ll ever be able to have kids. It’s something we’ve both had to come to terms with, even if it still hurts.

Her younger brother and his partner have been together for a while, and recently they invited us over a couple of times for family meals. Unfortunately, both times we had to cancel last minute because my wife wasn’t well, she still has periods where her health dips suddenly. Instead of understanding, some of the family seemed frustrated, and her brother even questioned whether she was actually sick, which didn’t sit well with me.

We eventually made it to a dinner with everyone. Toward the end of the night, they handed us a gift and said they wanted to record our reaction. Inside was a positive pregnancy test.

My wife immediately broke down crying. She said it was out of happiness and hugged them, but I could tell there was a lot more going on beneath the surface. For me, it was complicated too. I was genuinely happy for them, but at the same time, I couldn’t ignore the sadness and what this moment represented for us.

Then her brother made a comment about how now we understood why he had been upset about us missing the earlier gatherings. That didn’t sit right with me. I responded that they could have just told us the news without building it up like that, especially given everything we’ve been through.

The atmosphere got tense very quickly. People didn’t seem to know how to react, and my wife started feeling unwell again, so we left shortly after.

I know I probably came across as harsh, and part of me feels like I should apologize. At the same time, I’m struggling with the fact that no one seemed to consider how emotionally difficult that kind of reveal might be for us, especially for my wife.

I don’t want to take away from their happiness, but I also feel like our situation was overlooked entirely.

Was I wrong for reacting the way I did?
