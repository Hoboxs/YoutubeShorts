Was I wrong for skipping the party and asking to be paid after the time change?

A few weeks ago, a friend of mine asked if I could make handmade chocolates for her child’s first birthday party. She said she would reimburse me for the ingredients, but because the process is expensive and extremely time consuming, I decided on my own that I would treat the chocolates as the birthday gift instead of asking for any money. I never told her that explicitly, but in my mind, the time, effort, and cost would be my contribution.

I spent almost an entire day preparing everything. I was in the kitchen from morning into the evening, and then woke up very early the next day to finish the final steps so everything would be fresh. The invitation said the party started at 9:00 a.m., and the plan had always been a brunch style celebration because that was when her child was supposedly most alert and happy. I rearranged my schedule and gave up my Saturday morning to make sure I could be there on time with everything ready.

I arrived exactly at the listed start time with the chocolates, only to find the place completely empty. I double checked the invitation to make sure I hadn’t misread it, then called my friend. She told me, very casually, that the party time had been moved to the afternoon. She admitted she forgot to tell me and then added that I was the only guest who hadn’t been notified about the change.

That’s when I really lost my patience. I had already sacrificed my morning, lost sleep, and put in a lot of unpaid labor for something that was supposed to be a gift. On top of that, we had been talking constantly about the party details for weeks. I had helped her plan the menu, estimate quantities, and organize things, and I had even lent her some of my own decorations for the cake table. Given how involved I was and how much communication there had been, it felt hard to believe that I was simply forgotten by accident.

At this point, I haven’t confirmed whether I’ll even attend the afternoon version of the party. I’m honestly so annoyed that I don’t feel like celebrating anymore, and the morning already feels wasted. Because the chocolates were meant to be the gift, I’m now wondering if it would be wrong to ask her to reimburse me after all for the ingredients and the time I put in, especially if I don’t end up going later. I’m also considering just declining to attend altogether, saying I already have other plans, even though the truth is that I’m too frustrated to pretend everything is fine.

I moved to this city not long ago and have been trying hard to build friendships, which makes this situation sting more. I’ve gone out of my way to be helpful and supportive, and this whole thing has left me feeling overlooked and taken for granted. I can’t tell if I’m justified in feeling this upset or if I’m letting my emotions get the better of me.
