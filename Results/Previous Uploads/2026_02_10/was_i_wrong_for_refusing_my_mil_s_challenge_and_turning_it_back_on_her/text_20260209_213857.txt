Was I wrong for refusing my MIL’s “challenge” and turning it back on her?

I enjoy knitting and usually make things only for myself or my partner because it takes time, skill, and expensive materials, and I’ve learned that handmade items aren’t always appreciated once they leave my hands. Because of that, I generally don’t knit gifts for other people. One afternoon, my mother-in-law decided to present me with what she dramatically called a “challenge.” She showed me a photo on her phone and explained that the challenge was for me to knit her a specific designer-style scarf to match an outfit she owned, framing it as if she were testing my abilities rather than simply asking for a favor.

I asked her why she was calling it a challenge when it sounded like a straightforward request for me to make her something. She couldn’t really explain and just repeated that I could “try” if I was up to it. I talked to my partner about it later, and he suggested I just say no outright, but I felt awkward doing that and decided to compromise instead. I asked her to send me a photo of the outfit, went to a local yarn shop, picked out yarn in a suitable color, grabbed a pair of knitting needles from my own supplies, and also bought a beginner knitting guide.

The next time I saw her, I gave her the yarn, needles, and book and told her that this was everything she needed to make the scarf herself, aside from buying the pattern. I said I thought it would be more fitting for her to take on the challenge personally and learn to knit so she could make exactly what she wanted. She looked confused and disappointed and made a comment implying that I must not have been able to figure it out, even though I clarified that I’d already made similar scarves before.

She didn’t argue with me directly, but later she complained to my partner, saying she had only given me a fun challenge and that I hadn’t even tried. He told her that it was ultimately my decision. Since then, she’s been posting vague comments online about not understanding younger people and why everything has to be made so complicated. I didn’t intend to start drama, but I also didn’t want to commit to hours of unpaid work. Was I actually out of line for handling it this way?
