Was I wrong for refusing to translate after being put on the spot again?

I’m a nineteen-year-old guy who’s fluent in another language because it’s spoken in my family. A friend of mine, Leo, is the same age and has been “learning” this language for years, but in reality he only knows a handful of phrases and can’t hold a real conversation. The problem is that he constantly tells people he speaks it, and the moment someone actually starts talking to him, he turns to me and expects me to jump in and translate so he doesn’t look bad.

At first it felt harmless. We’d be out somewhere, he’d overhear someone speaking the language, say a confident sentence, and they’d respond naturally. Then he’d just stare at me like I was supposed to rescue him. I’d translate a bit to smooth things over because I didn’t want strangers to feel awkward. After this happened several times, I finally asked him to stop. I told him that if he wanted to practice, he should just say he’s learning instead of claiming fluency and turning me into his personal interpreter. He brushed it off, laughed, and said I was being dramatic, adding that it was “good practice” for him and a “cool skill” for me.

Last weekend things hit a breaking point. We were at a birthday gathering at a bar, and he started chatting with a couple at the next table. When he found out they spoke the language, he immediately told them that he did too, then pointed at me and said we could all talk together. The couple got excited and started speaking quickly, and almost immediately he began doing his usual thing, turning to me mid-conversation and telling me what to say for him. I tried to keep it light at first and told him in English that I wasn’t translating that night, but he ignored it and kept pushing lines at me while the couple awkwardly waited.

At that point, I’d had enough. I told the couple, in their language, that I wasn’t translating right now, smiled, and walked away to get a drink. He followed me, angry, saying I embarrassed him and made him look like a liar. He insisted I could’ve just helped for a couple of minutes and corrected him later. I reminded him that I’ve corrected him later many times before, and he keeps doing this anyway. Now he’s calling me petty and saying real friends wouldn’t do that to each other, and I’m stuck wondering if I actually handled this wrong or if I just finally set a boundary he didn’t like.
