Am I Wrong for Choosing a Quiet Christmas After the NICU Nearly Broke Us?

After giving birth in late November, I thought we had a clear plan for the holidays. The idea was to spend Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, and the day after with my partner’s family, giving us about a month at home first to settle in with our newborn and find our rhythm. That plan fell apart fast. Our baby ended up in the neonatal intensive care unit and stayed there for almost four weeks. On top of that, I had a serious medical scare myself and experienced a postpartum hemorrhage at home two weeks ago. Between medications, monitoring blood sugar levels, and the emotional whiplash of it all, the past month has been exhausting and overwhelming.

Our baby has only been home for three full days. We’re still adjusting, still anxious, still healing. Because of that, we decided the safest and calmest option was to stay home for Christmas. We told family they were welcome to come by briefly, and I even said I was open to a short visit on Christmas Day, but we wouldn’t be traveling anywhere or hosting long, drawn-out gatherings. Being in our own space feels essential right now.

This decision didn’t go over well with my partner’s mother. She’s extremely upset and has said she may not come at all. Her frustration grew when she found out another relative would be visiting around the same time. The difference is that this other person actually helped—bringing meals, doing laundry, and even cleaning up after my medical emergency at home. My partner’s mother tends to frame her help as wanting to hold the baby so I can “get things done,” which, right now, isn’t what I need most.

My partner admitted she expected this reaction and said it felt like an impossible situation where someone was bound to be hurt. She’s tried to stay neutral, not really taking sides, which leaves me feeling a bit alone in holding this boundary. I’ve already compromised more than I wanted to. I allowed a visit less than a day after we got home, even though I wanted more time. I’ve offered multiple short visits over the holidays. I even agreed to make the trip to see them in early January.

At this point, I’m not sure what else I can reasonably give without sacrificing my recovery or my peace of mind. I’m exhausted, still healing, and just trying to protect our baby and ourselves. Am I wrong for wanting a quiet Christmas after everything we’ve been through?
