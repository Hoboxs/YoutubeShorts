My Partner Volunteered to Coach Our Son’s Team and Now Wants Me to Share the Job.

My husband and I enrolled our nine-year-old son in a community soccer league this season. Before the season started, we agreed on a simple arrangement: we would alternate who took him to practices, and we would both attend his games whenever possible.

During the parents’ orientation meeting, the head coach mentioned that the team still needed an assistant coach and asked for volunteers. We were told anyone interested could sign up before the first practice.

Later that evening, my husband suggested that I volunteer because I had played soccer when I was younger. I immediately told him I had no interest in coaching. I enjoy watching my son play, but I did not want the commitment that comes with running drills, helping organize practices, and attending every team event.

He then said he might volunteer instead.

I told him very clearly that becoming an assistant coach would mean showing up to every practice and every game. I even asked if he was sure he wanted that level of commitment. He said it was completely fine and that he understood what he was signing up for.

At the first practice, he officially volunteered and was accepted as the assistant coach.

The issue started a week later.

When it was time for the second practice, I noticed he was still sitting on the couch and making no effort to get ready. When I asked what was going on, he said it was my turn to take our son to practice according to our original schedule.

I reminded him that he was now the assistant coach and needed to be there regardless of whose turn it would have been under our original arrangement.

That is when he argued that since we had originally agreed to split practice transportation, we should also be splitting the assistant coaching responsibilities. According to him, if he was doing extra work as a coach, I should be helping balance things out by attending practices and sharing some of that commitment.

I told him that was never part of our agreement.

I had specifically declined the coaching role, and before he volunteered, I warned him exactly what the position would require. He chose to take on those responsibilities anyway. I never agreed to coach, assist, or attend every practice alongside him.

Now he is frustrated and says I am refusing to share the workload fairly. He thinks I should attend practices with him so everything feels more equal. I feel that the coaching duties belong to the person who volunteered for them, while my responsibilities remain what we originally agreed upon: helping with our son's activities and attending games.

Now he is acting as though I changed the deal, while I feel like he is trying to rewrite the agreement after the fact.

Was I wrong for refusing to split the assistant coaching responsibilities after he volunteered for the position himself?
