Did I Cross a Line by Sending My Partner a Recipe Video?

I am a 38-year-old accountant, and my wife is 36. We have been married for several years and generally divide household responsibilities between us. One thing that has always been true, though, is that she handles almost all of the cooking while I take care of other chores around the house, including cleaning up after meals.

A few days ago, my wife asked what I would like for dinner the following evening. I told her I would think about it. Later that night, while scrolling through social media, I came across a video showing a beef noodle dish that looked delicious. Since she had specifically asked for ideas, I sent her the video and said it looked good if she wanted to try making it.

I did not tell her she had to cook it. I did not demand it. I simply forwarded the recipe because she had asked what I might like to eat.

Her reaction caught me completely off guard.

She became extremely upset and told me she was tired of being treated like the household cook. She said she was not a servant whose purpose was to prepare meals while I sat back making requests. She pointed out that she is always the one cooking and accused me of taking that for granted.

I tried to explain that I was only answering the question she had asked. I even suggested alternatives, such as going out to eat instead or making the dish together on a weekend when we both had more time. From my perspective, I had merely offered a suggestion, not an order.

For some background, I rarely cook. I usually work late, and when I am responsible for my own meals, I tend to make something very simple or grab takeout. Cooking has never really been my thing beyond basic meals. My wife, on the other hand, genuinely enjoys experimenting with recipes and has often talked about how much she likes trying new dishes.

The recipe itself was not particularly complicated. In fact, she has made meals that were far more involved in the past. That is part of why I never imagined sending it would be taken as an unreasonable request.

What bothered me most was not that she disagreed with me. It was how quickly the conversation escalated. Even if I unknowingly touched a nerve, I did not feel that sharing a recipe after being asked for dinner ideas deserved that level of anger.

Now I am left wondering whether I accidentally came across as entitled without realizing it, or whether her reaction was really about a larger frustration that had been building up for a long time.
