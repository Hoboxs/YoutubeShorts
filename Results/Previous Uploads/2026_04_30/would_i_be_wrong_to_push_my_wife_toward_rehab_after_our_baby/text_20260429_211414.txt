Would I be wrong to push my wife toward rehab after our baby?

I feel like I am walking a very thin line right now, and I cannot tell if I am seeing things clearly or letting fear take over. I am twenty seven, my wife is a few years younger, and we just welcomed our daughter into the world about a month and a half ago. She is perfect, and everything about becoming a parent has made me think much more seriously about the kind of home we are building.

When I first met my wife, I knew she drank regularly. Most evenings after work she would have a few drinks, and on weekends it could be more if she was out with friends. She never saw it as a problem because she kept it to the evenings and stayed functional. To be honest, she was never mean or aggressive when she drank. If anything, she became more open and warm, which made it easy to overlook at the time.

But I also knew she carried a lot from her past. Her childhood was unstable, and she has talked about how much she wants to build a different kind of life. I noticed over time that when her stress or old memories resurfaced, her drinking tended to increase. It seemed like her way of coping, even if she did not fully admit it.

When she became pregnant, she stopped drinking completely. It was not easy for her at first, but she pushed through, and I was proud of her for that. It gave me hope that things might shift long term.

Then, about a week after our daughter was born, my family came by to celebrate and brought a bottle of wine. That moment felt harmless at the time, but it seems like it opened the door again. Since then, she has slowly returned to her old habits. What started as an occasional glass has become a nightly routine again, usually a couple of drinks in the evening.

I brought it up with her recently because it worries me. Not just because of the drinking itself, but because of what it could turn into over time, especially now that we have a baby depending on us. She brushed it off and said she is just trying to relax, that she is not losing control or getting intoxicated. When I pushed a little more, she pointed out that I have used recreational substances occasionally in the past, which felt like she was trying to deflect rather than address the concern.

Now I am stuck. Part of me feels like I need to act now before this becomes something bigger, and I am considering telling her she needs professional help. But another part of me worries that bringing up something as serious as rehabilitation might push her away or make things worse, especially so soon after giving birth.

I do not want to shame her or make her feel like I am attacking her, but I also do not want to ignore something that could affect our daughter’s life if it grows into a real problem. I am trying to figure out if I am justified in taking a stronger stance, or if I need to approach this more carefully before suggesting something that serious.
