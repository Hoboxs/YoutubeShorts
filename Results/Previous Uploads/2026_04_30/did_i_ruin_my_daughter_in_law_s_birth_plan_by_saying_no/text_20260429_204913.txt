Did I ruin my daughter in law’s birth plan by saying no?

I am starting to feel like the villain in my own home, and I need to know if I really handled this as badly as everyone is making it seem. My son and his wife have been staying with me temporarily after their house was damaged in a flood. A burst hydrant caused serious water damage in their area, and their place needs repairs that make it unsafe to live in right now. Of course I opened my home to them, especially with the baby coming so soon.

My daughter in law is due in a few weeks with their first child, and since their home will not be ready in time, they set up a small nursery in my spare room. I thought everything was going as smoothly as it could under the circumstances, at least until this weekend, when I found out something that completely caught me off guard.

I had assumed she would be giving birth at a hospital. Instead, I learned she has been planning a home birth with a midwife, using one of those inflatable birthing pools. And not just any home, my home. Apparently, the plan was to set it up either in the living room or, if necessary, to move my own bed out of the main bedroom to make space.

I tried to take a moment and really think about it, but the more I pictured it, the more uncomfortable I became. My house is small. It is a two bedroom, one bathroom place, and I downsized years ago for a reason. The idea of a full labor and delivery happening in my living room, or having to give up my own bedroom for it, just does not sit right with me. It feels like a lot to ask, especially without talking it through with me first.

So I told her that I was not comfortable with that happening in my home. I tried to be calm about it, but it did not go over well. She became very upset and said I was ruining her birth plan. My son backed her up and asked me to reconsider, even to apologize, but I cannot bring myself to agree to something that feels so invasive and overwhelming in my own space.

Now the house feels tense, and I am being made to feel like I have taken something deeply important away from them. I understand that giving birth is a huge, personal moment, and I can see why she is upset. But at the same time, this is my home, and I feel like I should have some say in what happens inside it.

I am left wondering if I am being unreasonable for drawing this line, or if it is fair to say that some boundaries should not be crossed, even in a situation as emotional as this one.
