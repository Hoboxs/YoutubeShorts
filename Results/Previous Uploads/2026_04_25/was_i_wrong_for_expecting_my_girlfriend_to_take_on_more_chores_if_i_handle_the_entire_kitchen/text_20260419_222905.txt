Was I wrong for expecting my girlfriend to take on more chores if I handle the entire kitchen?

When my girlfriend and I first got together, we were on the same page about responsibilities. She made it clear she didn’t want to feel like she was taking care of me, and I agreed completely. I had already been living independently, cooking, cleaning, and managing everything on my own, so that wasn’t an issue.

After we moved in together, we had some friction about chores and decided to divide them more clearly. She said she really dislikes anything to do with the kitchen, cooking, washing dishes, cleaning up, so I agreed to take full responsibility for that entire area. That means I cook all our meals, do all the dishes, and clean the kitchen every day.

In exchange, the plan was to split everything else. Laundry, cleaning the bathrooms, general tidying, grocery runs, those would all be shared between us.

The problem is that this doesn’t feel balanced to me. The kitchen work is a daily responsibility, while most of the other tasks are weekly or less frequent. From my perspective, taking full ownership of something that has to be done every single day seems like a bigger load than splitting tasks that only come up occasionally.

Because of that, I suggested that it might make more sense for her to take on more of the remaining chores to even things out. For example, handling the laundry or bathrooms herself, since I’m already covering all kitchen duties for both of us.

She didn’t agree with that at all. Her view is that we already compromised by splitting the rest, even though she dislikes some of those tasks. She also pushed back when I asked for help with things like laundry, saying she doesn’t want to feel like she’s taking care of me.

From my side, it feels inconsistent. I’m doing a major portion of the shared workload for both of us every day, but when I ask for a similar level of support elsewhere, it’s framed as me expecting to be taken care of.

I don’t mind contributing more if it’s genuinely fair, but right now it feels uneven, and it’s causing frustration on both sides.

Was I wrong for expecting a different balance given the way we divided things?
