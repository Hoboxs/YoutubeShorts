Would I be wrong for telling my client I used to date her husband?

I’m a 27-year-old stylist, and one of my regular clients (around my age) has been coming to me for several months now. She’s genuinely kind, easy to talk to, and we’ve built a comfortable client relationship.

When she first started coming in, she mentioned that her daughter has the exact same uncommon name as me, which we both thought was a funny coincidence. At the time, it didn’t mean much beyond that.

Over the next few appointments, she shared bits about her life, including her husband. She mentioned how they met years ago through work and eventually got married. At one point, she told me where he went to school, and it happened to be the same university I attended before leaving early. That caught my attention a little, but I brushed it off as coincidence.

Recently, during an appointment, she showed me photos from a trip they took together, and that’s when everything clicked. I immediately recognized her husband as someone I had been in a serious relationship with years ago.

We had dated for a long time and were even planning to move in together at one point. Things didn’t end well between us, and we haven’t been in contact since. Seeing him again, even just in photos, brought back some uncomfortable feelings, especially given how things ended.

Now I feel stuck. On one hand, I don’t want to create unnecessary drama or come across as someone trying to stir up the past. I also don’t have any evidence that anything inappropriate is happening in their relationship now. On the other hand, it feels strange to keep something like this to myself while continuing to see her regularly, especially since she’s been open and kind with me.

Part of me thinks I would want to know if I were in her position. But another part of me worries that bringing it up would only make things awkward or even hurt her for no real reason.

I haven’t said anything and have continued acting professionally, but the situation feels uncomfortable, and I’m unsure if staying quiet is the right thing to do.

Would it be wrong for me to tell her about the past, or is this something better left unsaid?
