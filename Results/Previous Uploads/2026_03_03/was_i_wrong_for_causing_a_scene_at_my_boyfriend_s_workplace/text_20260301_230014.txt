Was I wrong for “causing a scene” at my boyfriend’s workplace?

I went to dinner with my boyfriend at the restaurant where he works as a pastry chef.

I ordered a Caesar tortilla. After waiting about 45 minutes, I was served a Caesar salad instead. I politely told the waitress, “Excuse me, but I ordered the tortilla.” She seemed annoyed but said they would fix it.

My boyfriend told me to wait and went to the kitchen to make it himself. A few minutes later, he came back with a tortilla, but it was clearly just the salad they had already made, stuffed inside a wrap. The crispy breadcrumbs from the salad were still inside, and it didn’t have the crispy bacon or melted cheese that the tortilla version is supposed to have (which was the whole reason I ordered it).

I told him calmly that I didn’t want to eat that because it wasn’t what I ordered. I wasn’t yelling or making a big deal, I just said we could go somewhere else to eat instead.

He told me the kitchen would be closing soon (it was 9pm, kitchen closes at 11pm) and that I had been saying for hours how hungry I was, so I should “just eat it.” I said that wasn’t the point, it simply wasn’t what I ordered.

He then called over his colleague (the waitress) and told her to take it off the bill. We left and went to a fast food place instead.

Afterward, he was upset and said I embarrassed him and “caused a scene” in front of his coworkers. From my perspective, I spoke quietly, didn’t argue with the staff, and simply declined to eat something I didn’t order.

For additional context: he’s only worked there a few months, and he got the job through a connection (my dad’s friend is a co-owner).

So now I’m wondering, was I wrong for not just eating it and avoiding the awkwardness? Or was it reasonable to refuse something that wasn’t what I ordered?
