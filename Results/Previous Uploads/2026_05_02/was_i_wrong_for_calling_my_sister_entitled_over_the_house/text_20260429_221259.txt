Was I wrong for calling my sister entitled over the house?

My sister is furious with me, and I am starting to wonder if I handled this too harshly or if I just said what needed to be said. Our mother owns a house she inherited a few years ago when our grandparents passed. From the very beginning, she made it clear she did not like the place and planned to sell it. The timing just never worked out, especially when everything shut down for a while, so she ended up holding onto it longer than she wanted and covering all the costs herself.

A couple of years later, my sister went through a difficult separation and needed somewhere to stay. She did not have much stability at the time, so our mother let her move into that house while she got back on her feet and went back to school. For years, my sister lived there without paying rent, while our mother continued to handle all the expenses tied to the property.

Now my sister has finished her training and is working in healthcare, earning a steady income. Throughout all this time, our mother has consistently talked about selling the house. She has never hidden that plan. She rarely even visits because she dislikes the place so much. What none of us realized, though, is that my sister had been putting effort into fixing it up, making changes and improvements without really discussing it with our mother.

Recently, our mother finally decided to move forward with selling the house and told my sister she would need to move out by the end of the year. That is when everything exploded. My sister came to me upset, saying she deserved to keep the house because of all the work she had put into it. The way she said it caught me off guard, and I reacted without thinking. I laughed, which only made things worse, and when she demanded to know why, I told her she was acting entitled.

I reminded her that she had been living there for years without paying rent and that our mother had always been clear about wanting to sell. I said it did not make sense to assume she would just be given a house, especially one worth a significant amount of money. The conversation turned into an argument quickly, and now she is angry that I took our mother’s side.

Looking back, I can see that I was blunt, maybe more than I needed to be. At the same time, I genuinely do not understand how she expected things to turn out differently. Now I am questioning whether I was right to call it out so directly or if I should have handled it with more understanding, even if I still disagreed with her.
