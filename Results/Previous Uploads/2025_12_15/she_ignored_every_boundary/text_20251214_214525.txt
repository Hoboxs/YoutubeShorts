She Ignored Every Boundary, Now I’m Supposed to Take It All Home?

I’m in my early thirties, married, and usually get along well with my husband’s mother, though she has a long history of pushing past boundaries while smiling through it. This year we’re spending the holidays with my husband’s side of the family, and well ahead of time we were very clear about one thing: for our toddler, who’s just under two, we wanted experiences instead of physical gifts. Things like passes to the aquarium, a zoo visit, or maybe a single book to unwrap. He already has more toys and clothes than we can manage, and at his age he doesn’t even understand gift-opening yet.

She was visibly unhappy about this and kept insisting she wanted to buy “real presents.” Eventually she asked for an online wishlist of practical things we could use later. Even though we truly didn’t need much, my husband and I carefully put together a small list and sent it to her, explaining she could pick anything from there if she really wanted to give something.

Then this morning everything blew up. My husband casually asked what she’d decided on, since my own mother wanted the list to grab a couple of items too. Instead of answering directly, his mom replied with a joking message about being “in trouble,” followed by a video. The video showed a massive pile of gifts she’d already bought. None of them were from the list. Not a single one. It was all oversized, noisy, space-hogging items she knows we have no room for, plus a kids’ tablet even though we’ve been very clear we don’t do screens at all.

I immediately told my husband that if she wants those things, they can stay at her house. We’re not hauling them back with us. To me, it felt incredibly disrespectful to ignore our wishes entirely, especially after asking for a list and then deliberately going against it. It felt less like generosity and more like a power move.

My husband thinks we should take at least some of it home so his mother doesn’t feel hurt, but all I can think is that doing so just teaches her that our boundaries don’t matter and that she can override us whenever she wants. On top of that, we genuinely don’t have the space for any of it.

So now I’m stuck between keeping the peace and finally standing firm, and I can’t shake the feeling that if I give in this time, I’ll be fighting this same battle every holiday for years to come.
