Am I Really a Monster for Not Wanting to Spend My Retirement Raising Another Baby?

My son married very young, barely a year after finishing school, and while my husband and I had concerns, he was determined and so was his spouse. Soon after the wedding, they decided she would stay home while he worked, and not long after that, they welcomed a baby boy.

During the pregnancy, my daughter-in-law spent a lot of time at my house, which I didn’t mind at first. After my grandson was born, the visits continued, and again, I didn’t think much of it. I love my grandchildren, and I’ve raised children before. But slowly, without any real conversation, those visits stopped being visits and started turning into full days of childcare. She began leaving the baby with me for longer and longer stretches, and when I said I couldn’t manage certain days, she would get upset or act hurt.

Eventually, she started asking every weekend if my husband and I could watch the baby so she and my son could go out. I agreed for a few weeks, until we already had plans of our own. When I said no, she exploded, telling me I was selfish and lacked empathy because she was “stuck at home all week” as a parent. I felt guilty, so my husband and I rearranged our lives and started watching the baby regularly on weekends, even postponing a trip we had been planning for months.

The truth is, we’re exhausted. We love our family deeply, but at this stage of our lives, we were looking forward to being partners again, not full-time caregivers. On top of that, my grandson is no longer a newborn. He’s crawling everywhere, getting into everything, and it’s physically difficult for us to keep up. My husband walks with a limp, and I struggle to bend and lift. What used to be manageable has become painful and overwhelming.

When I finally explained this to my daughter-in-law, she brushed it off, saying it was “only a couple of days a week.” That’s when I calmly said that if it was so small, then she could pay us the same way she pays the sitter she recently hired, around forty dollars a day. I admitted that lately it feels like she only comes around when she needs free childcare, not to visit, not to share a meal, not to spend time as family. That’s when she completely lost it, accusing me of being cruel for even suggesting payment for caring for my own grandchild.

Now she’s telling relatives that I’m heartless and greedy, and I’ve been left questioning myself. I adore my grandson, but I didn’t agree to raise another child, especially at a point in my life when my body simply can’t handle it. I can’t tell if I’m standing up for myself, or if I’ve crossed a line by finally saying out loud what I’ve been quietly feeling for months.
