Am I wrong for expecting my missing cookies to be replaced?

I am a twenty nine year old woman, and recently I ordered a few boxes of seasonal cookies through a coworker who was selling them on behalf of her granddaughter. I paid for three boxes in total. One was for me, and the other two were for coworkers who had already given me money to pick them up for them. I assumed I would receive the order directly from the person I paid, so I did not think much more about it.

A few days later, another coworker approached me and said he had been given my cookies to pass along. That was the first time I realized there had been a handoff I was never told about. He explained that he had taken the cookies home along with his own, and while they were there, his teenage son ended up eating one of the boxes. When he handed me what was left, I only received a single box, the one I had ordered for myself.

What made things more confusing was that he insisted he had only been given two boxes to begin with. That did not line up with what I had paid for, so I reached out to the original coworker who sold them to me. She confirmed that she had given him all three boxes. Now I am stuck in the middle of two conflicting stories, and I am the one left short.

The real issue is that two of those boxes were not even mine. I had collected money from other people, and now I either have to replace what they paid for out of my own pocket or explain a situation that sounds messy and unprofessional. None of this was within my control, and yet I am the one dealing with the consequences.

I feel like someone should take responsibility, especially since the cookies were handed off without my knowledge to someone who did not ensure they reached me safely. At the same time, I am wondering if I am expecting too much by asking for a replacement or a refund, or if it is reasonable to push back instead of quietly absorbing the loss myself.
