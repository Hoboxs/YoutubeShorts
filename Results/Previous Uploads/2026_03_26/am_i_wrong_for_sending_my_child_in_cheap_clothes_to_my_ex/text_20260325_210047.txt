Am I wrong for sending my child in cheap clothes to my ex?

I am a father in my mid thirties, and I share custody of my child with my former partner. During the week, my child lives with me, and on weekends they stay with their mother. I earn more than she does, and since my child spends most of their time with me, I make sure they have a full wardrobe of good quality clothes at my home. There is no financial support arrangement between us since I have primary care.

At first, everything seemed normal. When I picked my child up after the weekend, I would wash whatever they had been wearing and send those same clothes back in their bag for the next visit. Over time, though, I started to notice something strange. The nicer outfits I sent my child in were not coming back. It was not obvious at first, but eventually I realized that a significant portion of their wardrobe had disappeared.

When I asked my former partner about it, things escalated quickly. Instead of resolving it calmly, she claimed the clothes were hers and that I had no right to ask for them back. It turned into a serious dispute that even involved legal representatives. I had to gather proof of purchase to show that I had bought those items. After a long and frustrating process, she was told to return them.

That experience left a lasting impression on me. Since then, I have changed how I handle things. When my child goes to their mother’s place, I now send them in older outfits, less expensive items, or clothes that originally came from her home. These are things I do not mind losing if they do not come back.

Recently, she sent me a message saying I was being unfair and harsh by doing this. She argued that since I earn more, I should not worry about losing a few outfits and should just let her keep them. I told her plainly that if she wants our child to have nicer clothes at her place, she is capable of buying them herself. From my point of view, I already went through the trouble of replacing what was taken once, and I am not willing to repeat that situation.

When I spoke to my sister about it, she said I was being petty and should just let it go to keep things peaceful. That made me question myself. I do not feel like I am doing anything wrong, but I also do not want my actions to affect my child or create more conflict than necessary. Should I handle this differently, or is it reasonable to protect what I have already paid for?
