Was I wrong for suggesting something might be wrong with my wife’s appetite?

I feel like I stepped into a situation I did not fully understand, and now it has turned into a much bigger conflict than I expected. My wife, who is twenty eight, has always had a very strong appetite. I am not talking about the occasional big meal. I mean she eats large amounts of food throughout the day, far more than most people I know, yet her weight barely changes. She stays around the same size, and aside from some weight gain years ago tied to medication, nothing about her appearance suggests anything unusual.

What concerns me is not just how much she eats, but how she talks about hunger. She does not describe it as a mild feeling. She says it actually hurts, like real physical pain if she does not eat enough or soon enough. Watching her eat what seems like a full day’s worth of meals in just a few hours, and still hear her say she feels hungry, has started to make me uneasy. It does not feel normal to me, and I started to worry that there could be something medical going on.

The problem is how I expressed that concern. During a conversation, I said something along the lines of it not seeming natural that someone could eat that much without gaining weight, and that it made me think something might be wrong. In my head, I was trying to point out a possible health issue, but the way it came out sounded harsh. She immediately took it as me criticizing her eating habits or calling her overweight, which was never my intention at all.

From her perspective, it sounded like I was judging her or making a comment about her body. From mine, I was trying to make sense of something that honestly does not add up to me and suggest she might want to look into it. I even mentioned the idea that something internal could be affecting how her body processes food, not as an insult, but as a genuine concern.

She was hurt and upset, and even after I tried to explain that I was not judging her or calling her anything negative, the damage seemed to already be done. I apologized for how it came across, but I also said that I still feel concerned and do not think I was wrong to bring it up. Now things are tense between us, and I am left wondering if I handled this completely the wrong way or if my concern itself was reasonable but just poorly delivered.
