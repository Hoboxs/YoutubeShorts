Was I wrong for eating the deer meat stored in our freezer?

A couple of years ago, my husband’s friend asked if he could store some venison in our deep freezer because he did not have enough space at home. It was not just a small amount either. It was essentially an entire deer, broken down into different cuts like steaks, ribs, and ground meat, all properly sealed and labeled.

At the time, we agreed to help him out, and it did not seem like a big deal. But what was supposed to be temporary has turned into something much longer. It has now been sitting in our freezer for close to three years.

The friend comes by occasionally, maybe once a month, and every time he does, I ask if he wants to take some of the meat with him. Every single time, he says he will grab some next time. That “next time” has never come. So the meat just stays there, taking up space in our freezer while we continue to store it for him.

After a while, it started to bother me. Not only is it occupying space that we could use, but I also worry about it eventually going to waste. Even though it is vacuum sealed, it is not going to last forever. So a few times, I have taken some of it and cooked meals for myself instead of letting it sit there unused.

My husband does not agree with that decision. He feels that it is not ours to touch, no matter how long it has been there. From his perspective, it still belongs to his friend, and using it without permission is crossing a line.

I see it differently. We have been storing it for years, constantly reminding him to take it, and he never does. At some point, it starts to feel less like we are doing a favor and more like we are just holding onto something he has no intention of using. I would rather put it to use than let it eventually spoil and be thrown out.

Now I am wondering if I went too far. I feel like there should be some kind of limit to how long we are expected to store something for someone else, but at the same time, I did not explicitly tell him I would start using it. I am stuck between feeling practical about not wasting food and questioning whether I should have handled it differently.
