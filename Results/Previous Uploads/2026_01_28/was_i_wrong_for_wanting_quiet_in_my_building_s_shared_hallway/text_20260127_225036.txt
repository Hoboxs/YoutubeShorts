Was I wrong for wanting quiet in my building’s shared hallway?

I live in an apartment building and one of the nearby residents recently had a newborn. Ever since, they regularly walk the baby up and down the hallway whenever it starts crying, sometimes multiple times a day and often late into the evening. As I’m writing this, it’s already after ten at night and the same thing is happening again. The crying echoes through the hallway and carries straight into the surrounding units, including mine.

I deal with several long term health issues and I also work remotely from home, so consistent noise has a real impact on my ability to function, rest, and do my job. After weeks of this, I feel completely worn down. I’ve written a calm and respectful note asking if they could try comforting their baby inside their own apartment instead of using the hallway, since it’s a shared space and the sound travels easily. I haven’t left it yet because I’m worried about whether that would make me unreasonable.

I strongly dislike direct confrontation, so leaving a note feels like the least aggressive option. I do understand that apartment living comes with unavoidable noise and that babies cry. That part isn’t lost on me. What’s bothering me is the choice to bring the crying into a common area and expose everyone nearby to it at all hours, rather than keeping it contained within their unit. At this point, I’m torn between wanting to be understanding and feeling like my basic need for peace in my own home is being ignored.
