Was I wrong for walking out after my sister brought her boyfriend?

I had been really excited for a full day with my sister because we honestly do not get much one on one time anymore. I am twenty, she is a little older, and we had planned what was supposed to be a classic sister day together. The plan was brunch, shopping, getting our nails done, and maybe seeing a movie afterward if anything looked good. It sounded simple, relaxing, and exactly what I needed.

On the day of, I arrived first and got us a table. A few minutes later, my sister walked in, but she was not alone. She had brought the guy she had recently started seeing. I was caught completely off guard because she had never mentioned he would be joining us.

For context, my sister tends to move quickly through relationships. She will date someone for a short time, lose interest, and then move on, so I had not even expected this relationship to last long enough for him to become part of family outings. At this point, they had only been together a few weeks.

I tried not to make things awkward and stayed polite while we ordered food. But when he stepped away for a moment, I asked her quietly why she had brought him along when we had specifically planned a girls’ day together. She casually said she did not want him to feel excluded, which honestly confused me because he had never been invited in the first place.

Then things became even more uncomfortable. Since my sister and I usually take turns paying when we go out together, I mentioned that I assumed he would cover his own meal. She looked at me like I was being strange and initially implied that I should pay because technically it was “my turn.” When she saw my reaction, she quickly said she would pay for him herself.

At that point, though, the mood was already ruined for me. I could not stop thinking about how the entire day had changed without her even asking me. I had been looking forward to shopping together, trying on clothes, talking privately, and just spending time as sisters. Instead, I suddenly felt like a third wheel in my own plans, dragging around a guy I barely knew through activities he probably did not even want to do.

So once we finished eating and left the restaurant, I made an excuse and went home instead.

Now my sister keeps messaging me asking what happened and why I suddenly left. I know I probably should have explained myself directly in the moment, but I was frustrated and did not want to start an argument in public. At the same time, I cannot tell if I overreacted by leaving instead of just trying to make the best of it.
