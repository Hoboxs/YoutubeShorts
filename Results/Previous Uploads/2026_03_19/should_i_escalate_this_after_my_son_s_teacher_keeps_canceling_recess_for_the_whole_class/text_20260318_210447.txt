Should I escalate this after my son’s teacher keeps canceling recess for the whole class?

My son, who I’ll call Ethan, is 8 years old and in second grade. He’s a really good kid, polite, hardworking, and a strong student. He’s involved in things like chess club, robotics, and sports, but he can also be a bit anxious and shy.

The issue is with his math class, which comes right before recess. A handful of students in the class have been disruptive and not paying attention. Because of that, the teacher has been canceling recess for the entire class as punishment, rather than just addressing the kids who are misbehaving.

Ethan still does his work, keeps to himself, and currently has a high grade in the class. I reached out to the teacher to ask if there was anything he could do to still earn recess time, since it’s an important break for him. She told me no, that if her lesson is interrupted, the whole class will continue to lose recess until behavior improves.

I understand her frustration because I’ve seen firsthand how disruptive a few of the students can be. But at the same time, my son is being affected despite doing everything right. Recess is important for him to reset and stay focused for the rest of the day.

It’s gotten to the point where he’s crying every morning before school because he’s anxious about losing recess again. For the past week, it’s been canceled every day, and it’s really starting to affect him emotionally and make mornings difficult.

I’ve looked into the rules where we live, and it seems like younger students are supposed to have a minimum amount of recess each day that isn’t meant to be taken away as punishment.

I’m considering escalating this to the principal or school counselor, but my son’s dad thinks I’m overreacting. I also don’t want to undermine the teacher or make things harder for her, since I know teaching is already a tough job.

At the same time, I feel like my son, and the other well-behaved kids, shouldn’t be punished for something they didn’t do.

Would I be wrong for taking this further?
