Should I remove my doormat because a neighbor’s child is scared of it?

My wife and I have lived in our apartment building for several years, and we are both really into horror movies and spooky decorations year round. A couple of years ago we bought a horror-themed doormat featuring a creepy clown character, and we have kept it outside our door ever since. For us, it is not some seasonal decoration. It is just part of our style and something we enjoy.

Recently, a new family moved into the apartment directly across from us. They have two young children, one still a baby and the other probably preschool age. The first interaction we had with them was friendly enough. While carrying groceries inside, I introduced myself, and the father jokingly commented about us still celebrating Halloween. I laughed it off and did not think much of it.

Not long after that, though, strange things started happening. We would come home and find our doormat flipped upside down. At first we did not want to make a big deal about it, so we just turned it back over. But it kept happening repeatedly, and eventually we checked our door camera and saw that it was the neighbors doing it.

We finally confronted the situation after catching them in the act. When we asked what the problem was, they explained that their young son was apparently frightened of the doormat. The strange thing was that while this conversation was happening, the child himself seemed perfectly calm. He was not crying or hiding or reacting at all. The only person who seemed genuinely upset was the mother, who immediately became rude and started speaking to us aggressively.

My wife waited until they went back inside and quietly turned the doormat back the right way. I stayed outside and spoke with the father for a bit longer, and honestly, he seemed reasonable. He acknowledged that it was our apartment and our property, and he even said he thought his son was old enough to understand that the image could not hurt him.

So I thought the issue was mostly settled.

Then this morning we opened our door and found the doormat completely gone from outside our apartment. Someone had thrown it down outside the stairwell several floors below. It clearly was not an accident. Someone had deliberately carried it away and tossed it.

Now my wife wants to report the situation to building management because technically someone tampered with our property. Part of me agrees with her, but another part of me is exhausted by the entire situation and wonders if it would just be easier to throw the mat away and avoid more drama. At the same time, it frustrates me that we are even in this position over something sitting outside our own door.

So now I am stuck wondering whether we are being inconsiderate for keeping it despite the child’s fear, or whether the neighbors crossed the line by repeatedly touching and eventually throwing away something that belongs to us.
