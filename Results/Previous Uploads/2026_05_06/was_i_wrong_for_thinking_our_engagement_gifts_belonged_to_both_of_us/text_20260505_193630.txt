Was I wrong for thinking our engagement gifts belonged to both of us?

My partner and I recently got engaged after being together for several years, and this weekend her family hosted a big engagement celebration for us. It was a really nice event, and afterward we brought home a stack of cards and gifts from relatives and family friends. When we started opening them together later that evening, a lot of the cards had money inside, which honestly surprised me because people were very generous.

While we were going through everything, I commented on how kind her family had been and said it would really help with wedding expenses or things we need together as a couple. That is when she casually said the money was actually hers.

I was confused at first because the cards were addressed to both of us, and the entire event was celebrating our engagement, not just her individually. So I told her I thought the money should count as something for both of us, especially since we are planning a future together and a wedding together.

She disagreed immediately. Her argument was that because the gifts came from her side of the family, the money should belong only to her. I told her I did not think that made sense. To me, engagement gifts are meant for the couple, regardless of which side of the family they come from. I also admitted that it felt selfish to automatically claim all of it for herself when the cards clearly included both our names.

That did not go over well. She accused me of trying to benefit from her family’s generosity, which honestly frustrated me because from my perspective, she was the one treating shared gifts like personal income. We went back and forth for a while, but she kept repeating that because the money came from her relatives, it was hers to keep.

Now I am second guessing whether I am looking at this the wrong way. Part of me genuinely thought engagement gifts were intended for the couple as a unit, especially when they are addressed to both people. But maybe there are unspoken expectations around family gifts that I do not understand.

At this point, I cannot tell whether I was being reasonable in expecting the money to be shared, or if I came across as entitled by assuming I had any claim to gifts that originated from her family.
