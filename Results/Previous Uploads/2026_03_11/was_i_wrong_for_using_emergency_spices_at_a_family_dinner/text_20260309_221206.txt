Was I wrong for using “emergency spices” at a family dinner?

I was raised to always smile and “eat politely” when I’m a guest, even if I don’t enjoy the food. I usually try a little of whatever is served and, if I’m still hungry later, grab something else on my own.

A while back I found tiny bottles of Tajín at a store for 50 cents each, and I bought a bunch. I call them my “emergency spices”, little flavor boosters I can sprinkle on a meal I don’t enjoy so it’s easier to eat without drawing attention.

At a recent family gathering, my aunt served a dish of chicken sausage, peppers, and onions in a cream sauce over plain rice. Honestly, it was bland and kind of off-putting to me. While no one was looking, I sprinkled a little Tajín on my plate. The spice made the food taste better for me, but it also left red flecks on the dish, which people noticed. I admitted what I’d done and explained that it was just a way to make the meal more palatable.

My aunt was offended and asked if I hated her cooking. I said no, she normally makes great food, it was just this particular dish I didn’t enjoy. She asked why I hadn’t said anything, and I explained my upbringing about always being polite at someone’s table. That only made her angrier, and it turned into an argument with my parents.

Now the rest of the family seems annoyed with me, and I’m wondering, was I wrong for using a little emergency spice to avoid hurting anyone’s feelings?
