Did our dream trip turn into errands for her family?

I am a thirty seven year old man, and for as long as I can remember, visiting Japan had been one of my biggest dreams. Growing up, I was obsessed with shows like Dragon Ball Z, and over the years that grew into a deep interest in anime and gaming culture. I always imagined Japan as this incredible place that felt far ahead of the rest of the world. So when I finally had the chance to go, it meant a lot to me. I am currently here with my wife, who is thirty six, along with three of her friends.

At first, everything seemed fine. Her closest friend goes along with whatever she plans, and the other two are kind enough not to question anything. But as the trip went on, I started to notice a pattern that completely shifted the experience for me. My wife had taken on the task of buying items for her brother and sister. They had specific requests, and whether she volunteered or felt expected to do it, I am not entirely sure. What I do know is that fulfilling these requests started taking over our entire trip.

One day in particular pushed me over the edge. We spent hours traveling to a well known shopping district just to visit a specific shoe store because her brother wanted a custom pair. It was not a quick visit either. We left in the morning and ended up stuck there into the evening, waiting for the customization to be completed. The entire day revolved around this one errand. Meanwhile, I watched the hours slip away from what I had hoped would be time spent exploring the things I had dreamed about for years.

It did not stop there. Her sister wanted a designer bag, and because Japan is known for high quality second hand luxury items, we spent multiple days visiting different shops. Each stop involved video calls, showing options, waiting for approval, and then moving on to the next place if it was not exactly right. Even though they plan to pay us back, it felt like we were acting as personal shoppers rather than enjoying our own trip.

By the time I finally tried to carve out a moment for myself and suggested we visit an area known for anime and gaming, it was too late. The places I had been looking forward to had already closed for the day. I ended up standing there feeling frustrated and disappointed, realizing that something I had waited years for was slipping away because of someone else’s priorities.

That was when I told my wife I was going to speak directly to her family and make it clear that this situation was not acceptable. I said I did not care how they reacted because this trip was supposed to be for us, not for running errands on their behalf. She reacted immediately and accused me of disrespecting her family, saying I had no right to speak about them that way. The conversation quickly turned into an argument about loyalty and boundaries.

Now I feel stuck between wanting to stand up for myself and not wanting to create a bigger conflict. I cannot shake the feeling that this trip has been taken away from me, but I also wonder if confronting her family would only make things worse. Should I have kept quiet and just gone along with it, or was I right to finally push back?
