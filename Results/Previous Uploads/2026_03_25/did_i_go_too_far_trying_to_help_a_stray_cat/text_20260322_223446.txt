Did I go too far trying to help a stray cat?

I am someone who spends a lot of time in my garage, where I have a television set up and often chat with neighbors passing by. A few weeks ago, a young black cat wandered in and made itself comfortable. It had bright amber eyes and a friendly nature, but something about it felt off. It was wearing a thick metal chain around its neck that looked tight and unsafe, not the kind designed for cats. There was no tag or any way to identify an owner.

The cat seemed desperate for food. When I offered some dry kibble, it ate so fast that it scattered pieces everywhere. That alone made me think it had not been properly cared for. We also live near a very busy road, and the cat showed no fear of traffic, which worried me. When it stayed in my garage for hours without leaving, I decided to keep it overnight to prevent it from getting hurt.

The next day, I tried to do the responsible thing. I posted notices online, checked local community pages, and even took the cat to a nearby shelter to scan for a tracking chip. There was no chip, and no one came forward. I waited a full week, hoping someone would claim it, but I heard nothing. Based on that, I believed I was dealing with a stray or an abandoned animal.

Wanting to do what I thought was right, I scheduled an appointment with a veterinarian to have the cat neutered. I had always understood that this helps reduce aggressive behavior and prevents more unwanted animals from being born. After the recovery period, I released the cat back outside, hoping it would adapt like other roaming cats in the neighborhood.

That same day, everything changed. A young man came to my door and told me the cat belonged to him. I explained everything I had done to try to find the owner, but he did not seem interested in that effort. Instead, he was upset because he had planned to use the cat for breeding. He told me I had no right to make that decision. I responded that if the cat had proper identification, none of this would have happened.

Now I am left questioning myself. I truly believed I was helping an animal that seemed neglected and at risk, but I also made a permanent decision about a pet that was not mine. Was I acting responsibly for the situation, or did I cross a line that cannot be undone?
