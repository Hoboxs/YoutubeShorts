Was I wrong for telling my daughter she needs to help repay the wedding money after it was canceled?

My daughter was supposed to get married in about four months. Earlier this week I found out the wedding is completely off, and apparently it has been off for about a month already. No one told me until now.

From what I understand, her fiancé discovered that she had cheated on him early in their relationship, and because of that he called off the wedding.

Before all of this happened, I had given her money toward the wedding, but it wasn’t meant as a gift. I had some specific conditions attached to it. The main one was that the venue needed to be accessible for people with disabilities so that certain family members would be able to attend comfortably.

Because of that requirement, I paid the deposit for the venue myself.

Now that the wedding is canceled, the venue isn’t offering refunds. My daughter has already informed them that she won’t be using the date anymore, and nothing will take place there that day. However, the contract still requires the full payment, so I’m still responsible for the remaining cost.

When I realized this, I called her and told her that I need help paying for the venue since the wedding isn’t happening anymore. I said that if the event had gone forward as planned, I would have honored the agreement and covered it, but since it was canceled due to her actions, I think it’s reasonable that she help repay some of the money.

That led to a huge argument. She said I was being cruel for bringing money into it while she’s already going through a difficult breakup.

I told her that the wedding likely would still be happening if she hadn’t cheated, and reminded her that the money I put toward the venue was never intended to be an unconditional gift.

Now she’s extremely angry with me, and I’m stuck paying around $10,000 for a venue that won’t even be used.

Was I wrong for asking her to help pay it back?
