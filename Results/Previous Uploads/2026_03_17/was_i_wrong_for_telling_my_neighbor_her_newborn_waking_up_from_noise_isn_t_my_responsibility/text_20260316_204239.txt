Was I wrong for telling my neighbor her newborn waking up from noise isn’t my responsibility?

I recently moved into a one-bedroom apartment that had been empty for a while. I really like the place and it’s been a good fit so far.

I also have a two-year-old goldendoodle. He does occasionally bark when he hears something outside the door or in the hallway, but it’s usually just a couple of alert barks. I work from home, so I’m almost always there and I correct him when it happens.

Tonight around 7 p.m. I was having dinner with my sisters when my neighbor knocked on my door. She told me that my dog’s “constant barking” has been waking up her newborn and that it’s apparently been happening for weeks. She also mentioned that she has two dogs and claimed she trained them not to bark, so she thinks I should be able to do the same.

The thing is, my dog wasn’t even barking when she came to the door, and I honestly don’t believe he barks constantly. Since I’m home most of the time, I feel like I would definitely notice if it were happening a lot.

I told her I would do my best to manage it, but I also explained that the building seems to have pretty thin walls and sound travels easily.

For context, I regularly hear her baby crying through the wall, but I’ve never complained about it because that’s just part of living in an apartment building. Babies cry, and sometimes dogs bark.

Now the conversation has made me feel really anxious and hyper-aware. If my dog barks even once, I immediately start worrying that she’ll come knock on my door again.

Was I wrong for telling her that the building’s sound insulation isn’t something I can control?
