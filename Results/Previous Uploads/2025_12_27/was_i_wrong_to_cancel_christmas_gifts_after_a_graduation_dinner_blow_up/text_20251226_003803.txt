Was I wrong to cancel Christmas gifts after a graduation dinner blowup?

After nearly seven years together, I thought I understood my partner’s family dynamics, but his graduation weekend pushed everything over the edge. His relatives have always struggled with money, yet somehow still manage to spend far beyond their limits, living as if consequences don’t apply. When my partner finished his degree, my own family drove several hours to celebrate him, and I made it clear ahead of time that his family should be prepared to cover the cost of the graduation dinner. This was his milestone, after all. The price of the meal came up again and again in the days leading up to it, to the point that my partner felt so guilty he deliberately chose a cheap casual restaurant just to avoid tension.

Even then, the night ended in embarrassment. His family asked to split the bill anyway. I was stunned. At my own graduation, my parents covered a large, expensive dinner without hesitation and never would have expected anyone else to chip in. Yet here, my family was asked to pay their share for my partner’s celebration, and the amount—small as it was—felt insulting on principle. It wasn’t about the money; it was about the message. It felt rude, dismissive, and painfully uncomfortable for everyone involved.

What made it worse was what happened shortly after. Just days later, his relatives were happily upgrading their phones, casually dropping hundreds of dollars and adding it to their growing debt without a second thought. Watching them spend freely on luxuries right after asking my family to help pay for a graduation dinner felt like a slap in the face. That was the moment something in me snapped.

I told my partner that I was done extending myself for people who clearly didn’t respect us. I decided I wouldn’t be buying his family Christmas gifts this year, because I couldn’t bring myself to reward behavior that felt so inconsiderate and hypocritical. Now I’m being made to feel cold and petty for drawing a line, but to me it feels like basic boundaries after one too many disappointments. So was I wrong for finally saying enough and letting Christmas be the breaking point?
