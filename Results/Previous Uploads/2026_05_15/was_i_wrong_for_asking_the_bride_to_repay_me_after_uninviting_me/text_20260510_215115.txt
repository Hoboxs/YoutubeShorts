Was I wrong for asking the bride to repay me after uninviting me?

I ended up getting removed from a wedding one week before it happened after helping organize and pay for the bride’s bachelorette trip, and now I am wondering whether I crossed a line by asking her to reimburse me afterward.

I had known this woman for well over a decade, but I want to make it clear that we were never extremely close friends. We had a long history together, but our relationship mostly consisted of occasional catch ups where she vented to me about her life. She honestly did not know very basic things about me, including details about my education, my old career, or even major things happening in my life unless I brought them up myself.

Still, because of our long history, I stayed supportive.

Her relationship with her now husband had always been rocky. She spent years complaining about him to me. When he first proposed, she hated the proposal and hated the ring. At one point he even privately asked me for advice because he knew she was unhappy. I spent days trying to convince him to rethink things and plan something more thoughtful, but he ignored me. For nearly a year afterward she constantly vented about how disappointed she was.

Then suddenly everything changed. He proposed again in a more elaborate way, and overnight she acted like the first engagement never existed. From that moment on, everyone around her basically had to pretend this was the greatest romance ever created.

Around the same period, my own life completely fell apart health wise.

I developed a serious chronic illness that forced me to stop working entirely. My energy became unpredictable and I had to cancel multiple plans and appointments, including some wedding related events. Every time I canceled, I explained exactly why and apologized. I still attended what I physically could and tried to support her however possible.

What hurt me was that despite claiming she understood my condition, she never really checked on me or showed much concern about what I was going through. After a while I realized I was putting far more emotional energy into this friendship than she was.

Even so, I still helped plan and pay for part of her bachelorette celebration. Unfortunately my health flared badly again and I physically could not attend the actual trip.

A few days later, completely out of nowhere, she sent me a cold message saying she could not handle uncertainty around her wedding and felt I had not made enough effort during the planning process. She said she had decided I would no longer be invited to the wedding and that the decision was final.

That was it.

No conversation. No warning. No attempt to discuss anything beforehand. The message honestly sounded more like a formal workplace dismissal than something from a longtime friend.

The timing made it even more insulting because she had only invited me in the first place two days before the RSVP deadline months earlier. Looking back now, I honestly think she may never have truly wanted me there and only invited me out of obligation.

The last interaction we had before she removed me from the wedding was her wishing me a happy birthday. So the entire thing blindsided me.

I was furious.

By that point I had already contributed money toward her bachelorette celebration, bought clothes specifically to fit her required wedding color scheme, and my partner had arranged time off work because the wedding was happening on a weekday.

So I told her that if she was going to remove me from the wedding one week beforehand after accepting my help and money for months, then she should at minimum reimburse me for the portion of the bachelorette costs I paid.

Now I am wondering if asking for compensation made me petty and vindictive, or if it was reasonable considering how abruptly and coldly she cut me out after benefiting from my time, money, and support.
