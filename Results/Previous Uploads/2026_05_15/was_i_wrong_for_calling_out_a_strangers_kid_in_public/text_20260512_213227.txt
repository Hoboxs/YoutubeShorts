Was I Wrong for Calling Out a Strangers Kid in Public?

I’m a 27-year-old woman living in a mid-sized city in Ireland, and something happened at a supermarket yesterday that’s still bothering me. I stopped in after work to grab a few things before heading home, and while I was near the self-checkout area, two girls who looked maybe 13 or so came up to me acting nervous and giggly. One of them asked if I could “help them buy something.” The second she said it, I figured it was probably something they were too young to purchase themselves.

I asked what exactly they wanted, and the girl kept dodging the question, saying it was “for her cousin” and that it was “not a big deal.” That immediately made me uncomfortable, so I told them no. I explained that if they weren’t old enough or allowed to buy it on their own, I wasn’t going to step in and do it for them. I also told them they should be careful approaching random adults because not everyone out there is safe.

The girl got annoyed almost instantly and started mouthing off at me. At first it was typical rude teenager stuff, calling me ugly and saying I was probably miserable because nobody liked me. I tried to ignore it and started walking away, but then I heard her mutter something nasty about me being “one of those weird lesbian women.” That honestly caught me off guard more than anything else.

I turned around, walked back over, and told her very calmly that if she spoke to people like that, her parents would probably be ashamed of how she behaved in public. I said she needed to learn some respect before talking to strangers that way. She laughed in my face, grabbed her friend, and they both walked off still making jokes.

Now that I’ve had time to cool down, I’m wondering if I crossed a line. Part of me thinks I should’ve ignored the whole thing and kept walking, because arguing with a kid is never a good look. But another part of me feels like somebody needs to tell kids when they’re acting horribly, especially when they think insulting random people is funny.

I don’t have children myself, so maybe I’m overreacting, but the whole interaction genuinely shocked me. Are teenagers really this rude now, or was I wrong for saying something back?
