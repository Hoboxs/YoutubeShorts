Am I Losing My Mind Over a Simple Bathroom Habit?

I’m honestly still stunned writing this. Earlier today, the water in our apartment was shut off for a short repair. Nothing dramatic—our landlord said it would be back within the hour. In the meantime, we still had a small countertop filter for drinking water and plenty of hand sanitizer.

I used the bathroom first and, like a normal person, washed my hands using a bit of the filtered water. When my partner finished using the bathroom, she walked straight out and dropped onto the couch beside me like nothing happened. I asked, calmly at first, if she was going to wash her hands. She laughed it off and said she wasn’t “wasting drinking water” on that.

I suggested hand sanitizer instead. That’s when she casually told me she didn’t need it—because, according to her, she doesn’t wash her hands every time she uses the toilet. She said as long as she doesn’t “see anything” on her hands, she considers them clean.

Something in me snapped. I told her that was gross and an awful hygiene habit. Not screamed—just blunt. I pointed out that even if you’re careful, you’re still touching the toilet, the handle, the door. She rolled her eyes and said I was being dramatic and judgmental. For context, we do have a bidet, which she insists makes this whole thing a non-issue. To me, it really doesn’t.

Now she’s completely shut down, won’t speak to me, and says I owe her an apology for “shaming” her. Meanwhile, I’m sitting there wondering how long this has been going on and how many shared surfaces I’ve touched without knowing.

I’m not trying to control her. I’m not demanding perfection. I just can’t wrap my head around being told that basic handwashing is optional—and being expected to pretend that’s normal.
