Am I really endangering Christmas over a few cookies?

I’m hosting Christmas Eve at my home this year, something I’ve done for years, and my tradition is a big dessert table instead of a single cake. Baking is my thing, and I usually put out a wide spread of homemade cookies so everyone can pick their favorites. This year is a little different because my daughter-in-law has two children from a previous marriage, both under twelve, who have a peanut allergy. It’s not contact-based, but if they eat peanuts they break out badly and swell.

Because of that, I planned ahead. I decided to keep any peanut-based cookies on a separate table, clearly labeled, and bake them at different times to avoid cross-contamination. Everything else would be completely peanut-free. I explained this plan to my son, and he was fine with it. He said he’d remind the kids, who already manage their allergies at school and other events without issue.

A few days later, my daughter-in-law called and immediately shut the idea down. She insisted there couldn’t be anything with peanuts in the house at all and said having those cookies out would be “tempting” the kids. I told her I wasn’t comfortable scrapping recipes I’ve made for years, especially when precautions were already in place and other guests enjoy them. I suggested that keeping an eye on the kids, like they do everywhere else, would solve the problem.

That didn’t go over well. The call escalated quickly, and she accused me of being careless and selfish, even calling me names before hanging up. Later, my son reached out and said while he agreed the setup was reasonable, I could’ve handled the conversation with more diplomacy. Now I’m left feeling like what was supposed to be a thoughtful compromise somehow turned into a family standoff right before the holidays.
