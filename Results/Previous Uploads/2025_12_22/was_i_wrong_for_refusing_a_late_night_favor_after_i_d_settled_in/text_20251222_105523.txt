Was I Wrong for Refusing a Late-Night Favor After I’d Settled In?

I’ve always been very strict about how I end my days. Once I get home from my job, change out of my work clothes, shower, and switch into my comfortable home clothes, my day is done. That usually happens around early evening, and everyone close to me knows that once I reach that point, I don’t go back out unless there’s a real emergency. I don’t enjoy leaving the house after I’ve unwound, and I only make exceptions for serious situations.

One night, not long after I had already changed and settled in, my friend Mark called me. He asked if I could drive his partner, Lena, home from her evening sports practice later that night. I asked why he couldn’t pick her up himself, and he explained that his supervisor unexpectedly made him stay late at work due to issues earlier in the day. I reminded him that I was already done for the night, but he kept pushing, saying it would be quick. I still refused, because to me this didn’t feel urgent or unavoidable.

A few hours later, close to midnight, Mark showed up at my place visibly frustrated. He told me Lena had ended up waiting nearly two hours until he was finally able to leave work and get her. I asked why she hadn’t arranged a ride through a taxi or ride-share service, and he said she didn’t have one set up. That didn’t sit right with me, because it felt like a lack of planning on their part rather than something I was responsible for fixing. The situation apparently caused a major argument between them afterward.

From my point of view, there was no immediate danger. The weather was mild for the time of year, and it wasn’t raining or particularly cold. Growing up, I often waited long stretches for rides in far worse conditions, including harsh weather, and it was considered normal. Because of that, I’m struggling to see whether I truly did something wrong here or if I was simply holding a boundary I’ve always been clear about.
