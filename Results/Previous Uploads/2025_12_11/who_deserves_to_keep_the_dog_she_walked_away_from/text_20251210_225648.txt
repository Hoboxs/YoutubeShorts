Who Deserves to Keep the Dog She Walked Away From?

My cousin — I’ll call her Marin — adopted a fluffy retriever pup last year after her partner in Oslo, Norway claimed a dog would “help her feel centered.” They broke up less than five days later. About seven months ago, Marin called me sobbing, saying she needed a brief pause from caring for him. She promised it would be three days, no more. I’m 22, juggling a museum internship and evening classes in Valencia, but I’ve always loved animals, so I agreed.

Those “three days” stretched into more than half a year.

Marin stopped checking in entirely. She stopped sending any money. She even removed me from her social media while still posting beach trips, rooftop parties, and a new romance every other week. Meanwhile, her dog — a warm, honey-colored retriever with a goofy grin — became part of my life. He curls up against my legs every night, trots behind me from room to room, and plays gently with my two older cats like he’s always belonged here. I’ve covered everything: vets, food, grooming, training gear — easily over €900 by now.

Then two days ago, she suddenly reappeared with a cheerful, “I’m finally feeling balanced again! Can you bring my dog back this weekend?” as if she hadn’t vanished for half a year.

I told her I was glad she was doing better, but she had walked away from him for months, and taking him from a steady home now would be deeply unfair. That’s when she erupted — long messages accusing me of “destroying her life,” claiming I “stole her companion,” and threatening to involve authorities. My mother took her side, insisting that “family should cooperate,” but my aunt quietly told me she was proud of me for giving the dog the stability Marin never managed.

I told Marin that if she truly intended to take him back, she needed to reimburse the money I’d spent and sign a clear agreement accepting full, ongoing responsibility. She refused immediately, telling me to “stay gone forever,” and is now telling relatives I “snatched her dog” even though he’s currently asleep with his head on my knee, safe and trusting.

Was I wrong to keep caring for the dog she abandoned — or is she furious only because she suddenly wants something she didn’t bother to keep?
