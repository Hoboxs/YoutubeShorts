Is It Wrong to Ask for More Age-Appropriate Gifts for My Son?

My mother-in-law is very generous, and this year she bought Christmas gifts for both of my sons — my toddler, who just turned one, and my eight-year-old, who’s been having a hard time adjusting to no longer being the “baby” of the family. Because of that, I’ve been extra careful about making sure he feels seen and valued.

Here’s where things went sideways.

For the baby, my MIL bought several fun, flashy toys — a talking character plush, a ride-on car, the kind of big, exciting items little kids immediately latch onto. But for my eight-year-old, she chose a board game he’s never shown interest in, a nutcracker figurine, and… a Basquiat art coffee-table book. She said she thought it would “inspire him,” but he’s not an art-kid in that way. He likes drawing characters from games he plays, but he’s not spending afternoons studying modern art.

I told her I was worried. I can already picture Christmas morning: the baby has a huge unwrapped ride-on car and animated toys, and my eight-year-old, who’s been fragile lately, unwraps a board game he won’t touch and a giant art book meant for adults. I’m not asking for anything extravagant — just something age-appropriate that feels fun for him.

She pushed back immediately. She said she spent the same amount of money on both kids, the nutcracker was “the big gift,” and it’s unfair for me to ask her to buy anything else. She also seemed offended that I questioned her choices at all.

I’m trying to advocate for my son, not micromanage her gift-giving. But now I’m second-guessing myself.

So… am I wrong for asking her to get a more suitable present, or is it reasonable to worry about how an eight-year-old will feel on Christmas morning?
