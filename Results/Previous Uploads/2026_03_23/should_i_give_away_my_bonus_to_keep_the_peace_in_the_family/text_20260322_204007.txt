Should I give away my bonus to keep the peace in the family?

I am a twenty nine year old woman working as a hospital nurse, and my partner, who is thirty three, works in the same field. My partner’s brother, who is a few years older than me, also works as a nurse. We live on opposite sides of the country, but all three of us have taken short term work placements over the past several years. At my most recent job, I had an excellent recruiter, so I decided to recommend my partner’s brother to the agency. When I first joined, my partner had recommended me, so I knew how the process worked. The company offers a reward of about nine hundred dollars to the person who makes the referral after the new hire completes a set period, while the new hire receives a smaller amount of around three hundred dollars.

For the past few weeks, my partner’s brother had been making casual but pointed comments, saying things like we should send him the referral money or split it with him. He believed my partner would be receiving the reward, not realizing it was actually going to me. I chose to stay out of it at first and let my partner handle the comments, assuming it would fade away. Instead, everything escalated once he reached the required number of weeks at his placement and received his portion.

As soon as that happened, he began messaging my partner repeatedly, insisting that he deserved the full reward because he was the one doing the work. My partner tried to shut it down and explained that I was the one who would be receiving the payment. That is when he turned his attention to me. He began sending me messages saying the money should belong to him and that it was unfair for me to keep it. I told him clearly that without my recommendation, he would not have received any extra payment at all, and that this was simply how the system worked. The company pays the person who brings in a new worker more because they helped the business. I stopped replying after that, thinking the situation would settle.

Instead, the next day took a much harsher turn. He began sending angry messages to my partner, saying that he should leave me and calling me difficult and selfish, along with other hurtful comments. My partner became furious and defended me, and since then, his brother has cut off contact with both of us.

Now I am left feeling torn. Part of me hates that this has created such a deep conflict within the family and wonders if giving up the money would have avoided all of this. At the same time, it feels wrong to give in after being pressured and insulted over something that was never his to begin with. I cannot help but question whether standing firm was the right choice, or if I should have sacrificed the money to keep the peace.
