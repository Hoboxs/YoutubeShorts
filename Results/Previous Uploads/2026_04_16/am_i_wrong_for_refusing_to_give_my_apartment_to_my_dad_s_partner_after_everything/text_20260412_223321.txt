Am I wrong for refusing to give my apartment to my dad’s partner after everything?

I grew up in a pretty messy family situation. My parents split when I was young, and my dad remarried. Over time, things got strained between us, enough that we were basically estranged for years. A big part of that was financial. My dad makes very good money, but he avoided contributing to child support or helping with me and my siblings’ education. My mom carried most of that burden.

I moved a few hours away for university to finish an arts degree. During my final year, there was an issue with my student housing that made it temporarily unlivable. I was already supporting myself with part-time jobs, and suddenly I had nowhere to stay. My options were: drop out six months before graduating and move back home, or stay in the city and figure it out.

I stayed. For a period of time, I was effectively homeless, couch surfing when I could, and otherwise just getting by however possible. My dad was aware, at least generally, of what was happening. He never offered help.

Eventually, the housing situation got resolved, I moved back in, and I finished my degree.

Fast forward a couple of years: I moved back home briefly, worked two full-time jobs, and managed to get into an extremely competitive clinical graduate program. I also saved enough to get my own apartment near my school. I’m currently living there while completing my master’s.

Now here’s the issue.

My dad recently reached out asking if his partner could stay in my apartment for a while because she has a relative in a nearby hospital. On paper, I understand the situation is stressful for them. But I live in this apartment. It’s not sitting empty.

The thing is, they absolutely have the financial means to stay somewhere else. In the last couple of months alone, they’ve gone on two international vacations. Meanwhile, during the entire 7 years I’ve been pursuing my education less than 4 hours away, they’ve never once come to visit me.

I’ve tried to politely say no and set boundaries, but they keep pushing. On top of that, they’ve started criticizing my “financial decisions,” saying it’s wasteful to keep paying for my apartment if I’ll be back home working over the summer for a bit, basically implying I should just let them use it.

This whole situation is bringing up a lot of old resentment for me. Growing up, there was clear favoritism and some pretty unfair treatment, and it feels like that dynamic is still there. Like my needs and boundaries just don’t matter.

At the same time, part of me feels guilty. There is a medical situation involved, and I know that’s serious. But I also feel like I’ve worked incredibly hard to get to where I am, entirely on my own, and I don’t want to give up my space, especially to people who didn’t support me when I needed it most.

So am I wrong for refusing to let them stay in my apartment?
