Would I be wrong for keeping my dorm instead of switching?

I am in a situation that is making me feel guilty, even though I am not sure I have actually done anything wrong. At my boarding school, students get to choose their dorms, and I ended up with a really great setup. My room used to be a double, but because people complained it was too small, the school turned it into a single. That means I have my own space and a private bathroom, which most single rooms do not have. It also happens to be in a convenient spot near the laundry room and faces a nice area on campus. I chose it carefully, and I get to keep the same setup each year, just on a different floor.

Recently, a new student arrived, and she has autism. Before break, she came to talk to me and asked if I would consider giving her my dorm. She explained that my room would suit her needs better because of its location, layout, and how it feels. She also shared that she lives far away and has to stay on campus during weekends and shorter breaks, unlike me, since I live close enough to go home often. On top of that, she is still new and does not have a strong support system here yet, so she spends a lot of time in her room.

I could tell this was not an easy thing for her to ask, and it made me feel bad. I even spoke to the school about it, and they told me that I am not required to switch, but I could move into another available single if I chose to. The thing is, I really love my room. It feels comfortable, familiar, and honestly, I picked it for a reason. Giving it up would mean losing a space that works perfectly for me.

Now I feel torn. On one hand, I understand why she wants the room and I feel for her situation. On the other hand, I chose this space fairly, and I am not doing anything wrong by wanting to keep it. I cannot tell if holding onto it makes me selfish, or if it is reasonable to keep something that I was given the option to choose in the first place.
