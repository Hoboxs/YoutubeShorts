Was I wrong for not switching project topics after a classmate said she “planned” to take it?

In one of my university classes, we had to choose presentation topics from a shared Google Sheet. It was clearly first come, first served. The professor explicitly said once you put your name down, the topic is yours.

There was one topic that pretty much everyone wanted because it’s easier and has a lot of available sources. I checked the sheet the night it opened. No one had signed up yet, so I added my name to that topic.

The next morning, a girl from class messaged me saying she was planning to take that topic and had already told her friends she was doing it. She said she forgot to add her name before going to bed and asked if I’d switch with her. The only topics left at that point were more complex and would clearly require more work.

I told her I was sorry, but I chose it fairly and didn’t want to switch.

Now a few classmates are saying that while I was technically right, it was socially kind of harsh because “everyone knew” she wanted that topic and I could’ve just been nice.

My perspective is: if she really wanted it, she should’ve put her name down. It was first come, first served, and I followed the rules.

But I also don’t want to be seen as inconsiderate.

Was I wrong for not switching?
