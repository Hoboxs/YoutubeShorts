Was I wrong for “accidentally” confirming my sister’s pregnancy because I was tired of keeping her secret?

I’m 24 and my sister is 21. We’ve never been super close, more of a competitive sibling dynamic, but we’re not enemies.

About two months ago, she told me she was pregnant. It wasn’t planned, and she was really anxious about how our parents would react. They’re traditional and can be judgmental. She made me promise not to tell anyone until she was ready. I agreed, even though I felt a little unsure about being put in that position.

She still lives at home, and I visit often. My mom started making comments to me about my sister being “moody” and “gaining weight” and kept asking if I knew what was going on. Every time, I had to pretend I didn’t know anything.

I started feeling drained by it. I was constantly covering for her but not actually included in anything. She didn’t really update me or confide in me, she just expected me to lie for her. I felt used.

Last weekend, my mom directly asked me if my sister was pregnant. I hesitated and said, “I think you should talk to her.”

That pretty much confirmed it.

My mom confronted my sister immediately, and it turned into a huge fight. Now my sister is furious. She says I betrayed her and stole her chance to tell them on her own terms.

I told her I didn’t outright say she was pregnant, I just didn’t deny it. And honestly, I was tired of carrying the secret and constantly lying.

She says I made it about my discomfort for a few weeks, when she’s the one going through something life-changing.

She’s not responding to my texts now.

I do feel guilty because I promised. But I also feel like I shouldn’t have been put in the position of managing her secret with our parents in the first place.

Was I wrong?
