Was I wrong for canceling overnight visits because my sister-in-law keeps trying to control how I raise my son?

I lost my husband almost two years ago. Since then, I’ve really tried to make sure our son still has a relationship with his dad’s side of the family. It hasn’t been easy, but I’ve done my best to include them.

Right now, my son spends two weekends a month staying with them. But over time, it’s started to feel less like support and more like they’re trying to take over parenting decisions. His aunt in particular always seems to think she knows better. It can be small things, like criticizing his hairstyle, but it also goes deeper than that.

One of the biggest issues is that I never get my son back the same way I dropped him off. There are always changes, comments, or new “suggestions” that feel more like pressure than help. I’ve tried talking to them about it multiple times, but nothing really changes.

What bothered me most recently was picking him up last weekend and having him tell me that his aunt told him to say he wants to change schools, even though he admitted to her that he actually likes his current school. She had already brought up switching schools with me before, and I had clearly said no.

That crossed a line for me. It felt like she was using my son to push her own agenda.

So today, I called her and said that overnight visits are no longer happening. Instead, they’re welcome to come see him at my home for a few hours, but that’s it.

Now she’s accusing me of trying to push her and the rest of the family out of my son’s life and says this is something I’ve “always wanted,” which isn’t true. I know she was very close to my husband, and I understand she loves my son, but I don’t think that gives her the right to interfere in how I raise him.

I’m starting to question whether I was too harsh by cutting off the visits instead of trying to work it out again.

Was I wrong for setting that boundary?
