Was I wrong for telling a coworker to stop the “naughty candy” act?

I keep a small stash of snacks in my office for anyone who drops by. It is something I enjoy doing, and most people just grab a piece, say thanks, and move on. The issue is that a few coworkers turn it into a whole performance every single time. They stand there talking about how they should not have it, how it is “so bad,” and how they are being “naughty” for even considering it.

It is not just a quick joke either. It turns into this drawn out moment where they seem to expect me to react, reassure them, or somehow give permission. I usually just smile and say nothing, but they linger and keep going. Over time, it has started to bother me more and more. It feels awkward, and I do not enjoy being pulled into that kind of exchange over something as simple as a piece of candy.

Yesterday, one coworker came in and did exactly that again. She stood in the doorway, pointing at the candy, going on about how much she loves it but how she should not have it and how I am “bad” for keeping it there. After hearing this same routine so many times, I finally said something. I asked her to stop because it was making me uncomfortable.

She acted confused, like she did not understand what I meant, so I explained more directly. I told her that it feels strange when she talks like she needs permission and keeps calling it “naughty,” and that I would prefer if she just took one or did not without turning it into a scene.

She said she was only joking and left, but apparently she later told others that I overreacted and made her feel embarrassed. Now I am hearing that some people are avoiding coming into my office for snacks because they think I will react the same way.

I am not planning to stop offering candy, and I do not mind people taking it. I just do not like being pulled into that kind of repetitive, awkward interaction. At the same time, I am wondering if the way I said it came off harsher than I intended and if I could have handled it better.
