Was I wrong for telling my brother in law to stop helping his sick friend?

My younger sister is seven months pregnant with her third child, and this pregnancy has been especially hard on her. She is already taking care of two very young kids, and lately she has been exhausted, dealing with pain, nausea, and everything that comes with being that far along. To make things harder, she and her husband do not have family nearby because they moved for his job.

At the same time, my brother in law’s closest friend is in the late stages of a serious neurodegenerative illness. His condition has declined to the point where he needs a lot of physical support, and because he is a large man, his wife has been relying on my brother in law to help when professional caregivers are not there. I understand that this is a heartbreaking situation, and I know my brother in law is trying to be there for someone he cares deeply about.

The problem is how much time he is spending away. He is not just visiting for a day or two. He has been gone for long stretches, sometimes weeks at a time, staying with his friend and leaving my sister alone to manage everything at home. That means she is heavily pregnant, caring for two toddlers, and dealing with everything on her own.

A few nights ago, she called me in tears. One of her kids was sick, she had not slept, and she was feeling completely overwhelmed and physically worn down. Hearing her like that made me angry, and I reacted without thinking it through carefully. I called my brother in law and told him he needed to come home and take care of his own family. I said that while his friend’s situation is tragic, he cannot keep leaving his wife alone like this. I pointed out that his friend has other support, including a spouse and medical help, while my sister is essentially by herself.

He did not take that well. He accused me of telling him to abandon his friend, which I did not mean. I tried to explain that there should be some kind of balance, but the conversation ended badly, and he hung up. Later, it led to a big argument between him and my sister.

Now things have escalated. His parents are upset and believe my sister should be more understanding, saying she has handled pregnancies before and should be able to cope. They are also angry at me for stepping in and making the situation worse.

I am starting to question whether I overstepped. I was trying to defend my sister because she is clearly struggling, but I also understand that my brother in law is dealing with a very difficult situation of his own. I cannot tell if I was right to speak up or if I made an already complicated situation even more tense.
