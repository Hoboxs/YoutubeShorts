Should I Let My Wife Keep Using My Expensive Chef Knife on Plastic?

I do almost all the cooking in our home—every meal, every day. Two years ago, my wife gifted me a $130 Takumi Edge chef’s knife for Christmas. It was the first high-end knife I’ve ever owned, and I treat it like a fragile treasure: hand-wash only, dry immediately, never the dishwasher.

My wife, however, treats it like a $5 MegaMart utility knife. She leaves it on the counter, cuts lemons and tomatoes on it, and worst of all, slices through thick plastic packaging that ruins the blade. I’ve asked repeatedly for her to stop, but nothing changes.

This morning, she grabbed it to open a sausage pack. I told her to use scissors or one of the cheap knives in the drawer. She ignored me and used the expensive knife anyway. I finally snapped: “Using this knife like that is like taking one of your designer dresses and scrubbing the floor with it!” She shrugged, and our adult daughter said, “You gave it to her, she can do what she wants.”

Now, I’m hiding the knife after every use. I cook for my family every day, and it feels like something I love is being slowly destroyed in front of me. Should I keep letting her use it this way?
