Would I be wrong for suing my friend after she skipped my wedding but used the trip as her honeymoon?

I got married this January in Bali. It was a destination wedding, and while our parents paid for their own flights and hotels, my husband and I covered airfare and hotel stays for our close friends so they could attend. Each plane ticket was about $2,000 USD, and the hotel for the week was roughly $150–300 total per guest.

My friend “Gemma,” who I’ve been close with for over 10 years, was invited along with her newlywed husband, “John.” We paid for Gemma’s flight and hotel. She paid for John’s plane ticket herself.

Here’s the issue: Gemma and John flew to Bali, stayed in the hotel room we paid for… and did not show up to our wedding.

When I asked her why, she told me that since they couldn’t afford their own honeymoon, this trip was a perfect opportunity. She also said John “didn’t feel like going” to the wedding itself.

So essentially, they accepted thousands of dollars in travel and accommodation from us, used it as a vacation, and skipped the actual event the trip was meant for.

I’m honestly devastated. This wasn’t just about money, it was about a decade-long friendship and what I thought was mutual respect. We paid for her to be there to celebrate one of the biggest days of our lives. Instead, she treated it like a free honeymoon.

Now I’m considering taking her to small claims court to recover the cost of her plane ticket and hotel stay.

On one hand, I feel completely taken advantage of. On the other, suing a longtime friend feels extreme and would permanently end the relationship.

Would I be wrong for pursuing legal action to get the money back?
