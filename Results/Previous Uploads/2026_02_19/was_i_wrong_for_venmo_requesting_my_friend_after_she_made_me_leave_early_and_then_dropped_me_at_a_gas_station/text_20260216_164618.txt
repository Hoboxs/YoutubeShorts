Was I wrong for Venmo-requesting my friend after she made me leave early and then dropped me at a gas station?

I went out with a few friends last night. One of them, “Sarah,” offered to drive since she said she wouldn’t be drinking and would stay sober. That worked for everyone.

Around 1 a.m., she suddenly said she was too tired and wanted to leave. I told her I was fine staying longer and would just Uber home later. She kept insisting she “felt responsible” for me and didn’t want to leave me there. It turned into an awkward back-and-forth in the middle of the club, and I didn’t feel like making it a bigger scene, so I agreed to go with her.

Here’s where it gets frustrating.

Once we got to the car, she casually mentioned she was actually heading to her boyfriend’s place because it was closer for her. His place is about 20 minutes in the opposite direction from mine. She drove there, pulled into a gas station near his house, and told me to just call an Uber from there.

Because of surge pricing, my Uber home cost $25.

If she had just left when she originally wanted to, I would have happily stayed and paid for my own ride later. That wasn’t the issue. What bothered me was that she insisted I leave because she “felt responsible,” only to drop me off at a random gas station so she could go see her boyfriend.

So I Venmo-requested her the $25. Now she’s upset, saying I’m ungrateful because she “gave me a ride” at all. Some of our other friends think I’m being petty over what’s “just” $25.

But to me, it’s not really about the amount. It’s about being pressured to leave and then left halfway home at 1 a.m.

Was I wrong for asking her to cover the Uber?
