Am I Wrong for Wanting Five Minutes Alone in the Bathroom?

My husband and I have two kids, including a son who is about a year and a half old. Lately I feel like I can’t even use the bathroom without an audience. Every time I try to go, my son either bangs on the door crying or insists on climbing onto my lap while I’m sitting there. It sounds silly, but after dealing with it constantly, I was desperate for just a few quiet minutes by myself.

One morning I asked my husband if he could take our son with him while he drove our older child to school. I figured it would give me a chance to finally use the bathroom in peace.

Unfortunately, the timing turned into a disaster. There was traffic everywhere, road closures, and delays that kept them stuck in the car far longer than expected. What should have been a quick trip ended up taking close to an hour, and our son apparently screamed most of the drive.

When they finally got home, my husband was furious. He started yelling that it was unfair to our son to be “trapped” in the car for that long just so I could go to the bathroom alone. According to him, I was selfish for even asking.

I got irritated and replied that by his logic, I guess we can never take our son anywhere anymore, because toddlers hate car seats sometimes and complain about being strapped in no matter where you’re going.

Now he thinks I’m completely unreasonable, while I feel like wanting a few uninterrupted minutes in the bathroom is not some outrageous request.
