Should I Stop Going Out Late to Keep the Peace at Home?

I am in my early twenties and currently share a rental with my mother. About once a week, usually toward the end of the week, she hosts my sister’s young daughter for a sleepover to give my sister some time to herself. It is a routine everyone seems comfortable with, and it has been happening for a while.

I work full time with evening hours, and after every shift I go straight to the gym. Exercise is a big part of my routine, and I am training for a personal goal later this year, so consistency matters to me. I usually get off work around the late evening and make it home roughly an hour and a half later, depending on traffic.

The issue is that whenever I come home, my mother’s dog barks loudly. He always does this when anyone enters the house. On nights when my niece is sleeping over, the barking sometimes wakes her up. If that happens, I make sure to reassure her that it is just me so she does not get scared, and she usually falls back asleep without much trouble.

My mother has asked me to skip the gym on the nights my niece stays over so that I come home earlier and avoid waking her. I told her I do not think that is a reasonable request. From my perspective, being woken briefly by a barking dog on a night when she does not have school the next day is not a major issue. I also pointed out that the dog is not mine, and the barking is not something I am doing intentionally.

I explained that I do not want to cut back on my gym sessions, especially since I only have one day off a week and am working toward a goal that requires regular training. My mother feels that I am being selfish and unwilling to accommodate the family, while I feel like I am being asked to give up something important to me to solve a problem I did not create.

Now there is tension between us, and I am wondering whether standing firm on this boundary was fair or if I should have been more flexible.
