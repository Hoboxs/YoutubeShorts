Was I wrong for drinking more beers than the “limit” at a party?

I am a man in my early thirties currently in a graduate program where most of my classmates are a bit younger than me. Recently, one of them hosted a party and invited everyone to bring a six pack of beer so people could mix and match and try different options. My partner came along with me, although he planned not to drink since he had work to do the next morning.

Since we were coming together, we brought two six packs between us. They were nothing fancy, just a couple of different ales we thought people might enjoy. When we arrived, everyone placed their drinks on a shared table, and throughout the night people would walk over, grab something, and continue socializing. It felt relaxed and casual, exactly what you would expect from that kind of gathering.

As the evening went on, I ended up having eight beers. My partner did not have any, so from my perspective, we had contributed more than we used. I did not think much of it at the time since the whole setup seemed communal and flexible.

Toward the end of the night, though, the host approached me and said I had gone over the limit. He explained that each person was only supposed to have six drinks, regardless of how much they brought. I was surprised because that had not been clearly emphasized, and it did not seem to match how things were actually playing out. I pointed out that between the two of us, we had brought twelve beers and only consumed eight, so it did not feel like we had taken more than our share.

He disagreed and said that my reasoning did not matter, insisting that I had personally gone over the limit. He even asked me to pay for the extra drinks I had. That caught me completely off guard and made the situation feel unnecessarily tense over something that seemed minor.

Now I am left wondering if I misunderstood the expectations or if the whole situation was blown out of proportion. I did not intend to take advantage of anyone, but I also feel like the reaction did not match what actually happened.
