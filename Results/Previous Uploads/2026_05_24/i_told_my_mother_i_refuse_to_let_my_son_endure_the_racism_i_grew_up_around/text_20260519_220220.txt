I Told My Mother I Refuse to Let My Son Endure the Racism I Grew Up Around.

My mother is white and my father is Black. They divorced when I was very young, and I was mostly raised around my mother’s side of the family.

Growing up, racism was something I dealt with constantly from certain relatives. Some family members made “jokes” or little comments they thought were harmless. Others were much more blatant. One of my uncles openly used racial slurs without shame.

I am mixed race, but people do not always immediately recognize me as Black unless I wear my natural hair a certain way. Even so, the comments still happened regularly toward me and my brothers.

The part that hurt most was that my mother never really stopped it. She usually stayed quiet, brushed it off, or acted like confronting them would just create drama. As kids, we basically learned to sit there and tolerate it because nobody was going to defend us.

Now I’m 30 and pregnant with my first child. My partner is Black, so our son will very likely be clearly perceived as Black by most people.

Recently I was talking with my mother about eventually coming home for the holidays after the baby is born so extended family could meet my partner and our son. During that conversation, I told her very clearly that if anyone makes racist comments toward my child or my partner, we will immediately leave. No discussion, no second chances in the moment, just gone.

I explained that I am not willing to let my son grow up accepting the same treatment my brothers and I had to endure.

My mother got very emotional and said my comment basically implied she had been a terrible mother. I told her I was not trying to attack her, but honestly, I do believe parents should protect their children from racist behavior, even when it comes from relatives.

She started crying and said, “I guess I was just the worst mother in the world then.”

Now I feel conflicted because I was trying to establish boundaries for my own child, not destroy my mother emotionally. At the same time, I also feel like pretending my childhood experiences were acceptable would be dishonest.

For context, not everyone on my mother’s side behaves this way. It is a large extended family, and only certain relatives are openly racist. The issue is that the family still gathers together for major holidays, and everyone attends, including the people who caused problems growing up.

My partner and I currently live abroad in a very small place, so hosting visiting relatives ourselves is not realistic, which is part of why we were considering traveling there instead.

Still, I cannot shake the feeling that if I do bring my son around those relatives, it is my responsibility to protect him in ways I was not protected myself.
