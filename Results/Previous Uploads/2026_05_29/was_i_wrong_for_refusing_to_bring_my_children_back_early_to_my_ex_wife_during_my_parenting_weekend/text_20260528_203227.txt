Was I wrong for refusing to bring my children back early to my ex-wife during my parenting weekend?

My ex-wife, Melissa, and I divorced last year. We have two children together, ages 10 and 7. Right now she has primary custody, and I get the children two weekends a month plus some extra time during holidays and summer breaks. I’ve been trying to get more custody time, but one of the biggest obstacles is that I currently live too far from their school district, so I’m working on relocating closer.

This past weekend I had the children with me and took them to visit my parents at their lake house. We spent most of Saturday out on the water fishing, tubing, and just enjoying time together.

Later that evening, my ex-wife texted asking if we could talk. When I called her, she said she was having a difficult emotional day and asked if she could speak with the children for a little while. Of course I said yes.

After a few minutes, the children handed the phone back to me because she wanted to continue talking. That’s when she asked if I would bring the children back to her early for the rest of the weekend because she missed them and wanted them with her.

I told her no. I explained that this was my scheduled parenting time and I intended to keep it. I also asked what was going on because she normally only contacts me during my weekends with the children for logistical reasons.

Eventually she admitted she had been supposed to go on a date that night, but the man canceled or stopped responding, and now she was upset and lonely.

She then said she had already asked the children whether they wanted to come home early and claimed they were okay with it. That really bothered me because it felt manipulative to involve them in an adult issue before even discussing it properly with me.

I told her I was not giving up part of my already limited time with the children just because her evening plans fell apart. I also reminded her that she gets far more time with them overall, while I have to fight for every extra hour I can get.

She accused me of being selfish and said if the children wanted to return early, I should allow it. I told her the custody schedule is an adult responsibility, not a decision for the children to make in the moment, and that I would return them at the normal agreed-upon time.

The rest of the weekend went fine. The kids and I spent more time at the lake and had a great visit. But afterward she continued sending messages calling me cruel and acting cold toward me during the Monday drop-off.

Part of me understands that she was emotional and hurt after being stood up, but another part of me feels like she was trying to use the children to comfort herself during my parenting time, and I don’t think that’s fair.
