Was I Wrong for Handling All the Night Feedings Without Telling My Wife?

When my daughter was born, she went through the normal newborn phase of waking up crying several times a night. Her mother had a very difficult pregnancy and was exhausted most of the time afterward. I have always been a light sleeper, so whenever the baby cried, I would usually wake up first and handle it myself.

Most nights I would take care of everything quietly. I would warm a bottle, change diapers, rock the baby back to sleep, whatever needed to be done. I never really kept score or thought much about it. I assumed there were also nights when her mother woke up before I did, so I figured things naturally balanced out over time.

One night we were out with friends, and the conversation turned to parenting. My wife started talking about how lucky we were because the baby “mostly slept through the night” and how she rarely ever had to get up with her.

I did not want to embarrass her in front of everyone, so I stayed quiet. But once we got home, I gently mentioned that the reason she rarely woke up was because I had been getting up with the baby most nights.

That conversation exploded immediately.

She became extremely upset and accused me of taking important experiences away from her as a mother. She said that because I never woke her up, her body never learned to respond to the baby crying during the night. In her mind, I had “stolen” those maternal responsibilities from her.

Honestly, I never viewed it as a “mother’s job” or “father’s job.” I just saw it as parenting. Our baby needed care, I was awake, so I handled it. I genuinely thought I was helping both my daughter and her mother by letting her rest after such a rough pregnancy.

For more context, both of us were working at the time, and I was regularly doing long shifts six days a week while surviving on very little sleep. We barely had time to communicate properly outside of basic survival mode conversations. We also used an app to log feedings and diaper changes, so I assumed she could see how often I was up with the baby if she checked it.

Apparently she was not really looking at it, and we both failed badly at communicating about what was happening.

The argument itself happened months ago, and our daughter is older now and thankfully sleeping much better. But her mother still brings it up to family and friends as an example of something terrible I did to her, and now I genuinely do not know if I crossed a line by handling things the way I did.

I never meant to exclude her. I just wanted to take care of my daughter and let her mother get some sleep.
