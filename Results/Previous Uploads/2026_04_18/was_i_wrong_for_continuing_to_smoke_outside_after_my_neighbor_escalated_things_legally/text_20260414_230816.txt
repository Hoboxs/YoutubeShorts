Was I wrong for continuing to smoke outside after my neighbor escalated things legally?

My partner and I rent a detached home in a quiet suburban neighborhood with decent space between houses and fenced yards. We don’t smoke inside the house at all, only outside on our back patio, usually in the morning with coffee or occasionally during the day. It’s not constant, just here and there.

A few months ago, one of our neighbors started complaining about the smoke. At first, it was just comments, so we tried to be respectful. We moved where we sat outside and even tried smoking in the garage with an air filter to minimize any impact. That still wasn’t enough for her, so we stopped doing that and kept everything outdoors again.

Things started getting more intense. At one point, she actually climbed up high enough to see over our fence and recorded me while I was outside, criticizing me out loud about what kind of example I was setting in front of my kids. That felt very personal and invasive.

Then she posted about us in a local neighborhood group, openly saying she recorded me and pointing out which house was ours. A lot of people who saw it didn’t agree with her and said we had the right to do what we wanted on our own property, which made the situation even more uncomfortable.

More recently, she yelled over the fence that she had hired a lawyer and claimed that multiple neighbors were backing her up. Shortly after, our landlord received a formal letter accusing us of creating a nuisance and affecting her health, with threats of further action.

For context, we’re not in a shared building, there aren’t specific rules banning smoking where we live, and we’ve made efforts to be considerate without completely changing our habits.

At this point, it feels like things have gone way beyond a simple neighbor disagreement. I understand that cigarette smoke can bother people, but being recorded, posted about publicly, and now dealing with legal threats feels extreme.

I’m trying to figure out if I’m being unreasonable by continuing to smoke outside, or if this has crossed into something else entirely.
