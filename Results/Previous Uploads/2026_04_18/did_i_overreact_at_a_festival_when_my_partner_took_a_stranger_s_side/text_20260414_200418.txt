Did I overreact at a festival when my partner took a stranger’s side?

I’m a 25-year-old woman, and recently I went to a large medieval-themed festival with my partner and my cousin. My cousin and I attend these kinds of events all the time, but it was my partner’s first experience.

These festivals are very interactive, people dress up, shout, cheer, and play along with the performers. It’s part of the atmosphere. During a tournament-style show, I was doing what I always do: cheering, calling out support for one of the competitors, and just enjoying the moment like everyone else around me.

At one point, a man sitting in front of me turned around and said I was “too loud” and that I was upsetting his child, asking me to tone it down.

Before I could even respond, my partner stepped in and told me I should probably lower my voice.

That immediately rubbed me the wrong way. It felt like he sided with a random stranger instead of backing me up, especially in a setting where being loud and enthusiastic is completely normal. Someone else nearby even leaned over and said the man was being unreasonable, which made me feel a bit validated.

Still, I felt embarrassed and frustrated, so I decided to step away to cool off. My cousin came with me. It didn’t feel like a big deal at the time because we had already planned to split up after the show for a while to explore different parts of the festival and meet back later at a designated spot.

A few hours later, my partner found us and was clearly upset. He said I had left him stranded at an unfamiliar event and that I should have adjusted my behavior if a child was uncomfortable, regardless of the setting.

I didn’t see it that way. In my mind, I was acting appropriately for the environment, and I didn’t think it was fair to single me out for something that everyone else was also doing. Plus, we already had a plan to go our separate ways for a bit.

The situation escalated into an argument, and now I’m wondering if I handled it poorly or if my reaction was justified.
