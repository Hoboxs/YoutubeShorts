Was I wrong for sending my rental host a full damage walkthrough video before unpacking?

I recently checked into a short-term vacation rental with my girlfriend. Before we unpacked or touched anything, I walked through the entire place with my phone camera recording. I slowly filmed every room and pointed out any scratches, stains, dents, or other issues I noticed while narrating the video with timestamps.

Last year I had a really bad experience where a different rental host charged me several hundred dollars for damage that already existed before I arrived. I had no proof, and the platform sided with the host, so ever since then I’ve been extra cautious.

After finishing the walkthrough, I sent the video to the host along with a polite message basically saying, “Just documenting a few pre-existing issues before my stay so there’s no confusion later.”

The host responded almost immediately and seemed offended. They said the video was “incredibly insulting,” that no guest had ever done something like that before, and that I was treating them like they were dishonest.

My girlfriend thinks I escalated things unnecessarily and says recording and sending a four-minute inspection video made me come across as paranoid and difficult before the stay even really began. On the other hand, a couple of my friends think it was completely reasonable considering I’ve already been burned once over false damage claims.

Now I’m wondering if I handled it badly. I genuinely wasn’t trying to accuse the host of anything — I just wanted documentation in case there was ever a dispute later.
