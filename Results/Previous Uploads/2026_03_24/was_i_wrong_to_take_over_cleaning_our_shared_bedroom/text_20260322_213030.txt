Was I wrong to take over cleaning our shared bedroom?

I am a forty one year old man, and I have been married to my wife, who is forty three, for nearly two decades. Over the past several years, our financial situation improved, and we moved into a larger home filled with nicer belongings. Around that same time, something in our daily life began to change in a way I did not expect. My wife started setting aside items in our bedroom, saying she would deal with them later. At first, it was a few small stacks, nothing that seemed concerning. But over time, those stacks grew into large piles that now cover most of the floor. I would say nearly three quarters of the room became unusable. I have to carefully move things just to reach my dresser, and the only clear space left is the small area beside my side of the bed.

To be clear, the situation has never involved anything unsafe. There are no odors, no damage, and no issues beyond the clutter itself. The rest of the house is completely normal. The bedroom, however, has become a place that feels overwhelming to me. Despite this, my wife has been very firm that I should not touch any of it, insisting it is her space to manage.

One autumn, after a long discussion, I finally convinced her to tackle the room. I took full responsibility for our children for several days so she could focus without interruption. When it was finished, the room felt completely transformed. She even admitted how good it felt and suggested we could make it a yearly habit. That moment gave me hope that we had found a solution.

But when the next year came around and I gently reminded her, she refused. She said it was her space and that I had no right to push her about it. That was when I started to feel truly frustrated. To me, this is a shared room, and living in that level of disorder was becoming harder and harder.

After one unrelated disagreement, she left the house for a long walk, clearly upset. In that moment, I made a decision that I knew might cause problems. I gathered everything from the bedroom and moved it into the garage, placing it all on a large sheet. When she returned and saw what I had done, she was furious. Over the next few days, I carefully sorted everything into groups, separating what seemed like waste from what could be kept or donated. I made sure she had the final say on every item, but the process still caused tension. In the end, we cleared out a large amount of unnecessary belongings, and the room was usable again.

This year, I decided to set a clear boundary. I told her that if the room is not cleaned at least once by mid October, I will step in and do it myself. I explained that I am only asking for one day each year where the space we share is clear and comfortable. She was upset by this and said it felt controlling, though she did eventually clean the room again.

Now I am left wondering if I pushed too far. I feel like I asked for something small in a space we both live in, but I also know I went against her wishes in a big way before. Is it wrong to insist on this one yearly reset, or did I cross a line that cannot be justified?
