Did one comment about hair turn my coworker against me?

I am a twenty six year old woman working at a tiny marketing firm with only six people on the team. Because of how small it is, we have all become close over time. We spend long hours together every day, and it is normal for us to grab drinks after work and talk about everything going on in our lives. It has always felt easy and friendly, which is why what happened recently has been so unsettling.

Near the end of last week, I decided to try something different with my hair and came into the office wearing it in two neat braids. I felt good about it and was excited to switch things up. When I walked in, one of my coworkers, who I will call Melissa, immediately looked at me and said she had never noticed how thin my hair was before. Her tone caught me a little off guard, but I was not hurt. My hair has always been fine, and I have never been sensitive about it. I laughed it off and responded in a light way, saying that she had similar hair and that we matched.

The moment the words left my mouth, the atmosphere shifted in a way I did not expect. Her expression hardened instantly, and she snapped at me to leave her alone in a harsh tone that felt completely out of proportion. I stood there confused, trying to understand what I had said wrong, especially since she had made the exact same observation about me first. From that point on, she barely acknowledged me for the rest of the day.

Over the next several days, things only grew more tense. She has continued to avoid me entirely, refusing to speak to me even when work requires it. What has made it worse is hearing from others on the team that she has been talking about me behind my back, painting the situation as if I insulted her out of nowhere. I was even told she plans to bring a complaint to our manager when he returns, claiming I crossed a line at work.

Now I feel stuck in a situation that has spiraled far beyond what I ever intended. One of my coworkers quietly suggested that I apologize just to calm things down, but I cannot shake the feeling that I did nothing wrong, especially since I only echoed what she said to me. At the same time, I can feel the tension spreading through the office, and it is starting to affect everyone. I am left wondering if standing my ground is worth the fallout, or if saying sorry for something I do not believe was wrong is the only way to bring things back to normal.
