Was I Wrong for not allowing family dictate my baby's life before it's even born?

I am engaged to my partner, and we officially committed to each other late last year. In keeping with his cultural traditions, he visited my family home to complete the customary marriage negotiations, and everything went smoothly. At the time, I had already met his sister and had a positive impression of her; she was friendly, welcoming, and seemed genuinely supportive of our relationship.

Shortly after the engagement, I traveled back to the city where my partner lives so we could spend time together. The day after I arrived, his mother invited us to a holiday lunch, and we happily accepted. That same day, however, my partner insisted on taking me to a clinic because I had been dealing with a severe illness that wasn’t improving. During that visit, we unexpectedly discovered that I was already several months pregnant. I hadn’t experienced the typical symptoms, so the news came as a shock, but we were excited and grateful that everything appeared to be healthy.

At the family lunch, we shared the pregnancy news with his mother and sister, who initially reacted with excitement. A few traditional expectations were mentioned, including the idea that the baby’s first name should be chosen by his mother. My partner immediately made it clear that naming decisions would be made by the two of us alone, and the topic was dropped. Later, while helping his sister prepare food, she brought up the idea of a celebration for the baby. I explained that my own siblings and friends might organize something once I shared the news more broadly, and she expressed happiness for me, asking to be included in the planning later on. I agreed, and the conversation ended on what seemed like a good note.

A few weeks later, my partner and I attended a detailed medical appointment to check on the baby’s development. Everything looked good, and we asked the doctor to write the baby’s sex on a piece of paper and seal it in an envelope so we could find out later. We decided to leave the envelope with my older sibling for safekeeping. When we updated our families about the appointment, my side responded calmly and respectfully. His side, however, reacted very differently.

His sister became upset and told him that the envelope should never have gone to my family. She insisted that, as the aunt from his side, she should have been involved and felt deliberately excluded. She then involved their mother, who echoed those feelings and went further by saying she should have been the one to receive the envelope and reveal the information to us. She also reminded my partner that I was joining their family through marriage and implied that decisions about the baby should be discussed with them beforehand rather than made independently.

The situation escalated quickly. His mother stopped speaking to him entirely, blocked him, and told him she was stepping away from his life so he could make his own choices. His sister supported this stance, emphasizing that it had always been the three of them together and suggesting that my partner was choosing me over them.

Now I’m left wondering whether setting boundaries and making decisions about our child as a couple was wrong, or whether the reaction from his family crossed a line before our baby is even born.
