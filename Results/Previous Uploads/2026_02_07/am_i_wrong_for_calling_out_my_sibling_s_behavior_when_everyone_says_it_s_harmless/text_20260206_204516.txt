Am I wrong for calling out my sibling’s behavior when everyone says it’s harmless?

I’m a woman in my late twenties, married to my husband, who’s about the same age. We’ve been together for years and have a solid relationship that I’ve always felt secure in. Until recently, I never had a reason to question anything between us or feel uneasy about our dynamic.

My younger sister, who’s in her mid-twenties, has always been very involved in our lives. She visits often, chats with my husband frequently, and regularly mentions how safe and comfortable she feels around him. For a long time, I didn’t see this as a problem and assumed it was simply because he’s a kind, trustworthy person.

Things shifted when she decided to “be honest” with me and said she feels a very strong emotional connection to him. She stressed that she didn’t mean anything inappropriate and suggested I should take it as a compliment that she trusts him so deeply. Even though she said all the right disclaimers, the conversation left me unsettled in a way I couldn’t shake.

After that, I started noticing patterns I’d previously ignored. She often looks to him for reassurance instead of me, subtly dismisses my opinions, and seems hurt or withdrawn if I interrupt their conversations or try to set boundaries. What once felt like closeness began to feel intrusive and uncomfortable.

Eventually, I told her directly that her behavior was crossing a line for me and that I needed her to respect my marriage. She immediately became defensive and accused me of being insecure, controlling, and projecting my own fears onto her. She said that if I were truly confident, none of this would bother me at all.

When I turned to my parents for perspective, they sided with her. They told me she was just being emotionally honest and that I was turning something innocent into unnecessary drama. They keep pointing out that nothing overt has happened and imply that I’m the one creating tension in the family.

Now I’m questioning myself. I feel deeply uncomfortable, but everyone around me insists that I’m imagining problems that aren’t there. I can’t tell if I’m overreacting or if I’m being made to doubt my own boundaries for the sake of keeping the peace.
