Did I completely ruin a moment with my teen over a TV show?

I was relaxing in the living room one evening, watching a series, when my 15-year-old niece, who lives with me, came in and asked what I was watching. She immediately lit up and told me she had a show I had to see, saying the opening scenes were some of the best television ever made. I agreed to give it a try and started queuing it up, and while it played, she stayed right there, excitedly talking through every moment, pointing out details, explaining characters, and telling me why each scene mattered.

By the time we finished the opening, she asked me to pause it and wanted to know what I thought. I hesitated and said I didn’t really have any original thoughts yet, because I hadn’t had a chance to just watch and process it on my own. I explained that hearing everything in advance made it hard for me to form my own reaction. She immediately got upset, stood up, and walked down the hallway saying I could have at least pretended to care.

I told her that I genuinely wanted to care and that I probably would have enjoyed it even more if I’d been able to experience it quietly first. She shut herself in her room after that, and the whole house felt tense. I wasn’t trying to dismiss her or her interests, but now I’m wondering if I handled the moment badly and hurt her feelings more than I realized.
