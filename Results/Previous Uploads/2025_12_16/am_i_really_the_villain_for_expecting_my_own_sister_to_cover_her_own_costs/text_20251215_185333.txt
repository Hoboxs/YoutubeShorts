Am I Really the Villain for Expecting My Own Sister to Cover Her Own Costs?

I work full time in an office job and recently found myself driving my younger cousin Maya, her teenage son, and a couple of his teammates across state lines to attend a weekend soccer tournament, and while she generously paid for meals for the other kids even after their parents offered to reimburse her, she acted offended when I asked her to chip in for fuel despite the fact that her own car is barely functional and can’t handle long trips, something I brushed off until a second incident a few weeks later when I drove her and her son to a specialist appointment several hours away, knowing ahead of time that parking at the clinic wasn’t free, only for her to casually announce as I pulled into the garage that she hadn’t brought any money at all and expected me to cover it, which turned into a tense exchange where I pointed out how unreasonable it was to show up to an appointment completely unprepared and insisted she figure it out herself, something she eventually did while making it very clear how “stressful” I was being, and this pattern has started to feel deliberate given that she doesn’t work by choice despite being fully capable of taking a part-time retail job that wouldn’t affect her government assistance, leaving me to wonder if my stable income and lack of children has quietly turned me into her default financial safety net, especially after years of covering emergencies, repairs, and travel costs that never seem to end.
