Did I Completely Ruin a New Friendship by Leaning Too Hard at the Worst Moment?

I’m a woman in my mid-twenties who moved countries for work last year and quickly formed an intense friendship with a colleague a few years older than me, someone confident and outspoken who felt like the opposite of my people-pleasing, anxiety-ridden self, and when my long-term relationship collapsed right in the middle of the move, she became my emotional anchor, the person I talked to for hours, who listened to every detail, gave blunt wake-up calls, and made me feel less alone while I spent weeks coexisting with my ex in the same house, waiting for him to finally leave the country; the night I dropped him off for good, I went straight to her place, where we laughed, drank, and talked late into the night, and she even gave me a handwritten letter telling me she believed in me and that I’d be okay, which made the crash that came later feel even worse, because when I returned to my now-empty home and texted her about one small, lingering annoyance related to my ex, she abruptly told me she couldn’t engage anymore and that I was sharing too much, a sentence that hit like a punch to the chest because my ex had ended our relationship by saying I was “too much,” sending me into a panic spiral where I couldn’t breathe and immediately feared I was about to lose her too; she explained she’d nearly destroyed another close friendship by letting emotional dumping go on too long and was trying to prevent that here, and although I understood logically, emotionally I was shattered, so I asked if we could talk the next day, then sent a careful, apologetic message acknowledging I hadn’t realized how much pressure I’d put on her and saying I hoped we could still be close, only to be met with silence, days of being left on read while upcoming group dinners, work friendships, and shared travel plans loomed in the background, leaving me stuck replaying how just hours earlier she was cheering me on and now I was alone again, wondering if leaning too hard in the exact moment my life cracked open cost me the one friendship that made the breakup survivable.
