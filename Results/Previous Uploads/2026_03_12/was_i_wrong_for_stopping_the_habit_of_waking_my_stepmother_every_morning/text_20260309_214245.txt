Was I wrong for stopping the habit of waking my stepmother every morning?

I’m 17 and for the last few years I’ve usually been awake around 6 in the morning. Most mornings my younger brothers get up around then to get ready for school, and the house is already noisy, so it just became my routine.

But this year, during my junior year, I realized I really don’t need to be up that early. My schedule allows me to sleep a bit longer, and I wanted to move my alarm to a time that actually makes sense for me.

The issue is my stepmother, who is 36. She’s the one responsible for driving my brothers to school, but she constantly sleeps through her alarm. Because I was already awake, I started waking her up just to make sure she got up on time. At first I didn’t mind, I was just trying to be helpful.

But it kept happening. Several times a week I’d have to go wake her up so she wouldn’t oversleep. After a few months I got tired of it. I told her directly that waking her up in the morning shouldn’t be my responsibility. I’m the teenager and she’s the adult.

She didn’t take that well. She called me disrespectful and took away some of my devices as punishment.

Eventually I just stopped arguing and changed my alarm so I’d wake up later. Right now it’s set for 6 30, but I’m planning to push it back to 7 since that works better for me.

This morning she slept through her alarm again. Since I wasn’t awake to wake her up, she ended up getting up late and the kids were about 30 minutes late to school.

She immediately blamed me. When I told her I had changed my alarm time, she said something like, “Oh, so you just don’t care anymore.”

From my point of view, she’s the adult and it’s her responsibility to wake up on time if she’s the one in charge of getting the kids to school.

Was I wrong for deciding to stop waking her up every morning and letting her deal with the consequences?
