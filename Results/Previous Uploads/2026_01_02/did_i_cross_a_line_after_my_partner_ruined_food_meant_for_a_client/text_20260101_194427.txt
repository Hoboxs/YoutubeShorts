Did I Cross a Line After My Partner Ruined Food Meant for a Client?

I’m a 27-year-old guy, and my younger brother, who’s 24, runs a small café that specializes in custom desserts for events. Lately, he’s been overwhelmed because two staff members quit right before peak wedding season. To help him stay afloat, I offered to prep some fillings at home—things like pastry cream and fruit preserves—and bring them over so he could focus on assembling and decorating cakes for paying clients.

The problem is my partner, who’s 25. Every single time I’ve made these fillings, she’s dipped her bare fingers straight into the containers to “taste them.” Not once or twice, but repeatedly. Each time it happens, the entire batch becomes unusable, which means I have to throw it out and start over. I’ve explained calmly that this food is for customers, not our kitchen table, and that if she really wants a taste, she can use a clean spoon and take some from the side. She brushes it off, says using her fingers feels nostalgic, and sulks like I’m ruining something sentimental for her.

Earlier today, things came to a head. I was rushing to finish a chocolate custard because my brother was late on a large wedding order and stressed out of his mind. Before I jumped in the shower, I explicitly told my partner not to touch it, and if she absolutely couldn’t resist, to please use a utensil. When I came back, I saw her standing at the counter with her fingers in the bowl again, licking them like nothing I’d said mattered.

I snapped. After the fifth ruined batch, I lost my temper and yelled for her to stop eating the food I was making, because it wasn’t for her and she was ruining something meant for other people. She immediately burst into tears and accused me of shaming her and attacking her, even though I never mentioned her body or eating habits—only the fact that she was contaminating food that wasn’t hers to touch.

I know I raised my voice, and I hate that it escalated like that, but I also feel like my boundaries and the pressure I was under were completely ignored. Was I wrong for reacting the way I did after being pushed over the same line again and again?
