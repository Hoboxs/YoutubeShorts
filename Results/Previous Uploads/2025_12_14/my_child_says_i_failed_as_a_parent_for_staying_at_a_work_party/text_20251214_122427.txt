My Child Says I Failed as a Parent for Staying at a Work Party

I’m a woman in my late thirties, and I became a parent very young, which meant my oldest child and I grew up almost side by side rather than in clearly defined roles. Because of that, our relationship has always been intense, close, and emotionally charged. We live in a small city outside the capital, and I’m raising several kids while working full time, so my entire life revolves around schedules, responsibilities, and everyone else’s needs. Any time I do something just for myself, even something small and harmless, it tends to create tension, especially with my oldest, who takes it personally when I’m not home or not available.

Last night I attended a company holiday dinner that had been planned for weeks. Earlier that day, my oldest helped me pick out an outfit, joked with me about how fancy I looked, and acted genuinely supportive, which made me feel like we were in a good place. My younger kids were staying with relatives overnight, so there was no childcare issue at all. Before I left, I was asked when I’d be home, and I said I wasn’t sure whether I’d leave early but that the event officially wrapped up around ten. I checked my phone throughout the evening, made sure there were no urgent messages, and enjoyed myself for once. When the dinner ended a few minutes after the scheduled time, I looked at my phone and saw multiple missed calls within the span of two minutes, followed immediately by another incoming call.

As soon as I answered, I was met with anger and accusations. I was told I had lied about coming home early, that I should have been on my way already, and that I was failing in my role because something terrible could have happened while I was gone. No explanation seemed to matter, not the fact that the event had just ended or that I never promised an early return. Since then, I’ve been given the cold shoulder, and every attempt to smooth things over has only made things worse. Now I’m left wondering if I’m expected to never go out, never relax, and never exist outside of being a parent, and whether taking a few extra minutes for myself somehow crossed an unforgivable line.
