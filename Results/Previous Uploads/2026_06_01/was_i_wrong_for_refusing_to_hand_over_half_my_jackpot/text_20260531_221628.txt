Was I Wrong for Refusing to Hand Over Half My Jackpot?

A few months ago, I was stunned to discover that I had won the equivalent of about $6,800,000 in a national lottery drawing. I am a 21-year-old software analyst, and up until that moment, my biggest financial concern was paying rent and building a small emergency fund. After claiming the prize, I met with financial advisors and invested most of the money across several diversified funds and long-term investments rather than spending it right away.

Naturally, I was excited and shared the news with my family after keeping it to myself for a little over a week. My parents, who are both in their early fifties, were thrilled at first. We have always had a close relationship, so I expected the conversation to be full of celebration and support.

Instead, things took an unexpected turn. Within a few days, they began discussing the money as though it already belonged to all of us collectively. They talked about leaving their jobs immediately, purchasing a vacation property on the coast, upgrading their vehicles, taking extended trips around the world, and completely changing their lifestyle.

Curious, I eventually asked how much of the winnings they expected to receive. Without hesitation, they told me they assumed they would be getting half.

I was honestly shocked. While I understand that even after giving away half I would still be financially secure, I never felt that they were automatically entitled to such a large portion of something that was awarded solely to me. Before that conversation, I had already planned to give them a substantial gift as a way of thanking them for everything they had done while raising me. My idea was to give them roughly $1,200,000.

When I presented that amount, expecting them to be pleased, they reacted badly. They accused me of being selfish and ungrateful. According to them, I was failing to appreciate everything they had sacrificed over the years. They rejected the gift outright and insisted that the original amount they wanted was the only fair option.

For context, my parents are not struggling financially. They own their home outright, have retirement savings, and live comfortably. That is part of why I believed my offer was generous. To me, receiving over a million dollars with no strings attached would dramatically improve most people's lives.

The disagreement has continued for weeks. On one hand, I care about my parents and still want to share some of my good fortune with them. On the other hand, their expectation that they deserve such a large percentage of the winnings has left a bitter taste in my mouth. The more they push, the less comfortable I feel.

Now I am wondering whether I am being unreasonable for refusing to give them half of the money, or whether their expectation crossed a line.
