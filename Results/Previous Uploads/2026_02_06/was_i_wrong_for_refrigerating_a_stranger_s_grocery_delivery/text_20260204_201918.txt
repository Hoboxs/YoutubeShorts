Was I wrong for refrigerating a stranger’s grocery delivery?

After recently moving into a new apartment, I found several bags of groceries left outside my door that clearly were not mine. The order included frozen and refrigerated items, and I had no idea who they belonged to or how long they had been sitting there. Because I did not want the food to spoil, I decided to put everything into my refrigerator and freezer for safekeeping. I kept all of it inside the original delivery bags with the tags still attached and did not open or use anything. At the time, I assumed the previous tenant may have forgotten to update their address, and for all I knew they could have moved far away or might not realize the mistake for days.

A few hours later, a woman knocked on my door and showed me a photo on her phone of the groceries sitting outside my unit. Relieved that the owner had appeared, I went to retrieve the bags for her, but instead of thanking me she immediately accused me of stealing her food. She raised her voice, called me inconsiderate, and insisted that I should never touch someone else’s delivery. I tried to calmly explain that I had no way of knowing who the food belonged to or when they would come looking for it, and that I had only stored it to prevent waste. None of that seemed to matter to her, and she continued to scold me, telling me that next time I should leave the food outside even if it spoiled.

I live in an apartment complex where temperatures fluctuate a lot, especially in winter, so leaving perishables outdoors did not seem safe or reasonable to me. I also considered contacting the delivery company, but since I was not the one who placed the order, I doubted they could help much in the moment. I genuinely thought holding onto the groceries temporarily was the most responsible option. Now I am left wondering whether my logic was flawed, or if I was simply trying to do the right thing and became an easy target for someone else’s frustration.
