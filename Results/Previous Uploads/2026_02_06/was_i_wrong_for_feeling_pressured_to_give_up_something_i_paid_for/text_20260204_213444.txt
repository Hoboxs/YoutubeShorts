Was I wrong for feeling pressured to give up something I paid for?

Years ago, I bought a used motorcycle with my own money, spending a few thousand dollars after saving up for a long time. My partner at the time came along with me to look it over and talk with the seller. During the conversation, the seller casually mentioned that she had recently upgraded a part on the bike. My partner immediately asked if she still had the original part. She did, and he then asked if she would include it with the bike. She agreed, so when I paid for the motorcycle, it also came with that extra component, which was worth a few hundred dollars on its own.

Once we got back home, my partner took the extra part and started photographing it so he could list it online for sale. I told him I thought it was smart of him to think to ask for it and said I would be happy to split the money from the sale with him. He flatly refused and said the part was entirely his, arguing that we would not have it at all if he had not thought to ask for it in the first place. I disagreed and said that while the idea was his, the item only came with the purchase because I paid for the motorcycle. Still, I wanted to be fair, so I suggested we split the proceeds evenly.

What followed was a big argument. He insisted he was entitled to all the money, and I felt increasingly pressured and worn down by the conflict. Eventually, I gave in and let him keep the part and all the money from selling it, which amounted to a few hundred dollars. Even at the time, I had an uneasy feeling that I had been pushed into agreeing just to keep the peace.

Looking back, I can understand his argument to a degree, since the idea came from him, but I have always felt that I was bullied into giving up something that was included in a purchase I made with my own money. We are no longer together, and this happened a long time ago, but it has always stood out to me as one of those situations where I wondered whether I was being unreasonable or whether my instincts about the situation were right all along.
