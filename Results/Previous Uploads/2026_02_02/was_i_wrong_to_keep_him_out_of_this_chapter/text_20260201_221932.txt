Was I Wrong to Keep Him Out of This Chapter?

I’m a woman in my early thirties who was in a two-year relationship with a man slightly older than me, and the relationship itself was often tense and emotionally draining. Near the end, we had a particularly bad weekend after he became convinced that a stranger was flirting with me while we were out running errands. Because of that, he completely shut me out and refused to speak to me for days. We weren’t living together full-time at that point, and during that same weekend I ended up in the hospital after learning I was both expecting and losing that pregnancy at the same time.

I tried calling him from the hospital, but he ignored my calls. A close friend of mine, who lives in another state, managed to get through to him and explained what was happening. Even after hearing that, he didn’t come to the hospital, didn’t check on me, and didn’t reach out at all. I didn’t hear a word from him until several days later. What hurt most was that he never apologized, and when it eventually came up, he brushed it off by saying the situation had resolved itself. I couldn’t move past that, and we ended the relationship not long after.

About a year later, he contacted me again out of the blue. He said he wanted to talk about what happened back then and asked to see the medical imaging from the hospital visit. I told him no, explaining that because of how he behaved during that time, I didn’t feel he had any right to more information or to see those images. He reacted badly, lashing out and calling me names.

Now I’m left wondering if refusing his request was justified, or if I crossed a line by shutting him out completely after everything that happened.
