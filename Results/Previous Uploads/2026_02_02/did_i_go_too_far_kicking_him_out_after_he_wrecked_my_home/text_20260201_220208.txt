Did I Go Too Far Kicking Him Out After He Wrecked My Home?

A couple of nights ago, while I was out in the city with my partner, I got a call from a nearby resident letting me know there were several unfamiliar cars parked outside my place and loud music coming from inside. Confused and immediately irritated, I asked them to knock and see what was going on. They were told it was a party, which made my blood boil, because I had already been very clear that no gatherings were allowed without my explicit approval.

For some context, my sibling had recently split from his long-term partner and needed a temporary place to stay. I agreed to let him move into a spare room while he saved up from his job to get back on his feet. In exchange, we set a few basic expectations: help keep the house clean, pitch in where needed, and respect the rules of the home. One of those rules was absolutely no parties unless I said yes.

As soon as I heard what was happening, I headed straight home. When I walked in, the music was still blasting, the house was a mess, and there were drunk strangers everywhere. Cups were scattered across the floors, alcohol had been spilled, and it was clear the place had been completely disrespected. I shut the party down immediately and told everyone to leave. After they cleared out, my sibling and I got into a heated argument that escalated fast. In the end, I told him to pack up and leave that night.

The damage was obvious once things were quiet. The living area was trashed, there was broken glass on the floor, and sticky alcohol stains everywhere. The following day, he called me and started another fight, calling me names and accusing me of being heartless and unreasonable for throwing him out so late.

Now I’m stuck wondering whether I crossed a line by kicking him out immediately, even though he ignored my rules and left my home in ruins. Was I justified in drawing that hard boundary, or did I take it too far?
