When Helping a Coworker Backfires at Work, Was I Wrong to Say No?

I work on a small team, and a newer colleague, let’s call him Marcus, joined recently. Our desks are close and our responsibilities overlap, so I’ve answered questions and helped him get up to speed. Over time, I developed my own reporting system and layout that makes a recurring task faster and cleaner. It’s something I refined gradually and rely on heavily.

A while back, Marcus asked how I put my report together and whether I could send him my template. I told him I was happy to explain my process and walk him through it, but I wasn’t comfortable handing over my exact file. Instead, I stayed late one evening and showed him everything step by step, where the data comes from, the order I do things, what details matter, and what to avoid. He took notes, thanked me, and seemed appreciative.

At the next team meeting, Marcus presented his report for the first time. As he talked, it became obvious that the structure, phrasing, and flow were nearly identical to mine, even down to a couple expressions I usually use. When our supervisor asked how he built it, Marcus brushed it off as something he put together himself, without mentioning any help.

After the meeting, I privately told him that I was glad it went well but felt uncomfortable hearing my exact setup presented with no acknowledgment. He got defensive and said he didn’t owe anyone credit for assistance, then accused me of being insecure for bringing it up.

Since then, he’s repeatedly asked for the actual template again, saying it would save time and that he already understands it. I’ve said no, but told him I’d still answer questions if he gets stuck. He’s been distant ever since, and I’ve heard through others that he’s calling me a gatekeeper and saying I’m making things difficult. A few coworkers think I should just send the file to keep the peace because it’s a collaborative workplace.

I don’t want to cause tension, but I also feel like I already went out of my way to help and don’t trust how my work would be handled if I handed it over. Am I wrong for holding that boundary?
