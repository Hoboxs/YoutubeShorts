Is it unreasonable to withdraw support when wedding rules are ignored?

My spouse and I have always been very clear with our children about one thing. If we were going to pay for their weddings, there were a few basic expectations attached. These expectations were shared with all of them years ago, long before any engagements. The guidelines were simple in our minds. All family members were to be invited unless there had been a serious falling out. The wedding could not require guests to travel far or leave the country. Immediate family members were always allowed a guest, even if they were single. None of our children objected to these terms at the time, and our two older children followed them without issue, which is why we paid for their weddings in full.

Our youngest daughter, who is in her early thirties, is now planning her wedding to her partner. They recently secured a venue and started working through their guest list. During one of our conversations, my daughter casually mentioned that they were planning a child free wedding. I immediately told her that this directly conflicted with what we had always said. In our family, children are part of the family, and excluding them meant not inviting everyone.

We sat down to talk it through. She explained that she wanted a very refined, formal event and felt that children would disrupt the atmosphere. Her partner also shared that she has complicated feelings about kids due to how she was raised and does not enjoy being around them. I listened and told them I understood their perspective, but I also reminded them that I had been upfront about the conditions tied to my financial support. I told them I would take some time to think about it.

After giving it real consideration, I told them my answer was no. I made it clear they were absolutely free to have the wedding they wanted, including making it child free, but that I would not be paying for it under those circumstances. That decision did not go over well. Both of them were extremely upset, and my daughter accused me of punishing her and treating her unfairly compared to her siblings.

I explained that her siblings received financial support because they followed the same expectations. Nothing about this was new or sprung on her at the last minute. Now emotions are high across the board, extended family members are weighing in, and everyone seems angry at once. I am left wondering whether standing by boundaries that were clearly communicated years ago makes me unreasonable, or whether it is fair to step back when those terms are no longer being respected.
