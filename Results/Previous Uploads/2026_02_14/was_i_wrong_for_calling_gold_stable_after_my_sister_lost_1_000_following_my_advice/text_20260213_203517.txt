Was I wrong for calling gold “stable” after my sister lost $1,000 following my advice?

I’m 19M and have been trading for a few months. I’m up around 15%, nothing crazy, but decent for starting out. My sister (22F) noticed and asked what she should do with her savings.

I told her she could look into gold since it’s generally considered more stable and less volatile than a lot of stocks or crypto. I didn’t tell her to “go all in,” just said it’s usually seen as a safer investment compared to higher-risk assets.

She bought gold on January 22. The price ticked up a bit, so she felt confident and bought more on January 27. In total, she put in $10,000.

Right after that, the price dropped — around 10% in about a week. She panicked because $10k is a lot of money for her. On February 1, she sold everything, locking in a $1,000 loss. Not long after she sold, the price rebounded and is now higher than when she originally bought.

Now she wants me to pay her back the $1,000. She says I misled her by describing gold as “stable” when it dropped that much in a short period. She claims she wouldn’t have invested so heavily if I hadn’t made it sound low-risk.

I told her that “stable” doesn’t mean “never goes down,” and that markets fluctuate. I also pointed out that she made the decision to sell during a dip rather than holding. I didn’t force her to invest $10k, and I definitely didn’t tell her to panic-sell.

My mom, though, thinks I’m in the wrong for giving financial advice when I’m not qualified, and says it’s partially my fault she lost money.

So now I’m stuck wondering: was I irresponsible for calling gold stable and giving input at all, or is she responsible for her own investment decisions and panic-selling?
