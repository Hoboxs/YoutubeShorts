Was I wrong for not letting a woman with two items cut in front of me at the grocery store?

Earlier today, I was at a packed grocery store. Only two registers were open, and the lines were long. I had a full cart and had already been waiting for about 15 minutes by the time I finally reached the conveyor belt.

Right as I started unloading my groceries, a woman walked up holding just a carton of eggs and a gallon of milk. She asked if she could go ahead of me because she was “in a huge rush” and only had two items.

Normally, I might not have cared. But I was exhausted, my back was hurting, and I’d already waited my turn like everyone else. So I told her, “Sorry, but I’ve been waiting here for 15 minutes and I just want to get home.”

She immediately looked offended and started complaining loudly to the people behind me, saying things like “Some people are just miserable,” and that it would’ve taken her “30 seconds.” To my surprise, the guy directly behind me actually chimed in and called me a jerk for not being “neighborly.”

That caught me off guard. I didn’t insult her. I didn’t snap. I just said no.

From my perspective, the whole point of a line is that everyone waits their turn, regardless of how many items they have. Stores even create express lanes for small purchases — this wasn’t one of them. If I let her go, what happens if the next person also has three items? Or four? Where’s the line?

At the same time, I know it would have been a quick transaction, and maybe it would have been the kinder move. The public reaction made me second-guess myself.

So now I’m wondering, was I standing on principle, or was I just being unnecessarily stubborn?
