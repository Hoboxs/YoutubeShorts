Did I betray my wife by siding with my mother at the worst moment?

I am sitting in a crowded airport, staring at the departure board, and I cannot shake the feeling that I may have made a terrible mistake. Earlier this week, my daughter, who is just entering her early teen years, lost a classmate. It was sudden, and it shook her in a way I have never seen before. This is the first time she has had to face death up close, and it has completely unraveled her. The school dismissed the entire class for the day so they could attend the funeral, which is happening right now, and I am not there. I tried everything I could to stay, but my request for time off was denied, and we cannot afford for me to lose this job.

I had to leave midweek for work, and as I sit here waiting to return home, I keep replaying everything that happened. My daughter was very close with the girl who passed away, and we had agreed that my wife, who is currently not working, would take her to the service and simply be there for her. It seemed straightforward at the time, even though I knew it would not be easy for my wife.

She has always had a deep fear of anything related to death. Funerals, memorials, even discussions about the afterlife make her uneasy. She believes strongly in spirits and avoids these situations whenever she can. Still, we talked it through before I left, and she assured me she would take our daughter to the ceremony and stay long enough to support her.

Then everything fell apart.

I received a call from my daughter, and she was sobbing so hard I could barely understand her. She told me she was not going to the funeral because her mother refused to take her. When I finally reached my wife, she sounded panicked and said she could not bring herself to go, that she was too afraid. Hearing that, something in me snapped. I immediately called my mother and asked her to step in. Without hesitation, she left her own responsibilities behind, picked up my daughter, and took her to the service. As far as I know, they are there together right now.

Not long after, my wife called me back, crying. She said my mother had been harsh with her, accusing her of failing as a parent and saying it was unacceptable to refuse something so important for our child. My wife wanted me to demand an apology from my mother, but instead, I told her that my mother was right.

I told her that our daughter needed support today more than ever, and that fear could not come before that. I said that being a parent sometimes means facing things that terrify you, and that she had let our daughter down when she needed her most. The conversation quickly turned into a heated argument, with both of us saying things we cannot take back.

Now I am stuck here, miles away, wondering if I handled it all wrong. My mother has agreed to keep things calm and not let our daughter sense the tension between us, and I am grateful for that. But the damage between my wife and me feels very real, and I cannot tell if I stood up for my daughter the way I should have, or if I crossed a line that will be difficult to repair.
