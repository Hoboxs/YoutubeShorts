Can I Name My Daughter Something My Sister Hates?

I’m 32 and seven months pregnant with a baby girl. My husband, 33, and I finally agreed on the name “Margot.” It felt classic, elegant, and perfect for her.

At my baby shower, I excitedly shared the name with family. My younger sister immediately started criticizing it. She claimed it was “old-fashioned” and insisted our daughter would get teased for having a name like that. Ironically, she once tried to name her own daughter something unusual and complicated, and her husband overruled her.

Her comments didn’t stop there. She continued making snide remarks during the party and later bombarded me with messages on social media, demanding that we change the name. After a while, we asked her to leave the shower, and I blocked her on social media to prevent further harassment.

Margot feels perfect for our daughter, and I don’t want to change it. But my sister keeps insisting it’s a terrible choice. Am I wrong for ignoring her opinions and keeping the name we love?
