Did I Ruin Christmas by Locking the Kids’ Biggest Gift Before It Was Even Opened?

My son Evan works long rotations on international cargo vessels, which means he’s gone for weeks at a time and often unreachable. He’s married to Lena, and together they have two young children. The last couple of years have been brutal for them. Lena lost her job and hasn’t been able to find anything that fits around childcare while Evan is away, and the financial pressure has clearly taken a toll on the household.

Over time, I started noticing a pattern that made my stomach drop. Whenever money got tight, the kids’ belongings would quietly disappear. Not toys they’d outgrown or broken things, but newer, expensive gifts. A console game I bought for my granddaughter was sold online less than two weeks after she opened it, supposedly because she was “playing too intensely,” though Lena couldn’t explain what that meant. What made it worse was seeing that, despite these sacrifices, Lena still found money for regular personal splurges, which made the explanations feel hollow.

As Christmas approached, Lena asked if I could buy the kids two tablets. They weren’t cheap, and the moment she asked, I felt a knot in my chest. I imagined the kids tearing open the wrapping paper, their excitement lighting up the room, only for those tablets to quietly vanish a month later under the excuse of “discipline” or “necessity.” I didn’t want to disappoint the kids, but I also couldn’t stomach the idea of pouring that much money into something I believed wouldn’t stay with them.

So I made a decision that I knew might cause conflict. I bought the tablets, set them up myself, and locked the system settings with a passcode only I know. The kids can use them freely, but they can’t be wiped, reset, or sold. When I called Lena to tell her the gifts were bought and explained the passcode, the conversation exploded almost instantly. She accused me of spying on her, of treating her like a thief, of deliberately humiliating her right before Christmas. Within hours, my phone was blowing up with messages saying I’d crossed a line, undermined her authority, and poisoned the holiday spirit.

Now Christmas is days away, tensions are sky-high, and I’m left wondering if protecting the kids’ gifts was worth turning a fragile family situation into an outright war.
