Did I ruin dinner by reacting badly at a hibachi restaurant?

My husband and I have been together for more than two decades, and lately life has been stressful in ways I do not think he fully understands. He has been out of work for a while, so financially everything rests on my shoulders right now. Usually I can handle that pressure, but last week at work was brutal.

I got pulled into a situation involving mistakes made by the person before me, and for several days I genuinely thought I might lose my job over it. There were nonstop meetings, endless calls, and constant emails trying to sort everything out. Even though the issue was eventually cleared up, by Friday night I felt emotionally wrung out and exhausted.

All I wanted was a quiet evening at home.

My husband, however, was determined to go to his favorite hibachi restaurant. I told him repeatedly that I was tired and not really in the mood to go anywhere loud or social, but he kept insisting it would help me relax and that all I had to do was sit there and eat. Eventually I gave in because I did not want to argue anymore.

The restaurant was packed and incredibly noisy. Since it was just the two of us, we were seated quickly at a grill with a large, energetic group already there. They were laughing loudly, joking constantly, and clearly ready for a big night out. I already felt out of place before the cooking even started.

When the chef came over, he immediately looked at me and asked whether I was okay. I politely said yes. A few minutes later he asked again, saying I did not look well. I smiled as best I could and reassured him I was fine.

Then he kept going.

He started asking if I was angry. He joked dramatically about whether I was secretly upset with him. The table laughed, and I tried to laugh lightly too even though I was becoming increasingly uncomfortable. I know I naturally have a serious expression when I am tired, and I suddenly became hyper aware that apparently everyone at the table was now focused on my face.

Then one of the women in the group chimed in and said something beginning with, “Do not worry about her, she is just…”

Right at that moment there was a burst of noise around the restaurant, and I genuinely did not hear the rest of the sentence. But whatever she said caused the chef and several people at the table to laugh while looking directly at me.

That was the moment everything hit me emotionally.

After the week I had just survived, sitting there feeling singled out and laughed at by strangers was suddenly too much. I felt myself getting embarrassed and emotional, and I quietly told my husband I wanted my food packed up because I was going to wait in the car.

Instead of supporting me, he became irritated immediately and told me to stop making a scene and sit back down. I told him I felt like people were mocking me. He insisted nobody was making fun of me and acted like I was overreacting.

I ended up staying, but the atmosphere at the table completely changed afterward. Everyone became quiet and awkward. The loud joking stopped almost entirely, people boxed their food quickly, and the whole dinner fizzled out in uncomfortable silence.

On the drive home, my husband blamed me for ruining the entire evening. I kept asking him what exactly the woman had said because if I misunderstood things, I genuinely wanted to know. Eventually he admitted he had not actually heard her either. But he still insisted none of it justified my reaction.

Now I keep replaying the whole night wondering if I was simply too emotionally drained to handle harmless teasing, or if I had every right to feel humiliated after becoming the center of attention in a room full of strangers when I was already hanging on by a thread emotionally.
