Was I wrong for saying my dad acts like he has a permanent period?

I ended up in an argument with my dad after a family dinner, and I genuinely thought we were all joking around until he later told me I humiliated him in front of everyone.

We were having dinner with family friends, and the conversation somehow drifted into the usual “men versus women” type jokes. My dad started talking with his friend about how difficult it is living in a house full of women. He kept making comments about women becoming impossible during their periods and how the entire mood of the house changes every month because of it.

The thing is, he says stuff like this pretty often.

He was laughing while saying it, and his friend was laughing too, talking about how grateful he was to have sons instead of daughters. Meanwhile my sisters and I were just sitting there listening to them act like women become unbearable monsters for a week every month.

What annoyed me most was how exaggerated it all was. My sisters and I are honestly pretty calm people. We all played sports growing up, handled school, jobs, stress, and normal life without dramatic meltdowns. If anything, most of the tension in our house over the years usually came from my dad getting irritated over small things and then blaming everyone else for reacting to him.

So after listening to him go on for several minutes, I joked back that honestly having a son exactly like him would probably be worse because he is already moody and irritable all the time anyway. I said it was basically like he has a permanent period himself.

The entire table laughed immediately, including his friend and his friend’s wife. Then the wife jokingly turned to her own husband and said he acts the same way too.

At the time it genuinely felt like harmless back and forth. Nobody seemed uncomfortable. Nobody went silent. It felt like the exact same energy my dad had been using the entire conversation.

But later that night my dad pulled me aside and said I embarrassed him badly in front of his friend. He said it was disrespectful to compare a man to having a period and that I made him look weak and unmanly in front of people he cares about impressing.

That honestly surprised me because from my perspective, he had spent the entire dinner making broad jokes about women being emotional and difficult because of hormones. I felt like I was simply throwing the same kind of joke back at him.

Now he is acting like I crossed some major line, while I still feel like if someone dishes out those jokes constantly, they should be able to handle one aimed back at them too.
