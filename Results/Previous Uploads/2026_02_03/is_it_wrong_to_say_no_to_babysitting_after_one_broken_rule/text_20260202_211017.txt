Is It Wrong to Say No to Babysitting After One Broken Rule?

I have two young children, with my oldest just out of toddlerhood. For a long time, that child was extremely attached to a comfort item we eventually had to eliminate completely. It wasn’t a gentle process either. We went through weeks of disrupted sleep, stress, and setbacks before finally getting to a place where the habit was fully gone. It took months before there were no relapses at all, and even then it only worked because every adult in our child’s life followed the same rules without exception.

After we welcomed our second baby, things were surprisingly smooth at first. Our older child understood that the comfort items belonged to the baby and even helped pass them over when needed, without trying to use them personally. That balance fell apart after a family member watched the kids one afternoon. When we came home, we found our older child asleep using one of those items again. According to the babysitter, the child had been crying and she didn’t want to let that continue, so she used the quickest solution.

That single decision completely unraveled months of progress. Since then, we’ve been dealing with daily struggles: hiding items around the house, stopping our older child from taking them from the baby, and managing intense meltdowns whenever access is denied. It’s exhausting, and every episode feels like reopening a wound we worked incredibly hard to close.

Now, the same family member wants to watch the kids again. My partner and I decided we’re not comfortable with that, given what happened and the ongoing fallout. She’s taken this badly and has told other relatives and friends that we’re being unfair and punishing her for trying to calm a distressed child. We’ve stood firm, but the tension is growing and I’m starting to wonder if we’re being too rigid.

From our perspective, this wasn’t a small mistake—it had lasting consequences we’re still dealing with. But I’m curious whether others would see our refusal as unreasonable or overly harsh.
