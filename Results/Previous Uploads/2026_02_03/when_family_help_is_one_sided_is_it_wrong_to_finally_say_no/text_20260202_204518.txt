When Family Help Is One-Sided, Is It Wrong to Finally Say No?

I’m the oldest sibling in my family, and for as long as I can remember, I’ve been the one picking up the slack for my younger sister. When she was in university, I helped her revise papers and talked her through personal problems. If she got stranded somewhere, I’d rearrange my schedule to get her home. Over time, it became clear that this dynamic wasn’t mutual. She’s in her mid-twenties now, and both another sibling and I have noticed that she rarely goes out of her way to help anyone else in the family. I brought this up to her once, and she said she understood and would try to do better.

Not long ago, she asked if she could host her engagement celebration at my home. I agreed, even though it was a big ask, and I helped with planning details as well. Around the same time, I was juggling work, parenting, and logistics. One evening, while driving home, traffic came to a complete standstill due to a major accident. What should have been a short drive turned into several hours. My partner was also stuck elsewhere, and I started getting calls from my kids’ childcare provider asking why I hadn’t arrived yet. They charge steep late fees, and it was clear I wasn’t getting there anytime soon.

My sister lives very close to the childcare center, so I called her and asked if she could pick the kids up and keep them with her for a few hours until we made it back. I explained the situation and even offered to send her money to cover any inconvenience. She flatly refused, saying she wanted a quiet evening to herself, and then ended the call. I was left scrambling and eventually reached out to a relative who lives much farther away. They drove over, picked up the kids, and stayed at our place until we finally got home late that night.

That incident really stuck with me. I felt hurt and angry, especially given how often I’ve gone out of my way for her in the past. After talking it through with my partner, we decided we were done continuing a one-sided pattern. I messaged my sister and told her that family support should go both ways, and that it was clear she only wanted help, not to offer it. I also told her I would no longer be hosting her engagement event or doing favors for her.

She called after that, and the conversation quickly turned into a heated argument. I told her she was acting selfishly, and she accused me of being petty and overreacting. I’m still upset about the whole situation and genuinely want to know—was I wrong for finally drawing a hard line?
