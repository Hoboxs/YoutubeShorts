When does protecting someone’s routine cross into ignoring real pain?

I am a teenage girl living at home with my family, including my younger brother who is autistic. He has a very strict nighttime routine where he comes downstairs late, watches television alone in the living room, and eventually falls asleep on the couch. My parents have always treated this routine as non negotiable because disrupting it usually leads to meltdowns and conflict, so everyone else is expected to work around it.

Over the past year my physical health has gotten worse. I have a connective tissue condition that causes joint instability along with a curved spine, which leads to constant shoulder and upper back pain. My mattress is extremely old and was passed down through siblings before me, and the padding is completely worn out. I can feel the metal frame through it no matter how I lie. Spending too much time in bed causes sharp pain and stiffness, and I often wake up feeling worse than when I went to sleep. I also need a long period to wind down at night, which makes lying on that mattress even more painful.

To manage this, on nights when the pain becomes overwhelming, I go downstairs to sit on the couch and watch television for a while before going to bed. This helps reduce how long I am stuck on the mattress and actually makes a noticeable difference in my pain levels. I do not do this every night, only when the discomfort has built up too much. I also do not try to force my brother out of the room. I simply ask that if he stays, he uses headphones or keeps his volume low so we can both exist in the space.

Recently, my brother has started taking issue with this and complaining to my parents. Most of the time they immediately side with him, saying that his routine should not be interrupted. When I explain that I am dealing with real physical pain, it is brushed off as me exaggerating, even though these conditions run in the family. One night, after I warned him ahead of time that I would be downstairs, he went upstairs to yell at our parents. When he came back down, he was angry and verbally aggressive toward me and deliberately played videos at full volume to cause trouble.

What makes this harder is that he has other options. He has a computer and full access to entertainment in his own room. I also technically have a television in my bedroom, but using it means sitting or lying on the bed that is actively causing my pain. Despite this, the expectation is still that I should be the one to move, adjust, and endure the discomfort so his routine remains untouched.

I feel guilty for upsetting the balance in the house, but I also feel like my health is constantly being dismissed. It feels like I am expected to suffer quietly because it is easier for everyone else. I am not trying to take anything away from him, I am just trying to manage my pain in the only way that helps.
