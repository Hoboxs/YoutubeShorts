Is it unfair to suggest medical help when quiet solutions aren’t working?

I live in a shared house with several other adults and a handful of pets, and overall it’s a pretty loving, low-drama environment. I don’t believe anyone here is intentionally trying to upset anyone else, but lately there’s been growing tension around sleep, and I’m starting to wonder if I crossed a line.

One of my housemates is currently a student and is an extremely light sleeper. For months now, she’s been getting woken up by everyday household sounds—doors closing, the bathroom lid being shut, someone moving through the hallway. Even small, normal noises seem to wake her, and once she’s awake she says she can’t fall back asleep, sometimes losing four or five hours of rest in a night. I genuinely sympathize with how hard that must be, especially with school responsibilities.

As a household, we’ve tried to accommodate her as much as we reasonably can. She started running a fan in her room to create white noise. I added soft door bumpers to reduce slamming. We’ve all made an effort to move more carefully at night. The issue is that some sounds can’t be fully eliminated—like the click of a doorknob unless you consciously twist it every single time. Sometimes people forget, or accidents happen, and despite our efforts, the problem hasn’t improved.

At this point, it’s been going on for months, and nothing we’ve tried has made a meaningful difference. Compared to everyone else in the house, who either don’t wake up or fall right back asleep when there’s noise, her reaction feels extreme. I don’t mean that dismissively, but more in the sense that it might be something beyond what roommates can realistically fix.

In our group chat, I sent a message trying to be careful with my wording. I said something along the lines of not wanting to offend her, but that we’d tried multiple solutions already, and asked whether she had considered talking to a doctor about it. I wasn’t trying to imply anything was “wrong” with her—just that persistent, severe sleep disruption might have a medical solution that’s out of our hands.

Since then, she hasn’t responded at all. Not in the group chat, and not when I tried reaching out privately to smooth things over. Now I’m stuck wondering whether suggesting medical help came across as insensitive or dismissive, even though it felt like the last reasonable option after months of trying to adjust our behavior.

I didn’t intend to hurt her feelings or imply she’s the problem, but I also don’t know how much quieter a shared home can realistically become. Was it unfair of me to suggest she look into medical support when all the household fixes failed?
