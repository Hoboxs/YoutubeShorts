Am I a Monster for “Banning” a Service Dog and Ruining Christmas for Everyone?!

I’m a 38-year-old man who hosts our extended family’s Christmas Eve gathering every year. It’s a big, loud tradition with lots of relatives, food, kids running around, and people coming and going all night. I host because my house is the largest, it’s centrally located, and for years it’s just been understood that Christmas happens here. It’s stressful, expensive, and exhausting—but I do it because it matters to my family.

Last year, a relative of mine showed up with a service dog in training. I was told the dog was still very young, barely over a year old, and I didn’t really know what to expect. I’ve never owned dogs and don’t have much experience with them, especially not in a crowded house full of children, food, and noise. Unfortunately, it didn’t go well. The dog had multiple accidents in my house, caused disruptions, and there was one incident involving my child that really crossed a line for me. I later found out I wasn’t the only one uncomfortable—another family member quietly complained to me as well.

After that holiday, I decided I didn’t want a repeat. A few weeks ago, while planning this year’s party, I casually asked around whether that same relative would be attending. I was told by multiple people that she wouldn’t be there, so I assumed the issue had resolved itself. Then, just days ago, I was suddenly informed that she was coming after all.

Realizing I needed to address it immediately, I called her and told her she was absolutely welcome to attend—but the dog was not. I explained that last year’s experience wasn’t something I was willing to repeat in my own home, especially with kids and a large crowd. I tried to be calm and straightforward, not cruel.

That single conversation detonated the entire holiday. Her parents and siblings immediately threatened to boycott Christmas altogether. I was accused of being heartless, discriminatory, and selfish. In an attempt to compromise, I even offered to let the dog stay outside during the event—but I was told that suggestion was “offensive” and “completely unreasonable.”

Now the family group chats are exploding, people are taking sides, and I’m being blamed for potentially canceling Christmas Eve altogether. I feel like I’m being punished for setting a boundary in my own home after a genuinely bad experience last year. I’m exhausted, frustrated, and honestly shocked at how fast this spiraled.

So am I really the villain for not wanting an untrained service dog inside my house during Christmas?
