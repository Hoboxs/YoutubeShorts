Was I wrong for secretly keeping food aside for my niece?

About half a year ago, my brother moved into my house with his wife and their three children while they tried to rebuild financially after relocating across the country. My wife and I agreed because we had the space, and honestly things have been fairly peaceful overall.

The biggest adjustment has probably been the amount of food disappearing from the kitchen every day.

Their two sons are both teenage athletes, one heavily involved in basketball and the other obsessed with competitive running. Those boys eat like absolute machines. I am talking giant portions at dinner, multiple second helpings, and entire boxes of snacks disappearing almost overnight.

At first I just laughed about it because teenage boys are known for inhaling food.

But after a while I started noticing something uncomfortable.

Their daughter, who is around fourteen, was constantly ending up with scraps. I would hear her open the pantry and complain there was barely anything left. At meals, I noticed her brothers’ plates were piled high while she consistently got noticeably smaller portions. Sometimes by the time she got around to grabbing snacks after school, everything was already gone.

The more I watched it happen, the more wrong it felt.

So one day I carefully brought it up to my brother. I assumed maybe they just were not realizing how uneven it looked.

Instead, his response honestly shocked me.

He basically told me the boys “needed” more food because they were athletes and still growing, while his daughter was not active enough to justify eating as much. Then he casually added that he did not want her becoming overweight because she spends more time indoors and is not involved in sports.

The way he said it made my stomach turn.

His daughter is not overweight at all. She is just quieter and less athletic than her brothers. But after hearing him talk about her like that, I started paying closer attention and realized she genuinely seemed anxious around food, almost like she had learned not to ask for too much because she would either get judged or there simply would not be enough left anyway.

So I quietly started buying extra things.

Nothing dramatic. Just additional snacks, drinks, granola bars, fruit, easy meals, things like that. I kept them in the main kitchen area instead of the guest suite where my brother’s family mostly stays. Then one afternoon I privately told my niece that if she was ever hungry, she could help herself whenever she wanted and did not need permission.

I honestly thought I was just making sure a kid felt included and comfortable in my home.

Apparently not.

A few days later my brother found out and absolutely exploded at me.

He accused me of treating him like some abusive father who was starving his daughter. He insisted she gets “plenty” of food and claimed I was undermining his parenting by secretly creating a separate stash just for her. Then he got even angrier and said that if I was going to buy extra food for one child, I should also be giving his sons unlimited access to it too.

That part almost made me laugh because the entire reason I started doing this was because his sons already consume nearly everything before anyone else gets a chance.

I told him exactly that.

I said his daughter was the only child in the house consistently ending up without food left for her, and pretending otherwise was ridiculous.

Now things in the house feel tense and awkward, and my brother is acting like I crossed a huge boundary by getting involved in how he feeds his children. But honestly, I could not keep watching his daughter quietly get pushed aside every day without doing something.
