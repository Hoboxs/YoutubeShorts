Was I wrong for not giving my Aldi cart away for free?

For anyone who doesn’t shop there, Aldi requires you to insert a quarter to unlock a cart. When you return it, you get your quarter back. The whole system is designed to keep people from leaving carts all over the parking lot.

There’s kind of an unspoken understanding that if someone takes your cart before you return it, they hand you a quarter in exchange.

After I finished shopping, a woman asked if she could have my cart. I asked if she had a quarter. She said no.

I shrugged and returned the cart myself so I could get my quarter back.

She got visibly annoyed, and a couple of people nearby gave me side-eye, which made me feel like I had done something rude.

The thing is, I don’t carry cash or spare change. I keep that specific quarter in my car console specifically for Aldi trips. If I had just handed over the cart for free, I’d be stuck next time I shop there unless I go hunting for change.

It’s only 25 cents, but it’s also the whole point of the system.

So was I wrong for returning the cart instead of giving it to her for free?
