Was I wrong for asking a girl to get off a treadmill and then arguing with her mom?

I recently started running, and the gym I go to doesn’t have many treadmills. Last night, I went with my girlfriend and all the treadmills were in use. I did some weights first, then checked again.

I noticed one girl sitting on a treadmill while her friend was actively using the one next to her. She had been sitting there for at least 15 minutes without actually using the machine.

I went over and asked if she was planning to use it. She said she was “with her friend.” I told her she could stand or sit next to her friend’s machine instead of occupying a treadmill other people might want to use.

She shrugged and refused to move.

Then a woman approached and asked what was happening. I explained the situation. She said she was the girl’s mother and told me I shouldn’t be telling her daughter to move.

I responded that her daughter shouldn’t be taking up equipment she had no intention of using. The mother insisted her daughter was free to sit there and wasn’t doing anything wrong.

That’s when I said I could see where the daughter got her entitlement from.

The mom said I was out of line for criticizing her parenting. I replied that she shouldn’t be upset about criticism if she raised her daughter better.

Eventually, a staff member came over. After hearing what was going on, they told the girl she needed to either use the treadmill or free it up for someone else.

My girlfriend thinks I shouldn’t have escalated things by arguing with the mother, but I don’t feel like I did anything wrong.

Was I wrong for pushing the issue and responding the way I did?
