Was I wrong for refusing shared meals while expecting a baby?

I’m a woman in my early thirties who’s expecting a baby, and my partner and I live in a small duplex next door to some close relatives. To help us cut costs, they often cover groceries and prepare dinner for all of us, which I do genuinely appreciate, especially with a child on the way and expenses piling up.

The issue is that one of them is extremely rigid and controlling about food. He only eats a handful of very plain dishes—things like unseasoned poultry with starch, meat cooked in excessive fat, and shelf-stable vegetables—and once he decides he likes something, we end up eating it over and over again every week. Any suggestion to switch things up is immediately dismissed with comments about how it’s “simpler” or “better this way.” He also insists that certain ingredients make him feel ill, but only when he knows they’re there. If he doesn’t know, he praises the meal and asks how it was made.

Since becoming pregnant, I’ve developed intense food aversions, and most of the meals he prefers now make me nauseous just to look at. When I decline to eat something, he brushes it off by saying I’ve eaten it my whole life or that I used to like it. The truth is, I never did—I just ate what was put in front of me growing up because I didn’t have a choice.

Another relative asks me each week to help plan dinners, and I try to suggest meals I know I can tolerate. Every time, those ideas are rejected for being too difficult, too costly, or because the ingredients are supposedly impossible to find. I’ve even offered to do the cooking myself if they’d just pick up what’s needed, complete with a clear list, but it always circles back to the same limited rotation because anything different is labeled “too much trouble.”

Even though all four of us rotate cooking between our homes, it doesn’t really matter who’s at the stove. If the meal isn’t exactly to his liking, he complains the entire evening about how it’s wrong or how it should have been done differently. This has been going on for as long as I can remember. When I was younger, even ordering food outside the house meant everyone had to get the same thing because it was “easier,” right down to the toppings, and I was expected to just accept it.

Now, when I turn down meals I can’t stomach, the other relative gets upset and insists they’re doing their best to help us. I understand their intention, and I’m not asking for extravagant or expensive dishes. I’m just asking for small changes—basic seasoning, a different cooking method, or some variety—so I can actually eat without feeling sick. A recent example was a planned baked dish where I asked if the meat could be cooked and seasoned beforehand instead of prepared in the bland way he insists on, and that request was shut down immediately. At this point, I’m left wondering if I’m being unreasonable for wanting food I can tolerate while pregnant, or if it’s fair to finally push back.
