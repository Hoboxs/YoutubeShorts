Was I Wrong to Want My Child’s Clothes Kept Just for Her?

My daughter started preschool last week, and like most parents, I was given a list of things to send with her. The teacher asked for pull ups, wipes, and a full change of clothes in case of accidents. I made sure to send a generous supply of pull ups, a brand new unopened pack of wipes, and a clean outfit that I knew fit her well and was comfortable. I wanted to be prepared and make things as easy as possible for the classroom.

When I picked my daughter up a few days later, I noticed something that immediately unsettled me. Only the pants from her spare outfit were in her cubby. The shirt was missing. As I was leaving, I saw another child from her class wearing that exact shirt. I recognized it right away because it was one of the few nicer tops my daughter owns. In that moment, my stomach dropped.

I want to be clear that I understand preschool classrooms often need to share supplies. If another child needs a pull up or wipes, I truly do not mind my daughter’s being used. I know not every family is able to provide those things consistently, and I am more than willing to send extra if it helps another child get through the day comfortably. That part does not bother me at all.

What does bother me is her clothing being shared. Clothes are expensive, and my daughter does not have many to begin with. If a shirt gets sent home on another child, there is a real chance it never comes back. Even if it does, it could come back stained, damaged, or not at all. I simply cannot afford to replace clothing that disappears, and it worries me knowing something meant specifically for my child is being treated as communal property.

I am now debating whether to speak to the teacher the next time my daughter goes to school. I want to politely explain that while I am happy to share consumable items like wipes and pull ups, I am not comfortable with my daughter’s spare clothes being used for other children. I do not want to sound unkind or difficult, but I also feel like it is reasonable to want my child’s belongings to remain hers.

I keep going back and forth, wondering if saying something would make me seem selfish or uncooperative, even though the thought of losing more of her clothes genuinely stresses me out. All I want is to protect what little she has without causing conflict. Would I be wrong for bringing this up?
