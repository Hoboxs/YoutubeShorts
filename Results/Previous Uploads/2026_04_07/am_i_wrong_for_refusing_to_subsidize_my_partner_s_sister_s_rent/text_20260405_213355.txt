Am I wrong for refusing to subsidize my partner’s sister’s rent?

My partner and I currently share a one bedroom place and split the rent evenly, which works well for us. Recently, her sister and her sister’s husband have run into housing trouble. They have a young child and were staying with a friend, but that arrangement is ending, and they cannot afford to stay on their own.

They asked if they could move in with us. The only way that would work is if we upgraded to a two bedroom place, which would increase the rent by a few hundred each month. I am not against helping them out, but I want things to be fair.

My position is that if four adults are living in the apartment, the rent should be split evenly between the four of us. That means each person would be responsible for a quarter of the total. From my perspective, they are a couple, so how they divide their share between themselves is up to them, but together they should cover half of the rent.

They do not agree. Since my partner’s sister stays home with the baby and does not earn an income, they feel it is unfair to expect her to contribute. Their suggestion is that the three of us who work should split the rent into thirds, leaving her out of the financial responsibility.

That is where I pushed back. I do not feel comfortable taking on a larger share of the rent to make up for their situation. I understand that raising a child comes with challenges and expenses, but that was a choice they made as a couple. I do not think it is reasonable for that choice to become my financial responsibility.

Now there is tension. They feel like I am being cold and not supportive during a difficult time, while I feel like I am being asked to carry a burden that is not mine. I am open to them moving in, but only if the financial arrangement feels fair to everyone involved.

At this point, I am questioning whether I am being too rigid or if it is reasonable to set that boundary before agreeing to something that could impact my own stability.
