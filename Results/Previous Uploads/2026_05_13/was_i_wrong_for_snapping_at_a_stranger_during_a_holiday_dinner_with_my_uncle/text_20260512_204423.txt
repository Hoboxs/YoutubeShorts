Was I Wrong for Snapping at a Stranger During a Holiday Dinner With My Uncle?

I’m 20F, and last weekend I went out to dinner with my uncle (72M). He’s basically the person who raised me after I lost my parents when I was young, so we’re extremely close. It happened to fall on Father’s Day, but we honestly just wanted to get dinner together and spend time catching up.

The restaurant was crowded, and we ended up seated at one of those long padded booths where multiple tables are lined up side by side. Sitting near us was a middle-aged couple. As we sat down, the man smiled at my uncle and casually said, “Happy Father’s Day, sir.”

My uncle smiled and thanked him, and I smiled too because it seemed harmless and friendly. But the man’s wife immediately got irritated. She started muttering loudly about how people shouldn’t assume things and how “it’s not his day unless he’s actually a father.” Her husband tried to brush it off and said he was only being polite.

A little while later, the husband stepped away from the table, and the woman leaned over toward us. She asked if my uncle was actually my grandfather because he “looked too old” to be my dad. I explained that he was my uncle but that he raised me.

Instead of dropping it, she started complaining again about how Father’s Day is supposed to celebrate “real fathers” and that people shouldn’t accept congratulations that “don’t belong to them.” I tried ignoring her and went back to helping my uncle read the menu because his eyesight isn’t great anymore, but she kept repeating herself.

Finally she said something like, “Honestly, he should’ve corrected that man instead of pretending the holiday was about him.”

That’s when I lost patience. I turned to her and told her my uncle spent most of his life raising me after my parents died, and that he had done more for me than a lot of biological fathers ever do. I said if someone wanted to wish him a happy Father’s Day out of kindness, then he absolutely deserved it.

She looked stunned and immediately backed off. A few minutes later the couple paid and left, but as they walked out I heard her complain to her husband that I’d been disrespectful and made her feel embarrassed.

When I told a few friends afterward, most thought the woman was completely out of line. But one person said I probably could’ve just ignored her instead of making the situation awkward and emotional in the middle of the restaurant.

Now I’m wondering if I overreacted. Was I the asshole?
