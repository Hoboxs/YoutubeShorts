Did I Ruin My Coworker’s Engagement by Saying No?

I’m 27F and work at a sports bar with a guy I’ll call Jason (32M). About a month ago he started telling everyone at work about this elaborate proposal he had planned for his girlfriend. He bought tickets to a huge weekend music festival in another city and apparently wanted to propose during a fireworks show after the final performance. Every shift he talked about how perfect it was going to be.

The problem was that Jason somehow forgot to request the weekend off from work. He didn’t notice until the schedule was already posted, and unfortunately he was assigned one of the busiest Friday night shifts of the month. Once he realized, he started scrambling to get someone to take it.

He asked pretty much everyone first, but most people either already had plans or didn’t want to give up a profitable shift. I happened to be off that night, so eventually he came to me. At the time I had just finished a brutal stretch of workdays and was also helping my cousin move apartments that weekend. I didn’t have some huge event planned that night specifically, but I really wanted the evening to decompress and catch up on things.

I politely told him I couldn’t do it.

After that he got weirdly intense about it. He kept asking what I could possibly be doing that was more important than a proposal and saying this was “a once in a lifetime moment.” I told him I understood it mattered to him, but forgetting to request time off wasn’t really my responsibility to fix.

Nobody ended up taking the shift, so he had to work most of the evening before driving out to the festival afterward. According to him, by the time he arrived, the fireworks and the big closing song were already over. He still proposed later near the parking area outside the venue, and his girlfriend said yes, but he was furious that the original plan got ruined.

Ever since then he’s been making passive-aggressive comments at work about how I “destroyed the magic” and how some people “can’t do one favor for another human being.” A few coworkers think he’s being ridiculous because he created the problem himself, but another coworker said I could’ve just helped him out since I technically wasn’t busy with anything major.

Now I’m second-guessing myself a little. Was I wrong for refusing to cover the shift?
