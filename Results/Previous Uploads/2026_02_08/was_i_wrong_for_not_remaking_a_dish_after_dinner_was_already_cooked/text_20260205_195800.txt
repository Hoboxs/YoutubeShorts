Was I Wrong for Not Remaking a Dish After Dinner Was Already Cooked?

I hosted a small dinner at my apartment a few evenings ago and invited a few close friends over. I genuinely enjoy cooking and wanted to do something nice, so I offered to make a full meal for everyone. The day before, I checked in with the group and asked if anyone had allergies, dietary restrictions, or preferences so I could plan accordingly. No one mentioned anything at all, so I went ahead and cooked the meal the way I usually do for myself, with bold flavors and a fair amount of seasoning.

One friend, Maya, arrived early to help with setting the table and plating. While I was getting ready to serve the food, she casually mentioned that she does not like heavily seasoned dishes. I told her that I wished she had said something when I asked the day before, because everything was already finished. She then asked if I could make a separate portion just for her with very minimal seasoning.

At that point, I was honestly exhausted. The kitchen was already a mess, the food was done, and guests were about to arrive. I told her I could not remake part of the meal, but she was welcome to eat the sides, which included rice, bread, and a salad. I also offered to order something for her if she preferred. She seemed annoyed but did not say much in the moment.

Once everyone arrived and we sat down to eat, Maya barely touched her plate. She stayed quiet throughout the meal and ended up leaving earlier than everyone else. The next day, she messaged me and said I had been rude and inconsiderate as a host, and that I made her feel left out by not accommodating her taste preferences.

I understand that food preferences can be important, and I do feel bad that she did not enjoy the dinner. At the same time, I feel like I did what I reasonably could. I asked in advance, cooked one shared meal, and offered alternatives when the issue came up too late to change things. I do not host people often, so I am still figuring out what proper hosting etiquette looks like, but I truly did not mean to make anyone feel excluded.

Most of our mutual friends think I handled the situation fairly, but the conversation with Maya left me second guessing myself. I am wondering if I should have pushed through my exhaustion and remade food just for her, or if it was reasonable to say no once everything was already done.
