Is it wrong to skip a family dinner when your medical needs aren’t respected?

I have a lifelong eating disorder that severely limits what foods I can tolerate, and it isn’t about preference or attitude. Certain textures and flavors trigger intense nausea and make me physically ill if I try to eat them. Over the years, I’ve learned how to manage this quietly. I usually bring my own very simple meal to gatherings, something plain that I know is safe for me, and this has never been an issue with my family. Everyone has generally understood that this is about health, not being difficult.

Recently, my sibling married Alex, and she follows a strict vegetarian lifestyle. She invited the extended family over for dinner and specifically told everyone not to worry about bringing anything. Wanting to be respectful and avoid an awkward situation, I messaged her ahead of time to ask what she planned to serve. She said she would be making a vegetable-based baked dish with pasta, which I immediately knew I wouldn’t be able to eat without getting sick.

I explained this to her and reminded her that I have a diagnosed condition that affects my eating. I told her I was happy to bring my usual plain meal so I could still attend and spend time with everyone. Her response caught me off guard. She told me flat-out that I wasn’t allowed to bring my own food and that I should just eat what was being served. When I tried to explain again that I literally couldn’t do that, she brushed it off and said I was just being picky.

At that point, I felt dismissed and disrespected, so I told her I wouldn’t be coming to dinner. I later vented to my mom about what happened, and the situation spread through the family from there. As a result, several relatives decided not to attend the dinner either, which escalated things even more. Alex is now very upset and has tried calling me, though I haven’t answered. My sibling reached out and said I could bring my own meal after all, but by then the damage felt done.

Now I’m refusing to go, not because of the food anymore, but because of how easily my medical needs were invalidated and how I was spoken to. I feel like going would mean pretending that didn’t matter. At the same time, I know my absence is causing tension, and I’m being told I’m being stubborn. I’m left wondering whether standing my ground is reasonable, or if skipping the dinner entirely crossed a line.
