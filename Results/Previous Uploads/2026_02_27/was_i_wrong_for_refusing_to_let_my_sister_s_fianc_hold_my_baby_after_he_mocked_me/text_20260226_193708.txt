Was I wrong for refusing to let my sister’s fiancé hold my baby after he mocked me?

I’m 30 and have a 6-month-old baby. My sister has been dating her fiancé for about three months. I’ve only met him a handful of times.

We were at a family barbeque yesterday. My baby had been fussy all day, so I was holding him. My sister’s fiancé, who had clearly been drinking, came up and reached for my baby. I told him, calmly, “Please don’t touch him, he’s been fussy today.”

He laughed and said loudly, “Oh come on, you’re overprotective. Babies cry. You need to toughen him up.” Then he actually tried to grab my baby out of my arms.

I stepped back, and he kept joking, saying things like, “Stop being so paranoid. Babies are easy. You’re just making it worse.”

People kind of laughed awkwardly, but it was obvious he was being rude. My sister didn’t step in.

After the party, I told my sister that he’s not allowed to hold my baby at our house or at family events. She got really angry and said I was overreacting and that I can’t control other people.

But to me, this isn’t about control. It’s about safety and respect. He was drinking, ignored a clear boundary, mocked my parenting in front of everyone, and tried to physically take my baby from my arms.

So now I’m wondering, was I wrong to draw that line?
