Was I wrong for refusing to return expensive K-pop posters I bought secondhand after someone claimed they were stolen from her?

Last November, I bought two autographed posters from members of my favorite K-pop group through my country’s main fandom Facebook selling group. The seller provided authentication proof that seemed legitimate, and I felt comfortable going through with it. I spent a couple thousand dollars on them as a graduation gift to myself after finishing my Master’s and landing a full-time job.

A few weeks after I posted them on Instagram, a girl, Ana, from another country messaged me. She claimed the posters were stolen from her by her ex-roommate (“Maria”), who had apparently taken over $10,000 worth of rare merchandise and sold some of it abroad, including, allegedly, the two posters I bought.

Ana sent a photo of the posters with an earlier timestamp, February 2025, as proof they had been hers. She demanded that I immediately return them because they were stolen property. She was very confrontational and implied I should have known they were stolen because she had posted about it on social media.

The issue is: I live on the other side of the world. I don’t speak her language, and I don’t follow her country’s side of the fandom. From what I understand, Maria came to my country for graduate school and sold the posters here, likely to people who wouldn’t have seen Ana’s posts.

I told Ana I was sorry for her situation but asked whether she intended to reimburse me the full amount I paid. She said no, that she shouldn’t have to, since they were stolen from her.

I refused to return the posters without full reimbursement. I offered to share screenshots of the listing and the PayPal transaction to help her pursue action against Maria, but I wasn’t willing to give up property I bought in good faith based solely on her claim, especially without compensation.

I also mentioned that, given past scams in the K-pop buy/sell community, I had to consider the possibility that this could be an elaborate scam between her and Maria to get both the posters and the money.

I suggested she file a police report in her country if the items were truly stolen and worth that much.

I checked with my brother, who’s a lawyer, and he told me that legally I’m likely protected because I bought them in good faith and had no knowledge they were stolen at the time. He said I wouldn’t typically be liable unless I knew they were stolen.

Since then, Ana has sent people to my social media accounts to harass me. I had to make my Instagram and TikTok private. I’m getting messages calling me a thief and an awful person for not returning the posters.

Now I’m second-guessing myself. I do feel bad if they were truly stolen from her, but I also don’t feel like I should lose thousands of dollars over something I had no way of knowing.

So… am I the asshole?
