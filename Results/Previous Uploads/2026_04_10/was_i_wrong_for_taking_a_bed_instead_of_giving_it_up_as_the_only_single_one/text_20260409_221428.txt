Was I wrong for taking a bed instead of giving it up as the only single one?

For the past few summers, my group of friends and I have taken a trip together over the July holiday. We always go to the same coastal town, and one of my friends has family there who usually help arrange a place for us to stay. This time, they booked a house with four bedrooms, each with a double bed.

There were supposed to be four couples and me, the only one not in a relationship. Before the trip, it was casually mentioned that someone would bring an air mattress for extra sleeping space, which would be set up in the basement. There was also some talk about one couple possibly staying at a nearby family home if another pair ended up joining us, but that never happened. Aside from that, no one actually decided who would sleep where.

I ended up driving up with one couple, and they had brought the air mattress. On the way, they realized they had forgotten sheets for it, so we messaged everyone else to see if someone could bring extras. Nobody could. When we arrived at the house, it was just the three of us. I asked them directly if they thought it would be fine if I took one of the bedrooms, and they said yes without hesitation. So we each picked a room.

Not long after, another couple arrived and claimed one of the remaining bedrooms. Then the last two couples showed up together. The moment they walked in and saw that I had taken a room, the mood shifted immediately. One of the couples grabbed the final bedroom, which meant the last pair, the ones whose family had helped arrange everything, were left with the air mattress that still did not have sheets.

They were clearly upset and said I had put them in a bad position by taking a bed when I was the only one there without a partner. They kept saying I should have thought about them and that it was obvious I should have taken the air mattress instead. They refused to use it and ended up going to sleep at the nearby family house.

The rest of the trip felt uncomfortable and tense. What was supposed to be a fun weekend turned into quiet tension and awkward conversations. After we got back, that same couple, people I had known for nearly a decade, told me they did not want to continue the friendship because of what happened.

I have been replaying everything in my head since. I did not take anything without asking. I arrived first, checked with the people I was with, and there were no set rules about sleeping arrangements. At the same time, they seemed to believe there was an unspoken expectation that I should have stepped aside simply because I was single.

Now I am left wondering if I missed something obvious or if they expected something that was never actually agreed on.
