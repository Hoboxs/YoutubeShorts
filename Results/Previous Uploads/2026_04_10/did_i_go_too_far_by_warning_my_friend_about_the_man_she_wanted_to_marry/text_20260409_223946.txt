Did I go too far by warning my friend about the man she wanted to marry?

I feel like I may have set off something I cannot take back, but at the same time I cannot shake the feeling that staying quiet would have been worse. My husband has a long time friend, I will call him Daniel, who recently lost his wife after a long and painful illness. It was a sad situation, but the truth is their marriage was never healthy. Daniel has struggled for years with drinking, he does not keep a steady job, and from what I have seen, his late wife carried far more than her share while dealing with his behavior.

What shocked me most was how quickly he moved on. Within just a few weeks of her passing, he was already on dating apps, talking to new women, and even traveling between countries to meet them. He often comes to our country because his late wife was from here, and that is where he now looks for new relationships.

Not long ago, I went to visit a close friend of mine, I will call her Maya. She has had a very difficult past when it comes to relationships and had spent years avoiding dating altogether because of how badly things had gone for her. So when she told me, almost glowing with excitement, that she had met someone kind, charming, and completely different from anyone before, I was relieved for her. She even said they were already talking about getting married.

As she kept describing him, my stomach dropped. Every detail lined up. It was Daniel.

In that moment, I felt torn, but I knew I could not pretend I did not recognize him. I told her everything I knew. I told her about his drinking, about his lack of stability, about the way his last marriage had really been behind closed doors. I told her I was afraid she was stepping into something that could hurt her deeply, especially if she ended up moving to another country where she would be isolated.

The look on her face changed completely. She went from excited to shaken, and after a long conversation, she told me she would end things with him.

A few days later, Daniel started calling me, asking why she had suddenly stopped responding. I avoided the question at first, but then he called again, clearly drunk, accusing me of destroying his only chance at a future and saying I would regret it. It was intense and honestly a little unsettling.

Now my husband is upset with me. He says I should apologize and try to fix things, that maybe this could have been a turning point for Daniel, that marriage might have helped him change. But I cannot agree with that. I watched him stay the same even when his wife was suffering through a serious illness. I do not believe a new relationship would suddenly transform him.

I keep thinking about Maya and how hopeful she sounded. I could not let her walk into something like that without knowing the truth. Still, now everything feels tense, and I am being told I may have crossed a line I cannot uncross.
