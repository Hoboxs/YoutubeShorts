Was I wrong for telling my friend she couldn’t bring her one-year-old to my adults-only event?

My husband and I are childfree by choice. That said, we genuinely enjoy kids. I work professionally with children who have mental health needs, and we spend time every month with our nieces and nephews (ages 1–8, both biological and chosen family).

Since 2018, I’ve hosted an annual Valentine’s event. Historically, it’s been adults-only. I picked this year’s date back in December for late February.

This week, a friend texted me:

She said she might need to bring her one-year-old because her husband is working and her mom can watch her two older kids, but she feels nervous leaving the one-year-old since he “requires a lot more attention.”

For context: her older two are elementary-aged.

I replied that bringing him wouldn’t “ruin” the vibe, but it would change the vibe, and since people RSVP’d knowing it was a kid-free event, I didn’t feel it was fair to change expectations now.

She responded that she didn’t realize it was childfree because in past years she and another friend had brought very young babies. She added that if she didn’t feel comfortable leaving him with her mom, she would just skip it.

Am I the asshole for holding the boundary for this specific event?
