Was I wrong for giving my son his restored childhood dresser for the nursery without asking my daughter-in-law first?

When my son was growing up, he had a dresser handmade by my father. My dad was a skilled woodworker, and the dresser had beautiful hand-carved details and his personal stamp on it. It meant a lot to my son.

Unfortunately, a tornado damaged our home years ago, and the dresser was badly broken when part of the roof collapsed. By the time my son moved out after college, it wasn’t usable, so it stayed in our garage. I couldn’t bring myself to throw it away.

Now my son is married and recently moved into what they consider their forever home. He mentioned to me that it made him sad their child wouldn’t have something like that dresser to grow up with, especially since his grandfather (my father) has passed away. He said he wished he could pass something like that down. He talked about trying to build something small himself, but admitted he’s not very handy.

I decided to see if the dresser could be restored. I contacted several craftsmen, and one agreed to try. In the end, he managed to preserve much of the original wood, including my father’s stamp, and a lot of the original carvings. He replaced damaged sections and even recreated engravings based on photos. It turned out beautifully.

I invited my son and daughter-in-law over for dinner and surprised my son with it as a gift. He was thrilled and took it home that night. Later, he even sent me photos of it placed in the nursery.

About a week later, my daughter-in-law sent me a long text. She was very upset that I hadn’t told her beforehand. She said the dresser doesn’t match the nursery (which is decorated in very light tones), that I had no right to “decorate” their house, and that it was causing tension between her and my son. She asked me to take it back.

I told her no. I said if my son decides he wants to return it, that’s his choice, but I won’t demand it back.

She called me a jerk.

I plan to talk to my son about it, but now I’m wondering: was I wrong for giving him the restored dresser without checking with both of them first?
