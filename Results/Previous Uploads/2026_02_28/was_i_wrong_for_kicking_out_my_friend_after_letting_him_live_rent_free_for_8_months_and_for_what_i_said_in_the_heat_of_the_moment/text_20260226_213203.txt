Was I wrong for kicking out my friend after letting him live rent-free for 8 months, and for what I said in the heat of the moment?

I’m 28, and my friend is 24. About 8 months ago, his girlfriend of 5 years broke up with him. It hit him really hard. I live in a small two-bedroom apartment, and after thinking about it, I offered to let him stay with me rent-free. He just had to cover his own food and utilities.

At first, everything was fine. He mostly stayed in his room, kept to himself, and I gave him space.

But over time, his room and ensuite bathroom became absolutely disgusting. His bedroom smells so bad you can faintly smell it from the kitchen. There are piles of Uber Eats bags and literal stacks of garbage bags in the corner.

His bathroom is even worse: visible black mold in the shower corners and around the window, hair everywhere, toothpaste splattered across the mirror, unflushed toilet at times, and wet towels left on the floor until they stink.

I’ve gradually brought it up over the past couple of months, asking him to clean and take better care of the space. I tried not to be confrontational, but it kept getting worse.

Yesterday, he snapped and told me I needed to stop nagging him “like his girlfriend used to.” That comment really set me off. I lost my temper. I yelled about how filthy everything is and told him he needed to leave. As he was walking out, I shouted, “No wonder your girlfriend broke up with you if you live like this.”

I’m not proud of saying that. It was cruel, and I know it. But I also feel like I’ve been more than patient. I refuse to keep enabling this when it’s affecting my own living conditions.

I told him I’m willing to let him stay if he hires a professional cleaner and fixes the situation immediately. Otherwise, I’ll be packing up his things by the end of the month.

He told me I’m a terrible friend and that I should have more empathy because he’s struggling. I understand he’s depressed, but it’s been 8 months, and I feel like he’s starting to take advantage of my generosity.

Was I wrong for kicking him out and for what I said?
