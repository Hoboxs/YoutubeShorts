Was I Wrong for Refusing My Mom’s Sleeping Arrangement Rules in My Own Home?

I’m 26F and I recently moved into a new house with my boyfriend (27M). We both work full-time, split expenses, and have been living together for over a year without issues.

A couple of weeks ago, my parents came to visit for the first time since we moved in. They live a few hours away, so we offered them the guest room so they could stay overnight instead of driving back late. The visit started off fine — we gave them a tour, had dinner together, and things seemed normal.

Later in the evening, when everyone was getting ready for bed, my mom asked where my boyfriend was going to sleep. I said he would be sleeping in our bedroom as usual. She immediately said that was inappropriate and insisted he should sleep on the couch or in the guest room while they were there.

My mom has always been pretty traditional and has expressed disapproval about us living together before marriage, but I didn’t expect it to turn into her trying to set rules in my home.

I told her no, that wasn’t going to happen. I explained that we weren’t going to change our sleeping arrangements just because she was visiting, and that there was nothing disrespectful or inappropriate about us sharing a bedroom in our own house.

She didn’t drop it and said that as my mother she expected me to respect her values while she was under my roof. My stepdad also jumped in and said I should just “make an exception for one night” to avoid tension.

The conversation escalated, and eventually the visit ended early. My parents left and got a hotel for the night instead of staying with us. Afterward, my mom sent me messages saying I embarrassed her and that I was being disrespectful, and some relatives have since said I should have just agreed to keep the peace.

My boyfriend says I didn’t do anything wrong, and most people on his side agree, but now I’m second-guessing whether I handled it too rigidly.

What do you think?
