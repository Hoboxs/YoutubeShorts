Did I destroy my relationship with my father by finally telling him the truth?

For almost my entire life my father has treated my older brother like the center of the universe while my younger sister and I just existed around him.

From the outside, people probably thought we had a perfectly normal family. My father was around, he worked hard, he showed up sometimes, and he was never physically cruel. But inside the house, everything revolved around my brother, Evan. His sports mattered most. His interests mattered most. His schedule controlled the family calendar. If my sister or I had something important happening but it clashed with one of Evan’s events, we already knew who our father would choose.

Every single time.

It was not even subtle. My father talked about Evan constantly like he was some future legend. Meanwhile my sister and I learned very young not to expect too much attention. We became the easy kids because there was no point fighting for scraps of affection we knew were already reserved for someone else.

Over the years, that kind of thing changes you.

Eventually you just build your own life emotionally separate from them because it hurts less that way.

The reason everything exploded recently is because of my sister’s career. Growing up, my father and Evan both dreamed of getting into this extremely competitive industry. Neither of them made it. They ended up in decent careers, but there was always this bitterness around the fact that they never got their dream.

Then my sister actually did it.

Against all odds, she landed a major role in that exact world and has been doing incredibly well. It is honestly impressive, and I am unbelievably proud of her.

But suddenly my father transformed into this deeply invested parent overnight.

Now he constantly wants updates about her career. He wants details about projects, coworkers, opportunities, all of it. The thing is, my sister barely talks to him. She keeps conversations short and avoids opening up.

And honestly? I do not blame her.

A few weeks ago my father and I got into an argument over something stupid. Then out of nowhere he started complaining that my sister and I never call him unless we need something. He specifically brought up how hurt he was that my sister would not talk to him about her career.

That was the moment years of resentment finally burst out of me.

I asked him what exactly he expected after spending our entire childhood making it painfully obvious that only one child truly mattered to him. I told him the only reason he suddenly cared so much about my sister’s career was because she succeeded at the dream he and Evan failed to reach.

Then I really went in.

I pointed out that I work in a completely different field and he barely knows anything about my life because he has never bothered asking. I told him he had decades to build relationships with his daughters, but he chose over and over again to worship his son instead.

And then I said the part that completely changed the atmosphere.

I told him he dug his own grave.

I said if his daughters feel distant from him now, that is because he created that distance himself brick by brick for years. He cannot suddenly act shocked that we are not emotionally close after spending most of our lives feeling second place in our own family.

He just stared at me after that.

Ever since the argument, things have been icy between us. Apparently he has stopped asking my sister about her career completely now. My mother says I crushed him and that maybe he never realized how obvious the favoritism was.
