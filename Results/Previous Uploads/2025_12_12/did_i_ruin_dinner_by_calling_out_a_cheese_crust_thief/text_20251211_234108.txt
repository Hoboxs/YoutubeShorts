Did I Ruin Dinner by Calling Out a Cheese Crust Thief?

During a weekend golf trip in Riverton, I grabbed dinner at a busy little share-plate spot with two longtime pals. We always order things for the table, pick a few dishes each, and split everything without drama. One friend can’t handle shellfish, so we kept the menu simple and safe.

When the food showed up, one of the dishes was this smooth baked pasta with a perfect crunchy cheese layer on top—the part everyone usually hopes to snag a bit of. Before anyone else could even reach out, Victor scooped out the entire golden crust and slid it onto his plate like it belonged to him alone.

I said something in a playful tone—half joking, half serious—like, “Whoa, what is that move? Who takes all the good stuff?” He laughed awkwardly, sliced off part of the crust he’d taken, and dropped it back in the shared bowl, which honestly just made it weirder.

I figured that was the end of it, but the next day on the course he brought it up again, and suddenly we were in a full-blown argument over baked cheese like it was a national crisis.

So now I’m wondering, was I out of line for calling him out, even lightly?
