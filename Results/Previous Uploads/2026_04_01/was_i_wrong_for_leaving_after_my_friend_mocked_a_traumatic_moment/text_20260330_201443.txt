Was I wrong for leaving after my friend mocked a traumatic moment?

A few years ago, something happened that still stays with me in a way I cannot fully shake. My wife and I were hiking in a remote mountain area, and during the climb, she slipped and fell in a dangerous spot. It all happened so fast, and in that moment, I truly believed I might lose her. She ended up stuck in a terrifying position, and I could not get to her or pull her up on my own. I remember the look on her face, the fear, and the helplessness I felt. Eventually, another hiker came along and helped get her to safety, but it took hours, and those hours felt endless.

Even though she has never blamed me, I have never fully let go of that feeling. It is something that weighs on me more than I like to admit. Not being able to help her in that moment has stayed with me ever since.

Recently, we were out at a brewery with a small group of friends. The atmosphere was relaxed, and the conversation turned to hiking plans. One of the guys started talking about an upcoming trip, and since I had experience in that area, I offered a bit of advice. Out of nowhere, he turned to me and made a comment suggesting that I had left my wife behind during that incident.

It completely caught me off guard. My wife immediately corrected him, but he just laughed, like it was some kind of joke. I felt stunned and honestly hurt. I did not know how to respond in the moment, so I stepped away under the excuse of getting another drink, but instead I went outside to get some air.

My wife followed me shortly after and could tell something was wrong. I told her I was not okay and that I wanted to leave. She agreed, and we left together.

The problem is that we had driven one of those friends there, including the one who made the comment, and I did not say anything before leaving. Later, he started messaging me, asking where I had gone and why I had left him there. Since he did not have a ride, he ended up paying for a trip home.

When I confronted him about what he said and why he would bring up something so personal and painful, he responded by making light of it again, sending mocking messages and saying that I needed to toughen up. That response only made things worse.

Now I feel conflicted. On one hand, I know I left someone stranded when I had agreed to give them a ride, which is not something I would normally do. On the other hand, I feel like what he said crossed a serious line, especially given how much that experience affected me.

I keep wondering if I should have handled it differently in the moment, maybe said something before leaving, or if walking away was the only way to deal with it.
