Why Did My Closest Friend Suddenly Cut Me Out of Her Life?

I’m a woman in my late twenties, and for over a decade my closest friend “Lena” and I were inseparable in every sense—daily calls, shared routines, holidays together, constant plans, the kind of bond everyone joked would outlast marriages. About half a year ago, Lena started dating “Evan,” and at first I was genuinely excited for her, fully expecting some schedule shifts because of the honeymoon phase. I didn’t complain when she began canceling on me, even when it happened repeatedly and always at the last minute, but after months of being pushed aside I finally told her, gently, that it hurt to feel like I was no longer a priority. She apologized and promised to do better, and for a brief stretch it seemed like things were improving—until I saw online that she’d gone to a tasting event the same weekend she told me she was too overwhelmed to come to a dinner we’d planned weeks earlier. I tried to keep things light when I mentioned it, but she ignored me for days and brushed it off with a vague explanation. I swallowed my feelings again, not wanting to seem needy, until I learned from someone else that she was planning a birthday gathering and I wasn’t invited, despite never missing one in all the years we’d been friends. When I finally asked her outright if I’d done something wrong or if she was upset with me, she told me she needed distance and said I’d been “too intense,” which felt like a slap after all the patience and restraint I’d shown. I asked for clarity, she shut the conversation down, went silent for nearly two weeks, and when I sent one last message saying I loved her and wanted to understand what happened, she blocked me entirely. I’m grieving someone who’s still alive, replaying every interaction to figure out what I did wrong, and wondering if asking for honesty from the person who once called me family really makes me the villain here.
