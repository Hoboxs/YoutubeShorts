Surprise Birthday or Surprise Bill? Was I Wrong to Be Upset?

I’ll change identifying details, but keep the situation the same. For my birthday, two close friends planned what they framed as a surprise outing. I was dropped off at a café in a nearby city with no idea what was happening, only to find them waiting for me inside. They had arranged a birthday brunch deal that included food and drinks, brought flowers, a small dessert, and a wrapped gift, and afterward we walked to a dessert shop nearby. One of them covered the bill at the restaurant, and the whole day felt like a thoughtful birthday treat that I hadn’t been asked to plan or budget for.

About two weeks later, that same friend sent me a payment request for my share of the brunch and dessert, roughly fifty dollars, without any message or explanation. That’s what threw me off. It wasn’t about the money itself—I could afford it and would have paid without issue if it had been discussed ahead of time. What bothered me was that this had been presented as a surprise, and in my experience, when someone plans a surprise birthday outing, it usually means either they’re covering it or they clearly communicate costs in advance.

I brought it up calmly and explained that I was okay paying, but that the surprise charge felt awkward and confusing. She immediately got defensive and said she had simply forgotten to pay for me at the time, that I shouldn’t have assumed it was covered, and that expecting that made me ungrateful considering she’d already done a lot by organizing the day, bringing gifts, and traveling to see me. She also said that whether a surprise means it’s paid for is subjective and that other people agreed with her.

I tried to explain that my discomfort wasn’t about wanting more, but about the mismatch between how the day was framed and what happened afterward. To me, planning something without my input and then charging me later felt like offering a gift and then asking to be reimbursed. It left me feeling like she wanted the credit for doing something special without actually following through on what that implied.

We talked it out, but she didn’t acknowledge any fault or see where I was coming from at all. That’s what’s still bothering me. It’s hard for me to believe she truly doesn’t understand why this felt off, especially since I wasn’t rude or demanding. Now I’m stuck wondering if I missed some perspective here, or if I’m justified in feeling uncomfortable about being charged for a birthday surprise I didn’t ask for.
