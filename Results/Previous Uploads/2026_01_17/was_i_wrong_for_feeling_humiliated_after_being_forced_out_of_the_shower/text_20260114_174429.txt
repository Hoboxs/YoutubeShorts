Was I wrong for feeling humiliated after being forced out of the shower?

After a long, exhausting shift, I finally got home to our small, drafty apartment in a cold mountain town and told my partner, Evan, that I was going to take a quick shower before we settled in for the night. The house was freezing because the old heater hadn’t been turned on yet, and I was looking forward to those few warm minutes more than anything. I barely had time to rinse before I had shampoo worked into my hair when the bathroom door suddenly flew open. Evan stood there panicked, apologizing over and over, saying he needed the bathroom immediately and that it wasn’t optional. I tried to laugh it off at first and told him I’d just close the curtain and he could use the toilet, but his tone changed fast. He insisted I had to get out right now, acting like I was being unreasonable for even suggesting otherwise.

For context, we’ve lived together for over two years and are generally comfortable with each other, but we still have boundaries—this being a big one. Evan has a digestive condition that makes bathroom emergencies sudden and intense, so I understand that when he has to go, it’s urgent. Still, what bothered me was how non-negotiable it became. I ended up stepping out of the shower mid-wash, water dripping off me, shampoo still in my hair, freezing in the hallway while he locked the bathroom door behind him. I stood there shivering, barefoot on cold floors, feeling ridiculous and strangely embarrassed, waiting for him to finish while my hair dried stiff and soapy.

It wasn’t some massive crisis in the grand scheme of things, but the longer I stood there, the more it sank in how uncomfortable and unnecessary it felt. I couldn’t shake the feeling that I’d been treated like an inconvenience instead of a partner, especially when there were other ways the situation could’ve been handled with a little more consideration. I keep replaying it in my head and wondering if I’m overreacting—or if it really was an unreasonable thing to ask someone you live with to abandon a shower like that just so you can use the toilet.
