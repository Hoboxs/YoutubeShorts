Was I wrong for asking my friends to pay deposits up front after last year?

My friend group (6 of us) takes a trip together every year. I’m usually the one who plans everything, booking the hotel, organizing logistics, coordinating dates, etc.

Last year, when it came time to book the hote;, everyone told me to just put it on my card and they’d “pay me later.” The total came out to about $600 that I covered up front.

Only half of them actually paid me back.

I ended up eating the remaining $600 because I didn’t want to keep chasing people down and make things awkward.

Now we’re planning this year’s trip, and I suggested that everyone send their share of the deposit before I book anything. That way I’m not fronting the money out of my own pocket.

Some of them got annoyed and said I was making things complicated and overthinking it. One friend even said, “You’re usually good at this — what’s changed?”

What’s changed is that I don’t want to lose another $600.

I’m not refusing to plan the trip. I’m just asking for deposits up front so I’m not financially responsible for everyone again.

Was I wrong for setting that boundary this time?
