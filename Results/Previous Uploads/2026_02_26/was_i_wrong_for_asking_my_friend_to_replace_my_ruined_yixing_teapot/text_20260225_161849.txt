Was I wrong for asking my friend to replace my ruined Yixing teapot?

When I went to China, I bought myself a Yixing teapot. It’s made of unglazed clay and “seasons” over time, kind of like cast iron. You’re not supposed to use soap or abrasive sponges on it — ever. You just rinse it with hot water and let it air dry. That’s it.

Recently, I went on a work trip and paid a friend to watch my cat. I told her she could use anything in the kitchen. My Yixing teapot was not in the kitchen. It’s kept elsewhere, along with my specialty loose-leaf teas. I also have a regular kettle in the kitchen specifically for guests.

When I came home, I found my Yixing teapot sitting in the sink. It smelled strongly of soap and had visible scratches inside.

I called her, and she told me she used it because she liked the tea I make with it. She said she washed it with a rough wire sponge and soap because she couldn’t find my regular sponges and didn’t want to put it in the dishwasher.

I tried to salvage it, but now everything brewed in it tastes like soap. The scratches seem to be worsening each time I try to boil water in it. It’s essentially ruined.

I asked her to replace it since she damaged it. She told me to just clean it. I explained that I’ve tried and it’s not fixable. I found a similar teapot from the same store (actually cheaper than the one I originally bought in China) and sent her the link.

It’s about $500.

She called me furious and refuses to pay. She says I should have specifically told her not to use it. I pointed out that it was displayed behind glass and not kept with everyday kitchen items, so I didn’t think I needed to spell that out.

Now she’s angry, and I’m wondering if I should just cut my losses.

Was I wrong for asking her to replace it?
