Was I out of line asking a child to be quiet on a night flight?

I was on a long overnight flight across the ocean, the kind where everyone is hoping to get at least a few hours of sleep to function the next day. I had settled into my seat, already knowing I can be a bit of a difficult sleeper, so I was doing everything I could to rest. Unfortunately, I ended up seated beside a couple traveling with a young child who, for reasons I could not understand, stayed fully awake until what felt like the middle of the night.

This was not a baby crying or fussing, which I would have understood and tried to be patient with. This child was wide awake, chatting away in full, clear sentences, speaking loudly to her parents as if it were the middle of the afternoon. It went on and on, and the volume never really dropped. I tried to ignore it at first, telling myself it would settle down soon, but as time passed, my frustration kept building.

Eventually, I reached a point where I just could not take it anymore. I leaned over and calmly asked the mother if they could try to keep the noise down. Her reaction was immediate and sharp, as if I had said something completely unreasonable. She looked at me and asked if I was serious. I told her that yes, I was.

After that, nothing changed. If anything, I felt like I was being deliberately ignored. The child continued talking at the same volume for another long stretch before finally falling asleep much later into the flight. By that point, most of the night was already gone.

The whole situation left me feeling frustrated and honestly a bit shocked. I understand that traveling with very young children can be unpredictable, and I would never expect silence in that case. But this felt different. This was a child old enough to understand being asked to lower their voice, especially in a quiet, shared space where people are trying to sleep.

At the same time, I cannot help but wonder if I crossed a line. I am not a parent, so maybe there is something I am not considering about how difficult it is to manage a child on a flight like that. Still, it felt like a reasonable request in the moment.

Now I am left questioning whether I was justified in speaking up, or if I should have just stayed quiet and dealt with it like everyone else.
