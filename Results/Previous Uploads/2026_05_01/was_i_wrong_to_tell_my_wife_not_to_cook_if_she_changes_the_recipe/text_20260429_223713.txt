Was I wrong to tell my wife not to cook if she changes the recipe?

I feel like this turned into a much bigger issue than it should have, and now I am second guessing how I handled it. In our home, I do almost all of the cooking. I enjoy it, and my wife openly admits that I am better at it, so it has never really been a point of conflict. That said, there is one dish she makes that I absolutely love. It is her version of spaghetti and meatballs, and to me, it is perfect exactly the way she has always made it.

The problem is that it takes a long time. It is not a quick meal you throw together. It takes hours of preparation and cooking, so I only ask her to make it occasionally, maybe every couple of months. When I first asked recently, she agreed but said she wanted to try changing the sauce. I went along with it, even though I was unsure. When she made it, it just was not the same. I did not say anything at the time, but it stuck with me.

A couple of months later, I asked again and specifically requested the original version. She agreed, but then decided to skip part of the process because it took too long. I even offered to do that part myself just to keep it the same, but she said it would not make a difference. It did, at least to me. Again, I kept my thoughts mostly to myself, but I was disappointed.

Then, a while later, I asked again, and this time she told me she planned to change multiple things, including not making the meatballs at all and using the newer version of the sauce. That is when I finally said something more direct. I told her that if she was not going to make it the way she usually does, then she did not have to make it at all, because that was not how I wanted to eat it.

That did not go over well. She took it as criticism and said I was being disrespectful and ungrateful. From my point of view, I was not trying to insult her cooking. I just really value that one dish the way she originally made it, especially since it is something she only makes occasionally. To me, it felt reasonable to want it prepared the same way.

Since then, I have avoided bringing it up altogether because I do not want to start another argument. But I still wonder if what I said crossed a line. I cannot tell if I was simply expressing a preference for something special to stay the same, or if I came across as controlling and dismissive of the effort she puts in when she cooks.
