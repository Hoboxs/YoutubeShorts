Is It Unreasonable to Demand People Use My Real Name?

I’m a 17-year-old student who recently moved from abroad to attend high school in the U.S., and from day one my name became an issue. When I introduced myself, classmates immediately struggled with it and decided it was “too complicated.” Without asking, they picked a simpler, more American-sounding name and started using that instead. What surprised me most is how fast it stuck—now almost everyone, including a couple of teachers, calls me this substitute name as if it were mine.

I’ve tried handling it calmly. I’ve explained more than once that I don’t care if people mispronounce my name or spell it wrong, as long as they actually use it. I’ve said this politely, without snapping or embarrassing anyone. Instead of adjusting, a lot of people reacted with eye-rolls and sarcasm, and some even escalated it by deliberately calling me other random names just to get under my skin.

The frustrating part is that, aside from this, I genuinely get along with my classmates. They’re friendly, they joke with me, and they don’t seem hostile—but this one issue keeps coming up and makes me feel dismissed. It’s no longer about pronunciation or convenience; it feels like my identity is being treated as optional, like something that can be renamed for other people’s comfort. Am I overreacting for wanting my actual name to be respected, or is it reasonable to expect people to call me what I’m actually called?
