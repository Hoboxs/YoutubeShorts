Did I Go Too Far When My Own Parent Took Everything I Had Left?

I’m a woman in my late thirties, currently in the middle of aggressive medical treatment, and today was one of my hospital days. Because I physically couldn’t manage childcare, my mom came over to watch my two young kids while I was gone. She arrived, stayed just long enough for me to leave, and then left almost immediately after I returned home. I was disappointed but too drained to argue, and I focused on getting through the afternoon.

Not long after she left, my youngest kept opening the refrigerator, saying she was hungry. I assumed she just wanted a snack, so I went to help her and immediately realized something was wrong. The fridge was almost completely empty. The day before, I had done a full grocery run and stocked up for the entire week: milk, juice, fruit, easy meals, snacks, and things I could manage while sick. Every single item was gone. No milk. No juice. Nothing I could quickly give my kids. It slowly dawned on me that my mom must have packed up the groceries and taken them with her when she left.

I tried calling her over and over, but she wouldn’t answer. I then called my brother, who lives across town, and when I explained what had happened, he was furious and said he would confront her directly. Meanwhile, I was sitting at home, weak from treatment, days before Christmas, staring at an empty fridge, knowing I couldn’t easily replace several hundred dollars’ worth of food that my children and I needed to get through the week.

I feel sick over it in more ways than one. I’m hurt, angry, and honestly scared about how I’m supposed to manage right now. Part of me is seriously considering taking formal action, not out of revenge, but because I don’t know how else to recover what was taken or make sure my kids are fed when I’m already stretched to my limit.
