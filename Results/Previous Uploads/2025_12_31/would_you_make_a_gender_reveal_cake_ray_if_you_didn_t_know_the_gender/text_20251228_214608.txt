Would You Make a Gender Reveal Cake Grey If You Didn’t Know the Gender?

I (25M) have been baking as a hobby for a few years and have gotten pretty good at it—sometimes people even pay me under the table for birthdays or showers. So when my sister, Mia, asked me to make the cake for her gender reveal, I was excited. I asked her to tell me the baby’s gender so I could plan the filling—classic pink or blue—but she just smiled and said she wanted to be surprised too. “Just make it neutral,” she said, “and we’ll all see together.”

I was a little confused—so she wanted a reveal cake that didn’t reveal anything? She promised her friend would email me the info, but the email never came. The deadline came and went, and I still didn’t know the gender. I didn’t want to guess and risk messing it up, so I made the cake entirely grey—grey inside, grey outside, with little fondant clouds and silver sprinkles. It tasted fine, but visually it looked like a storm cloud.

On the reveal day, the family gathered around. They cut the cake. Silence. Mia looked furious. Her husband, Connor, stared at the grey cake like I had personally ruined the moment. Cousins whispered behind hands. My mother piped up, “Why would you make it grey? It looks passive-aggressive!” I calmly explained that no one had given me the gender and I wasn’t going to gamble on a cake.

Mia stormed out, muttering about how I had ruined everything. My mom said I embarrassed the family and “should have tried harder.” Tried harder at what, exactly? Divining the baby’s gender through vibes? The cake became a legend—dramatic, moody, completely neutral—and honestly, could anyone else have handled this situation better?
