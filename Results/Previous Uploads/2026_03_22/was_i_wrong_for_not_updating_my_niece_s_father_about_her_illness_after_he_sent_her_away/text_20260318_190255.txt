Was I wrong for not updating my niece’s father about her illness after he sent her away?

My niece, who I’ll call Lila, is 15 and has been living with me for the past four years. Her mother left when she was very young, and for a while she was raised by her father and grandparents. They provided her with a stable life, including private school and extracurricular activities, until her grandparents passed away when she was still a child.

When Lila was around 10, her father remarried a woman named Danielle, who had three children of her own. From the beginning, Lila didn’t get along with Danielle or her kids. Danielle made it clear she didn’t want to take on a parental role for Lila, but at the same time insisted that Lila’s father treat all the children equally. That meant he could no longer afford Lila’s previous school or activities, and she was pulled out of both. That change hit her hard, and she started acting out.

About a year later, Danielle gave her father an ultimatum: either Lila left, or she would. Not long after, I got a call and suddenly Lila was being sent across the country to live with me.

The transition wasn’t easy. She struggled at first, but with therapy and a lot of consistency and reassurance, she’s doing really well now. She’s back in school, involved in sports again, and much more stable. Her father’s involvement has been minimal over the years, occasional calls on holidays and only a couple of visits.

Eight months ago, Lila was diagnosed with a serious, life-threatening illness. We ended up relocating so she could be closer to top specialists, and thankfully her condition is now being managed.

I never informed her father about the diagnosis. He never asked about her health, and given how little involvement he’s had in her life, I didn’t feel obligated to update him.

Recently, he found out through a post a relative made on social media. Since then, he’s been calling and texting me nonstop, furious that I didn’t tell him. He said it was devastating to learn his child was seriously ill through Facebook and has even threatened legal action to try to regain custody.

Realistically, that outcome is very unlikely, but I’m still considering discussing more permanent arrangements with Lila, like adoption, just to be safe.

Some friends have told me that regardless of everything, I should have at least sent him a message when she got sick and given him the opportunity to be involved, especially early on when things were uncertain.

Now I’m starting to wonder if I let my feelings about how he treated her influence my decision too much.

Was I wrong for not telling him?
