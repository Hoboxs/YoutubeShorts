Was I wrong for calling out my friend in front of others after she implied I’m an alcoholic?

I was hanging out with a friend and some of her other friends, who I don’t really know. During the conversation, she brought up an acquaintance of hers and then turned to me and said, “She’s an alcoholic, so you probably know her.”

The implication was obvious, that I’m an alcoholic.

The thing is, I barely drink. I might accept a glass of wine or champagne at a social event, maybe once a month at most. I’ve never been drunk in my life and have never had more than one glass at a time. I’ve never even had a drink in front of this friend.

I was caught off guard and said, “Why would you think I know her because she’s an alcoholic? I’m not an alcoholic. I hardly even drink.”

She rolled her eyes and replied sarcastically, “Of course, I’m sureee you’re not,” like I was some kind of addict in denial.

That really upset me. Not only was she making a serious accusation, but she did it in front of people I had just met, making it seem like I have some kind of problem. I felt embarrassed and angry.

I didn’t let it go. I kept pushing back because I felt like she was casually damaging my reputation. She eventually said, “Oh my god, it’s not that deep. Move on already,” and her friends started defending her, acting like I was overreacting and ruining the mood.

Now I’m wondering if I should have just ignored it instead of confronting her in front of everyone.

Was I wrong for not letting that comment slide?
