Did I Do Something Wrong by Keeping a Day Off I Planned a Year Ahead?

I work in an office where time off is awarded strictly by seniority, and everyone submits their main vacation requests at the same time each year, so if you want a specific day you really have to plan far in advance or hope there are leftover slots. Back in early spring last year, I requested the Tuesday after a major winter holiday in the following January because that day is always miserable at work with extra workload and late hours, and I was lucky enough to be the only person who asked for it, so I got it approved almost a full year ahead. Toward the end of the year, a coworker I barely know pulled me aside and quietly told me she was planning a very private wedding and had already secured nearly a full week off, but she really wanted that extra day I had booked and hoped I might cancel, and while I agreed to let her know if my plans changed, I never promised to give it up. She seemed disappointed and later implied she thought I would cancel since weddings are a big deal, but our working relationship stayed distant and polite as it always had been. Not long after, we were assigning mandatory overtime based on seniority, and when it was her turn followed by mine, she asked if I would swap with her because she had personal plans, and I declined simply because I did not want to work late that day either. Since then she has completely stopped speaking to me, and a few other coworkers have been noticeably colder, which makes me wonder if she has been venting about me. The truth is that I am not doing anything special on my scheduled day off and did not have a major reason to avoid overtime beyond not wanting to work, but I planned that time well in advance and followed the rules, so now I am questioning whether holding my boundaries makes me unfair or if I am being made to feel guilty for not accommodating someone else’s life events.
