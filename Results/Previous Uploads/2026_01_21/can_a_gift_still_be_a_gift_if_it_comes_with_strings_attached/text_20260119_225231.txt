Can a Gift Still Be a Gift If It Comes With Strings Attached?

Last winter, my partner’s mother gave our household and our children several restaurant gift cards as holiday presents. They were all for modest amounts, roughly enough to cover part of a casual meal. I thanked her, put the cards with our other gift items, and did not think much more about it. To me, they were simply gifts we could use whenever we wanted, like any other present.

A few weeks ago, she messaged both my partner and me and suggested we all go out to eat that same day and “use one of the gift cards.” I was genuinely confused and asked which cards she meant, because I assumed she was talking about something she still had. She clarified that she meant the restaurant cards she had given to us. She also said she did not have them with her because I had taken them home. I responded honestly that I did not realize I was not supposed to, since they were given to us as gifts.

That is when she explained that she expected to be included every time we used one of those cards. According to her, the cards were meant for shared outings with her, not for us to use on our own. This caught me off guard, especially because the amounts on the cards would barely cover a single adult meal if we went together. It felt less like a generous present and more like a way to obligate us into plans.

For additional context, I have kept some emotional distance from her over the past year due to ongoing tension, and my partner tends to stay quiet and avoid conflict when issues with his mother come up. From my perspective, this makes the situation feel even more uncomfortable. It seems as though the gift cards are being used as leverage to force time together rather than as a no-strings-attached gesture.

I am struggling with whether it is unreasonable to want to use these gift cards freely, without conditions attached, or whether I am overreacting to something that should be harmless.
