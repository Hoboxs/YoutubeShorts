Did I Cross a Line for Resenting an Uninvited Stranger Living in My House?

I’m a woman in my late twenties living by choice with my grandfather, not because either of us needs help but because we genuinely enjoy sharing a home, and until recently our life together felt warm, peaceful, and deeply familiar, full of small routines like him making tea while I vent about work or quietly braiding my hair while I ramble about my love life. That sense of safety cracked at a relative’s very traditional wedding, where I was already drained and overstimulated when I was pushed into participating in a loud, public family dance I didn’t want to do, only to sit down afterward and have an older woman I had never met slowly look me up and down before making a sharp, humiliating comment about my body and how rarely I must dance. I swallowed my embarrassment to avoid causing a scene and assumed I’d never see her again, but to my shock my grandfather invited this same woman—an acquaintance from the wedding, not even a close friend—back to our house without asking or telling me, turning what I assumed was a brief stop into a stay that stretched from one night into more than a week. Since then, she’s lingered endlessly in our shared space, inserting herself into intimate conversations that aren’t directed at her, criticizing my body when I mention cramps, scolding me for crying over work stress, and speaking to me like a misbehaving child rather than an adult in her own home. I haven’t raised my voice, insulted her, or confronted her directly; I’ve only expressed privately to my grandfather, and my siblings that I feel uncomfortable, disrespected, and blindsided by having a judgmental stranger living in my space, yet somehow that has been twisted into me being labeled “rude” and ungrateful, leaving me wondering whether I’m actually wrong for wanting basic control over my own home and feeling hurt that my comfort seems to matter less than the feelings of someone I never invited?
