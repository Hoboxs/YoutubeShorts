My Boyfriend Thinks I’m Selfish for Scheduling Surgery During His Recovery.

Over the past several years, I worked incredibly hard to lose around 120 pounds. Reaching my target weight felt amazing, but the aftermath has been rough. I have a lot of excess skin now, and it has caused constant irritation. I deal with painful rashes, sores, and skin infections from the friction, especially during warmer weather or when I am active for long periods.

For a long time, I talked with my boyfriend about eventually getting surgery to remove the loose skin, but I wanted to wait until I was fully done losing weight before spending the money or going through a major recovery.

A few months ago, my boyfriend decided he wanted corrective eye surgery for his crossed eye. It would help with his vision and depth perception, and I fully supported him because I know it has affected his confidence and daily life for years.

The problem started when I finally got the opportunity to schedule my own surgery.

We both work in education, so summer break is really the only realistic time either of us can recover from a major procedure without risking our jobs or using an impossible amount of leave time. Recently my skin problems became much worse, and my surgeon had an opening available at the beginning of June. Timing-wise, it honestly seemed perfect because I would have the entire summer to heal before returning to work.

When I told my boyfriend I wanted to book it, he got upset immediately. He said I was being inconsiderate because his surgery is happening during the same month, and he thinks it is selfish for both of us to be recovering at once.

What confuses me is that I never expected him to delay or cancel his procedure for me. I also made arrangements for my cousin to stay with us temporarily and help out while we both recover so neither of us would be overwhelmed.

From my perspective, we both have legitimate medical issues that impact our quality of life, and we are both trying to use the only recovery window available to us. But he keeps acting like I deliberately planned this at the worst possible time for him, and now I am wondering if I really am being unfair by going ahead with it anyway.
