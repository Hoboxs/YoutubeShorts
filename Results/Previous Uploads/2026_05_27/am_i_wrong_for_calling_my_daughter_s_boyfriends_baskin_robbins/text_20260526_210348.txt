Am I wrong for calling my daughter’s boyfriends “Baskin Robbins”?

I’m a father in my 40s with two children, including my youngest daughter, Emma, who is 17. She’s a sweet, funny kid with a very romantic, almost fairy-tale view of relationships. She really wants an ideal long-term love like she sees in movies, and she wants it right now. My wife and I have a strong, happy marriage, and Emma often compares her expectations to what we have, even though I’ve tried explaining that it took years of growing up, mistakes, and patience to get here.

Over the past couple of years, Emma has gone through a long list of short teenage relationships. Nothing inappropriate—just quick changes from one boyfriend to the next. It’s been enough that I’ve honestly lost track of names. At some point, I made a joke to my wife that I wasn’t even going to try anymore and would just call them “Baskin Robbins,” because it feels like a different “flavor” every few weeks.

I never said this to Emma directly. It was just a private joke in frustration, not meant to insult her or reduce her to anything negative. I love her and I’m proud of who she is; I just find the constant turnover a bit overwhelming to keep up with.

The issue came up when I mentioned the joke to a friend, and she got upset, saying it made my daughter sound like she’s being disrespected or treated like a joke. That reaction surprised me because I didn’t see it that way at all—I thought I was just commenting on how fast teenage relationships can change.

Now I’m wondering if I’m misreading how that comes across. Am I wrong for thinking of it as harmless humor?
