Would I be wrong if I ignored the added pie option and made one of the original poll choices instead?

For context, I work in an office where I’m known as “the baker.” With Pi Day (March 14) coming up, I decided on my own to make a pie for everyone. No one asked me to, I just thought it would be fun.

To get input, I created a poll with 13 different pie options so people could vote on what they wanted. My name wasn’t on the poll itself, but I told people I was the one putting it together and that I’d be making the pie.

At some point, someone added two extra pie options that weren’t part of the original list. Now one of those added pies has the most votes overall.

The issue is: I didn’t offer that pie as an option. I carefully chose the original 13 based on what I’m comfortable making and what seemed realistic. I’m worried that if I don’t make the added pie, the one that now has the most votes, people will be disappointed.

Part of me feels like since I’m the one baking (voluntarily, for free), I should stick to the options I originally offered. But another part of me wonders if ignoring the top-voted pie will seem petty or disappointing.

So would I be wrong if I made the most-voted pie from my original list and ignored the added option that ended up winning?
