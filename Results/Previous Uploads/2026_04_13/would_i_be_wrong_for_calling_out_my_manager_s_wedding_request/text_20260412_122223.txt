Would I be wrong for calling out my manager’s wedding request?

I feel like I am sitting on something that is bothering me more than I expected, and I am not sure if speaking up would make things better or much worse. I run a very small business, and one of my managers, who I will call Rachel, is getting married in about six weeks. We have worked closely together for years. We talk often, support each other through stressful situations, and I have gone out of my way to help her in many parts of her life, both at work and outside of it. Because of that, I always thought we had a strong and respectful relationship.

Recently, I received an email from her that caught me completely off guard. She asked if we could close the business for an entire Saturday so that she could invite the staff to her wedding. Saturdays are our busiest days by far, the kind of day that helps keep everything afloat. We have been struggling financially for a long time, to the point where I have been working full time without paying myself just to keep things running. Losing a full Saturday of income would not be a small inconvenience. It would hurt us in a very real way.

What made it harder to process was realizing that everyone on the team was invited to the wedding except for me. Even people who no longer work for the business were included, but I was not. I never expected an invitation as something I was entitled to, but given how close we have been and everything we have gone through together, it still felt personal. It is difficult not to take it that way when I am being asked to shut down the business so everyone else can attend.

Since reading that message, I have felt a mix of frustration and hurt. On one hand, I understand that it is her wedding and she can invite whoever she wants. On the other hand, asking to close the business on our most important day, especially when I am already sacrificing my own income to keep things going, feels incredibly out of touch with our reality.

I have not responded yet because I do not want to react purely out of emotion, but I also feel like saying nothing would mean ignoring something that does not sit right with me. Part of me wants to explain how much this request impacts the business and how it came across to me personally. Another part of me worries that bringing up the invitation issue will only make things more uncomfortable and damage the working relationship we have built over the years.

Now I am trying to figure out whether speaking honestly about how this feels is reasonable, or if it would come across as inappropriate given that it is her personal event.
