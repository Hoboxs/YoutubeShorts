Was I wrong for walking out after my friend kept testing me?

I feel like I finally hit my limit with a close friend, and now I am questioning whether I handled it too harshly or if it was a long time coming. My friend, who I will call Mia, has this habit of setting little “tests” in our friendship. She will do things on purpose just to see how I react, and later she admits it by saying she wanted to see if I would notice something or go out of my way for her. I have told her more than once that this bothers me and that I would rather she just be honest if she needs something.

Last weekend, we went out for dinner with a couple of other friends. At the end of the meal, we all asked for separate bills. When mine came, I noticed that one of Mia’s drinks had been added to my total. She had already paid her own bill, so I decided not to make a scene in front of everyone or confuse the staff. I figured I would just mention it to her afterward.

As we were leaving, I casually brought it up and said it looked like one of her drinks ended up on my bill. I kept it light and did not make a big deal out of it. Her reaction was immediate and strange. She went quiet and distant, which made things feel uncomfortable, but I did not push it in that moment.

Later that night, she sent me a message saying she felt I was selfish and that she did not feel appreciated in our friendship. That is when she revealed that she had noticed the billing mistake but said nothing on purpose. She wanted to see if I would offer to cover the drink as a way of thanking her for helping me move months earlier. Hearing that made me incredibly frustrated. To me, helping a friend should not come with hidden expectations or delayed tests. If she wanted me to treat her or show appreciation, I would have gladly done it if she had simply asked.

Instead, it turned into another one of her silent experiments, and I told her that directly. I said it would have been better to communicate clearly rather than turning something small into a test of loyalty. She responded by saying she should not have to remind me to show appreciation, which only made things feel more tense.

We agreed to meet the next day to talk it out. I got there first, hoping we could have a calm conversation and clear the air. But as soon as she arrived, she immediately started speaking in a way that made it sound like I had done something seriously wrong and needed to reflect on my behavior.

At that point, I felt completely drained. It felt like no matter what I said, I would be put in the wrong again. So instead of arguing, I stood up, paid for my coffee, and left. Later, I sent her a message explaining that I cannot keep being part of these “tests” and that I need friendships where people communicate directly instead of setting traps.

Now I am wondering if walking out made things worse, or if it was the only way to finally set a boundary after feeling pushed so many times.
