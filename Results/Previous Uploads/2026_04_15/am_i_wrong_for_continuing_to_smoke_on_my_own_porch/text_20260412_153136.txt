Am I wrong for continuing to smoke on my own porch?

I feel stuck between wanting to mind my own business and not wanting to create tension with my neighbors. I own my own detached house, there is no shared building and no community rules, and the homes are spaced out with a decent amount of distance between them. I usually keep to myself and try to be considerate of the people around me.

From time to time, I smoke on my screened in porch. It is enclosed and somewhat private, and I try to be mindful about when I do it. If I notice my neighbors outside, I usually wait. I also try to choose times when the weather helps carry the smell away. It is not something I do constantly, just occasionally during the day or in the evening.

Recently, while I was on my porch, I overheard my neighbor loudly complaining about it. She was talking to her husband and saying she could not believe what I was doing, that she might come over to confront me, and that she was going to take action. Her daughter was encouraging her, which made the whole thing feel more intense. Hearing that caught me off guard and honestly made me uncomfortable in my own space.

Part of me feels like I am being judged unfairly, possibly because of my age. I also use it for personal reasons, and I am aware that the smell can be strong, which is why I try to limit when I do it. At the same time, I am on my own property and not breaking any rules where I live.

Now I am questioning whether I should change what I am doing just to avoid conflict, or if it is reasonable to continue as I have been, given that I have already been trying to be considerate. I do not want to escalate things with my neighbor, but I also do not feel like I should be pushed out of using my own space.
