Was I wrong for telling my future sister-in-law her opinion isn’t more important than mine?

I am getting married this September, and I’ve honestly been having so much fun planning everything. My fiancé and I both have pretty different interests, so we decided to make the wedding feel really us by weaving in subtle references to things we love.

He’s super into coding and tech stuff, and is obsessed with The Matrix. I’m more into movies, especially things like Breaking Bad, awards season films, and I love Ariana Grande. So we worked with a wedding planner to include small, hidden nods to those things, like little details in the invitations or décor that you wouldn’t notice unless someone pointed them out.

Nothing over-the-top, just fun, personal touches.

Last week, I invited my mom, future mother-in-law, sister-in-law, and my sisters to one of our planning meetings. Everyone seemed to like what we’d done, except my future sister-in-law.

Out of nowhere, she said we should cut all of it because it was “tacky” and that we should aim for something more “classy.” My fiancé calmly explained that this was something we both loved and had planned together.

She didn’t let it go.

She started listing off changes, saying we shouldn’t have chocolate cake, that it should be strawberry. That my dress shouldn’t be a princess style because it’s “too 80s.” That I should dye my hair brunette because it would suit me better. It stopped sounding like suggestions and started feeling like instructions.

At that point, I told her plainly: everything she just suggested changing, I actually like, so it’s staying.

She went quiet after that, but later pulled my fiancé aside and told him I was being disrespectful and that I should value her opinions more, apparently because she’s always dreamed of planning a wedding and I’m her future sister, so I shouldn’t “exclude” her.

Now she’s really offended, and my fiancé suggested maybe I should apologize to keep the peace. I didn’t think I did anything wrong, but I’ll admit I can be a bit blunt sometimes, so now I’m second-guessing myself.

Was I out of line for shutting her down like that, or was I just setting a reasonable boundary?
