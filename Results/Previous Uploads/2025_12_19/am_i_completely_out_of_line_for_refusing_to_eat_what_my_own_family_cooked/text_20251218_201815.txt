Am I Completely Out of Line for Refusing to Eat What My Own Family Cooked?

I’m a 19-year-old college student studying food science, and I’m currently burned out from nonstop exams and studying. Because of my major, I’ve learned exactly how foodborne illnesses happen and how quickly unsafe kitchen habits can make people seriously sick. I’ve tried explaining this at home in a calm way, pointing out small things like washing boards or not leaving food out too long, but my family always laughs it off and says I’m being dramatic or showing off because I’m in college.

Everything finally boiled over one night while dinner was being prepared. I watched a family member cut raw poultry and then immediately use the same board for fresh vegetables without cleaning it. When I spoke up, I was told to stop overthinking and just trust that everything would be fine. The idea that prayer mattered more than basic hygiene made my stomach drop, and I realized no one was taking the risk seriously.

I refused to eat the meal. That decision instantly turned the situation hostile. I was accused of acting superior, of thinking I was smarter than everyone else just because of my degree. Later, I noticed a large batch of food that had been sitting out at room temperature all night. Knowing it had been in unsafe conditions for hours, I threw it away despite how expensive it was, because the thought of someone getting sick felt worse than wasting money.

Since then, the house has gone quiet and tense. One parent feels personally insulted, as if my refusal to eat means I’m rejecting them rather than protecting myself. Another is furious, saying I embarrassed them and wasted food by acting like an expert instead of a family member. I never wanted a fight. I just didn’t want to gamble with my health while being treated like the problem for caring.

Now I’m stuck wondering if setting a boundary based on what I’ve learned really makes me unreasonable, or if I’m being punished for not pretending everything was fine.
