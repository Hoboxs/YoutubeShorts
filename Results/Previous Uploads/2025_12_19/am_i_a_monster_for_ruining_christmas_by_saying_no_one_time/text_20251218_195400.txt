Am I a Monster for Ruining Christmas by Saying No One Time?

My adult son and his wife have two small children, both under seven. I help with childcare occasionally, and we usually get along well enough. The plan this year was simple and already set: they were supposed to come over for Christmas dinner.

A few days before Christmas, I got a sudden call asking me to watch the kids for most of the day. When I asked why, I found out they had bought expensive tickets to a professional football game scheduled on Christmas Day. Their original backup babysitter—my daughter-in-law’s mother—had canceled because the drive was too long, so I became the last-minute solution.

I said no. I told them Christmas shouldn’t hinge on me sacrificing my day so they could attend a game, and that maybe the holiday should be spent with their children instead of at a stadium. That was apparently the wrong answer.

They immediately told me not to expect them at Christmas dinner at all. Now they’re angry, I’m being treated like the villain, and all because I refused to rearrange my holiday so they wouldn’t lose money on tickets they chose to buy.

So… did I really destroy Christmas by not stepping in, or am I being punished for setting a boundary?
