Am I wrong for refusing a sleepover with kids I don’t know in my home?

I’m a woman in my early thirties, and I share a young child with my former partner, a man a couple of years older than me. We separated more than two years ago, and the split wasn’t amicable. Our communication is strictly limited to matters involving our child, and that arrangement works best for me. I know very little about his personal life beyond the basics. He’s in a new relationship with a woman I’ve met exactly once, and she has several children of her own whom I’ve never met.

The only reason I even know these children exist is because my child talks about them occasionally and once showed me a birthday card they all signed. I don’t know their exact ages, only that they’re all in elementary school and older than my child. I’ve never interacted with them, never spoken to their mother, and have no real information about their routines, needs, or personalities. I’m comfortable with this distance and have never felt the need to change it.

Recently, my child’s bedroom was renovated with a fun, game-themed design he’s very proud of, and he’s talked about it a lot. He’s already had one overnight guest before, a close relative from his father’s side of the family whom I know well and trust completely. Because of that, my former partner approached me with the idea that two of his partner’s children should come to my home for a sleepover.

I immediately said no. I’m not opposed to sleepovers in general, but only in very limited circumstances. I’m not even ready to host overnight stays for my child’s closest friends yet, let alone children I’ve never met. I don’t feel comfortable taking responsibility for kids whose parents I don’t know and whose needs and boundaries I’m unfamiliar with. To me, that feels like an unreasonable expectation.

My ex pushed back, saying that I know him well enough and that the children are now siblings, so they should have shared experiences together. That argument didn’t reassure me at all. I pointed out that they already sleep under the same roof when my child is with him, so they’re not missing out on bonding time.

He accused me of being cruel and said I was creating unnecessary divisions by not allowing his partner’s kids to experience staying in my child’s newly decorated room. I explained that it’s just a bedroom, not a special event, and even suggested that if he wanted all the kids to have a similar experience, he could redesign a room in his own home. That suggestion didn’t go over well.

What matters most to me is that my child understands my boundaries and feels secure. When I explained that the sleepover wouldn’t be happening, he accepted it without argument and hasn’t brought it up again. He seems perfectly content spending time with his step-siblings when he’s at his father’s house.

Still, the situation has left me wondering whether I’m being unfair or simply protecting my space and my responsibilities as a parent.
