Am I wrong for ordering water at dinner even if I didn’t finish it?

My spouse and I earn a comfortable living and generally keep our expenses modest, but we’re currently on a winter getaway at a high-end mountain resort. The trip costs several thousand euros for the week and includes breakfast and dinner, though drinks at dinner are extra. One thing that stood out to me was that the restaurant charges a few euros for a liter of plain tap water, sourced from a local mountain spring. I think it’s overpriced, but it hasn’t ruined the vacation for me.

I have a respiratory condition that tends to leave me especially thirsty after long days outdoors in cold mountain air. At dinner one evening, my partner joked that the amount of water I drank cost as much as filling our swimming pool back home. We ordered a bottle of wine and a jug of water when we sat down, and partway through the meal I ordered another jug, just like the night before.

When my partner returned from the restroom and saw the extra water on the table, he looked visibly irritated. He didn’t touch the water for the rest of the meal. After dessert, I finished my wine and ice cream, and there was still about one glass of water left in the jug. He asked if I planned to drink it, and I said no, I was full and no longer thirsty after the multi-course dinner.

That’s when he scolded me for ordering more water if I wasn’t going to finish every drop. He poured himself a glass, drank it quickly, and said I should have asked before ordering more, even though our young child’s glass had also been empty earlier. I told him I felt he was being petty and that I’m an adult who can order water when I’m thirsty. I also said I shouldn’t be made to feel guilty for leaving a small amount behind when everyone at the table had already had enough.

Now I’m wondering if I was actually in the wrong for ordering another jug of water for the table, knowing I’d likely drink most of it myself but might not finish it entirely.
