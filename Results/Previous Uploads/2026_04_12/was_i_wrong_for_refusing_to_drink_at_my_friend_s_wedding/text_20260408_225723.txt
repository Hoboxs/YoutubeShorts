Was I wrong for refusing to drink at my friend’s wedding?

I have never been someone who drinks alcohol. There is no big story behind it, it is just not something I enjoy, and all of my friends know that about me. My friend Marissa and I have known each other since we were about thirteen, so when she asked me to be a bridesmaid at her wedding, I was genuinely happy and honored.

The tension started the night before the wedding at a dinner her family hosted for the wedding group. Throughout the evening, drinks were passed around, and every time I politely declined. At one point, everyone at the table was handed a shot for a toast. I took the glass so I would not stand out, but when it came time to drink, I left it untouched. After everyone finished, Marissa looked straight at me and asked if I was really not going to drink it. I calmly said no and even offered it to her so it would not go to waste. Instead of brushing it off, she stared at me and made a comment that sounded irritated, asking if I really had to make this into something right now. I remember feeling caught off guard because I had not made a scene at all. I had just quietly not taken the drink, like I always do.

The next day, things only became more uncomfortable. While we were getting ready, drinks were constantly being offered again. One of the bridesmaids asked if I never drink at all, and I explained that it just is not for me. That should have been the end of it, but it seemed to make me stand out more. I kept getting asked over and over if I wanted something to drink, and each time I said no. On top of that, I had driven myself to the wedding, so I was not planning to drink anyway.

Later during the celebration, the photographer set up a moment where all of us bridesmaids were supposed to hold glasses of wine and drink them together for a video. I did not want to ruin the moment, so I pretended to take a sip and then quietly passed my drink to another bridesmaid who offered to take it. I thought I handled it in a way that did not draw attention, but Marissa noticed. She came over clearly annoyed and told me that it would not hurt me to just drink it, saying it was no different than having juice or water.

That comment stuck with me. It felt like something small had turned into a much bigger issue for her, and I could not understand why. I had not refused loudly, I had not judged anyone else for drinking, and I had tried to go along with things without actually crossing my own boundary. Still, it seemed like my choice not to drink upset her far more than I ever expected.

Now that the wedding is over, I keep replaying everything in my head, wondering if I missed something or if I somehow made her feel uncomfortable on a day that was supposed to be about her. At the same time, I cannot shake the feeling that I was being pressured to do something I have always been clear about not wanting to do.
