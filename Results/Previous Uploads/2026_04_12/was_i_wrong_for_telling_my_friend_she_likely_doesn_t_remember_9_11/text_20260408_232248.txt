Was I wrong for telling my friend she likely doesn’t remember 9/11?

I have a friend, Marissa, who is twenty seven now. She often tells this story about where she was on September eleventh. According to her, she was sitting in a classroom when teachers rolled in televisions and showed the children what was happening live. She tells it in a very detailed way, like she clearly remembers everything, and she brings it up almost every time the topic comes up.

The thing is, her timeline never quite adds up. She was only three years old at the time. In the past, she told me she never even went to preschool and started school later at five, so the idea of her being in a classroom already did not make sense. Recently, when someone asked what kind of class a three year old would even be in, she quickly changed her answer and said it must have been a daycare instead.

Then people started asking more questions, and more parts of the story began to shift. She said she remembered everything so clearly because it was so traumatic that it became a core memory. Then she added that her mother had to evacuate a tall office building in another state because people were afraid more attacks would happen. But I clearly remember her telling me before that her mother did not even have a job during those early years. When that was pointed out, she changed the explanation again and said that event was actually why her mother stopped working.

At that point, it turned into a group conversation where a few people kept questioning her, and she kept adjusting details to make it fit. I stayed quiet in front of everyone because I did not want to embarrass her. Later, when we were alone, I told her gently that her story did not really line up and that it seemed unlikely she would have such a clear memory from that age. I suggested that maybe she was remembering things she had heard later or seen in documentaries, rather than something she personally experienced in detail.

She did not take it well. She got upset and said I was dismissing her and calling her a liar. Now things are tense between us, and I am wondering if I crossed a line by saying anything at all. I did not mean to attack her. I just felt like someone needed to be honest with her, especially since the story keeps changing and does not seem consistent.
