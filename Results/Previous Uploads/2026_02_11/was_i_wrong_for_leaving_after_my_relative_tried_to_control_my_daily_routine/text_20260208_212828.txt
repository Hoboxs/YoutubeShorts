Was I wrong for leaving after my relative tried to control my daily routine?

I agreed to stay with a family member by marriage for a few days to help out while her partner was out of the country for work. She has several young children, and since I live nearby and have a flexible schedule, I thought it would be helpful to stay over and assist with childcare and household tasks. At first, everything seemed fine, and I genuinely believed I was there to support her during a stressful period.

For years, I’ve had a consistent daily habit of going on a walk. It’s something I do no matter the weather or circumstances, usually for about half an hour, sometimes longer if conditions allow. While staying at her house, I continued this routine on a nearby path, shortening it compared to my usual walks and timing it during the kids’ downtime so it wouldn’t interfere with helping out. I even offered to watch the kids so she could take time for herself, but she brushed it off with comments that felt dismissive and oddly resentful.

Over the next couple of days, her remarks became more pointed. She questioned whether I really walked every day, suggested it was excessive, and implied there was something unhealthy or anxiety-driven about it. One morning, I couldn’t find my shoes. When I asked her about them later, she acted as though I had simply overlooked them, even though I was sure they hadn’t been where she claimed. I told her I didn’t find the situation funny and went on my walk anyway.

When I returned, she criticized me further, telling me I needed professional help and insisting that my routine was proof of an obsession. I asked her directly why my walks bothered her so much, and she said I was supposed to be there to help with the kids and that my absence—even though brief—was unfair to her. I explained again that my walks were short and scheduled carefully, but the tension didn’t ease.

The next morning, my shoes were missing again, this time after I’d placed them with my other belongings. That was the breaking point. I packed my things and left without making a scene. While driving home, she called and accused me of overreacting, being immature, and even made personal insults about my life choices. I told her I wasn’t willing to play games or be controlled, but I would still help in limited ways if absolutely necessary.

Now I’m being told by others that I took things too far, but I can’t shake the feeling that hiding my belongings crossed a serious line.
