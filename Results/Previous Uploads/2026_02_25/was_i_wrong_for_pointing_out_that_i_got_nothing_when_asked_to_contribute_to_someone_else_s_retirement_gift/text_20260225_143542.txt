Was I wrong for pointing out that I got nothing when asked to contribute to someone else’s retirement gift?

I retired from the Army Reserve after 22 years in the summer of 2024. When I left, I didn’t receive any kind of retirement gift, not from my unit, battalion, brigade, nothing. No plaque, no certificate, not even something small. Honestly, I told myself it didn’t matter. I was just glad to close that chapter of my life.

Recently, I got a text from some of the same guys asking if I wanted to chip in for a retirement gift for another soldier who’s about to retire. They’re planning to buy him a 1911.

I told them yes, but I made a joking comment along the lines of, “I didn’t even get a used Starbucks gift card when I retired.” I didn’t hear anything back.

A couple days later, I followed up and clarified that I wasn’t trying to be a jerk. I explained that I was just caught off guard being asked to contribute to a pretty significant gift when I hadn’t received anything myself, and that it felt like a bit of a gut punch.

Still nothing. No response at all.

These aren’t random acquaintances. I deployed to Iraq, Afghanistan, Kuwait, and elsewhere with these guys. Some of them stood in my wedding. I’ve known a few of them for nearly 20 years.

For what it’s worth, I did send $100 at the beginning of this whole thing before the conversation went quiet. But since then, it’s been complete silence.

Was I wrong for bringing up that I got nothing when I retired, or was that a fair thing to point out?
