Was I wrong for not being “discreet” about getting dentures at 34?

I’m 34 and I’m about to have a total dental extraction. I’ll most likely have dentures, with a small chance of implants down the line. Honestly? I’ve made peace with it. I’m actually excited. I’ve dealt with pain and insecurity about my teeth for a long time, and I’m looking forward to being pain-free and confident enough to smile without worrying.

Since I’ll be out of work for about a week to recover, I told my team what was going on. They were all supportive and happy for me.

One coworker, didn’t say anything at first. But later, when we were alone, she told me I should be more “discreet” about having dentures at my age. I asked what she meant, and she said she would be embarrassed to have dentures that young and that I shouldn’t be telling people.

I just stared at her and said something along the lines of, “I’m sorry you’d feel insecure about that, but I’m not going to be insecure about something millions of people have, especially something my coworkers will clearly notice.”

I’ll admit, I wasn’t warm about it. I was short. She got short with me back, and we didn’t really speak after that.

Now I’m wondering if I was too harsh. Maybe I could have been kinder in how I responded. But at the same time, I’m exhausted from years of pain and embarrassment about my teeth. I’m finally at a place where I feel hopeful and confident about fixing it, and I don’t feel like I should have to hide that.

So, was I wrong for responding the way I did?
