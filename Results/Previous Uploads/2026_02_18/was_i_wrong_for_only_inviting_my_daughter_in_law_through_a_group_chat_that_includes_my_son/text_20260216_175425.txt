Was I wrong for only inviting my daughter-in-law through a group chat that includes my son?

I’ll call my daughter-in-law “Ashley.” The ongoing issue is that she’s terrible at responding to texts or invitations. She often doesn’t reply at all, which has caused repeated misunderstandings.

For example, I’ll text her directly to invite her to something. She won’t respond. The event happens. Then I get criticized, usually by my son, for “not inviting” her. But I did invite her. She just didn’t answer.

At first, I even wondered if I had the wrong number. I don’t.

The biggest blow-up happened at Christmas. The women in the family were invited on a one-night ski trip. Ashley was included in the group chat and I also personally texted her. She never responded. We went on the trip, people posted pictures, and I received an angry call from my son accusing me of excluding her. When I told him she’d been invited, he didn’t believe me.

They then refused to come to Christmas unless I apologized. I refused, because I had invited her.

Since then, I’ve made a point to send all invitations in a group chat that includes my son. That way, there’s proof she was invited and no room for accusations. There have only been two events since then.

The latest was just a casual dinner last weekend. My son called me afterward asking me to stop including him in the invitation texts. I told him no. I said I’m done being accused of lying about not inviting her, and including him prevents that.

He says it’s causing problems between him and Ashley and that I’m making things worse. I told him that isn’t my responsibility, I’m simply protecting myself from being blamed unfairly.

Now he isn’t speaking to me, and some other family members have started following my lead and using group chats as well.

Am I being unreasonable for insisting on including him in the invitations so there’s no confusion?
