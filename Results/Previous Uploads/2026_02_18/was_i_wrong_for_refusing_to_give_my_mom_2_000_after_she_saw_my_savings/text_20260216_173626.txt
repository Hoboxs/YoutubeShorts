Was I wrong for refusing to give my mom $2,000 after she saw my savings?

I’m 19, in college, and still living at home. I’ve been working part-time since I was 16 and I’m very careful with money. I don’t go out much, I don’t spend on expensive things, and I’ve managed to save about $4,000.

Last week, my mom asked me to log into my banking app to transfer money for groceries because her card “wasn’t working.” While I was doing that, she saw my total savings.

Later that night, she told me that since I live under her roof, I should be contributing more and that it’s selfish to “hoard” money while she’s struggling. She asked me to give her $2,000 to help the family and said I could always earn it back.

For context, she works full-time. We’re not wealthy, but we’re not facing eviction or anything extreme. I already cover my own school expenses, my phone bill, and sometimes groceries. The money I saved is meant for moving out eventually or for emergencies.

When I told her I wasn’t comfortable handing over half my savings, she called me ungrateful and said I’m acting like a tenant instead of a daughter.

Now some relatives are saying that family helps family and that I should just give her the money. But it feels like this only became an issue once she realized how much I had saved.

I’m not refusing to ever help, I just don’t want to give up half of everything I worked for.

Was I wrong for saying no?
