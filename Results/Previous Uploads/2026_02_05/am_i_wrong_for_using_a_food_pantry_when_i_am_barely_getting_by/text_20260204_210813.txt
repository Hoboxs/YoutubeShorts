Am I wrong for using a food pantry when I am barely getting by?

I recently shared a photo online of groceries I received from a local food pantry because I was honestly relieved and excited to finally have consistent access to food. After an intake interview, I was approved for one prepared meal a day and one grocery pickup each week. For the first time in a while, I felt a little bit of pressure lift.

Not long after I posted, an acquaintance reached out to criticize me and accused me of taking resources away from homeless people. This caught me off guard, especially since we barely talk. It made me feel embarrassed and conflicted about something that had felt like a small win.

For context, my partner and I are both in our early twenties and together we bring in around eighteen hundred dollars a month. Our rent alone is fourteen hundred, and that does not include utilities, transportation costs, insurance, or basic necessities. By the time everything is paid, there is almost nothing left. Most months we are scraping by and stressing about how to eat.

Some people in my life seem to think I am doing something wrong because I have parents and grandparents who are better off financially. I have asked them for help before, but I am not comfortable having to rely on family every single week just to meet a basic need like food. I am actively applying for a second job and my partner is doing the same. We are trying hard to improve our situation on our own.

What hurts the most is being made to feel guilty for feeling relieved. I was not bragging or trying to take advantage of anything. I was simply grateful to have help during a really difficult time. Food is a basic necessity, and it feels unfair that anyone should be shamed for needing support to survive.

I keep going back and forth in my head wondering if I did something wrong, or if I am just someone who genuinely needs help right now and accepted it. I am trying my best to get back on my feet, but I am struggling to understand why that makes me a bad person in someone else’s eyes.
