Is privacy too much to ask on a long family trip?

I’m feeling completely worn down after a situation with my partner’s family that’s left me questioning whether I was wrong for finally speaking up. His relatives strongly pressured everyone to travel out of province for a milestone birthday celebration for an elderly family member, a trip that would take close to twenty hours round-trip by car. Because my partner and I are under serious financial strain right now due to ongoing fertility treatments and related expenses, they offered to cover the hotel costs so we could attend. At first that sounded generous, until it became clear there was an expectation attached. Every other sibling and their partner were automatically given their own private hotel rooms, but because we don’t have children, it was assumed that we would share a room with the older relatives. This isn’t new either; I’ve been put in this position multiple times in the past, and each time I’ve felt extremely uncomfortable but stayed quiet to keep the peace.

This time, though, the idea of another long trip with no personal space felt overwhelming, so I finally said something. I calmly explained that my partner and I needed our own room, not as a luxury, but as a basic boundary for our comfort and mental well-being. The response caught me off guard. I was told that if we wanted our own room, we would need to help pay for it ourselves, even though no one else was being asked to do so. It felt like we were being singled out and quietly punished for not having kids, something that’s already a painful topic for us. I tried to explain that privacy is important to me and that sharing a room makes me anxious and on edge, especially during an emotionally loaded family event, but the conversation quickly turned into guilt and pressure.

Eventually, feeling dismissed and cornered, I said that if having our own room wasn’t an option, then we simply wouldn’t be able to attend. That decision immediately created tension, and now I’m left sitting with a mix of anger, sadness, and doubt. On one hand, I feel like I finally set a reasonable boundary after years of discomfort. On the other, I’m being made to feel like I’m ungrateful or difficult for not just going along with what was expected of me. I can’t help but wonder whether standing up for my need for privacy was unfair, or if it was something I should have done a long time ago.
