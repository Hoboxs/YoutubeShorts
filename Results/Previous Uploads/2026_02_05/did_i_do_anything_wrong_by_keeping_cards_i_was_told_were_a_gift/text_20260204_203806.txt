Did I do anything wrong by keeping cards I was told were a gift?

In early winter, a colleague from work offered me a large collection of trading cards that she said her partner no longer wanted. I agreed, and she ended up handing over several briefcases packed full of cards. As I started sorting through them, I realized there were a significant number of valuable ones mixed in with the bulk, some worth quite a bit individually. That raised a red flag for me, so I messaged my colleague to double-check that her partner truly meant to give away the entire collection, especially knowing there was real value involved. She reassured me that it was intentional, said he no longer played, and told me to enjoy them.

Taking her at her word, I kept the cards. I built a few decks for myself, traded some with other players, and sold a portion to a local game shop, making a few hundred dollars in the process. Everything seemed settled until months later, when my colleague approached me at work and asked for all the cards back. She admitted that she had never actually asked her partner before giving them away and that he was furious when he found out. I told her I was willing to return whatever cards I still had, but explained that many had already been traded or sold and were no longer in my possession.

That answer upset her further, and she insisted that I also hand over the money I had made so her partner could try to replace the missing cards. I refused, explaining that I had acted in good faith based on what she told me and that I was already compromising by returning what remained. The conversation escalated, she accused me of making things worse, and eventually stormed off. Since then, she has barely spoken to me, and I am left holding the remaining cards while the situation stays tense at work.

To be clear, I had warned her up front that some of the cards were valuable, and the collection itself was mostly low-value items with a smaller number of expensive ones mixed in. I have no direct relationship with her partner, and she is the only channel I have to return anything to him. Later, I managed to contact him directly, shared proof that I had confirmed the gift beforehand, and arranged to give back what I still had. He told me not to worry about the sold or traded cards and planned to resolve the financial side directly with her. Even so, the whole situation has left me wondering whether I handled this as fairly as I could, or if I crossed a line by keeping what I was told was mine.
