Was I wrong for sharing my birthday cake instead of eating most of it?

I’m a man in my mid-twenties, and cooking has always been a big part of my life. I learned almost everything I know in the kitchen from my late grandfather on my dad’s side. One of his signature desserts was a caramelized banana dish that my family absolutely loves, and over the years it’s become something I make for nearly every family gathering. Ironically, bananas have never been my favorite flavor. I usually taste a small bite to make sure it turns out right, then serve myself a modest portion and let everyone else enjoy it.

I celebrated my birthday recently, and a lot of friends and relatives came over, including my aunt, my father’s slightly older sister. During the party, she handed me a homemade cake she had baked as a birthday gift. It was a banana-based cake inspired by the same dessert my grandfather used to make. I thanked her, put the cake on the dessert table with the rest of the food, and later cut myself a slice. I’ll admit it wasn’t a huge slice, but I did eat it, genuinely enjoyed it, and even told her at the party that it tasted great.

The next day, my aunt called me. She asked whether I liked the cake, and I said yes, reminding her that I’d already told her that in person. Then she asked why I had taken a smaller slice than usual. I hesitated, debating whether to smooth things over with a harmless excuse, but decided it was better to be honest. I told her that bananas aren’t really my favorite flavor, but I still wanted to try the cake since she made it for me.

That didn’t go over well. She said the cake was meant for me and that I should have eaten most of it myself. According to her, putting it on the dessert table felt like I had “re-gifted” her present to everyone else, which she found rude and ungrateful. I tried to explain that it didn’t make sense for me to eat a large amount of a flavor I don’t love, and that I thought sharing it was better than letting it go to waste. I also pointed out that the cake was completely gone by the end of the night, so clearly others really enjoyed it.

She then questioned why I keep making my grandfather’s banana dessert if I don’t even like bananas that much. I explained that I make it because it was special to him and because the rest of the family loves it, even if it’s not my personal favorite. She dismissed that explanation, said I was being disrespectful and ungrateful, and ended the call.

Afterward, I talked to my parents, siblings, and even some cousins on her side of the family, and they all felt she had overreacted. Still, the conversation stuck with me and made me wonder if I’d unknowingly crossed a line by not eating more of the cake she made for my birthday.
