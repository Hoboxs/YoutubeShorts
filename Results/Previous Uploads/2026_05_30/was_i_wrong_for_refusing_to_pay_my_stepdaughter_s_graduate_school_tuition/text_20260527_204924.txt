Was I wrong for refusing to pay my stepdaughter’s graduate school tuition?

I am 48 and have been married to my husband for several years. He has a 22-year-old daughter from his first marriage. From the beginning, she made it clear she did not want a close relationship with me, and I respected that completely. I never tried to replace her mother or force any kind of parental role. I stayed polite, supportive from a distance, and tried not to overstep. Our relationship has always been civil but emotionally distant.

Recently, she got accepted into graduate school. My husband had been saving money for years specifically to help with her education, but the amount he saved was based on the cost of a local university. Instead, she applied to a much more expensive program and was accepted there. The tuition is roughly double what he had planned for.

When my husband explained that he simply could not afford the full amount, she did not really seem concerned about the financial reality of it. Meanwhile, my husband became increasingly stressed trying to figure out how to cover the difference.

I have inheritance money that I have always kept separate from our shared finances. My husband and I had previously agreed that this money would eventually go toward buying a house together. At one point, he asked whether I would consider using part of it to cover the tuition gap for his daughter.

I told him no. I did not think it was my responsibility, especially given that we had always treated that money as separate and untouchable. I did agree to think about it further because I could see how overwhelmed he was, but I never promised anything.

Apparently, my sister-in-law somehow got the impression that I had agreed to pay. Without checking with me, she told my stepdaughter that I would be covering the tuition.

A couple of days later, my stepdaughter suddenly called me sounding warmer and friendlier than she ever has before. She thanked me repeatedly for helping with her future. I was completely confused and had to tell her there had been a misunderstanding because I was not paying for the school.

The mood changed instantly. She became angry and accused me of never caring about her future. She said I was selfish for “hoarding” money while she struggled with tuition costs. She even implied I was trying to control her with money. After years of emotional distance from her, it felt shocking that the first time she acted affectionate toward me was when she thought I was funding her education.

I eventually lost my patience and called her entitled. Since then, my husband has technically said he understands my decision, but he has become very quiet and withdrawn. I can tell he wishes I would just give in to avoid conflict.

Now I feel like I have somehow become the villain in a situation I never created. I never offered the money, yet everyone seems upset with me for refusing to hand it over once the expectation existed.
