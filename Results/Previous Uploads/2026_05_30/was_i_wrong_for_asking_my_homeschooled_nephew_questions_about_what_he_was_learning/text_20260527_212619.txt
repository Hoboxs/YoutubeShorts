Was I wrong for asking my homeschooled nephew questions about what he was learning?

My nephew is in sixth grade and has been homeschooled by my sister since the start of this school year. She pulled him out of school because she felt the local schools were not teaching enough and that she could give him a better education at home. His older sister still attends regular high school.

I have always been close with my sister and her children, and I work as a math tutor, so education naturally interests me.

Yesterday I was visiting their house and casually asked my nephew how homeschooling was going. He said it was going well, so I asked him what they had most recently covered in math. He struggled to explain it. He could not really remember the topic name or give an example problem.

At that point my sister stepped in and explained what they had been working on. Since the school year is almost over, I mentioned a few math topics that are normally introduced around sixth grade and asked whether he had learned those yet. My sister replied that they were “working at their own pace.”

I dropped the subject after that, but privately I was a little surprised. A few years ago, he seemed very advanced for his age, especially with mental math. My sister used to encourage me to ask him questions because he enjoyed answering them quickly. I know that being good at mental math does not automatically translate to being advanced in every area, but it still caught me off guard that he seemed unsure about basic curriculum questions.

Apparently afterward my sister told our mother that she felt I was acting judgmental and making her son uncomfortable. She said I was basically quizzing him and implying she was failing at homeschooling. She also pointed out that I am not a parent and do not understand how difficult it is to balance teaching, parenting, and running a household at the same time.

When my mother brought this up to me, I explained that I genuinely was not trying to embarrass anyone. I was curious because of my background in tutoring, not because I was trying to test him or criticize her choices. Still, I ended up telling my mother that my sister probably knows what is best for her own son and that maybe I should not have asked so many questions.

Now I am wondering whether I crossed a line without realizing it. I thought it was a normal conversation about school, but maybe it came across more like an interrogation than I intended.
