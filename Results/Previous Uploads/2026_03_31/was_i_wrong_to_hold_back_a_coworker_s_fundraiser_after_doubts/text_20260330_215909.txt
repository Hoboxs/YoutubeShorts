Was I wrong to hold back a coworker’s fundraiser after doubts?

I am a twenty six year old woman working in a small office where people tend to support each other when someone is going through a difficult time. Recently, one of my coworkers shared that she was dealing with a sudden medical situation and was struggling financially. She said she might not be able to cover her rent, and naturally, a few of us wanted to help.

I ended up organizing a small fundraiser. I reached out to everyone, collected contributions, and even added some of my own money. Altogether, it turned into a meaningful amount that could genuinely make a difference for her that month. The plan was simple, gather the money and give it to her directly.

Before handing it over, I checked in with her to see how she would prefer to receive it. Instead of answering right away, she hesitated and said she would get back to me. That caught me off guard, but I did not think too much of it at first.

The next day, another coworker approached me privately and mentioned that this same person had been talking about planning a trip out of town around the same time she had said she could not afford rent. That information made me uneasy. I did not want to assume the worst or rely on office gossip, so I tried to gently ask her again about her situation. Her responses felt unclear and did not quite match what she had told us earlier.

At that point, I started to feel uncomfortable handing over the money without understanding what was really going on. I did not accuse her of lying, but I decided to pause and hold onto the funds while I figured things out. I also let the people who contributed know that I wanted to clarify the situation before giving it to her.

She was upset when she found out. She said I had embarrassed her and made it seem like I did not trust her. Some coworkers agree with her and feel that once the money was collected for her, it should have been given without question. Others think it was reasonable to be cautious, especially since the situation did not fully add up.

Right now, the money is still set aside, untouched. I am trying to figure out the right way to handle this without creating more tension at work or mishandling something that other people trusted me to coordinate. I feel stuck between wanting to do the right thing for her and wanting to respect the intentions of everyone who contributed.
