Am I wrong for setting limits after covering for a friend’s cancellations?

I am twenty eight and, for a while now, I have been the one in my friend group who organizes outings. That usually means I handle booking tickets in advance and then everyone pays me back afterward. It has worked fine most of the time, but there has been an ongoing issue with one friend, who is twenty four and deals with a medical condition that can make them cancel plans at the last minute. When that happens, their partner also stays behind to care for them.

I understand that their situation is not something they can control, and I have tried to be flexible about it. The problem is that when they cancel after tickets have already been purchased, I end up covering the cost for both of them. This has happened more than once, and until recently I just absorbed the loss because I wanted to keep things easy and inclusive for everyone.

But my situation has changed. I recently lost my job, and I can no longer afford to keep covering those costs. After thinking it through, I spoke to my friend and explained that moving forward, if I am booking something in advance, I either need them to still pay for their share even if they cannot attend, or they should sit out those specific events. I also made it clear that they are still welcome at any gatherings that do not require upfront payment or reservations.

They did not take it well. They told me I was excluding them because of their condition and that it was unfair to expect them to pay for something they might not be able to attend. From their perspective, it feels like they are being pushed out of activities they enjoy because of something beyond their control.

Now the group is split. Some people think I am being unreasonable and that we should all just pitch in a little extra when needed so no one feels left out. Others think I have already done more than enough and that it is fair for me to set a boundary now that my financial situation has changed.

I feel torn. I do not want to exclude anyone, especially over something they cannot help, but I also do not think it is fair for me to keep taking on a cost I cannot afford anymore. I am trying to figure out if I handled this poorly or if this is simply a necessary boundary I should have set earlier.
