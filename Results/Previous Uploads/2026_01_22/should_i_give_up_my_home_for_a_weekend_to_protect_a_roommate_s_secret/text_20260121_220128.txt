Should I Give Up My Home for a Weekend to Protect a Roommate’s Secret?

I share a three bedroom apartment with two other students, and we all pay the same rent, utilities included. I will call myself Daniel, and my roommates Alex and Mira. Alex is barely around because he stays with his girlfriend most of the week, which usually leaves just Mira and me in the apartment. For me, this place is not a social hub, it is simply where I live. I go to lectures, study at the library, come home, and keep to myself. I am polite and friendly, but I value my space, and I pay for it.

A few days ago, Mira approached me with what she framed as a request but delivered more like an expectation. Her brothers are visiting next weekend, and her family does not know she lives with men. Because of this, she wants the apartment to appear as if she lives alone. Her solution was for me to leave the apartment for the entire weekend and move all my toiletries and personal items into my bedroom. I explained that I had nowhere to go. I cannot afford a hotel, I do not have friends nearby to stay with, and my girlfriend will be out of town visiting her family. Her response was to shrug and tell me that figuring it out was my responsibility. That response alone made me feel disrespected and angry.

I am now stuck feeling like the bad guy for refusing. Alex has already agreed to be gone, which makes me look even worse by comparison, even though he already spends most weekends elsewhere. I understand that Mira’s family situation is complicated and culturally sensitive, but I do not think it is fair for me to be asked to give up my home and spend money I do not have while still paying rent. I am not trying to expose her or cause drama, I just do not want to be forced out of my own living space. Am I wrong for refusing to leave and insisting that this is not something I should have to sacrifice for?
