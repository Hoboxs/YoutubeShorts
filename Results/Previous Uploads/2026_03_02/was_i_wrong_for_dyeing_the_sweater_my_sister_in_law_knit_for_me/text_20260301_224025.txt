Was I wrong for dyeing the sweater my sister-in-law knit for me?

My sister-in-law offered to knit me a sweater. I’m a knitter too, so I understand how much time, effort, and money go into a handmade piece. When she offered, I said yes, but asked if I could choose the color and offered to pay for the yarn.

She declined and said she wanted to use yarn from her own stash, she’s very into stash-busting. I didn’t push it.

She made the sweater, and it fit beautifully. The craftsmanship was great. But the color was beige, a shade I genuinely never wear. My wardrobe leans toward plums, roses, terra cottas, olives, and other soft autumn tones. I don’t gravitate toward plain beige at all.

I really did love the sweater itself, though. So instead of letting it sit unworn, I dyed it a rich green that suits me. Now I wear it all the time.

Recently, I wore it to a family gathering. She commented that it looked like the one she’d made me. I told her it was, but I had dyed it.

She stared at me for a long moment and then said, “I wish you would have told me you were going to do something like that. I wouldn’t have wasted my Malabrigo on you.”

I was honestly shocked. I told her I absolutely loved the sweater, I just wouldn’t have worn it in beige. She got visibly upset and said she would remember not to knit for me again. Then she left the room. We didn’t speak the rest of the evening.

I live in a different city, so I won’t see her for a while. I understand she has every right to decide not to knit for me again. But was dyeing the sweater really that disrespectful? I thought it was better to modify it into something I’d actually wear and appreciate than let it sit unused.
