Am I wrong for refusing a trip if we cannot share a bed?

This situation has turned into something much bigger than a simple vacation, and now I feel completely stuck. My brother and I were excited to plan a big family trip, the first one in years where all the cousins, who are now adults, could finally be together again. We started looking at large rental homes and agreed we would split the cost so everyone could stay under one roof like we used to.

At the beginning, my mother mentioned that she would prefer if my fiancé and I did not share a room. That caught me off guard, but I was very clear in my response. I told her that was not something I was willing to agree to. We have lived together for over two years, we share a home, and our wedding is only a few months away. From my point of view, being asked to sleep separately felt unreasonable and out of step with our reality as a couple.

After that conversation, things seemed to move forward normally. We spent weeks sharing rental options, discussing details, and no one brought up sleeping arrangements again. I thought the issue had either been dropped or quietly accepted.

Then everything shifted. My brother told me that our mother had already booked a house without telling me. When I called her, she calmly said that my fiancé and I could either sleep in separate beds or find somewhere else to stay. I was stunned. I asked why she had not been direct earlier, and she said her earlier “preference” was meant to communicate her expectation all along.

That is where things really broke down for me. I reminded her that I had clearly set a boundary, and that calling something a preference is not the same as setting a rule. She responded by saying that since she booked the house, she could treat it like her own home and decide how things work. I pointed out that we are still expected to pay a full share, which is a significant amount, so it does not feel right that she gets to control something as personal as where and how we sleep.

Now the only alternative is staying in a nearby hotel, which is more expensive and changes the entire experience. It means being separated from the group, losing the shared space, and constantly traveling back and forth. It turns what was supposed to be a relaxed family trip into something far less enjoyable.

The only reason I am even considering going at this point is because my brother, who has special needs, is very excited about the trip. That makes it harder to walk away completely. My fiancé has even said she is willing to go along with the separate sleeping arrangement for his sake, but I am struggling with the idea of giving in. It feels like I would be accepting something that disrespects our relationship, especially coming from family members who already do not fully support us.

Now I am left trying to decide what matters more, standing firm on what I believe is a reasonable boundary, or compromising to keep the peace for the sake of family, even if it means feeling uncomfortable the entire time.
