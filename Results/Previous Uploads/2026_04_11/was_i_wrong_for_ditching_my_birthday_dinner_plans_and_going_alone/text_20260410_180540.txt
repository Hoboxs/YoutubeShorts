Was I wrong for ditching my birthday dinner plans and going alone?

My birthday turned into a situation I did not expect at all, and now I am wondering if I handled it the wrong way. For some background, my wife is someone who needs structure and detailed plans for almost everything. She has been this way for years, and I know it is tied to how her mind works, so I try to be understanding. But lately I have been completely worn down from work, and all I wanted for my birthday was one simple, relaxed evening with no pressure.

When she asked me what I wanted to do, I told her very clearly that I did not want anything planned. I said I just wanted to go somewhere easy, like a casual chain restaurant, and decide in the moment what I felt like eating. I even gave examples like maybe pasta or maybe takeout, depending on my mood that day. I repeated several times that I did not want reservations, did not want to dress up, and did not want anything fancy. I just wanted to come home, go out for a laid back meal, and relax.

As the week went on, she kept asking for more details. What time, what place, whether we needed a reservation. Every time, I gave the same answer. I wanted to decide when the day came. I thought I was being clear, but she seemed frustrated that I would not lock anything in.

On the day of my birthday, I finally decided I wanted something simple and familiar. I was honestly looking forward to just sitting down somewhere casual, eating, and having a quiet night. When I got home around five, I was expecting to head out together like we talked about.

Instead, I walked in and saw her completely dressed up, ready to go somewhere formal. She told me she had made a reservation at an upscale restaurant in the city as a surprise, and that we needed to leave soon to make it on time. The moment she said it, I felt my mood drop. It was the exact opposite of everything I had asked for.

I told her I did not want that. I reminded her of all the times I said I wanted something simple. She got upset and said she had put in a lot of effort to plan this for me, and that it was supposed to be special. The conversation quickly turned into an argument, with both of us feeling unheard.

In the end, I told her to forget it and left on my own. I went to a casual restaurant by myself, had the quiet meal I originally wanted, and came home later. But instead of feeling better, I walked into another argument. She was hurt that I rejected what she planned, and I was frustrated that my wishes had been ignored in the first place.

Now things feel tense between us. I understand she was trying to do something nice, but I cannot shake the feeling that she did not actually listen to what I wanted. At the same time, I wonder if leaving like that made everything worse than it needed to be.
