Was I really wrong for playing Solitaire late at night in my own bed?

It was a quiet Saturday night, edging toward midnight, and I was finally relaxing after a long, draining week. I was in bed with my laptop resting on my knees, the screen dimmed low, volume muted, and the apartment completely still. No TV, no music, no phone calls—just me, my sleeping pets, and a single calming game of Solitaire, the kind where you slowly match tiles and click your way through patterns.

I live in an ancient, three-story house that’s been chopped into separate apartments sometime before soundproofing was ever invented. Every creak echoes, every pipe sighs dramatically. I’m in the middle unit, sandwiched between neighbors above and below, so I’m always hyper-aware of noise. That’s why what happened next felt unreal.

Out of nowhere, angry shouting blasted up through the floor. Someone below me yelled, “Can you do this tomorrow?” followed by “You’ve had all day, why now?” Then it escalated into full-on swearing. Before I could even process what was happening, they started slamming on the ceiling—hard—like I was throwing a midnight rave instead of quietly clicking cards.

My heart started pounding. I felt embarrassed, rattled, and honestly a little scared. I immediately closed my laptop, abandoning my unfinished game, and sat there in the dark listening to the silence settle back in. I hadn’t even finished matching the dragon tiles.

What makes this worse is that I’ve never spoken to these downstairs neighbors in real life—not once. The only interaction we’ve ever had is them yelling through the ceiling. Meanwhile, the neighbors above me, who live directly over my bedroom and whom I’ve talked to before, have never complained or hinted that I’m noisy. Not even once.

Now I’m stuck wondering if the gentle clicking of a trackpad while playing Solitaire really counts as unacceptable noise, or if I was just aggressively shut down for daring to exist quietly after 11:00 PM. Was I actually being inconsiderate, or did I get bullied into silence for playing a calm puzzle game in my own bed?
