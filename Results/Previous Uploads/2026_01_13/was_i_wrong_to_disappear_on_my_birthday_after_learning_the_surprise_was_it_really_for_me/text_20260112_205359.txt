Was I wrong to disappear on my birthday after learning the surprise wasn’t really for me?

I’ve never enjoyed big birthday productions and everyone close to me knows I prefer something quiet, but despite repeatedly saying this, my sister Lena—who thrives on grand gestures—started acting suspicious in the weeks leading up to my birthday, dodging questions and teasing plans I explicitly asked her not to make. The night before, a friend accidentally revealed there was a full-blown party planned at our parents’ house with extended family, coworkers, and, to my shock, my ex, whom I broke up with only weeks earlier after a painful ending and have clearly asked to avoid entirely. When I confronted Lena, she admitted the party wasn’t just for my birthday at all but a calculated attempt to force a reunion, saying it would be “good for everyone” and that I’d eventually thank her, even accusing me of being ungrateful and dramatic for objecting. Feeling blindsided and furious at the idea of being ambushed into emotional reconciliation on my own birthday, I turned off my phone the next day and spent it quietly with a close friend instead, only to return to a flood of angry messages accusing me of humiliating my sister and ruining something she claims was done “from the heart,” leaving me wondering if I was truly wrong for choosing my own peace over a party that was never really about me in the first place?
