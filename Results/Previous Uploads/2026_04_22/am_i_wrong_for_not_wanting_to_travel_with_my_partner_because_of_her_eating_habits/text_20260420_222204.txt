Am I wrong for not wanting to travel with my partner because of her eating habits?

I’m a 37-year-old man and have been with my partner (35) for a while now. Overall, things are good between us, but there’s one issue that keeps coming up and is starting to affect bigger parts of my life, her extremely limited diet.

She doesn’t eat vegetables at all, and eating in general can be a challenge for her. For example, if she orders something like a burger, it has to be completely plain, just the meat and cheese. She won’t pick off toppings like lettuce or tomato; they simply can’t be there in the first place. When it comes to other foods, she sticks to very specific versions, like only eating tacos from certain fast-food places instead of more traditional ones.

Because of this, going out to eat together can be difficult. I’ve noticed that I often end up choosing restaurants based on whether there’s something she’ll eat, rather than what we both might enjoy. Over time, this has gotten frustrating for me, especially because trying new food and exploring different cuisines is something I really value.

We’ve tried to compromise a bit. Right now, I go out to eat by myself about once a month so I can enjoy whatever I want without worrying about whether she’ll be able to eat anything there. It helps a little, but it doesn’t fully solve the issue.

Recently, I’ve had opportunities to travel more, and that’s where this concern is really coming to a head. For me, traveling is heavily tied to experiencing new food and cultures. But when I imagine traveling with her, I worry that every meal will turn into a stressful situation, trying to find something she can eat, limiting where we go, or dealing with tension if there aren’t familiar options available.

At this point, I’m honestly considering traveling on my own instead of bringing her along. I feel guilty about it because I don’t want to exclude her, but I also feel like I’d be constantly compromising something that’s important to me.

I don’t think she’s doing this on purpose, and I know everyone has their own preferences and comfort zones. But it’s getting to a point where I feel restricted and frustrated.

Am I being unreasonable for wanting to travel without her because of this?
