Am I wrong for refusing to help my ex-wife financially and telling my kids it’s not my responsibility?

I divorced my ex-wife about five years ago after discovering she had secretly built up a large emergency fund. At the time, she had been laid off, and I believed we were struggling financially. I was working extremely long hours, sometimes 60 to 80 hours a week, just to keep up with bills, thinking we were barely getting by. It turned out she had saved around $50000 without telling me and was still adding to it.

The divorce itself was messy, and during the process I found out about a lot of spending and credit card debt I hadn’t known about. We ended up selling the house and going our separate ways.

Our kids were in high school at the time. Now they’re both in college and split their time between staying with me and their mom.

The current issue is that my ex-wife is struggling financially. I’m in a stable place now and recently bought a house, but she’s having trouble holding down a job and is at risk of being evicted from her apartment.

My kids have been asking me to help her financially. I’ve said no multiple times, and it’s turned into a bigger conflict. They think I’m being heartless and even say that her situation is partly my fault because I initiated the divorce.

I told them that their mother’s financial problems are not my responsibility anymore. Since then, they’ve been distant and are currently not speaking to me much.

Now I’m starting to question whether I handled this the wrong way.

Was I wrong for refusing to help and saying it’s not my responsibility?
