Is it wrong to draw a line over public hygiene?

I’m a woman in my late thirties who had planned all day to visit a large, busy retail complex after finishing work. My partner, who is a few years younger than me, came over that evening, and I gave him several reminders that we would be leaving soon so he had time to get ready. Before we left, I asked if he had brought shoes with him, which is a long-standing point of tension in our relationship. He told me he hadn’t.

I have explained many times that while he is free to choose not to wear shoes when he leaves the house, I am also free to decide whether I want to be involved in that choice. I told him that I find going barefoot in crowded, upscale public spaces unsanitary and uncomfortable. He immediately accused me of insulting him and calling him disgusting as a person. I clarified that I wasn’t attacking him personally, only the behavior of walking barefoot in a packed shopping center.

During the drive, I told him that if he wasn’t willing to wear shoes, he would need to stay in the car while I went inside. I reminded him again that this was the result of his decision, not a punishment. He then escalated things by accusing me of not wanting to spend time with him, not wanting to be with him at all, and deliberately shaming him.

From my perspective, this boundary has been clearly communicated many times, and I followed through on exactly what I had said before. From his perspective, he feels judged and rejected. Now I’m left wondering whether standing firm on this issue makes me unreasonable, or whether it’s fair to set limits around behavior that makes me deeply uncomfortable.
