Is it unfair to enjoy my own meals during a roommate’s recovery?

I live with a housemate who recently underwent a medical procedure and has been restricted to a very plain recovery diet that excludes most of the foods she usually loves. She’s been understandably frustrated about it for weeks. I’m in my late twenties and work a demanding job that often keeps me out late, especially one evening a week when I have to commute to a distant branch office, which adds a long drive on both ends of the day. By the time I get home on those nights, I’m exhausted and starving, so I usually pick up takeout as a small way to decompress and enjoy my evening. One night, I grabbed a dish I’d been craving, which also happens to be one of my housemate’s favorite meals. I ate it casually in the shared living space while watching a show, didn’t comment on it, and stored the leftovers in a separate fridge so they wouldn’t affect her at all.

The next morning, though, she was noticeably passive-aggressive and told me I’d been inconsiderate for eating foods she likes while she can’t have them. She went on to say that everyone in the house should avoid eating her favorite things around her until she’s fully recovered, because seeing them makes her feel worse. I sympathize with the fact that recovery is hard and limiting, but I also feel that working long hours and taking care of myself includes being able to eat what I want, especially when I wasn’t intentionally rubbing it in or interfering with her diet in any way. Now I’m stuck wondering whether enjoying my own food in shared spaces during her recovery really makes me unfair, or if this is an unreasonable expectation being placed on everyone else.
