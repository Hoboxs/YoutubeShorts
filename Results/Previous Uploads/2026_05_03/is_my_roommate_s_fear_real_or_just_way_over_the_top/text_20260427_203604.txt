Is my roommate’s fear real or just way over the top?

I want to be fair here, because I do understand that fears can be very real and very intense. I am twenty two, and I live with two roommates around my age. One of them insists she has a serious fear of spiders, and while I am not exactly fond of them either, the way she reacts has started to feel completely overwhelming to live with.

For some context, I have dealt with my own irrational fear in the past and even went through therapy for it, so I know how powerful these things can be. That is part of why I have tried to be patient. But lately, it has started to feel like something else entirely. For example, one day I was casually talking about going out for sushi with my partner, and I mentioned a roll that had the word “spider” in its name. The moment I said it, she let out a full scream and told me I was not allowed to use that word around her, insisting I replace it with something else. Another time, someone else said the word in conversation, and she immediately covered her ears and started making distressed sounds like she could not handle even hearing it.

The situation that really pushed me over the edge happened recently when we were coming home. Outside our building, near the stairs, there was a spider underneath the steps. This is not unusual for where we live. There are trees and greenery everywhere, so we see them from time to time. But this time, she froze completely, screamed, and refused to go up the stairs at all.

My other roommate tried to help by blocking the spider from view so she could walk past it, but that was not enough. She still would not move. Then my other roommate went and got cleaning spray and killed the spider right in front of us. I watched it fall away, completely gone, but even then she refused to go up.

We spent about fifteen minutes trying to convince her it was fine. Eventually, my other roommate had somewhere to be, and I had work deadlines I could not ignore, so I went upstairs. She stayed there alone for almost an hour before finally coming up. When she did, she was furious with me. She told me I was not supportive and that I should have stayed with her and helped her through it because of her fear.

That is where I am struggling. I understand that fears can create real mental barriers, and I do not want to dismiss that. But at the same time, this feels extreme to the point where normal life becomes impossible. Being told I cannot even say a common word, or being expected to stop everything for an hour because of a spider that is no longer there, feels like too much.

Now I am questioning myself. Am I being insensitive and failing to support someone who is genuinely struggling, or is this situation crossing into something unreasonable that I am allowed to push back on?
