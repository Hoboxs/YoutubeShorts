Was I wrong for telling my sister her situation is her own fault?

I’m a 29-year-old woman, and my older sister (31) and I have never had the easiest relationship. I care about her, but I keep some distance because of her patterns in relationships, she’s cheated in every serious relationship she’s been in, and she tends to act in ways that revolve entirely around her own wants and needs.

Her husband (30), on the other hand, is someone I’ve known since school. He’s a genuinely kind person, the type who goes out of his way to help others. They were together for years and married for a while before everything recently fell apart.

They separated after my sister had an affair, with his longtime best friend. It understandably hit him very hard, though he’s been trying to stay composed for the sake of their child and to keep things civil.

I stayed out of the situation as much as I could. I checked in with both of them briefly, but I didn’t want to get involved in the fallout.

Recently, my sister came to visit me, something she’s never done before, even though I’ve lived a few hours away for years. During the visit, she spent a long time venting about her current situation. She said the man she left her husband for isn’t treating her well, isn’t contributing at home, and hasn’t changed despite how things started. She also said she regrets leaving her husband and had even told him so, but he turned her down.

I tried to steer the conversation away because I really didn’t want to get pulled into it, but she kept going for quite a while. Eventually, I got frustrated and told her that she should have thought about all of this before starting a relationship with someone else while still married, that her current situation didn’t just happen out of nowhere.

She left shortly after that.

My partner thinks I was just being honest, but my mom feels like I was too harsh, especially since my sister seems to regret what she did.

Now I’m wondering if I crossed a line by saying it so bluntly, even if there’s truth to it.

Was I wrong for responding that way?
