Am I wrong for not leaving work when my spouse ran out of gas?

I’m a 42-year-old man, married to my wife (39) for over a decade, and we have two kids in elementary school. I work full-time, while she works part-time and is also finishing a degree online. She deals with anxiety and attention-related challenges, which can sometimes make day-to-day situations more overwhelming for her.

Last week, on a day she wasn’t scheduled to work, she decided to visit a relative who lives about 45 minutes away. I didn’t know she had gone there, so I was surprised when she called me around midday, clearly upset. It took a bit to understand what was going on, but she eventually explained that she had run out of gas somewhere unfamiliar and didn’t know what to do.

I was at work at the time with meetings lined up that I couldn’t miss. I asked what she needed from me, and she said she wanted me to come get her. I explained that I couldn’t leave, but I tried to help her think through other options. I suggested calling the relative she had just visited, roadside assistance, a rideshare, or even another friend. She felt overwhelmed by all of that and kept insisting I come help her directly.

I stayed calm and told her I understood she was stressed, but I genuinely couldn’t leave work at that moment. I encouraged her again to try one of the alternatives, but she got frustrated and ended the call.

After that, I tried reaching her again, but she didn’t answer calls or texts. Meanwhile, I started worrying because she’s usually the one who picks up our kids from school, and I didn’t know if she was okay or if I needed to step in. I contacted the school just to make sure I’d be notified if needed, and later found out she did make it there.

When I got home, she wouldn’t speak to me and stayed distant all weekend. I apologized several times for not being able to leave work, but she didn’t engage. Eventually, she told me she feels like she can’t rely on me anymore.

Now I’m wondering if I handled this poorly. I didn’t ignore her, I tried to offer solutions, but I also didn’t drop everything to go help her.

Was I wrong for not leaving work in that situation?
