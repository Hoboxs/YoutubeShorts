Did I Ruin a Family Visit by Changing My Mind About a Sleepover?

I am currently in the middle of a very stressful exam period for law school, and I also have two young children to look after. My husband’s relatives are visiting from abroad, and they have been staying at a large countryside home outside the city. Over the past few weeks, we have already made several trips there to spend time with them, and during one of those visits we casually said they were welcome to come see us in the city as well.

Last night, very late in the evening, they suddenly announced that they planned to drive up the next day and stay overnight at our house.

The problem is that nothing about our situation is ready for guests. The house is messy, I have an early morning appointment to take one of my children to the dentist, and I desperately need quiet time to study. I suggested they come the following day instead for lunch so we could prepare properly, and I made it clear that hosting a sleepover on such short notice would not work for me.

My husband did not give them a direct answer. Instead, he kept things vague and commented that the house was not in great shape but did not actually say no. Later, during a phone call with his sister, he put the call on speaker and when she directly asked if they could stay over, he physically handed me the phone.

I felt completely put on the spot. I did not want to reject them directly in that moment in front of everyone, especially after my husband had already left the decision open, so I awkwardly agreed to them staying over even though I was already overwhelmed.

As soon as the call ended, I told my husband I was upset. I felt like he had avoided taking responsibility and instead placed me in a position where I had to either agree or be the one to disappoint his family. I told him it felt unfair and that he should have clearly said no earlier instead of letting it get to that point.

I then asked him to call them back, explain the situation, and cancel the overnight stay because I could not realistically manage it right now.

Now I feel torn. I do not want to come across as rude or inconsiderate, especially since this is one of their last chances to visit before traveling back abroad. At the same time, I feel overwhelmed, exhausted, and frustrated that I was put in a position where I felt pressured into agreeing in the first place.

So I am wondering if I handled this poorly by changing my answer after already saying yes.
