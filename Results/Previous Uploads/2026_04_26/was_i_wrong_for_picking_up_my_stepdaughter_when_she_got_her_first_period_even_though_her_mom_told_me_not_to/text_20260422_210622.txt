Was I wrong for picking up my stepdaughter when she got her first period, even though her mom told me not to?

I’m a 25-year-old woman, currently six months pregnant. My husband (29) has a 10-year-old daughter from a previous relationship. I love her deeply and care about her like she’s my own, but I’m careful not to overstep, especially when it comes to discipline or major parenting decisions.

Recently, something happened that has me questioning whether I crossed a line.

While at school, my stepdaughter called my husband complaining of severe stomach pain. She later realized she was bleeding, and he quickly understood that she had started her first period. He couldn’t leave work, and her mom initially wasn’t answering calls. I was at home, and I’m listed as an approved pickup person at her school.

I offered to go get her so she wouldn’t have to sit there uncomfortable. Around that time, her mom finally called back, and the conversation became tense. She said this was something she wanted to handle herself and described it as a special moment between her and her daughter. I understood that perspective, but the immediate issue was that her daughter was in pain and stuck at school without proper support.

Her mom insisted that she would handle it later and said my stepdaughter could just manage in the meantime. Meanwhile, my stepdaughter called me directly, crying and asking for help because she felt so unwell and wanted to go home.

My husband told me to go ahead and pick her up, so I did. I signed her out, got her some pain relief and a few essentials, and brought her home to rest. After that, she seemed much more comfortable.

Several hours later, her mom called, very upset. She said I had overstepped and taken away a meaningful moment she wanted to share with her daughter. She also felt I had ignored boundaries and accused me of trying to take on a role that wasn’t mine.

I explained that my only goal was to help her daughter in that moment, not replace her or take anything away from her as a parent. But she made it clear she saw it differently.

My husband supports what I did and believes it was the right call given the situation. Still, I can’t shake the feeling that maybe I should have handled it differently.

Was I wrong for stepping in to help?
