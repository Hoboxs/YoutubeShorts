Am I wrong for not wanting to turn our family vacation into a group trip?

My partner and I (early 30s) have a 4-year-old daughter. We’ve been pretty intentional about how we raise her, limiting screen time, encouraging outdoor activities, reading, and building hobbies. It’s taken a lot of effort, but she’s gotten to the point where she can handle hikes and even camping trips. Of course she still has moments, she’s a kid, but overall she does really well.

This summer, we planned an international trip focused on outdoor activities. It’s something we’ve put a lot of thought, time, and money into, and we’re really excited about it.

I mentioned the trip to a friend of mine who has a 6-year-old. She immediately got excited and started suggesting that her family should join us. I told her we were planning this as just a family trip and already had most things booked, so it wouldn’t work.

She kept pushing, first suggesting we change our accommodations to go together, then saying her family could just book separately and join in. The issue is, I really don’t want this to turn into a shared vacation.

From experience, her child isn’t used to the kind of activities we’ve planned, like long hikes or spending extended time outdoors. In the past, when we’ve done things together, plans often shift to accommodate her child’s needs if he’s struggling or bored, which I completely understand in a casual, local setting, but this is a big, carefully planned trip for us.

I also know that if we overlap, there will likely be pressure, spoken or unspoken, to adjust plans or spend time together, even if I’ve said otherwise. I want this trip to stay focused on what we’ve planned for our own family.

I tried to explain that the activities might not be enjoyable for her child, but she brushed that off and said they’d figure it out or we could adjust as needed. That’s when I made it clear that we would not be changing anything and that while she’s free to travel wherever she wants, we wouldn’t be coordinating plans or meeting up unless it happened to align exactly with what we were already doing.

She’s now upset and says I’m missing out on creating shared memories and that trips like this are special when families do them together.

Now I’m wondering if I was too firm or if I should have handled it differently.

Was I wrong for setting that boundary?
