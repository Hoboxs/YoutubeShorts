Was I wrong for stepping down as best man because I can’t afford my brother’s bachelor party?

I’m 26 and supposed to be the best man for my older brother, “Daniel,” who is 29 and getting married next month. I love him, but he’s always been someone who wants to look like he’s living a luxury lifestyle, especially online, even though his actual budget doesn’t really match that image.

The other night he called what he described as an “important planning meeting” with all the groomsmen over video chat to go over the bachelor party. I assumed we’d be talking about something simple, maybe renting a cabin for a weekend, grilling, hanging out, or going to a brewery.

Instead, he shared his screen and started presenting a full slideshow outlining a four-day trip to Las Vegas. The plan included splitting a penthouse suite, booking bottle service at multiple nightclubs, and renting exotic sports cars for an afternoon. After the call he emailed everyone a detailed cost breakdown.

My share alone, not including my own flight, food, or any spending money, would be a little over $3,500.

To make it even stranger, he also added a “group outfit” to the list of required expenses. He wants all the groomsmen to wear matching maroon velvet jackets and these huge novelty watches shaped like roulette wheels so we “look legendary” walking through casinos together.

The part that makes this harder to swallow is that Daniel is usually extremely frugal when it comes to his own spending. His fiancée has mentioned before that he spends a lot of time hunting down the absolute cheapest options online, and apparently he even bought bulk groomsmen gifts from a wholesale site to save money. So it feels odd that he’s completely comfortable asking everyone else to spend thousands for one weekend.

After the meeting I called him privately and explained that I simply can’t afford something like that right now. I work in logistics and I’m trying to save money so I can eventually buy a home. Dropping four thousand dollars on a bachelor party, especially on short notice, just isn’t realistic for me.

He got really angry and accused me of not supporting him. He said this was a once-in-a-lifetime celebration and that as the best man it’s my job to help make the trip happen.

Now my parents are also weighing in and telling me to just put the expenses on a credit card and deal with it later because “it’s your brother’s wedding.”

I told Daniel that if this trip is truly mandatory for the wedding party, then I might have to step down as best man because I can’t responsibly spend that kind of money.

Now the whole situation feels tense and I’m questioning whether I’m being unreasonable.

Was I wrong for drawing that line?
