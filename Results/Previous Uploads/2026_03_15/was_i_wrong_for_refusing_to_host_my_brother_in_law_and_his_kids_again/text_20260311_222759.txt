Was I wrong for refusing to host my brother-in-law and his kids again?

My husband and I have been together for about 15 years. His brother and his brother’s wife have also been together for roughly the same amount of time. Both of our families have two kids. Ours are 7 and 4, and theirs are 10 and 4.

We live on the same street, so naturally the kids like spending time together and playing. My relationship with my sister-in-law isn’t great, but it’s civil. We mostly keep our distance and let the kids see each other through my husband and his brother.

Over the last couple of months, the kids have started having regular weekend playdates, which I think is great. The issue is that almost all of those playdates happen at our house.

Whenever they come over, my husband and his brother usually go off to another room and play video games. That leaves me supervising four kids, managing the chaos, and cleaning up the mess afterward.

Last weekend when they were over again, I half-jokingly mentioned that the next playdate should happen at their house. I said it would be nice if things rotated so I could also have a quiet weekend sometimes or just take care of things around the house without constantly managing kids.

The very next day I came home from work and found my brother-in-law there again. He and my husband were playing video games while the living room was covered in toys from the kids playing. I was really frustrated. Eventually everyone went outside to the playground, but by that point I was already irritated.

It’s been a few days since then. My husband keeps telling me that his nephew wants to come over again and tries to make me feel guilty for saying no. What bothers me is that they still haven’t arranged any playdates at their house, and apparently haven’t done so in over a month.

I’m not trying to stop the kids from seeing each other. I just asked that the visits rotate between houses so the responsibility isn’t always on me.

Now my husband is acting like I’m the one being unfair.

Was I wrong for putting my foot down and saying they shouldn’t keep coming over if it’s always going to fall on me to host and clean up?
