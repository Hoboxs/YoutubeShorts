Was I wrong for using my phone in the locker room to distract my toddler?

My wife and I take our 2-year-old son to swim class every Saturday morning. After class, we have a routine: I change first, then my wife showers him. After that, I change him while she gets dressed.

This particular morning, after I dressed our son and gave him a snack, he was very wiggly and wanted to run around the locker room. I couldn’t take him to the lobby because he would cry for his mom. So I brought him into one of the private changing stalls and pulled the curtain halfway closed. From inside the stall, the only visible part of the locker room is the sink area, not the main changing area. There were plenty of empty stalls available.

To keep him from running around and potentially making others uncomfortable, I put a YouTube Kids video on at the lowest possible volume and set the phone on his lap while I packed our bags. My wife was still changing in the main locker room area.

A woman who had been at the sink came over and told me to put my phone away. I asked if she worked there. She said no, but that there’s a sign saying no phones in the locker room.

I explained that I had intentionally moved into a private stall to avoid making anyone uncomfortable and that I was only using the phone briefly to keep my toddler from running up to people while they were changing. I then pulled the curtain fully closed.

About three minutes later, she came back, opened the curtain, and said she would call an employee if I didn’t put the phone away. I told her to leave me alone and closed the curtain again.

A few minutes later, my wife finished changing and we left. On the way out, we spoke with the front desk employees. They told us the no-phone signs are primarily to prevent video recording, voice recording, or calls, and that they appreciated that we used a private stall before taking the phone out.

I understand that people are sensitive about phones in locker rooms, and I would never record or invade someone’s privacy. I genuinely thought I handled it responsibly.

Was I the asshole here?
