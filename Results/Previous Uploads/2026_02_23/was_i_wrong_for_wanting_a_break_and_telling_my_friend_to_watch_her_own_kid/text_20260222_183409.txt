Was I wrong for wanting a break and telling my friend to watch her own kid?

I’m 22 year old women and have a 2-year-old. My friend who is a 24 year old woman also has a 2-year-old. Recently, our friend group planned a girls’ overnight beach trip.

This was a big deal for me because it was the first time I’ve ever left my child overnight. My husband is very involved and does a lot, but he travels frequently for work, so I’ve never really been away from my child for more than a few hours. I was really looking forward to finally being completely kid-free for the first time in two years.

My friend decided to bring her child on the trip. No one objected, that wasn’t the issue.

The issue is that she has a habit of mentally checking out and letting the rest of us end up parenting her child. She’ll scroll on her phone, wander off, or just disengage, and her kid ends up gravitating toward whoever is nearby.

On this trip, that person was me.

Her child kept asking me to play, walk, and do typical toddler things. Normally I wouldn’t mind helping out for a few minutes, but I specifically left my own child at home so I could have one night where I wasn’t “on.” Meanwhile, my friend was glued to her phone.

I tried redirecting her child by saying, “Why don’t you ask your mom to play?” Without even looking up, she said, “No, you have more patience with him anyway. You play with him.”

That’s when I got really upset.

I told her I left my child at home because I wanted to be kid-free and not babysit someone else’s. She rolled her eyes at me, and I snapped. I told her to stop being lazy and watch her own kid.

She was furious. The rest of the group agreed with me and backed me up, but she’s still very angry, and now I’m wondering if I crossed a line.

Was I wrong for speaking up the way I did?
