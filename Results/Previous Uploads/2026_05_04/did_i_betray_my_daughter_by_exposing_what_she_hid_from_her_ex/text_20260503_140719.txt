Did I betray my daughter by exposing what she hid from her ex?

I am in the middle of a massive family fallout right now, and I honestly cannot tell whether I did the right thing or completely crossed a line. My daughter and her former partner, whom I will call Daniel, are both in their mid twenties now. They became parents very young and spent years building a life together. They never married, although his family constantly pushed for it because of their strict religious beliefs. I heard more than one comment over the years about them “living in sin” because they were unmarried.

Everything fell apart about a year ago when my daughter discovered Daniel had been unfaithful. During the chaos that followed, it also came out that he was attracted to men. The breakup was ugly, emotional, and painful for everyone involved, especially with a child in the middle of it. In the end, they settled on shared custody.

What happened next was heartbreaking. Daniel’s family reacted horribly when they found out about his sexuality. They completely rejected him. I was there helping my daughter pack her things when I heard his own mother scream hateful insults at him. It was one of the ugliest things I have ever witnessed. After that, his family cut him off entirely.

Despite disowning their own son, they still expected access to their grandchild. Daniel refused, understandably. At one point, they even contacted me directly while I was babysitting, asking if they could see the child without him knowing. I refused immediately because I thought it was completely inappropriate.

The custody arrangement has been difficult for my daughter emotionally. She struggles with missing holidays and important moments when the child is with Daniel. Sometimes Daniel has been flexible, allowing her extra time for special occasions, which I thought was generous considering how tense things had been between them.

Then recently, my daughter came by my house to pick something up and showed me photos from a weekend outing. In the background, I recognized Daniel’s mother and other members of his family. I was shocked and asked what they were doing there. That is when she casually admitted she had been secretly taking their child to visit them for months.

I immediately asked whether Daniel knew they had reconnected, assuming maybe there had been some reconciliation. She told me no, that he had no idea.

I was furious.

I told her it was deeply wrong to hide this from him, especially given how cruel those people had been to their own son. To me, it felt unforgivable that she would willingly bring her child around people who had rejected and humiliated their own family member over his identity. She became defensive immediately, saying they still deserved a relationship with the child. The argument escalated fast, and I finally told her I was disgusted by what she was doing.

Afterward, I made the decision to tell Daniel everything.

He thanked me for being honest with him, but the fallout has been intense. My daughter is livid with me now. Apparently Daniel has stopped being flexible with custody arrangements after learning the truth, which means she no longer gets extra time with the child outside the official schedule.

Now my daughter blames me for damaging what little cooperation they still had left, while I feel like I could not stay silent about something so serious. Still, part of me wonders whether I should have stayed out of it entirely instead of stepping into the middle of their parenting situation.
