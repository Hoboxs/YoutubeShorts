Was I Wrong for Refusing to Finish My Story After My Wife Kept Interrupting Me?

I was talking to my wife one evening and trying to tell her a funny story from my day. It wasn’t anything important, just something I thought she’d enjoy hearing.

The problem was that I could barely get through it because she kept interrupting me. Every time I started building up to the point of the story, she would jump in with a comment, question, or something related that she wanted to say. It happened five separate times. I know that sounds oddly specific, but I actually counted because it was becoming frustrating.

After the first couple of interruptions, I tried to continue. After the third and fourth, I was getting annoyed. By the fifth time, I had completely lost my train of thought and honestly wasn't interested in telling the story anymore.

I finally said something like, “Why don’t you just tell me what you want to say since it seems more important?”

She immediately realized I was upset and started apologizing. She said she wasn’t trying to be rude and asked me to finish the story. She apologized several more times and kept saying she genuinely wanted to hear it.

But by then the moment was gone. The story was supposed to be a funny conversation, and after being interrupted over and over, I just wasn’t in the mood anymore. I told her I didn’t want to finish it.

She continued apologizing and asked me again to tell her what happened, but I refused. Eventually she got quiet and looked like she was about to cry. I could tell she felt genuinely bad about it.

Now I’m wondering if I took it too far. I was frustrated and felt like I wasn't being listened to, but she did apologize repeatedly once she realized what she had done. At the same time, I don’t think I should be obligated to restart a story after the conversation has been derailed multiple times.

Was I wrong for refusing to finish the story after being interrupted so many times?
