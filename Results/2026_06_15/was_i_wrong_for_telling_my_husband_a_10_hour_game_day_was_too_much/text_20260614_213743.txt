Was I Wrong for Telling My Husband a 10-Hour Game Day Was Too Much?

I’m a 31-year-old woman and have been married to my husband, who is 37, for nearly a decade. Overall, we have a good marriage, and I genuinely like that he has close friends and hobbies outside of our relationship.

Every couple of weeks, he gets together with the same group of friends. They usually spend the evening playing tabletop games at someone’s house. Their gatherings normally run from around 7:00 p.m. until after midnight. I’ve never had an issue with that. It’s his time to relax, and I think maintaining friendships is important.

The complication is that we currently share one vehicle. A few months ago, his car finally died, and replacing it hasn’t been financially possible yet. Because of that, we both rely on the same car for errands, appointments, and family responsibilities.

One of those responsibilities is helping care for my younger brother, who has special needs. On weekends, I often take him out to activities because he spends most weekdays at home and really looks forward to getting out of the house. Having access to transportation makes a huge difference for him.

Earlier this week, my husband asked whether I minded if he spent Saturday with his friends. I said that was fine because I didn’t have any specific plans yet.

Then he told me he would be leaving around noon and wouldn’t be back until about 10:00 that night.

That surprised me because his game nights are normally much shorter. When I asked why it would take the entire day, he explained that the group planned to attend a gaming event during the afternoon and then continue with their usual tabletop session afterward.

I asked whether he could ride with one of his friends so that I could keep the car. He said that wouldn’t work and that he needed to drive himself.

I told him that taking our only vehicle for ten straight hours felt excessive, especially when it would leave me and my brother stuck at home all day. I suggested shortening the outing or finding some kind of compromise so that both of us could use the car that weekend.

He didn’t react well.

He argued that he had missed the previous two gatherings and deserved a full day with his friends. The thing is, he didn’t miss those events because of me. One weekend was canceled because a friend was traveling, and the other never happened for unrelated reasons.

From my perspective, the issue isn’t that he wants to spend time with his friends. I’ve always supported that. The issue is that we share one vehicle, and his plans would make it unavailable for nearly the entire day.

The argument eventually became frustrating because I felt less like a spouse discussing logistics and more like a parent telling a teenager he couldn’t spend all day with his friends. I don’t want that dynamic in my marriage. I want us to be able to discuss practical limitations and find compromises without it turning into a fight.

My husband thinks I’m being unreasonable and controlling. I think expecting access to our only transportation for part of the day is a fair request.

Was I wrong for pushing back on the 10-hour outing?
