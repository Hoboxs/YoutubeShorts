Was I Wrong for Telling My Parents Their Advice Came Too Late?

When I finished high school, my parents made a huge deal out of it. There was a celebration, speeches, photos, and plenty of talk about how proud they were of me. I left for university feeling like I had a strong support system behind me.

That feeling disappeared pretty quickly.

My first year was rough. I was living on my own for the first time, struggling academically, and having a hard time adjusting socially. There were days when I felt completely overwhelmed. More than once, I called my mother in tears looking for support. Both times, she basically told me I would figure it out because I was smart and independent. The conversations ended almost as quickly as they started.

My father wasn’t much more involved. Outside of the occasional holiday or birthday message, we barely spoke. It felt like once I moved into my residence, I stopped being part of their daily lives.

There were financial issues as well. Before I left for school, I was under the impression that certain expenses would be covered. Once I got there, I learned that wasn't actually the case. Rather than argue about it, I found ways to make it work through part-time jobs, student loans, and cutting expenses wherever possible.

The moment that really changed things happened during my second year. I went through a difficult personal situation and desperately needed someone to talk to. I reached out to my mother, and she told me she had a lot going on and that we would talk later.

We never did.

After that, something shifted in me. I stopped expecting support, stopped asking for help, and accepted that I was responsible for handling my own problems. It wasn't what I wanted, but it felt easier than being disappointed over and over.

Now I'm in my final year and living at home temporarily to save money. Suddenly, both of my parents are taking a huge interest in my life. They ask about my schedule, my career plans, my finances, my sleep habits, and every decision I make. My father constantly sends me articles and career advice. My mother regularly tells me what she thinks I should be doing differently.

For weeks, I kept my thoughts to myself because I didn't want unnecessary conflict.

Then one evening at dinner, my mother commented that I needed to be more focused about my future. My father immediately agreed and started offering advice.

Something in me finally snapped.

I told them it was difficult to take guidance from people who weren't there when I actually needed it. I reminded them about the times I called looking for help and support and got brushed off. I said I spent years figuring things out alone because I had no other choice. I told them that I didn't understand why they suddenly wanted to parent me now when they seemed uninterested during the years I was struggling the most.

The table went silent.

My mother started crying. My father said I was being unfair and disrespectful. I told them I was simply being honest and left the room.

Since then, several relatives have reached out. Most of them agree that my parents love me and probably thought they were encouraging independence rather than abandoning me. I don't doubt that they love me. That's not really what I'm upset about.

What hurts is that when I actively reached out for support, I felt ignored. Now that I've learned how to manage without them, they're suddenly full of opinions about how I should live my life.

Part of me feels guilty for making my mother cry. Another part feels like I finally said something that had been building up for years.

Was I wrong for telling them their advice came too late?
