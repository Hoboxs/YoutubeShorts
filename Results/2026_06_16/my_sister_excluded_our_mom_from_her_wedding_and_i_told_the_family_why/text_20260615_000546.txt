My Sister Excluded Our Mom From Her Wedding, and I Told the Family Why.

I’m a 42-year-old man, and my younger sister is getting married this year. Our mother has dementia following a stroke. While she has memory and cognitive issues, she is still physically capable and travels regularly. In fact, I take her on trips often because she has always loved seeing new places.

When my sister sent out wedding invitations, she invited me but not our mother. At first, she claimed that traveling would be too difficult for Mom. I disagreed because Mom still travels without any major problems.

After several conversations, my sister finally admitted the real reason. She said it was too painful for her to see our mother the way she is now. The strong, dependable woman we grew up with is gone, and she didn’t want that reminder at her wedding.

I told her I didn’t agree, but it was her decision. I also told her that if Mom wasn’t welcome, I wouldn’t be attending either. My sister was upset because she wanted me to play an important role in the ceremony.

A month later, I was talking to one of our aunts while Mom was with me. My aunt mentioned how sad it was that Mom could no longer travel and therefore couldn’t attend the wedding. Confused, I asked who told her that. She said my sister had.

I corrected her. I explained that Mom still travels regularly and that the real reason she wasn’t invited was because my sister found it too difficult emotionally to see her in her current condition.

My aunt was shocked and ended up telling other relatives. The story spread quickly, and now several family members are angry with my sister. Some have even said they may not attend the wedding.

My sister is furious and says I should have kept my mouth shut. She claims I’ve damaged her wedding by telling people the truth.

I didn’t go out of my way to tell anyone. I was asked a direct question and answered honestly.

Was I wrong for telling my family the real reason my mother wasn’t invited?
