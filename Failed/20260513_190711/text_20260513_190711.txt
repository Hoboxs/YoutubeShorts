Was I Wrong for Telling My Friend He Is Ruining Our Group Trip?

I am currently on a three-week backpacking trip in Portugal with a group of friends I have known since university: Ella, Jordan, Priya, and Caleb.

This trip was planned almost a year in advance as a graduation celebration, and it was meant to be our first long international holiday together.

One of my friends, Caleb, has been struggling with his weight and self-esteem for the past couple of years. Some time ago, he opened up to us about binge eating and how it affected his confidence. We tried to be supportive at the time and invited him to join us for workouts, but he did not stick with it for long. After that, he asked us not to bring up his weight anymore because it made him feel pressured, so we respected that and stopped.

When we originally booked the trip, Caleb said he was planning to work on his health before we left. We did not push him, but we were hopeful things might improve by the time we travelled. About a month before the trip, he told us he had not made progress and had actually gained weight instead.

Since arriving, things have been more difficult than expected. Caleb often talks about feeling uncomfortable walking long distances, worries about being judged in public, and avoids certain activities like hiking, sightseeing, and water park plans. He also sometimes refuses to join activities at the last minute, which has disrupted our itinerary more than once.

The situation came to a head when we went to a water park. On the way there, Caleb kept saying he did not think he could go in because he felt insecure about his body and believed people would stare at him. When we arrived, he refused to enter and caused a delay at the entrance while the rest of us were ready to go in.

At that point, I told him it was okay if he did not want to participate, but that the constant complaints and hesitation were starting to affect everyone’s experience on the trip. I also said he should have thought more carefully about whether a trip with this level of activity was right for him before coming.

After that, he left with another friend and went back to our Airbnb. Since then, the group atmosphere has been tense. Two of my friends think I was justified because his behavior has been disruptive, while one thinks I was too harsh and should have been more considerate of his mental health.

We still have several days left on the trip, and things feel awkward between all of us.
