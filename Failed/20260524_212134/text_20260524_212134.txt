My Ex Wanted Me to Change My Convention Costume Because His New Girlfriend Felt “Uncomfortable”

About eight months ago, my ex and I ended our relationship on relatively good terms. It was mutual, there was no cheating or huge betrayal, and since we share the same friend group, we tried to keep things civil.

After the breakup, I started focusing more on myself. I got serious about therapy, cleaned up my eating habits, started working out consistently, and generally became more confident than I had been in years. My friends noticed the difference immediately and kept telling me I seemed happier and more comfortable in my own skin.

One of my biggest hobbies is cosplay. I’ve always been really into gaming and conventions, but in the past I mostly chose costumes that hid most of my face or body because I was self-conscious. Over the last year though, I finally felt confident enough to try something more ambitious and fitted.

A close friend and I spent nearly six months working on matching convention outfits inspired by characters from a popular competitive game. Between sewing, props, makeup tests, and modifications, we poured an insane amount of time and money into them.

A few weeks before the convention, I posted a test photo in our group chat asking for opinions on some finishing details.

Not long after, my ex messaged me privately asking me not to wear the costume because it would supposedly make his new girlfriend uncomfortable. I was confused because I did not even know she was attending the convention with our group. I asked what specifically about the costume was a problem, but he would not explain.

I told him I was sorry if she felt awkward, but I had spent half a year making this costume and was not going to abandon it over someone I had never even met.

So I wore it.

The convention itself went really well. People kept stopping me for photos, complimenting the costume, and asking questions about how I made parts of it. I mostly stayed away from the larger friend group to avoid drama, but we still crossed paths throughout the day.

That is when things got uncomfortable.

My ex’s new girlfriend kept making passive-aggressive comments toward me whenever people complimented the costume or asked for pictures. She would laugh under her breath, whisper to people nearby, or make little remarks clearly meant to insult me without directly starting a confrontation. Everyone around us seemed uncomfortable, but nobody wanted to address it.

Later that evening, a bunch of us went out to dinner together. At one point another friend casually asked the new girlfriend whether she had ever considered trying cosplay herself.

She responded by saying she did not need strangers giving her attention because she already had a boyfriend and was “not desperate.”

The implication was obvious, especially after the comments all day, and I finally lost my patience. I told her to stop acting bitter and insulting me just because I got attention at the convention.

She immediately burst into tears.

My ex jumped in and demanded I apologize to her. I said I would apologize when she apologized for spending the entire day making nasty comments toward me first. The argument escalated fast, other people started taking sides, voices got louder, and eventually the restaurant staff told our whole group to leave.

Now my ex is threatening to leave the friend group entirely unless I apologize to his girlfriend. Some mutual friends are pressuring me to just say sorry so the group can move on, while others think she deliberately provoked me all day and cannot act shocked that I eventually snapped back.

At this point, I honestly do not know whether I should stand my ground or just apologize to keep the peace.
