Did I Go Too Far by Not Telling My Parents I Was in the Hospital?

I live a few hours away from my parents, who are both in their late fifties. Overall, we have a solid relationship. I visit during holidays, we call regularly, and we generally get along well.

The one ongoing issue that has frustrated me for years is how impossible they are to reach by phone.

Neither of them treats their cellphone like an actual communication device. They constantly leave them in random rooms, accidentally mute them, or let the batteries die for entire days. Sometimes I’ll call over and over with no response until many hours later.

I’ve brought it up repeatedly because they no longer even have a home phone. Their cellphones are literally the only way anyone can contact them. Every time I mention it, they laugh it off or act like I’m overreacting.

I’ve told them before, “What if there’s an emergency?” but they never take it seriously.

About a month ago, I got extremely sick from food poisoning and ended up admitted to the hospital for nearly a week. It was awful, although not life-threatening.

During the first couple of days there, I tried calling both of my parents multiple times. No answer at all. Eventually I learned one phone had died and the other had apparently been left somewhere around the house.

At that point, I got irritated and stopped trying.

To be clear, I easily could have contacted my aunt, who lives nearby and talks to them often. She absolutely would have passed the message along immediately. But after years of dealing with this same issue, part of me felt like maybe this was the natural consequence of never answering their phones.

So I chose not to tell anyone else.

Yesterday, during a normal conversation with my mother, I casually mentioned that I had spent a week in the hospital last month.

She panicked and immediately became upset that I had never informed them. I explained that I had tried reaching them several times but couldn’t get through. She said I still should have contacted my aunt because a hospitalization counts as an emergency.

I admitted that I could have done that, but I intentionally didn’t because I wanted them to finally understand why being unreachable all the time is a problem.

That made her even angrier. She accused me of deliberately using my hospitalization to “teach them a lesson” and said I caused unnecessary hurt and worry.

My father thinks both of us are being stubborn about it.

I honestly did not think it was that serious because I recovered completely and was never in actual danger, but now I’m second-guessing whether I handled this immaturely just to make a point.
