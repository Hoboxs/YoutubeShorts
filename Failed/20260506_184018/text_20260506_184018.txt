Was I wrong for refusing to defend my sister after she got kicked out?

My sister and I are both in our twenties, and despite having full-time jobs, we still live with our parents. Honestly, in our area it just makes financial sense. Rent anywhere near the city is outrageously expensive, and neither of us wants to spend most of our income on a tiny apartment or waste hours every week commuting from somewhere cheaper. My parents live close enough to the city that we can both get to work quickly, so staying home has allowed us to save a lot of money.

The arrangement has always come with one major rule though. Nobody is allowed to bring someone home overnight without asking our parents first.

To some people that might sound strict considering we are adults, but there is a reason behind it. Years ago, one of my older brothers brought someone home casually, and the situation turned into a complete disaster. The woman ended up stealing from the house and accidentally starting a small kitchen fire before leaving. Nobody got hurt, but after that my parents completely changed their attitude about strangers staying over.

Ever since then, the rule has been non-negotiable.

Personally, I do not really care. If I am dating someone, I would rather go to their place anyway. I also honestly find it uncomfortable bringing people back to the room I grew up in. So the rule has never affected me much.

My sister, however, absolutely hates it.

She argues with my parents about it constantly and says they are controlling and treating her like a child. My parents always tell her the same thing: if she wants to spend the night with someone, she can stay at their place or get a hotel. Their view is simple. As long as she lives under their roof for free, she follows their rules.

Well, last night everything exploded.

My sister brought a woman home anyway, apparently assuming our parents would not notice. Unfortunately for her, my parents saw the woman leaving early in the morning on the security camera by the front door.

The argument that followed was massive.

My parents completely lost their patience and told my sister she has one month to move out. There was yelling all over the house, accusations flying back and forth, and my sister crying that they were overreacting.

Afterward she came to me expecting me to back her up and convince our parents to change their minds. But I refused.

I told her honestly that she knew exactly what the rule was and why it existed. It was not some surprise policy they suddenly invented. She chose to ignore it anyway, assuming there would not be consequences.

That answer made her furious.

She accused me of secretly enjoying this because I was jealous of her attention from other people and jealous of her appearance, which honestly came out of nowhere. I told her I genuinely did not care who she dates or hooks up with, but I also was not going to pretend she was blindsided when she knowingly broke the one rule my parents care most about.

Now she is angry that I will not stand beside her against our parents, and some relatives are saying I should at least try to help keep the peace. But from my perspective, she created this situation herself and expected everyone else to clean it up afterward.
